# GK

**Category**: Specialist  
**Core Attributes**: Agility, Footwork, Handling, Reactions

> Shot stopping, distribution, and command of the box. The last line of defense and the first point of attack.

## Tactical Habitat

The goalkeeper archetype changes dramatically by system. Different teams may need:

- a build-up spare player
- a box-dominant shot-stopper
- a sweeper behind a high line
- a calmer distributor in possession-heavy structures

That is why goalkeeper evaluation cannot be flattened into generic reflex talk.

## What They Solve

A strong goalkeeper helps a team:

- secure the last line
- support [Build-Up](/Users/solid-snake/Documents/chief-scout/kb/concepts/build-up.md) when needed
- protect space behind aggressive defending
- stabilize the team emotionally in dangerous phases

## Scouting Indicators

- body shape and decision quality under pressure
- range of distribution
- command of box and depth
- reaction quality after structural breakdowns

## Common Limitations

- shot-stopping alone can hide poor sweeping or distribution fit
- some keepers look stronger in deep blocks than high-line systems
- system mismatch is often more important than raw talent ranking
