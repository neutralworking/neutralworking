Engine

Category: Tactical
Core Attributes: Intensity, Pressing, Stamina, Versatility

Tireless work rate. Presses, runs, recovers, and does it again for 90 minutes. The oxygen of the team.

Tactical Habitat

Engines thrive in structures that demand:

  • repeated Gegenpressing actions
  • sharp reactions to a Pressing Trigger
  • recovery into Compactness after aggressive jumps
  • strong Counterpressing Recovery

They are especially valuable in teams that want to sustain pressure rather than just produce one good pressing action.

What They Solve

A real engine helps a team:

  • maintain intensity across phases
  • arrive in the next duel after the first duel is lost
  • keep transitions from becoming open races
  • connect pressing effort to collective recovery

An engine is useful not because they run a lot, but because their running keeps the structure alive.

Scouting Indicators

Useful signs of an engine:

  • reacts quickly after turnovers
  • repeats high-intensity actions without losing coordination
  • recovers lanes, not just distance
  • supports both the first press and the second action
  • maintains useful movement late in matches

Common Limitations

Engines can be overrated when:

  • work rate masks poor positioning
  • they chase every action and damage team shape
  • they lack enough technical quality for possession-heavy teams
  • the role demands more control than intensity
