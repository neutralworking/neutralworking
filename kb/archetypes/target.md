# Target

**Category**: Physical  
**Core Attributes**: Aerial Duels, Heading, Jumping, Volleys

> Dominant in the air, wins aerial duels, and provides a focal point for crosses and set pieces. Physical presence in both boxes.

## Tactical Habitat

Targets are strongest in teams that use:

- direct progression
- crossing and second-ball attacks
- pinned center-backs and last-line occupation
- structured support around knockdowns and rebounds

## What They Solve

A target helps a team:

- secure a route into the attack under pressure
- establish a physical reference point
- create layoff structures for runners
- occupy defenders who would otherwise step out freely

## Scouting Indicators

- protects the ball and body shape under contact
- wins aerial or ground reference duels cleanly
- directs layoff actions into useful zones
- pins defenders even when not receiving

## Common Limitations

- can become static if the team mistakes reference play for sterile long balls
- some targets win duels but do not connect the next phase
- value drops sharply if the surrounding structure does not attack second balls
