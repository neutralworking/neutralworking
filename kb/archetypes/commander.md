# Commander

**Category**: Mental  
**Core Attributes**: Communication, Concentration, Drive, Leadership

> The vocal leader who organizes teammates and drives standards through communication and sheer force of will. The dressing room heartbeat.

## Tactical Habitat

Commanders matter most in structures that demand:

- coordinated pressing off shared cues
- line management under stress
- calm collective reactions in unstable phases
- leadership in high-pressure match states

They are less about one tactical mechanism than about holding the team's behavior together across them.

## What They Solve

A commander helps a team:

- stay synchronized through transitions
- recover shape faster after disruption
- maintain clarity under emotional pressure
- improve the consistency of the whole unit

## Scouting Indicators

- talks and points with purpose
- organizes teammates before the problem fully forms
- stays composed in chaotic phases
- raises collective discipline rather than only their own level

## Common Limitations

- authority without tactical clarity is just noise
- some commanders organize well but lack enough game impact themselves
- leadership value is easiest to misread without context
