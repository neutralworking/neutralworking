# 4-1-2-1-2

http://outsideoftheboot.com/2015/07/07/a-guide-to-the-4-1-2-1-2/


The 4–4–2 diamond (also described as 4–1–2–1–2) staggers the midfield. The width in the team has to come from the full-backs pushing forward. The defensive midfielder is sometimes used as a deep-lying playmaker, but needs to remain disciplined and protect the back four behind him.[[25]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formation_(association_football)#cite_note-25) The central attacking midfielder is the creative player, responsible for picking up the ball, and distributing the ball wide to its full-backs or providing the two strikers with through balls.[[26]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formation_(association_football)#cite_note-auto-26) When out of possession, the midfield four must drop and assist the defence, while the two strikers must be free for the counter-attack.[[26]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formation_(association_football)#cite_note-auto-26) Its most famous example was Carlo Ancelotti's Milan, which won the [2003 UEFA Champions League Final](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2003_UEFA_Champions_League_Final "2003 UEFA Champions League Final") and made Milan runners-up in [2005](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2005_UEFA_Champions_League_Final "2005 UEFA Champions League Final"). Milan was obliged to adopt this formation so as to field talented central midfielder [Andrea Pirlo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andrea_Pirlo "Andrea Pirlo"), in a period when the position of offensive midfielder was occupied by [Rui Costa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rui_Costa "Rui Costa") and later [Kaká](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kak%C3%A1 "Kaká").[[27]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formation_(association_football)#cite_note-27) This tactic was gradually abandoned by Milan after [Andriy Shevchenko](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andriy_Shevchenko "Andriy Shevchenko")'s departure in 2006, progressively adopting a "[Christmas tree](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formation_(association_football)#4–3–2–1_(the_"Christmas_tree"_formation))" formation.
