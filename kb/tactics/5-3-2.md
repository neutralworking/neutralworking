# 5-3-2

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fa/Association_football_5-3-2_formation.svg/110px-Association_football_5-3-2_formation.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Association_football_5-3-2_formation.svg)

5–3–2 formation

This formation has three central defenders, possibly with one acting as a [sweeper](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sweeper_(association_football) "Sweeper (association football)"). This system merges the winger and full-back positions into the [wing-back](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wing-back_(association_football) "Wing-back (association football)"), whose job it is to work their flank along the full length of the pitch, supporting both the defence and the attack.[[79]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formation_(association_football)#cite_note-79) At club level, the 5–3–2 was famously employed by [Helenio Herrera](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Helenio_Herrera "Helenio Herrera") in his [Inter Milan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inter_Milan "Inter Milan") side, influencing many other Italian teams of the era.[[80]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formation_(association_football)#cite_note-80) The [Brazil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brazil_national_football_team "Brazil national football team") team which won the [2002](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2002_FIFA_World_Cup "2002 FIFA World Cup") FIFA World Cups also employed this formation with their wing-backs [Cafu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cafu "Cafu") and [Roberto Carlos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roberto_Carlos "Roberto Carlos") two of the best known proponents of this position.[[81]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formation_(association_football)#cite_note-81)[[82]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formation_(association_football)#cite_note-82)

#### 5–3–2 with sweeper or 1–4–3–2

A variant of the 5–3–2, this involves a more withdrawn [sweeper](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sweeper_(association_football) "Sweeper (association football)"), who may join the midfield, and more advanced full-backs. The [1990](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1990_FIFA_World_Cup "1990 FIFA World Cup") world champion [West German teams](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Germany_national_football_team "Germany national football team") was known for employing this tactic under [Franz Beckenbauer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_Beckenbauer "Franz Beckenbauer"), with his successor [Berti Vogts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Berti_Vogts "Berti Vogts") retaining the scheme with some variations during his tenure.
