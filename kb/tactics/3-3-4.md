# 3-3-4

The 3–3–4 formation was similar to the WW, with the notable exception of having an inside-forward (as opposed to centre-forward) deployed as a midfield schemer alongside the two wing-halves. This formation was commonplace during the 1950s and early 1960s. One of the best exponents of the system was the [Tottenham Hotspur](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C. "Tottenham Hotspur F.C.")'s double-winning side of 1961, which deployed a midfield of [Danny Blanchflower](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Danny_Blanchflower "Danny Blanchflower"), [John White](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_White_(footballer,_born_1937) "John White (footballer, born 1937)") and [Dave Mackay](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dave_Mackay "Dave Mackay"). [Porto](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/FC_Porto "FC Porto") won the [2005–06 Primeira Liga](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_Primeira_Liga "2005–06 Primeira Liga") using this unusual formation under manager [Co Adriaanse](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Co_Adriaanse "Co Adriaanse").
