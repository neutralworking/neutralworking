3–4–3 formation

Using a 3–4–3, the midfielders are expected to split their time between attacking and defending. Having only three dedicated defenders means that if the opposing team breaks through the midfield, they will have a greater chance to score than with a more conventional defensive configuration, such as 4–5–1 or 4–4–2. However, the three forwards allow for a greater concentration on attack. This formation is used by more offensive-minded teams. The formation was famously used by [Liverpool](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liverpool_F.C. "Liverpool F.C.") under [Rafael Benítez](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rafael_Ben%C3%ADtez "Rafael Benítez") during the second half of the [2005 UEFA Champions League Final](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2005_UEFA_Champions_League_Final "2005 UEFA Champions League Final") to come back from a three-goal deficit. It was also notably used by [Chelsea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chelsea_F.C. "Chelsea F.C.") when they won the Premier League under manager [Antonio Conte](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antonio_Conte "Antonio Conte") in the [2016–17 season](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2016%E2%80%9317_Premier_League "2016–17 Premier League") and when they won the [2021 UEFA Champions League Final](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2021_UEFA_Champions_League_Final "2021 UEFA Champions League Final") under [Thomas Tuchel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_Tuchel "Thomas Tuchel").[62](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formation_(association_football)#cite_note-62)[63](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formation_(association_football)#cite_note-63)[64](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formation_(association_football)#cite_note-64)
