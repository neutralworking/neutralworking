# 4-3-1-2

http://theinsidechannel.com/juventus-shift-4-3-1-2/
http://thenewlibero.tumblr.com/post/99078419025/why-the-4-3-1-2-makes-sense-in-the-modern-game

A variation of the 4–3–3 wherein a striker gives way to a central attacking midfielder. The formation focuses on the attacking midfielder moving play through the centre with the strikers on either side. It is a much narrower setup in comparison to the 4–3–3 and is usually dependent on the [attacking midfielder](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Midfielder#Attacking_midfielder "Midfielder") to create chances. Examples of sides which won trophies using this formation were the [2002–03 UEFA Cup](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2002%E2%80%9303_UEFA_Cup "2002–03 UEFA Cup") and [2003–04 UEFA Champions League](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2003%E2%80%9304_UEFA_Champions_League "2003–04 UEFA Champions League") winners Porto under José Mourinho's; and the [2002–03 UEFA Champions League](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2002%E2%80%9303_UEFA_Champions_League "2002–03 UEFA Champions League") and [2003–04 Serie A](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2003%E2%80%9304_Serie_A "2003–04 Serie A")-winning Milan team, and [2009–10 Premier League](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Premier_League "2009–10 Premier League") winners Chelsea, both managed by Carlo Ancelotti. This formation was also adopted by [Massimiliano Allegri](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Massimiliano_Allegri "Massimiliano Allegri") for the [2010–11 Serie A](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Serie_A "2010–11 Serie A") title-winning season for Milan. It was also the favoured formation of [Maurizio Sarri](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maurizio_Sarri "Maurizio Sarri") during his time at Empoli between 2012 and 2015, during which time they won promotion to Serie A and subsequently avoided relegation, finishing 15th in the [2014–15 Serie A](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_Serie_A "2014–15 Serie A") season.
