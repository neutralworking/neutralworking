4–2–2–2 formation

Often referred to as the "magic rectangle" or "magic square",[[33]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formation_(association_football)#cite_note-33) this formation was used by France under [Michel Hidalgo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michel_Hidalgo "Michel Hidalgo") at the [1982 World Cup](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1982_FIFA_World_Cup "1982 FIFA World Cup") and [Euro 1984](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UEFA_Euro_1984 "UEFA Euro 1984"), and later by [Henri Michel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Henri_Michel "Henri Michel") at the [1986 World Cup](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1986_FIFA_World_Cup "1986 FIFA World Cup")[[34]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formation_(association_football)#cite_note-34) and a whole generation, for Brazil with [Telê Santana](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tel%C3%AA_Santana "Telê Santana"), [Carlos Alberto Parreira](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carlos_Alberto_Parreira "Carlos Alberto Parreira") and [Vanderlei Luxemburgo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vanderlei_Luxemburgo "Vanderlei Luxemburgo"), by [Arturo Salah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arturo_Salah "Arturo Salah") and [Francisco Maturana](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Francisco_Maturana "Francisco Maturana") in Colombia.[[35]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formation_(association_football)#cite_note-35) The "Magic Rectangle" is formed by combining two box-to-box midfielders with two deep-lying ("hanging") forwards across the midfield. This provides a balance in the distribution of possible moves and adds a dynamic quality to midfield play.

This formation was used by former Real Madrid manager [Manuel Pellegrini](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manuel_Pellegrini "Manuel Pellegrini") and met with considerable praise.[[36]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formation_(association_football)#cite_note-36) Pellegrini also used this formation while with [Villarreal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Villarreal_CF "Villarreal CF") and [Málaga](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M%C3%A1laga_CF "Málaga CF"). The formation is closely related to a 4–2–4 previously used by [Fernando Riera](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fernando_Riera "Fernando Riera"), Pellegrini's mentor,[[37]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formation_(association_football)#cite_note-37) and that can be traced back to Chile in 1962 who (may have) adopted it from the Frenchman [Albert Batteux](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Albert_Batteux "Albert Batteux") at the Stade de Reims of 50s.

> It is two 6s, two 10s, two number 9s, so our fullbacks do a lot of the work up and down and create the width for us. Sometimes our 10s can sit in the halfspaces or they can pull out wide to create that width. We always need at least one player to create width for the team.

[Denise O'Sullivan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Denise_O%27Sullivan "Denise O'Sullivan"), captain, [North Carolina Courage](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Carolina_Courage "North Carolina Courage")[[38]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formation_(association_football)#cite_note-otb-osullivan-38)

The 4–2–2–2 with a box midfield was deployed by the [North Carolina Courage](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Carolina_Courage "North Carolina Courage") of the [NWSL](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Women%27s_Soccer_League "National Women's Soccer League") from 2017 to 2021, using a front four with freedom to fluidly switch sides and move wide while served by high-playing fullbacks.[[39]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formation_(association_football)#cite_note-39)[[38]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formation_(association_football)#cite_note-otb-osullivan-38)[[40]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formation_(association_football)#cite_note-40) The Courage won the league in three consecutive seasons using the formation and the NWSL playoff championship twice, setting season records in wins, points, and goals scored in the process.[[41]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formation_(association_football)#cite_note-41)[[42]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formation_(association_football)#cite_note-42)

This formation had been previously used at Real Madrid by Vanderlei Luxemburgo during his failed stint at the club during the latter part of the 2004–05 season and throughout the 2005–06 season. This formation has been described as being "deeply flawed"[[43]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formation_(association_football)#cite_note-43) and "suicidal".[[44]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formation_(association_football)#cite_note-44) Luxemburgo is not the only one to use this although it had been used earlier by Brazil in the early 1980s.[[45]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formation_(association_football)#cite_note-45)[[46]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formation_(association_football)#cite_note-vickery-46) At first, Telê Santana, then Carlos Alberto Parreira and Vanderlei Luxemburgo proposed basing the "magic rectangle" on the work of the wing-backs. The rectangle becomes a 3–4–3 on the attack because one of the wing-backs moves downfield.[[47]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formation_(association_football)#cite_note-47)

In another sense, the Colombian 4–2–2–2 is closely related to the 4–4–2 diamond of Brazil, with a style different from the French-Chilean trend and is based on the complementation of a box-to box with 10 classic. It emphasises the triangulation, but especially in the surprise of attack. The 4–2–2–2 formation consists of the standard defensive four (right back, two centre backs, and left back), with two centre midfielders, two support strikers, and two out and out strikers.[[48]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formation_(association_football)#cite_note-48) Similar to the 4–6–0, the formation requires a particularly alert and mobile front four to function. The formation has also been used on occasion by the [Brazil national team](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brazil_national_football_team "Brazil national football team"),[[46]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formation_(association_football)#cite_note-vickery-46)[[49]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formation_(association_football)#cite_note-49) notably in the [1998 World Cup](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1998_FIFA_World_Cup "1998 FIFA World Cup") [final](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1998_FIFA_World_Cup_Final "1998 FIFA World Cup Final").[[50]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formation_(association_football)#cite_note-50)
