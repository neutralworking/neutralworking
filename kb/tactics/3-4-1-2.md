# 3-4-1-2

3–4–1–2 is a variant of 3–5–2 where the wingers are more withdrawn in favour of one of the central midfielders being pushed further upfield into the "number 10" playmaker position. [Martin O'Neill](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Martin_O%27Neill "Martin O'Neill") used this formation during the early years of his reign as Celtic manager, noticeably taking them to the [2003 UEFA Cup Final](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2003_UEFA_Cup_Final "2003 UEFA Cup Final"). [Portland Thorns FC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portland_Thorns_FC "Portland Thorns FC") used a 3–4–1–2 formation to win the [2017 NWSL championship](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2017_NWSL_season "2017 NWSL season"), withdrawing forward [Christine Sinclair](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christine_Sinclair "Christine Sinclair") into the playmaker role rather than moving a midfielder up. The Thorns transitioned to a 4–2–3–1 in 2018 after opposing teams countered the 3–4–1–2 with a stronger midfield presence.[75](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formation_(association_football)#cite_note-75)[76](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formation_(association_football)#cite_note-76)
