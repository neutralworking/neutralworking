# 1-6-3 (Kamikaze)

The 1–6–3 formation was first used by [Japan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japan_national_football_team "Japan national football team") at the behest of General [Yoshijirō Umezu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yoshijir%C5%8D_Umezu "Yoshijirō Umezu") in 1936. Famously, Japan defeated the heavily favoured [Swedish](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sweden_national_football_team "Sweden national football team") team 3–2 at the 1936 Olympics with the unorthodox 1–6–3 formation, before going down 0–8 to Italy. The formation was dubbed the "[kamikaze](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kamikaze "Kamikaze")" formation sometime in the 1960s when former [United States national team](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_men%27s_national_soccer_team "United States men's national soccer team") player [Walter Bahr](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walter_Bahr "Walter Bahr") used it for a limited number of games as coach of the [Philadelphia Spartans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philadelphia_Spartans "Philadelphia Spartans") to garner greater media and fan attention for the struggling franchise.[[](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formation_(association_football)#cite_note-12)
