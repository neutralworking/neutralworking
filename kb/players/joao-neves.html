João Neves, a 21-year-old Portuguese central midfielder, currently plays for Paris Saint-Germain, having joined the club in August 2024 from Benfica for €65 million. Standing at 174 cm and preferring his right foot, Neves has quickly established himself as a key figure in the PSG midfield. He recently extended his contract with the Parisian club until 2030, reflecting his rapid development and importance to the team.

Recent Form

João Neves has demonstrated exceptional form during the 2025/2026 Ligue 1 season, recording 5 goals and 1 assist in 1,035 minutes played, earning an average FotMob rating of 7.39. His performances have seen him score highly on goals and overall rating compared to other central midfielders in the league. Notably, he played a crucial role in PSG's Champions League victory during the 2024-25 season.

His strong contributions have been consistent, as evidenced by his recent match statistics, including a 7.3 FotMob rating in a 2-0 away win against Liverpool on April 14, 2026, and an 8.3 rating with an assist in a 2-0 home victory against Liverpool on April 8, 2026. Neves' market value has nearly doubled since his arrival at PSG, reaching €130 million, positioning him as the eighth most expensive player globally and the top-ranked central midfielder according to January rankings from the CIES Football Observatory. His impressive trajectory also saw him ranked 19th in the 2025 Ballon d'Or rankings.

Tactical Profile

Neves operates primarily as a central midfielder but can also be deployed as a defensive midfielder and occasionally as a right-back. He is recognized for his mature and highly developed mental attributes, combining quickness, agility, and technical giftedness with notable passing and tackling skills. His playing style is characterized by excellent close ball control, quick reactions, and composure, enabling him to retain possession effectively in tight spaces under pressure.

Described as an advanced playmaker, box-to-box midfielder, and deep-lying playmaker, Neves's tactical versatility is a significant asset. He excels in holding onto the ball, delivering strong through balls and key passes, and demonstrating strong concentration and tackling abilities. Despite his relatively slight frame, he performs well in aerial duels. His technical refinement, exceptional ball control, and sharp decision-making allow him to dictate the tempo of matches with crisp passing and intelligent positioning, contributing significantly both in and out of possession. He forms a complementary midfield partnership with Vitinha, known for his power and aggressiveness in ball recovery and his technical quality in possession.

Career Highlights

João Neves's career has been marked by significant achievements at both club and international levels. He was instrumental in Benfica's UEFA Youth League triumph in the 2021-22 season. With Benfica's senior team, he secured the Primeira Liga title in 2022–23 and the Supertaça Cândido de Oliveira in 2023.

Following his move to Paris Saint-Germain, Neves quickly added to his trophy cabinet, winning the Ligue 1, Coupe de France, Trophée des Champions (2024, 2025), and the UEFA Champions League in the 2024–25 season. He also contributed to PSG's FIFA Intercontinental Cup victory in 2025 and was part of the squad that finished as FIFA Club World Cup runner-up in the same year. On the international stage, Neves made his debut for the Portugal national team in 2023 and was a key member of the squad that won the UEFA Nations League in 2025. He notably scored a hat-trick for Portugal in a World Cup qualifier against Armenia on November 16, 2025. His individual accolades include being a finalist for the Golden Boy award in 2023.
