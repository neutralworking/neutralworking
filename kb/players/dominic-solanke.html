Dominic Solanke is a 28-year-old English center forward for Tottenham Hotspur, known for his right-footed play and 187 cm stature.

Recent Form

Dominic Solanke's 2025-2026 season has seen him score 3 goals in 13 Premier League appearances, playing 870 minutes, and registering no assists. Across all competitions for the 2025/2026 season, he has scored 6 goals in 19 matches, accumulating 1028 minutes of play. He also contributed 5 goals in 14 UEFA Champions League games during the same season. A notable individual achievement this season includes winning the Premier League Goal of the Month award in February 2026.

His season has been impacted by injuries, including an ankle injury that kept him sidelined from August 2025 until January 2026, requiring minor surgery in September 2025. He also experienced a knock injury in March 2026 and another ankle injury in February 2026. Solanke himself acknowledged the difficulty of his injury timeline, stating he initially underestimated the extent of his ankle issue.

Tactical Profile

Solanke primarily operates as a striker, but his versatility allows him to also function as an attacking midfielder or winger. He is recognized for his strong work ethic, physical presence, and excellent ball control, demonstrating an ability to retain possession even in tight situations. His playing style is characterized by adaptability, enabling him to lead the attack as a traditional number 9, drop deep to link play, create space for teammates, or exploit defensive gaps by moving wide.

His tactical intelligence is evident in his smart off-ball movement, consistently finding open spaces behind defenders and making well-timed runs. Technically, Solanke possesses impressive dribbling skills, utilizing quick feet and feints to navigate past opponents, and exhibits calmness and accuracy in his powerful and precise shooting from various angles. He has also shown improved aerial prowess, contributing significantly in both open play and set-piece situations. Furthermore, he is effective in a high-pressing system, acting as an outlet during high turnovers, and his overall unselfish play and clever runs are considered well-suited to Tottenham's system under Ange Postecoglou.

Career Highlights

Dominic Solanke joined Tottenham Hotspur in August 2024 and was part of the squad that won the Europa League in 2025, marking the club's first trophy in 17 years. Prior to his move, he had a highly successful 2023-24 season with Bournemouth, scoring 19 Premier League goals and earning the Premier League Player of the Month award in December 2023.

Internationally, Solanke has achieved significant success at youth levels, winning the FIFA U-20 World Cup in 2017, where he was also awarded the Golden Ball as the tournament's best player. He also secured the UEFA European Under-17 Championship title in 2014, finishing as the tournament's joint top goalscorer and being named in the Team of the Tournament. Other accolades include the UEFA Youth League title in 2014–15, the Chelsea Academy Player of the Year award in 2014–15, and the England Men's Youth Player of the Year in 2014. He was also recognized in the EFL Championship Team of the Season and PFA Team of the Year for the 2021–22 season during his time at Bournemouth. In May 2025, he scored his 100th career goal in a Europa League semi-final match.

Personality

Dominic Solanke is characterized by his determination, adaptability, and consistent drive for improvement. He is known for his strong work ethic and team-oriented approach, constantly striving to develop his skills and contribute to the team's success. His calmness and keen game sense are evident in his decision-making on the field. Solanke's desire to win was notably displayed during the 2017 FIFA U-20 World Cup final, where he returned to the pitch after a collision to help his team secure victory. He has publicly stated his commitment to hard work and maintaining fitness.

Condition / Medical History

Dominic Solanke has experienced several injuries throughout his career, with a notable ankle injury impacting his 2025-2026 season at Tottenham Hotspur. This particular ankle issue, which had been a recurring concern, necessitated minor surgery in September 2025, leading to an extended period of absence from August 2025 until January 2026. Other recent ailments include a knock injury in March 2026, another ankle injury in February 2026, a thigh injury in May 2025, and a knee injury from January to March 2025. He also dealt with an illness in December 2024 and an ankle injury in August-September 2024. Solanke has acknowledged the challenges of managing his injury timelines, noting that the full extent of some issues was not immediately clear.
