CountryColombia

ClubDeportes Tolima

PositionForward

Born24 May 2000

The nimble and tricky winger scored on his professional debut this year when rifling an effort into the top corner from the edge of the box in a 3-0 league win over Tigres. He was 16. The younger brother of a professional footballer, Jaminton began his career as a defensive midfielder but was converted into a winger in the Tolima youth ranks and has since operated principally on the left. Direct and with a wicked long-range shot, he stood out for Colombia in the under-17s South American championships in Chile by scoring three goals on the road to World Cup qualification. October’s finals in India will provide another platform for this raw talent to shine.Carl Worswick

October 2018 update One of Colombia’s better players during a disappointing Under-17s World Cup in India last October, Jaminton has been promoted to the Under-20s side preparing for the South American championships in Chile at the start of 2019.

October 2019 update Came on and scored in Tolima’s 4-0 rout of Santa Fe in August, and Jaminton has consolidated himself as a regular in the first-team squad this year. Narrowly missed out on a spot in the Colombia Under-20 World Cup squad.

October 2020 update Has been superb as Tolima’s star player this year, regularly finding the net from outside of the box with his left foot. Won’t be long before he takes the step up.

October 2021 update Made his debut for Colombia at this summer’s Copa América a month after helping club side Deportes Tolima to only their third Colombian league championship. “He’s the best player in the league,” gloated manager Hernán Torres. “He’s out of this world.” Gremio manager Luis Felipe Scolari was convinced, paying $5m for the 21-year-old in August.

September 2022 update Could not prevent Brazilian giants Gremio sliding into the second division despite playing his part in a bold great-escape attempt. Has been ever-present in the Gremio first team as they fight for promotion this year and is still floating around the Colombian national team.
