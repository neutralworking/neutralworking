Snapshot

A centre-back who has become genuinely valuable to Atalanta through his ball-playing ability and calm under pressure. Hien's left foot is a weapon, and he's progressive in his carrying and passing — he's one of the few defenders in Serie A genuinely comfortable on the ball in dangerous situations. Defensively, he's adequate rather than exceptional; he relies on positioning and intelligence rather than explosive athleticism, which means he can be caught out by truly explosive forwards.

Recent Form

Isak Hien joined Atalanta in January 2024, transferring from Hellas Verona, where he had already played 10 Serie A matches in the 2023/2024 season. During the remainder of the 2023/2024 season with Atalanta, he featured in 16 matches. In the current 2025/2026 season, Hien has made 42 appearances across all competitions, contributing one goal and receiving seven yellow cards over 3133 minutes of play.

His recent performances for Atalanta include participation in Serie A, Coppa Italia, and Champions League fixtures, though he sustained a thigh injury in late March 2026 while representing Sweden in a World Cup playoff semi-final against Ukraine. This injury forced him off the pitch after 37 minutes and subsequently ruled him out of the playoff final against Poland. Despite this recent setback, Hien has maintained a solid presence in Atalanta's defense, contributing to 13 clean sheets this season.

Tactical Profile

Isak Hien primarily operates as a centre-back, a position he transitioned to around the age of 21 after previously playing as a striker. He is recognized as a "stopper" and has also been utilized as a ball-playing and ball-winning defender. Standing at 191 cm tall and weighing 88 kg, Hien possesses a strong physical presence, favoring his right foot.

His tactical contributions extend beyond defensive duties, as evidenced by his passing accuracy of 89.56% in the UEFA Champions League during the 2025/26 season. In the same competition, he averaged 0.82 tackles and 5.64 balls recovered per match, highlighting his effectiveness in disrupting opposition play and regaining possession.

Career Highlights

Hien's professional career began with Vasalunds IF in 2017, where he made his senior debut. He later moved to Djurgårdens IF in 2021, and during the 2022 season, he played 17 games and scored two goals in the Allsvenskan before his transfer to Italy. In August 2022, he joined Hellas Verona, making 33 Serie A appearances in his debut season, including a crucial relegation playoff match.

A significant step in his career was his transfer to Atalanta on January 2, 2024, for a reported fee of €9 million, signing a contract until June 2028. Hien earned his first call-up to the Sweden national team in September 2022 and made his full international debut on September 24, 2022. A notable achievement in his career includes winning the Europa League with Atalanta in the 2023/2024 season.

Condition / Medical History

Isak Hien has experienced a series of injuries throughout his career, with several instances of muscle and thigh issues. Most recently, in late March 2026, he sustained a thigh injury during a World Cup qualifying playoff semi-final for Sweden against Ukraine, which led to his early substitution and subsequent absence from the playoff final against Poland. This particular injury was diagnosed as a myotendinous left biceps femoris strain.

His injury history also includes a muscle injury in December 2023, a thigh injury in late September 2025, and other muscle-related setbacks in February-March 2025 and October 2024. He has also missed time due to illness in December 2025 and August 2024, and has faced suspensions due to disciplinary points.
