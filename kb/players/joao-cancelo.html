João Cancelo, the 31-year-old Portuguese full-back, is currently on his second loan spell at Barcelona from Saudi Pro League club Al-Hilal, having rejoined the Catalan side in January 2026 for the remainder of the 2025/26 season. Known for his versatility and attacking prowess, Cancelo continues to be a key figure for both club and country.

Recent Form

João Cancelo has demonstrated strong form since his return to Barcelona in January 2026, featuring in all 27 available matches for club and country in the current year. In the 2025/26 season, he has played 9 matches for Barcelona, scoring one goal. A standout performance in April 2026 against Atlético Madrid saw him play a crucial role in creating a goal-scoring opportunity, completing 42 out of 47 passes, winning 7 possessions, and delivering 3 key passes, earning him a high rating. He also contributed significantly in a match against Levante in April 2026, providing an assist and creating 3 chances, including 2 big chances, over the full 90 minutes.

Tactical Profile

Operating as a right-footed full-back, João Cancelo is highly valued for his "Invertido" (inverted) role, showcasing exceptional versatility across both defensive flanks. He is adept at drifting into midfield, providing defensive solidity, and facilitating smooth transitions from defense to attack. Cancelo functions as an attacking full-back who can orchestrate play like a midfielder, allowing his team to maintain a higher attacking line. His tactical intelligence enables him to select optimal routes and angles, stepping inside or outside as needed, and dictating the tempo of the flank.

Career Highlights

Cancelo's career is marked by significant achievements across multiple top European leagues. He secured a league title and the Supercoppa Italiana with Juventus in the 2018-19 season. During his tenure at Manchester City, he won three Premier League titles and the EFL Cup, playing a pivotal role in their run to the UEFA Champions League final in 2020-21. He also added a Bundesliga title to his accolades during a loan spell with Bayern Munich. On the international stage, Cancelo was part of the Portugal squad that won the inaugural UEFA Nations League in 2019 and reached the 2015 UEFA European Under-21 Championship final. He was also included in Serie A's Team of the Year during his loan at Inter Milan in the 2017-18 season.

Personality

João Cancelo describes himself as a "very impulsive and sentimental" individual who is "easy to read" due to his transparent expression of feelings, noting that such transparency can be challenging in today's demanding world. His strong personality has, at times, been linked to reported "attitude and behavior problems" that contributed to his departures from Manchester City and Valencia. His relationship with former manager Pep Guardiola reportedly deteriorated after he was dropped from the starting lineup. However, recent reports from April 2026 indicate a positive attitude at Barcelona, where he thrives when given trust and freedom to play instinctively.

Condition / Medical History

João Cancelo has experienced several injuries throughout his career. Most recently, he sustained a hamstring injury between September and November 2025. In January 2024, he suffered a strained medial collateral ligament in his left knee, which forced him off the field after just 11 minutes in a match against Las Palmas. Other notable past injuries include a knock in March 2024, bouts of illness in March 2022 and June 2021, a foot injury in September 2020, an ankle injury in January 2020, a broken nose in May 2019, and earlier calf and knee issues in late 2018.
