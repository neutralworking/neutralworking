Álex Grimaldo, a 30-year-old Spanish left-footed defender, currently plays for Bayer Leverkusen. Known for his "Virtuoso" archetype and "Invertido" role, Grimaldo has continued to be a key figure for his club.

Recent Form

During the 2025/2026 season, Álex Grimaldo has maintained a strong performance for Bayer Leverkusen. Across 33 matches played, he has recorded 11 goals and 9 assists, accumulating 2864 minutes on the field. In the Bundesliga alone, he has made 24 appearances, contributing 8 goals and 7 assists. His offensive output is notable, with 46 shots taken and 19 on target, resulting in a shooting accuracy of 41.30%. Grimaldo has also contributed defensively, with his team keeping 14 clean sheets in matches he played, though they conceded 38 goals. He has received 4 yellow cards this season.

Tactical Profile

Grimaldo's tactical profile is characterized by exceptional technical skills and versatility, stemming from his training at Barcelona's La Masia academy. He is adept at ball control, quick passing, and navigating tight spaces, allowing him to play a significant role in chance creation from the left side and control the game's tempo with his vision. While traditionally a left-back or attacking wing-back, he has reportedly transitioned into a central midfield role under Kasper Hjulmand at Bayer Leverkusen in the 2025/2026 season, highlighting his adaptability. He often moves into central positions when approaching the opposition's penalty area, creating shooting opportunities or acting as a target for crosses.

His playing style involves a calm demeanor in possession, enabling Leverkusen to transition quickly from defense to attack. Grimaldo's passing accuracy remains high, even under pressure, with 87% of his passes finding a teammate, and his successful dribble rate of 77% indicates his ability to take on opponents. He is also noted for his ability to shoot from distance and is a strong set-piece taker. However, areas identified for improvement include aerial duels, finishing, tackling, discipline, and overall defensive contribution.

Career Highlights

Álex Grimaldo has amassed a significant collection of team and individual honors throughout his career. With Benfica, he secured four Primeira Liga titles (2015–16, 2016–17, 2018–19, 2022–23), a Taça de Portugal (2016–17), a Taça da Liga (2015–16), and three Supertaça Cândido de Oliveira titles (2016, 2017, 2019).

Since joining Bayer Leverkusen, Grimaldo has continued his success, winning the Bundesliga in 2023–24, the DFB-Pokal in 2023–24, and the DFL-Supercup in 2024–25. Internationally, he was part of the Spain squad that won the UEFA European Championship in 2024. Individually, he has been recognized in the UEFA Europa League Squad of the Season (2018–19), Primeira Liga Team of the Year on multiple occasions, and the Bundesliga Team of the Season (2023–24). He was also named Bundesliga Player of the Month in April 2024.

Condition / Medical History

Grimaldo has experienced several injuries throughout his career, though he is currently listed as fit. Recent injury records indicate some periods of absence in late 2025, including an injury from December 11, 2025, to January 7, 2026, and another from November 9 to November 21, 2025. He also sustained a head injury in October 2025. Earlier in his career, he had a significant knee injury in 2020 that kept him out for over two months, and a longer absence due to an abdominal injury in 2016-2017. His injury history also includes ankle, thigh, and muscular problems.
