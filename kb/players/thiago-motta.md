Motta is usually deployed as either a defensive or central midfielder, but he is capable of playing in various other midfield positions due to his tactical versatility. In the Italian national team, under Prandelli, he was on occasion deployed as a deep-lying playmaker or an attacking midfielder.

His most prominent traits are his ball control, vision and passing range, and he was also praised for his tackling and defensive abilities, strength and aerial ability, also possessing a powerful long-range shot.

"He spent his early career at Barcelona, where he was injury-prone. He also played two and a half seasons with Internazionale before joining Paris Saint-Germain, winning 20 major titles between the three clubs combined.

Born in Brazil, Motta is also a citizen of Italy. He represented the latter national team since 2011, appearing at Euro 2012 and the 2014 World Cup and finishing second in the first tournament."
