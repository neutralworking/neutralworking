Snapshot

A winger with electric feet and the ability to isolate defenders in one-v-one situations through pace and directness. His off-the-ball movement is generally intelligent, and he can arrive in dangerous positions. However, there's inconsistency in his end product — he'll beat defenders and then make poor decisions or deliver underwhelming crosses. His defensive application is also a recurring issue; he doesn't always track runners or press with conviction. He's a squad player with flashes rather than a nailed-on starter.

Recent Form

Dango Ouattara is currently in his first full season with Brentford, having joined the club in August 2025 for a club-record fee. In the 2025/2026 Premier League season, the winger has made 25 appearances, recording 5 goals and 3 assists across 1,808 minutes of play. His performance metrics include an average FotMob rating of 6.95 and 4 yellow cards. Ouattara's contributions place him highly among right wingers in the league for minutes played, goals scored, and starts. He has also generated 0.14 expected goals (xG) per shot and completed 287 successful dribbles from 358 attempts this season.

Tactical Profile

Ouattara is a versatile attacker primarily deployed as a right winger, where he effectively cuts inside onto his preferred left foot. His playing style is characterized by exceptional pace, strong dribbling ability, and a direct approach that consistently threatens opposing defenses. He is known for his "instinctive" play, often acting first and exhibiting a "hard-running, fun-as-hell brand of football". Ouattara excels in attacking defenders aggressively, making sharp runs behind the defensive line, and converting transitional moments into scoring opportunities. Beyond his offensive contributions, he demonstrates a notable defensive work rate, evidenced by an interception rate that ranks in the top 5% among Premier League forwards, indicating his active role in winning back possession. While strong in headed attempts, aerial duels, and finishing, his passing accuracy (57%) is comparatively lower, though this is partly attributed to his tendency to attempt ambitious, vertical deliveries rather than safer sideways passes.

Career Highlights

Ouattara's professional career began in Burkina Faso with Majestic FC before moving to France to join Lorient B in 2020, eventually progressing to the first team. In January 2023, he made a significant move to AFC Bournemouth for a fee of €22.5 million. During his time at Bournemouth, he scored a memorable 95th-minute winner against Tottenham Hotspur in April 2023 and achieved his first professional hat-trick in a 5-0 Premier League victory over Nottingham Forest in January 2025. His impressive performances led to a club-record transfer to Brentford in August 2025, with a fee reaching up to £42.5 million (€42.8 million). On the international stage, Ouattara has been a key player for the Burkina Faso national team since his debut in December 2021, notably scoring the decisive goal against Tunisia in the semi-final of the 2021 Africa Cup of Nations, securing a 1-0 win. He has accumulated over 34 senior caps and 12 international goals for his country.

Condition / Medical History

Dango Ouattara has experienced several injury setbacks throughout his career. His injury history includes multiple instances of knocks, a groin injury (May-July 2025), a muscle injury (October-November 2024), and recurring ankle injuries (September 2024, August 2023, December 2023). He has also faced "health problems" in August 2025. Notably, Ouattara has frequently returned from international duty with injuries. Despite these issues, he is noted for making "very good recoveries".
