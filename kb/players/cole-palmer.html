Recent Form

Cole Palmer has demonstrated a mixed but impactful performance during the 2025/2026 season for Chelsea. Across all competitions, he has made 27 appearances, scoring 10 goals and providing 3 assists. In the Premier League specifically, Palmer has featured in 21 matches, contributing 9 goals and 1 assist.

However, Palmer experienced a period of poor form between March and May 2025, during which he missed a penalty and failed to score in 11 Premier League matches, ending an 18-game goalless streak. Chelsea manager Enzo Maresca attributed these struggles to a "mental thing" at the time. As of January 2026, Palmer was medically "100 percent fine" but was working on regaining full match fitness after a groin issue that had sidelined him for over two months. Despite not being at his peak, he managed to score a penalty in a game during this period.

Tactical Profile

Cole Palmer operates primarily as an attacking midfielder, often deployed in a central number 10 role, but he is also proficient as a winger on either flank. His playing style is characterized by his role as a playmaker, known for his passing, ball-carrying, vision, and attacking intelligence, often described as a "street football style, playing freely and creatively". He frequently cuts inside from wide positions to either shoot or create opportunities for teammates.

Palmer possesses a notable ability to dissect defensive lines with precise, bending through balls, showcasing excellent vision to create chances for runners. His dribbling combines finesse with strategic movement, compelling defenders to retreat rather than relying on sheer pace, and he exhibits a "killer shift" and change of direction, particularly effective in tight central areas. Furthermore, Palmer is a significant goal threat, known for strong finishing, powerful long shots, and proficiency from the penalty spot. He often places and bends his shots into open spaces, making them difficult for goalkeepers to save. Off the ball, Palmer is recognized for his intelligent movement, exploiting open spaces and making well-timed runs in behind defenses. He also contributes defensively by pressing intelligently and aggressively, supporting a high press and contributing to counter-pressing efforts.

Career Highlights

Cole Palmer has accumulated significant club and individual accolades in his young career. With Manchester City, he was part of the squads that won the Premier League (2022-23), FA Cup (2022-23), UEFA Champions League (2023), and UEFA Super Cup (2023). Following his move to Chelsea, Palmer played a pivotal role in their triumphs in the UEFA Conference League and FIFA Club World Cup in 2025, where he was named man of the match in both finals and received the Golden Ball in the latter tournament. He also secured the Community Shield with Chelsea in 2024.

Individually, Palmer's breakout 2023-24 season saw him earn numerous awards, including England's Men's Player of the Year, Premier League Young Player of the Season, PFA Fans' Player of the Year, and PFA Young Player of the Year. He was also recognized as the London Football Awards Premier League Player of the Year in 2025 and received the FIFA FIFPro World XI selection in 2025. Palmer holds the distinction of being the youngest player to score four goals in a single Premier League match for Chelsea and recorded the most goal involvements (33: 22 goals, 11 assists) of any player in the 2023-24 Premier League season. Internationally, he was a key member of the England U21 squad that won the UEFA European Under-21 Championship in 2023 and made his senior England debut in the same year, scoring his first senior international goal in June 2024. He was also a participant and runner-up in Euro 2024.

Personality

Cole Palmer is consistently described as a humble and simple individual, a trait that Chelsea manager Enzo Maresca notes has remained unchanged despite his significant success. He is characterized by an "easy-going demeanour" and a "carefree" attitude off the pitch, suggesting he cares deeply about his performance but is unconcerned with external opinions. This contributes to his calm and composed approach to the game, even under pressure. Maresca has also highlighted Palmer's profound love for football, stating that he enjoys the game "like a kid," always eager to have the ball and play.

Condition / Medical History

Cole Palmer has experienced a number of injuries throughout his career, with a recurring groin issue being a notable concern. He has struggled with this groin problem since before the Club World Cup in 2025, leading to several weeks of absence. As of January 2026, while medically declared "100 percent fine," he was actively working on regaining full match fitness, with surgery deemed unnecessary for the groin issue.

His injury history also includes various other ailments such as thigh injuries in January 2026, March 2025, and August/September 2024, and October 2023. He sustained a toe injury in November 2025 and has dealt with illnesses in March 2025 and April 2024. Other recorded injuries include a knee injury in November 2024, ankle injuries in January 2025 and May 2023, and a significant 93-day foot injury in the 2021/22 season. As of early 2025, Palmer had missed 18.5% of matches, totaling 134 days out due to four injuries, primarily muscle issues and viruses.
