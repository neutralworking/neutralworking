Player Research Brief: Bremer

Recent Form

Bremer has experienced a mixed period following his return from a significant ACL injury sustained in October 2024. After a long rehabilitation, he returned to action for Juventus in August 2025. Initially, his performance was strong, and by September 2025, he was noted for leading Juventus in blocks, clearances, recoveries, and interceptions, while also winning the majority of his duels. He was also second in ball progression for the team.

As of February 2026, Bremer had contributed three goals and two assists in the Italian top flight, with Juventus demonstrating improved performance metrics, conceding fewer goals and scoring more, when he was on the pitch. Notably, he scored his 14th and 15th career set-piece goals via headers, making him the defender with the most headed goals in Europe's top five leagues since the 2019-2020 season. However, his season since returning in August 2025 has also been described as challenging due to several injury setbacks.

Tactical Profile

Bremer operates as a central defender, known for his formidable physical strength, acute positional awareness, and exceptional tackling ability. His aerial prowess is a significant asset, and he has demonstrated a "scorer's instinct," netting 13 goals in three full seasons in Italy, with 11 in Serie A, a record for a central defender during that period. During the 2021-22 season, he was an exceptional man-marker, leading Europe's top five leagues in interceptions with 105 and recoveries with 284, alongside winning 217 duels and 132 aerial challenges.

In Juventus's 3-4-2-1 tactical setup, which transitions to a 5-2-3 without possession, Bremer functions as the central figure in the back three. In this role, he is responsible for defending the central areas, reading the game, anticipating plays, and winning critical duels. His presence allows Juventus to commit more players forward, as he effectively wins first balls and prevents opponents from building attacks. He also poses an offensive threat from set pieces, having scored three goals from such situations in the current season (as of February 2026).

Career Highlights

Bremer joined Torino in 2018, where he made 110 appearances, starting 105 of those matches. His standout performances earned him the Serie A Best Defender award for the 2021-22 season. In July 2022, he transferred to rival club Juventus on a five-year deal. He scored his first goal for the Bianconeri in September 2022 and extended his contract with the club until 2028 in December 2023.

He played a crucial role in Juventus's 1-0 victory against Atalanta in the Coppa Italia final in May 2024, playing the full match. On the international stage, Bremer received his first call-up to the Brazil national team in September 2022, making his debut against Ghana. He was part of Brazil's squad for the 2022 FIFA World Cup, featuring in matches against Cameroon and South Korea, and was also included in the squad for the 2024 Copa América, although he did not make an appearance in the tournament.

Condition / Medical History

Bremer suffered a significant anterior cruciate ligament (ACL) injury in October 2024, which necessitated surgery and a lengthy period of rehabilitation. He was out of action until August 2025, when he returned to the field. While he showed strong form upon his return, the current season (2025-2026) has been marked by several injury setbacks. His injury history also includes a meniscus injury from October to December 2025, and muscle injuries in February 2026 and September-October 2025. Concerns about his fitness persist, with speculation that his ability to remain fit will be a key factor for potential suitors in the transfer market.
