## Scouting Notes


ClubRed Bull Salzburg

PositionMidfielder

Born23 June 2003

When Maurits Kjærgaard was 16, he moved from Lyngby to Red Bull Salzburg. It was a calculated career step. He knew he needed to move from a club focused on attack to the high-intense, counterattacking style of Red Bull Salzburg. He was immediately sent out on loan to the feeder club FC Liefering. According to the Denmark Under-18 national coach, Lars Knudsen, always looking to improve, to adapt his play, is one of Kjærgaard’s strengths. “If he wants to become a really good player, he needed to challenge himself – and he has succeeded. He is the youngest player in the starting lineup, and he has played 90 minutes for the last eight to 10 games,” says Knudsen. The midfielder is tall – 1.89m – has a good physique but he is not a bulldozer. He does a lot of running, has a good football brain, scores goals and creates chances. Although still only 17, he has featured twice for the Under-20 national team. **[Thomas Lund Hansen](https://twitter.com/thomaslund71?lang=en)**  
[Watch Maurits Kjærgaard in action](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=b1PEr3r_pVg)  
  
**October 2021 update** One of five youngsters who were called back from Liefering to Red Bull Salzburg and is currently dividing his time between the first team and the reserves. Has made his under-21 debut for Denmark.  
  
**September 2022 update** It is probably fair to say that the youngster has had his breakthrough now, becoming a regular starter for Salzburg at the age of 19. The attacking midfielder has repaid the trust with goals and assists.  
  
**October 2023 update** Has continued to develop and has, at the time of writing, a remarkable 73 first-team appearances for RB Salzburg. Captain of the Denmark Under-21 side, he was named Talent of the Year in his home country in February. Previous winners include Simon Kjaer, Christian Eriksen, Pierre-Emile Höjbjerg and Mikkel Damsgaard. "There are some incredibly big games on that list," he said. "But, as always, I won't put myself under any pressure."
