## Snapshot

A veteran Portuguese centre-forward operating in the lower tiers, Beto still possesses the hold-up play fundamentals and positional awareness that sustained a long career, but at 43 his mobility is predictably shot and he's well past the point where he can impose himself on defenders through explosive movement or aggression. He remains a functional target man for a side without pretensions, capable of linking play and winning aerial duels in a lower-division context, but offers nothing beyond that—no dribbling threat, no press resistance, no ability to play off the shoulder and turn. He's a relic, useful only in the most modest of settings.
