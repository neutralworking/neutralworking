Jeremy Doku: Player Research Brief

Recent Form

Jeremy Doku has established himself as a key member of Manchester City's squad during the 2025-2026 season, demonstrating improved consistency and versatility. As of April 1, 2026, he has contributed 3 goals and 12 assists in 36 appearances across all competitions, showcasing a significant step forward in his overall game. His performances in major fixtures, including matches against Liverpool, Arsenal in the Carabao Cup final, and Real Madrid, have been particularly notable.

Doku also made a strong impact in the FIFA Club World Cup in June 2025, scoring two goals. For the Belgian national team, he played a crucial role in securing their spot at the 2026 World Cup, scoring his first brace against Kazakhstan and another against Liechtenstein in late 2025. More recently, he scored a goal and provided an assist in Manchester City's 3-0 victory over Chelsea on April 12, 2026. In the latter half of the 2024-2025 season, Doku set a Premier League record with 13 successful dribbles against Liverpool on February 23, 2025, and delivered a "brilliant" performance against Ipswich Town in January 2025, scoring and assisting a goal.

Tactical Profile

Jeremy Doku is a dynamic winger renowned for his exceptional speed, explosive acceleration, and elite dribbling ability. Pep Guardiola has lauded him as "the best player in the world in the first few meters" and "unstoppable in the final third". Doku is adept at playing on both the left and right wings, and in the 2025-2026 season, he has increasingly drifted into central areas, enhancing his threat and making him a more challenging opponent for defenders.

His playing style is characterized by flair and trickery, frequently employing shoulder drops and drag-back spins to bypass defenders, maintaining close control and balance even at high speeds. Beyond his individual brilliance, Doku possesses excellent vision and crossing ability, with over 80% pass completion, and demonstrates strong off-the-ball movement to find space and create opportunities for teammates. While historically criticized for a lack of end product, his focus is on being a dangerous performer who creates space for others, a quality Guardiola appreciates despite noting he may not be a prolific goalscorer.

Career Highlights

Jeremy Doku began his professional career at Anderlecht, making his debut at the age of 16 in November 2018. He later moved to Rennes in October 2020 for a club-record fee of €26 million. In August 2023, Manchester City secured his services for €65 million.

In his debut season with Manchester City (2023-2024), Doku was part of the squad that won the Premier League title. His trophy cabinet with Manchester City also includes the Club World Cup, EFL Cup, UEFA Super Cup, and Community Shield. On the international stage, Doku has been a regular for Belgium since 2020, representing his nation at UEFA Euro 2020, the 2022 FIFA World Cup, and Euro 2024. He has accumulated 41 caps and scored 7 goals for Belgium as of April 1, 2026.

Personality

Jeremy Doku exhibits strong confidence in his dribbling abilities, a trait he attributes to his success on the pitch and his upbringing playing street football. He believes the Premier League is a "perfect match" for his style of play and thrives under pressure. Doku is described as a player who demands self-improvement and is receptive to coaching, valuing strong performances and creating opportunities for his team over individual statistics.

He maintains a deep connection to his Ghanaian heritage, having grown up in a Ghanaian household in Belgium and speaking the Twi language. Doku has publicly stated that his improved performances are linked to "having God in his life" and his baptism after a Manchester derby. He appreciates Pep Guardiola's guidance, particularly on tactical details, while also being allowed to express his natural playing style.

Condition / Medical History

Jeremy Doku has a history of injuries throughout his career, primarily involving hamstring, calf, and other muscle issues. He has missed approximately 31.8% of matches due to these setbacks.

In the current 2025-2026 season, Doku experienced a calf injury from late January to late February 2026, which Pep Guardiola noted was a recurrence but in a different part of the calf. Earlier in the same season, he also sustained a leg injury in December 2025, sidelining him for a few weeks. During the 2024-2025 season, Doku missed nearly a month due to an unspecified injury in January-February 2025, and also dealt with muscle issues in late 2024. Despite his injury history, his rate of injuries at Manchester City has reportedly "slightly decreased".
