Snapshot

Recent Form

During this period, he has not scored or assisted any goals, and has received two yellow cards. His average FotMob rating for the season stands at 6.54. He also played 90 minutes in Morocco's 2-1 win against Paraguay and 1-1 draw against Ecuador in late March 2026.

Tactical Profile

Issa Diop is recognized as a towering and athletic central defender, standing at 1.94 meters (6 ft 4 in). He is noted for being "a tremendous athlete, excellent in the air and quick across the ground". Diop primarily operates as a right-sided centre-back. His playing style is characterized by strong aerial duel capabilities.

His defensive attributes are highlighted by his EA Sports FC 26 ratings, which include high scores for physicality (Strength 87, Jumping 82) and defending (Interceptions 77, Heading Accuracy 77, Defensive Awareness 77, Standing Tackle 76, Sliding Tackle 75). While strong in defensive aspects, some analyses suggest that passing is a relative weakness in his game.

Career Highlights

Issa Diop began his professional career at his hometown club, Toulouse, making his senior debut at 18 in November 2015. He quickly established himself, becoming the club's captain in 2017. After 95 appearances and six goals for Toulouse, Diop transferred to West Ham United in June 2018 for a reported club-record fee of £22 million.

During his time at West Ham, he made 121 appearances and scored eight goals. A significant career achievement came with the France U19 national team, where he won the 2016 UEFA European Under-19 Championship, scoring in the final and being named in the Team of the Tournament. Diop joined Fulham in August 2022 on a five-year contract.

Condition / Medical History

Issa Diop has experienced several injury setbacks throughout his career, particularly in recent seasons. In the 2025/2026 season, he has dealt with multiple knock injuries, leading to a total of 51 days of absence and missing seven matches. The 2024/2025 season saw him miss 28 days due to an unknown injury, resulting in four missed games.

The 2023/2024 season was significantly impacted by injuries, including calf problems (36 days, 5 missed games), a hamstring injury (10 days, 1 missed game), and a foot injury that kept him out for 69 days and nine matches. Earlier in his career, Diop was notably the first concussion substitute in English football in February 2021 following a head injury. He also tested positive for COVID-19 in September 2020.
