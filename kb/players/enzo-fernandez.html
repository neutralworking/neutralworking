Recent Form

Enzo Fernández has demonstrated strong form for Chelsea during the 2025-2026 season, contributing significantly to the team's attacking and midfield play. By February 2026, he had already registered 4 goals and 2 assists, building on an impressive 2025 where he led Chelsea in goals, assists, goal contributions, chances created, and successful passes into the final third. His ability to score crucial goals in decisive moments has been noted, including a 94th-minute winner in a match and a goal in Chelsea's 4-1 victory over Real Betis in the Conference League final in the 2024-2025 season.

Fernández has been a consistent presence in the Chelsea squad, making 46 appearances across all competitions in the 2025-2026 season, scoring 12 goals and providing 6 assists. He has been particularly effective in the Premier League, with 8 goals and 3 assists in 30 matches, and in the UEFA Champions League, contributing 3 goals and 2 assists in 10 appearances. His performances have seen him recognized for his ability to dictate tempo and make things happen for Chelsea's attack.

Tactical Profile

Enzo Fernández operates primarily as a central midfielder, showcasing versatility that allows him to play as a deep-lying playmaker, a box-to-box midfielder, or even in a more advanced attacking role. He is noted for his exceptional vision, accurate long and short passes, and ability to break defensive lines with his distribution. His press resistance and comfort in receiving the ball in tight spaces enable him to maintain possession and control the tempo of the game.

Defensively, Fernández is combative in midfield duels, effectively breaking up play and recycling possession. He exhibits strong positional awareness and a high work rate, proactively disrupting opposition attacks and intercepting passes. His athleticism allows him to cover significant ground, contributing to both defensive duties and supporting offensive movements, including making runs into the opponent's penalty area. Chelsea's managers have utilized his versatility, deploying him deeper to set the tempo or in more advanced roles to leverage his scoring instincts.

Career Highlights

Enzo Fernández has accumulated several significant achievements in his relatively young career. He was a key player for Argentina in their 2022 FIFA World Cup victory, where he also earned the tournament's Young Player Award. His impact in the World Cup final was notable, with the most touches, most completed passes, and most tackles of any player on the pitch. He further added to his international accolades by winning the 2024 Copa América with Argentina, providing two assists during the tournament.

At club level, Fernández joined Chelsea in January 2023 for a British-record transfer fee after an impressive six-month spell with Benfica. During his time at Benfica, he quickly became a starter and was named the Liga Nos' best midfielder in August, October, and November 2022. With Chelsea, he has been part of teams that won the 2025 Conference League and the 2025 FIFA Club World Cup, scoring in both finals. Prior to his move to Europe, he won the Copa Sudamericana and Recopa Sudamericana with Defensa y Justicia, and the Argentine Primera División with River Plate.

Personality

Enzo Fernández is recognized for his strong character and natural leadership qualities, which have seen him take on significant responsibilities at Chelsea despite his young age. He has been described as having an "alpha" personality and is seen as a central figure in Chelsea's leadership group. Fernández himself has expressed a long-held dream of being a leader for a major club and an example to his teammates.

However, his strong personality has also been linked to controversy. In July 2024, a video surfaced showing Fernández and other Argentine players chanting alleged racist remarks about French national team players after their Copa América triumph, leading to criticism and an internal disciplinary procedure at Chelsea. More recently, in April 2026, he faced a two-game internal suspension from Chelsea for comments regarding his future and potential transfer links to Real Madrid, though he has since been reintegrated into the squad.

Condition / Medical History

Enzo Fernández has experienced several periods of injury and illness throughout his career. In the 2025-2026 season, he dealt with an illness in January 2026, which caused him to miss training and raised concerns about his fitness for upcoming matches. Earlier in the 2025-2026 season, in October and November 2025, he suffered from knee injuries, including synovitis and a bone edema, which led to him returning early from international duty. He had been managing a knee problem for four months prior to November 2025.

In the 2024-2025 season, Fernández underwent surgery for a groin issue in April 2025, sidelining him for the remainder of that season. He also experienced a thigh injury in July 2025 and a knock injury in January 2025. Other reported issues include illness in September 2024 and a muscle injury in December 2023. Despite these setbacks, he has generally maintained a high volume of play, often featuring in a significant number of games for Chelsea.
