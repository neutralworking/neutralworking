## Snapshot


ClubBologna

PositionMidfielder

Born7 September 2004

At the age of 15 Urbanski became the second youngest player to make his debut in the Ekstraklasa in December 2019. He was scouted by several clubs with Everton and Wolfsburg showing early interest. Covid stopped an early move but Bologna's manager Sinisa Mihajlovic felt he had seen enough to bring him to the Serie A club without even going to see him in the flesh. Urbanski moved to Italy with his family – his younger brother Dominik is reportedly an even more talented footballer – having learned Italian for a few months before. He has not been shy on arrival in Italy, to the extent that the goalkeeper Lukasz Skorupski was asked by his teammates how old this kid really is. Urbanski made his Bologna debut late last season aged 16 years and eight months, making him the sixth youngest stranieri ever in the top flight and youngest Pole to play in one of the top five European leagues. **[Maciej Slominski](https://twitter.com/maciejslominski?lang=en)**  
  
**September 2022 update** Continues to be on the radar of the first-team coach, Sinisa Mihajlovic, and was included in the pre-season training camp before making the bench for the first three Serie A games of the season.  
  
**October 2023 update** Huge progress. Finished the 2022-23 season with nine goals to his name for Bologna's Primavera team. Promoted to the first squad in the summer and has made two substitute appearances in Serie A already, contributing to the 2-1 win over Cagliari. Very highly rated by the coach Thiago Motta.
