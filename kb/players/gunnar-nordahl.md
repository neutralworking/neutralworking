## Snapshot

CF		Striker			SWE	B	0.0	A highly prolific, powerful, and physically strong striker, with an eye for goal.		Possibly the greatest striker to ever to come out of Sweden, Gunnar Nordahl formed part of a prolific Swedish trio at AC Milan in the late 1940s.  He hold numerous goalscoring records in Italian football including a club record total for Milan and more 'Capocannoniere' awards as Serie A's leading goalscorer than any other player.  Although his international career was interrupted by turning professional, Nordahl also won an Olympic gold medal in 1948. He is best known for his spell at A.C. Milan from 1949 to 1956, in which he won the Scudetto twice, and also the title of pluricapocannoniere, with an unprecedented five top scorer (Capocannonieri) awards, more than any other player in the history of the Italian championship. He is considered one of the greatest Swedish football players of all-time.
