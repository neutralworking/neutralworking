Christian Pulisic, the 27-year-old American winger for AC Milan, has experienced a significant career resurgence since his move to Italy in the summer of 2023. Known for his right-footed play, Pulisic's internal archetype is a Virtuoso, operating as a Wide Playmaker.

## Recent Form
Christian Pulisic has enjoyed a period of strong performance since joining AC Milan, marking a rejuvenation in his career. The 2023/24 season saw him achieve a career-high league goal tally and finish as AC Milan's top scorer across all competitions with 17 goals and 10 assists, including nine league assists, placing him second in Serie A. His impressive form earned him a spot on the 2023-24 Serie A Team of the Season.

Continuing his impact, Pulisic won the Italian Super Cup with AC Milan in the 2024/25 season, contributing an equalizer and a critical game-winning pass. He was recognized as Serie A Player of the Month in December 2024. In the current 2025/26 season, Pulisic had a strong start, scoring seven Serie A goals in nine matches and nine goals in 12 games across all competitions by December 2025, at one point leading the league in scoring alongside Lautaro Martinez. Notably, he surpassed Clint Dempsey's record for most goals by an American in Europe's top five leagues with a brace against Udinese in September 2025. However, his form has seen a recent dip, with reports of a nine-game goal drought in Serie A and no goal contributions in five appearances since the start of 2026.

## Tactical Profile
Pulisic primarily operates as a right winger within AC Milan's 4-2-3-1 or 4-3-3 formations, though his role is dynamic. He functions as an inverted winger, often cutting inside to leverage his strong right foot and operate in the half-space between the opposition's full-back and centre-back, facilitating combinations with strikers and attacking midfielders. His positioning between the lines allows him to receive progressive passes, and his heatmap indicates significant activity in the right half-space and inside-right channel, with frequent diagonal runs into central attacking areas, positioning him closer to the striker than a traditional touchline winger.

His playing style is characterized by speed, agility, technical ability, and keen attacking instincts, making him a potent threat in transitions due to his acceleration and control during counter-attacks. Pulisic has evolved into a more complete attacker, demonstrating efficiency in transitions and tactical intelligence. Under new manager Massimiliano Allegri, he has been deployed in various forward roles, including occasionally as a striker, and has adapted to operate closer to goal, drifting into left-sided half-spaces, which has contributed to a higher goal-per-minute ratio. Furthermore, he contributes defensively, ranking among Milan's top three attackers for pressing actions per 90 minutes.

## Career Highlights
Christian Pulisic has accumulated a distinguished list of achievements throughout his career. He made history as the first American to both play in and win a UEFA Champions League final in 2021 with Chelsea. His club honors also include the DFB-Pokal with Borussia Dortmund in 2016/17, and the UEFA Super Cup and FIFA Club World Cup with Chelsea in 2021. Most recently, he secured the Italian Super Cup with AC Milan in the 2024/25 season.

On the international stage, Pulisic has been a pivotal figure for the United States Men's National Team (USMNT), leading them to three CONCACAF Nations League titles (2019/20, 2022/23, 2023/24), and earning the Player of the Tournament award in 2023. He is tied with Landon Donovan for the record of four U.S. Player of the Year awards and ranks as the fifth-highest all-time leading goalscorer for the USMNT. Pulisic also holds the distinction of being the youngest player ever to captain the United States national team, achieving this at the age of 20. His transfer to Chelsea in 2019 for $73 million made him the most expensive North American player of all time. During the 2022 FIFA World Cup, he assisted Timothy Weah's goal and scored the winning goal against Iran, earning two Player of the Match awards.

He is described as not being overtly vocal, often soft-spoken in interviews, but when he does speak, his teammates listen. Pulisic is known for his humble demeanor and for treating everyone equally, earning the respect of his peers.

He understands the importance of his leadership role, particularly with the national team, and strives to set a positive example for younger players. Pulisic has shown growth in his leadership, taking responsibility for team performances and demonstrating mental fortitude, thriving under pressure in high-stakes matches.

## Condition / Medical History

In his first season with Milan (2023/24), he missed three games, followed by six games in his second season (2024/25), and has already missed six matches in the current 2025/26 campaign.

Recent injury concerns in the 2025/26 season include a hamstring injury sustained in October 2025, which sidelined him for approximately 25 days, and subsequent muscle issues in November 2025, keeping him out for nearly a month. In February 2026, he was diagnosed with bursitis, an inflammation near his hip, causing him to miss further matches. Other recent issues include a thigh injury in December and October 2025, an illness in December 2025, and muscle discomfort in January 2025 that led to individual training.
