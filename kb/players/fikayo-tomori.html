Fikayo Tomori, the English center-back for AC Milan, continues to be a key figure in his team's defense, known for his aggressive and forward-thinking approach. At 28 years old, he has established himself as a prominent defender in Serie A, contributing significantly to his club's recent successes.

Recent Form

In the 2025-2026 Serie A season, Fikayo Tomori has made 28 appearances for AC Milan, recording two assists but no goals. He has accumulated five yellow cards across these matches. His performance data indicates 2,222 minutes played with an average FotMob rating of 7.09, highlighting his consistent presence and impact on the field. Milan has achieved 15 clean sheets in the 33 matches he has played this season, conceding 21 goals.

Tomori has recently returned from a minor adductor strain in September 2025, which saw him work individually before rejoining the squad and starting against Juventus. He also featured for England in a friendly against Uruguay in March 2026, playing 69 minutes. Despite some recent injury concerns, he has maintained a strong presence in the starting lineup when fit.

Tactical Profile

Tomori is characterized by his impressive speed, hard tackling, and confidence in possession, making him adept at recovery runs and covering for teammates who are out of position. His tactical versatility allows him to operate effectively across various defensive roles, primarily as a right-sided center-back, but also on the left and occasionally as a full-back. He is particularly suited to a high defensive line due to his pace and assertive pressing style, though this aggression can sometimes leave space behind him if not perfectly timed.

He excels in one-on-one defensive situations and possesses a good long-range passing ability, utilizing both feet to switch play, though his vertical passes are more accurate with his preferred right foot. While strong in tackling, concentration, and blocking, his aerial dueling remains an area for potential improvement. Tomori's adaptation to Italian football has seen him develop a more tactical understanding of positioning and passing, contrasting with the more instinctive style often found in England.

Career Highlights

Fikayo Tomori has achieved significant success since joining AC Milan, most notably winning the Serie A title in the 2021-2022 season, a triumph where he was a pivotal figure in the team's defense. His individual contributions were recognized with a place in the 2021-2022 Serie A Team of the Year. Before his move to Italy, he was named Derby County Player of the Year for the 2018-2019 season and won Chelsea's Goal of the Season in 2019-2020.

Internationally, Tomori was part of the England U-20 squad that won the FIFA U-20 World Cup in 2017. He made his senior debut for the England national team in 2019 and has since earned six caps. His youth career at Chelsea also saw him win two UEFA Youth League titles and two FA Youth Cups.

Personality

Off the pitch, Tomori describes himself as relaxed and chilled, enjoying time with family and friends, but transforms into an aggressive and determined player on the field who relishes defending and contributing to team victories. His upbringing, influenced by his Nigerian parents, instilled in him a strong sense of discipline, focus, and respect. Tomori has demonstrated a remarkable commitment to cultural immersion, quickly learning Italian to near fluency, a decision he considers the "best" he ever made for both his football and personal life. He also pursued a degree in business management during a lockdown period, showcasing his dedication beyond football.

Condition / Medical History

Tomori has experienced several injuries throughout his career, with recent issues including a thigh injury in September 2025 and a knee injury in October 2025. He also dealt with a neck injury in May 2025. In late 2023, he suffered a muscle injury that kept him out for over two months, and an ankle injury in November of the same year. A more significant injury occurred in January 2022, when he underwent key-hole surgery for a medial meniscus injury in his left knee, requiring approximately 30 days for recovery.
