The south Londoner had a big decision to make in the summer after being voted as player of the tournament at the European under-17 championship this year. With Manchester City keen to retain the forward they signed from Watford’s academy in March 2015 and Arsenal and Tottenham interested in bringing him to north London, in the end Sancho’s decision to join the youth revolution at the Westfalenstadion on the final day of the transfer window was intriguing. That he was immediately handed the No7 shirt recently vacated by the new Barcelona forward Ousmane Dembélé was an indication of how highly he is rated by the Bundesliga side and, although he has yet to make his first-team debut for Dortmund, it surely cannot be too far away. The under-17 World Cup in India will be another opportunity for the tricky forward, who is comfortable on either flank or as a support striker, to showcase his ability. **[Ed Aarons](https://www.theguardian.com/football/ng-interactive/2017/oct/04/writer's Twitter link)**  
  
**October 2018 update** Having been given the No 7 shirt at Dortmund he made his debut in October and his first league start in January. Has impressed in Germany with his maturity and was called up to the England Under-19 team for the first time in November 2017.  
  
**October 2019 update** A remarkable year for a remarkable player. One of the most exciting young forwards in Europe’s top five leagues, Sancho had by mid-September made 63 appearances and scored 18 goals (and made 29 assists) for Dortmund, and he has two goals in eight games for England.  
  
**October 2020 update** The success story continues. Managed to improve on his already impressive Bundesliga figures for 2018-19 (12 goals and 17 assists) with 17 goals and 17 assists in 2019-20. The fact that Dortmund told Manchester United not to bother bidding €90m for the 20-year-old says it all really.
