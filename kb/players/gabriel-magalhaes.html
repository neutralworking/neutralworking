Snapshot

A centre-back with the rare combination of genuine ball-playing ability and old-fashioned defensive dominance — he's comfortable taking the ball in tight situations and progressing it, while also capable of the no-nonsense clearance when required. His athleticism and reading of the game are genuinely elite. The only whisper of weakness is that against the very best number nines in Europe, his positioning can occasionally be beaten by intelligent movement rather than pure pace.

Recent Form

Gabriel Magalhães has maintained a strong presence in Arsenal's defense, contributing both defensively and offensively. During the 2023-24 season, he scored his first goal in a 1-1 draw against Liverpool at Anfield. He later netted his first brace for the club on January 20, 2024, scoring two goals in Arsenal's 5-0 victory over Crystal Palace. He also marked his 100th Premier League appearance for Arsenal on September 17, 2023, in a win against Everton.

In the 2024-25 season, Gabriel continued to be a goal threat, scoring against Manchester City and Tottenham. Notably, he secured a 1-0 away win for Arsenal in the North London derby on September 15, 2024, with a header. The following week, he scored in a 2-2 draw against Manchester City and continued his goalscoring form with headers in consecutive away matches against Sporting C.P. and West Ham in November 2024. For the ongoing 2025-26 Premier League season, Gabriel has played 26 matches, keeping 13 clean sheets and conceding 15 goals. He has scored 3 goals and contributed a total of 7 goals and assists, while receiving 3 yellow cards.

Tactical Profile

Gabriel Magalhães operates as a left-sided center-back, forming a notable partnership with William Saliba in Arsenal's defense. His playing style aligns with both a "Conductor" and "Stopper" archetype, demonstrating a blend of defensive solidity and ball-playing ability. As a "Stopper," Gabriel is recognized for his strength, aggression, tackling prowess, and aerial threat, positioning him among the world's top defenders. He adopts an aggressive defensive approach, pressing attackers and utilizing his 190cm frame to outmuscle opponents and dominate aerial duels, having won over 70% of his aerial duels in the 2019-20 season. His tactical flexibility allows him to be either proactive or reactive, and he exhibits strong game reading, consistently blocking crosses and cutbacks.

In his "Conductor" role, Gabriel is known for his distribution and passing range, frequently looking to play the ball forward or to the left-back. During the 2019-20 Ligue 1 season, he completed the most passes into the opposition half. He possesses excellent long-passing accuracy, completing 72.3% of his long balls in the 2019-20 season, and can effectively break lines of pressure with these passes to switch play or find quick attackers. Gabriel is comfortable on the ball, capable of carrying it forward to aid Arsenal's build-up play, and is considered a very progressive passer and ball-carrier. His speed, combined with his tactical intelligence, makes him challenging to beat in one-on-one situations, and he actively communicates with and organizes his fellow defenders.

Career Highlights

Gabriel Magalhães began his professional career at Avaí in 2016 before moving to Lille in 2017. During his time in France, he had loan spells with Troyes and Dinamo Zagreb, where he notably won a league and cup double in the 2017-18 season. He joined Arsenal in September 2020 for £27 million, making an immediate impact by scoring on his debut in a 3-0 victory against Fulham.

His early performances at Arsenal earned him multiple Player of the Month awards in late 2020. Gabriel signed a new long-term contract with Arsenal in October 2022, and again in June 2025, securing his future with the club. He made his senior international debut for Brazil in 2023 and scored his first international goal during the 2026 FIFA World Cup qualification campaign. He was also part of Brazil's squad for the 2024 Copa America in the USA. To date, Gabriel has scored 20 Premier League goals for Arsenal and won the English FA Community Shield with the club in the 2023-24 season.

Condition / Medical History

Gabriel Magalhães has experienced several injuries throughout his career. In July 2021, a knee injury forced him to withdraw from Brazil's squad for the 2020 Summer Olympics. He also sustained a thigh injury in June 2022 and a muscle injury in 2023.

More recently, in April 2025, Gabriel suffered a hamstring injury during a match against Fulham, which required surgery and sidelined him for the remainder of that season. In November 2025, while on international duty with Brazil, he sustained a right thigh muscle injury, with initial reports suggesting an absence of one to two months. Most recently, in April 2026, Gabriel left the pitch during a game against Southampton due to a knee problem, prompting concern from manager Mikel Arteta and being described as a "knock injury".
