**José Miguel González Martín del Campo**, known as **Míchel** (Spanish pronunciation: [ˈmitʃel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish "Help:IPA/Spanish"); born 23 March 1963), is a Spanish former professional [footballer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Association_football "Association football") who played as a [right midfielder](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Midfielder#Wide_midfielder "Midfielder"), currently a [manager](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manager_(association_football) "Manager (association football)").

He was most noted for his stellar [crossing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cross_(association_football) "Cross (association football)") ability, also contributing with a fair share of goals.[2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M%C3%ADchel_(footballer,_born_1963)#cite_note-BIO-2)During his career he represented mainly [Real Madrid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Real_Madrid_CF "Real Madrid CF") – over a decade – achieving great team and individual success.

Míchel earned 66 [caps](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cap_(sport) "Cap (sport)") for [Spain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spain_national_football_team "Spain national football team") from 1985 to 1992, and appeared for the nation in two [World Cups](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup "FIFA World Cup") (scoring four goals in the [1990 edition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1990_FIFA_World_Cup "1990 FIFA World Cup")) and one [European Championship](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UEFA_European_Championship "UEFA European Championship"). He started working as a manager in 2005, notably leading [Olympiacos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olympiacos_F.C. "Olympiacos F.C.") to two [Super League Greece](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Super_League_Greece "Super League Greece") accolades.

## Playing career

### Club

The son of a footballer who had to retire from the game at 27 after a [road accident](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Traffic_collision "Traffic collision"), Míchel was born in [Madrid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Madrid "Madrid") and joined [Real Madrid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Real_Madrid_CF "Real Madrid CF") at the age of 13,[3](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M%C3%ADchel_(footballer,_born_1963)#cite_note-FANS-3) his technique and physicality on the pitch quickly standing out.[2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M%C3%ADchel_(footballer,_born_1963)#cite_note-BIO-2) He moved quickly through the ranks to the first team, appearing – and scoring – once in the [1981–82 season](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1981%E2%80%9382_La_Liga "1981–82 La Liga"), in a 2–1 away win against [CD Castellón](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CD_Castell%C3%B3n "CD Castellón") on 11 April 1982.
