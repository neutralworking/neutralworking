You would be hard pressed to find anyone at Lyon who has been taken aback by the youth team graduate’s swift progress over the past 12 months, least of all Cherki himself. Asked in an interview with the club’s website in June whether he had been surprised to have already become part of the first team squad despite only turning 16 in August, the attacking midfielder displayed all the confidence he has on the pitch. “No, because I have very high goals,” he admitted. Having become the club’s youngest ever player at just 16 years and 43 days when he came off the bench against Dijon in October, Cherki – who joined Lyon at the age of seven after starting out at local club Saint-Priest, which also produced Nabil Fekir – set another record in January as the youngest goalscorer in their history. The powerfully-built playmaker, one of five brothers born to a father of Italian origin and a mother with Algerian roots, has proved more than capable in mixing it with senior players since being introduced to the first team and looks certain to be the next superstar off Lyon’s famed production line. **[Ed Aarons](https://twitter.com/ed_aarons)**
