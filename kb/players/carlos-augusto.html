Snapshot

A left-winger of genuine technical quality who inverts into the half-space with real intelligence and can play as a fullback or midfield shuttler depending on shape. He's direct, press-resistant, and capable of both stretching play wide and driving through the middle. His limitation is that his end product can be wasteful, and he's not the kind of player who'll terrorise you one-on-one at top speed—he's subtler than that.

Tactical Profile

Carlos Augusto, primarily a left-back or left midfielder, demonstrates significant versatility, capable of operating as a centre-back in both four-man and three-man defensive systems. He is recognized for his strong defensive attributes, including high elevation and defensive prowess, making him well-suited for Italian football. His attacking contributions are also notable, particularly his crossing ability.

At Inter Milan, Augusto has frequently excelled as a centre-back within the team's 3-5-2 formation and can play anywhere along the left flank. He considers his versatility a positive asset, providing him with more opportunities at both club and international levels. His playing style incorporates an indirect set-piece threat and a preference for short passes, while his strengths include aerial duels and passing, though discipline is noted as a weakness. He has expressed a desire to perform well in both defensive and offensive aspects of the game.

Career Highlights

Carlos Augusto has accumulated several significant achievements throughout his career. With Inter Milan, he has won the Serie A title and the Supercoppa, both in the 2023/2024 season, and was part of the squad that reached the UEFA Champions League runner-up position in the 2024/2025 season. His move to Inter Milan initially came as a one-year loan in 2023, which was made permanent in 2024 for €13.3 million.

Prior to his time at Inter, Augusto played a crucial role in Monza's first-ever promotion to Serie A. During his tenure at Monza, he was named in the Serie B Team of the Season for 2020-21. Earlier in his career with Corinthians, he secured the Campeonato Paulista title twice, in 2018 and 2019, and also won the Copa São Paulo de Juniores with the Corinthians U20 team in 2017. He made his debut for the Brazil national team on October 17, 2023.
