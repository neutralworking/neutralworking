Kiernan Dewsbury-Hall is a 27-year-old English central midfielder who currently plays for Everton. Standing at 178 cm and preferring his left foot, he operates as a playmaker within the team.

His impact has been particularly notable in recent matches, including a goal in a 2-2 draw against Brentford on April 11, 2026, and another in a 2-0 win against Burnley on March 3, 2026.

Career Highlights

Kiernan Dewsbury-Hall began his career within the Leicester City youth system in 2006, progressing through their ranks before signing his first professional contract in 2017. He gained valuable first-team experience through loan spells at Blackpool in League One in 2020 and Luton Town in the Championship during the 2020-2021 season. His impressive performances during these loans paved the way for his breakthrough into Leicester's senior squad.

He played a crucial role in Leicester City's successful 2023-2024 Championship campaign, where they secured promotion back to the Premier League as champions. During this season, he was recognized with several individual accolades, including Leicester's Players' Player of the Season and Men's Player of the Season awards, and was also named in the EFL Championship Team of the Season and PFA Team of the Year. Following this, Dewsbury-Hall transferred to Chelsea in the summer of 2024, where he contributed to their UEFA Conference League victory in 2025 and was part of the squad that won the FIFA Club World Cup in the same year. He then moved to Everton in the summer of 2025, where he has quickly established himself as a key player.
