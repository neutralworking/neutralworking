Exequiel Palacios: Midfield Dynamo with a Winning Pedigree

Exequiel Palacios, the 27-year-old Argentine central midfielder for Bayer Leverkusen, continues to be a vital presence in the heart of the pitch, known for his comprehensive skillset and significant contributions to both club and country. Standing at 177 cm and preferring his right foot, Palacios embodies the "Heartbeat" archetype and "Tuttocampista" role, demonstrating his ability to influence all phases of play.

Recent Form

In the 2025-2026 season, Exequiel Palacios has made 13 appearances for Bayer Leverkusen in the Bundesliga, contributing one assist. Across all competitions, he has played 19 matches, accumulating 901 minutes on the field. His performance in recent matches has been notable, including an 8.1 FotMob rating in a 1-0 away win against Borussia Dortmund and an 8.5 rating with an assist in a 6-3 home victory over Wolfsburg in April 2026. He also earned an 8.2 rating in a 1-1 draw against Arsenal in March 2026.

While his goal and assist numbers for the current season are modest (0 goals, 1 assist in Bundesliga), Palacios's impact extends beyond direct goal contributions. He has taken 7 shots, with 2 on target, and received 2 yellow cards in the Bundesliga this season. His consistent presence and high ratings in key matches underscore his continued importance to Bayer Leverkusen's midfield.

Tactical Profile

Palacios operates as a multifaceted central midfielder, showcasing exceptional work rate, stamina, and intensity, making him an effective box-to-box player. He is adept at pressing the opposition, consistently making himself available for passes, and diligently tracking back to support the defense. His playing style is characterized by strong passing, incisive through balls, and key passes, often breaking opposition lines with sharp forward distributions.

Under Xabi Alonso's system at Bayer Leverkusen, Palacios thrives in a box midfield, forming a formidable partnership with Granit Xhaka. He is recognized for his reliability in possession, helping to maintain the team's rhythm and ball movement. Defensively, he is an intense presser and a chief ball winner, demonstrating an ability to anticipate danger and execute a high volume of defensive actions, which perfectly complements Xhaka's more expansive role. While strong in ground duels and playmaking, his aerial duels are noted as a weakness.

Career Highlights

Exequiel Palacios boasts an impressive list of achievements at both club and international levels. With his boyhood club River Plate, he won the Copa Libertadores in 2018, the Copa Argentina in 2016–17 and 2018–19, and the Recopa Sudamericana in 2019. He was also recognized as part of the 2018 South American Team of the Year.

His move to Bayer Leverkusen brought further success, culminating in a historic German Championship (Bundesliga title) and German Cup victory in the 2023/24 season. On the international stage, Palacios has been a key member of Argentina's golden generation, winning the FIFA World Cup in 2022, the Copa América in both 2021 and 2024, and the CONMEBOL–UEFA Cup of Champions in 2022. He made his international debut for Argentina in 2018 and was included in the squad for the 2024 Copa América.

Personality

Exequiel Palacios is perceived as a "great guy" with a polite and humble demeanor, often seen smiling when interacting with fans. On the field, he is known for his fighting spirit and battling commitment in every match. His winning mentality and willingness to learn from more experienced players are also highlighted as key aspects of his character.

Condition / Medical History

Palacios has experienced a notable history of injuries throughout his career, which has at times impacted his consistent availability. In 2025, he sustained groin injuries in April and September, as well as a muscle injury in August. The previous year, 2024, saw him sidelined with a knee injury from July to September and two separate muscle injuries in March-April and January-March.

Earlier significant injuries include a knee injury in November 2023, a hip injury in April-May 2023, and a hamstring injury in April 2023. In 2020, he suffered a fractured spine while on international duty with Argentina, which kept him out for approximately three months. His injury record also includes ankle, calf, and other muscle issues in previous seasons. Despite these setbacks, he has demonstrated resilience, making seamless returns to play, such as after a thigh muscle problem in the 2023/24 season.
