Recent Form

João Pedro is currently enjoying a standout season at Chelsea, which he describes as "one of the best of his life." In the 2025-26 Premier League season, he has registered 14 goals and 5 assists across 32 appearances, placing him fourth in the league's scoring charts and among the top ten for assists. Across all competitions, he is approaching the 20-goal milestone in his debut campaign for the Blues.

His goal-scoring prowess has been evident in crucial matches, including his first Premier League goal for Chelsea against West Ham on August 22, 2025. He also scored his inaugural UEFA Champions League goal on December 9, 2025, against Atalanta, followed by a brace against Napoli on January 28, 2026, in the same competition. A significant highlight was his first hat-trick for Chelsea in a 4-1 victory over Aston Villa on March 4, 2026. Under the guidance of new coach Liam Rosenior since January, Pedro has seen an "explosive improvement," contributing 11 goals and 5 assists, bringing his total season tally to 18 goals. His shot conversion rate stands at an impressive 24.6%, surpassing that of Erling Haaland.

Tactical Profile

João Pedro is a highly versatile forward, capable of operating as a central striker, a secondary striker, or even drifting wide as a winger or attacking midfielder. He thrives in fluid attacking systems, adept at finding pockets of space between defensive lines and linking play effectively. His playing style is characterized by a blend of finesse and physicality, allowing him to engage in aerial duels, execute clever layoffs, and maintain possession through dribbling. He is also known for his ability to draw fouls from opponents.

His tactical intelligence is a key asset, as he demonstrates a keen understanding of space, enabling him to drift into advantageous positions and create opportunities for himself and teammates. Pedro's capacity to drop deep to connect play and create central overloads is a significant factor in Chelsea's tactical fluidity under Enzo Maresca and Liam Rosenior. He possesses strong ball-carrying ability in central areas and can effectively play as a left-sided number 10, tucking inside to influence the game. His diverse goal-scoring methods, utilizing both feet and his head from various distances, further highlight his adaptability and technical skill.

Career Highlights

João Pedro's football journey began in the youth system of Fluminense, where he transitioned from a defensive midfielder to an attacking midfielder before settling as a striker. He made his senior debut for Fluminense in 2019, quickly catching the eye of European clubs. In January 2020, he made the move to English football, joining Watford, where he scored his first goal on his 19th birthday and later his first Premier League goal against Manchester United in November 2021.

His career progressed with a transfer to Brighton & Hove Albion in May 2023 for a club-record fee. During his time at Brighton, he notably finished as the outright top scorer in the Europa League group stage with six goals in six matches. A significant moment came on August 24, 2024, when he scored a stoppage-time winner against Manchester United. João Pedro then joined Chelsea on July 2, 2025, for a reported fee of £55 million plus add-ons. He made an immediate impact, debuting in the 2025 FIFA Club World Cup quarter-finals, scoring a brace in the semi-final against his former club Fluminense, and netting the final goal in Chelsea's 3-0 victory over Paris Saint-Germain to secure the trophy. On the international stage, he made his Brazil U23 debut in August 2023 and his senior national team debut in November 2023.

Personality

João Pedro exhibits a strong, ambitious, and family-oriented personality. He has openly expressed his immense satisfaction with his current season, labeling it "one of the best seasons of my life." His mentality is geared towards continuous improvement and maintaining high performance levels, driven by a desire to become one of the best strikers globally.

Family plays a crucial role in his life, with Pedro frequently acknowledging the unwavering support of his mother and grandmother throughout his career, particularly during challenging periods of adaptation to new cities and countries. He embraces pressure, viewing it as a characteristic of top-tier players, and maintains a humble approach despite his successes. Pedro's ambition extends to winning titles with Chelsea and fulfilling his dream of representing Brazil in the World Cup, aspiring to emulate the achievements of Chelsea legends like Didier Drogba. He is also noted for his strong chemistry with teammates, such as Cole Palmer.

Condition / Medical History

João Pedro underwent and completed his medical examination prior to joining Chelsea in July 2025. His career has seen a few minor injury concerns, including a "knock injury" in September 2025 and an "ankle injury" in January 2025 and May 2024. He also experienced a "hamstring injury" in February 2024 and a "muscle injury" in August 2024. A past "knee injury" occurred in 2021.

In terms of disciplinary record, he received a red card for violent conduct in April 2025, leading to a three-game suspension. He was also notably dropped from the squad in May 2025 following a "training ground incident." During a Brazil international match in September 2025, he vomited on the pitch after a collision, though this was confirmed not to be due to illness or injury. In the 2025-26 Premier League season, he has accumulated 4 yellow cards in 32 matches.
