Recent Form

Jonathan David has shown a significant upturn in form at Juventus since the arrival of new coach Luciano Spalletti in late October 2025, following an initial struggle under previous coach Igor Tudor where he scored only once in 11 appearances. Under Spalletti's guidance, David has contributed four goals and two assists across all competitions. This positive momentum has seen him score in consecutive games for Juventus, playing a key role in recent victories against US Sassuolo and US Cremonese.

As of early 2026, David has accumulated four goal contributions in his last four games, demonstrating a resurgence despite an earlier "Panenka" penalty miss. His shooting statistics have notably improved under Spalletti, with an increase in both shots taken and expected goals (xG). This improved individual performance has coincided with Juventus' strong run, as the team is currently undefeated in six Serie A matches and seven games across all competitions. For the 2025/2026 Serie A season, he has registered 5 goals and 4 assists.

Tactical Profile

Jonathan David operates as an "exotic" striker, characterized by his tendency to roam across the final third rather than remaining static in the penalty box. He frequently drops deep to participate in build-up play before moving into opportune spaces or latching onto through-balls, aligning with his "Raumdeuter" archetype. His effectiveness extends beyond goal-scoring, as he excels in chance creation, averaging 2.75 chances per game, and boasts an impressive overall output of 0.65 goals plus assists per match.

David is also noted for his efficient passing, rarely misplacing a touch, and his ability to link up with teammates through quick combinations, one-twos, and well-timed passes into the box. His passing accuracy in the final third is particularly high for a striker. Furthermore, his proficiency with both feet makes him a versatile and unpredictable threat, capable of striking the ball effectively from various angles and positions, which complicates defensive marking. His intelligent movement allows him to exploit space by drifting deep, making runs in behind, or moving wide, thereby creating opportunities for himself and drawing defenders out of position for others.

Career Highlights

Jonathan David made a significant move to Juventus as a free agent in the summer of 2025. Prior to his transfer to the Italian giants, he established himself as a prolific goalscorer during his tenure at Lille OSC, where he consistently delivered high returns, netting 25, 26, and 26 goals in his final three seasons with the French club. His time at Lille also saw him achieve team success, winning the Ligue 1 title and the Trophée des Champions in his first two seasons.

His consistent performance at Lille earned him recognition as one of the top global players for goal contributions in 2024, placing him in an elite group alongside Kylian Mbappé, Mohamed Salah, and Harry Kane. David began his professional career in Europe with Gent, where he maintained an impressive scoring rate, averaging a goal every other game, which ultimately paved the way for his move to Lille.

Personality

Jonathan David describes himself as possessing a "calm, chill personality" and prefers simple leisure activities, such as spending time with friends. He emphasizes that his primary focus as a striker is to score goals, rather than seeking the spotlight as a "headline player". David values coaches who prioritize mentality and work ethic while also fostering an environment that allows for creative freedom and experimentation. He is also known to seek out clubs that demonstrate genuine confidence in his abilities.
