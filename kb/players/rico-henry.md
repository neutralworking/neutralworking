## Scouting Notes

A left-back who understands the modern brief completely—he's comfortable inverting into midfield, he can play a 40-yard progressive pass, and his one-v-one defending is intelligent rather than reckless. The issue is that he's not remotely quick in the conventional sense, so when he has to defend a pure pace advantage on the flank, he's vulnerable. Age is creeping in; you're seeing the legs tire slightly in the final 20 minutes of matches.
