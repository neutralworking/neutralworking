Houssem Aouar is a 27-year-old Algerian central midfielder for Al-Ittihad, known for his playmaking abilities and right-footed preference. Standing at 175 cm, he continues to be a key figure in his club and national team.

Recent Form

In the 2025/2026 Saudi Pro League season, Houssem Aouar has registered 4 goals and 2 assists across 1,454 minutes of play, maintaining an average FotMob rating of 7.12 and accumulating three yellow cards. His performance in terms of goals and overall rating is considered high among attacking midfielders in the league. During the 2024/2025 season, his debut year with Al-Ittihad, Aouar contributed significantly with 13 goals and 4 assists in 35 appearances across all competitions, helping the club secure both the Saudi Pro League and the King's Cup titles. Despite these contributions, reports in August 2025 suggested that Al-Ittihad was considering offloading Aouar due to a perceived "noticeable dip in form" and the team's disappointing exit from the Super Cup. However, he scored a goal in the AFC Champions League Elite (ACLE) 25/26 Match Day 7 in February 2026.

Tactical Profile

Aouar operates primarily as a central midfielder but exhibits characteristics of a player who can excel further up the pitch. He is recognized as an exceptional ball progressor from half-spaces and is proficient at creating dangerous shooting opportunities for himself and his teammates in the final third. His tactical versatility allows him to function effectively in the center of a three- or four-man midfield, or in a more advanced attacking role. Aouar is considered one of Europe's most sought-after goal-scoring number 8s, possessing excellent ball-carrying skills and remarkable one-on-one abilities. His playmaking style is often compared to that of a number 10 or an inverted winger, and his high XGChain90 value indicates a significant impact on offensive sequences despite a moderate number of touches. Furthermore, Aouar demonstrates strong press resistance, utilizing his intelligence and composure to manage pressure and distribute the ball effectively. Defensively, he averages 4.49 defensive duels per 90 minutes with a 50% win rate, along with 1.9 interceptions and 4.42 successful defensive actions per 90.

Career Highlights

Houssem Aouar has accumulated several significant achievements throughout his career. With Al-Ittihad, he won the King's Cup and the Saudi Pro League in the 2024/2025 season. Prior to his move to Saudi Arabia, he secured the Emirates Cup with Lyon in 2019. On the international stage, Aouar was part of the France squad that won the UEFA Nations League in 2021. He made 233 appearances for Olympique Lyon, contributing 41 goals and 34 assists, after making his senior debut in February 2017. Aouar joined Al-Ittihad in the summer of 2024 from AS Roma, where he had made 25 appearances and scored 4 goals. He switched his international allegiance to Algeria in March 2023, making his debut for the national team in June 2023 and participating in the 2024 Africa Cup of Nations.

Personality

Houssem Aouar is often characterized by his calm and composed demeanor, both on and off the pitch. He is commonly identified as an Enneagram 9w8, or "The Peacemaker," known for being easygoing, agreeable, and accommodating, with a strong desire for inner and outer peace. Some also associate him with the INTP personality type, suggesting independent thinking, abstract analysis, and a drive to understand underlying mechanisms. Aouar is described as quiet, understated, focused, and consistent, regardless of external pressures. He maintains a close relationship with his mother, who he credits as a significant source of strength and motivation. His former coach, Bruno Genesio, praised Aouar's ability to remain cool under pressure and his willingness to always engage, while Nabil Fekir highlighted his maturity, technical skill, and understanding of the game. Aouar is also involved in charitable work, serving as a "heart partner" for ASP (Aider Son Prochain), an association that supports sick children and those in hardship.

Condition / Medical History

Houssem Aouar has experienced several periods of injury and illness throughout his career. In 2023, he dealt with a muscle injury in December, an illness in May, a knock injury in February, and an unspecified issue in January. The previous year, 2022, saw him sidelined with a muscle injury in December, an ankle injury in August, and a thigh injury in January. In 2021, he sustained muscle injuries in both March and January. More recently, he was reported to have a "Broad Injury" in April 2024. In December 2025, Aouar withdrew from the 2025 Africa Cup of Nations due to an injury. Additionally, in August 2020, he tested positive for COVID-19, which required him to isolate and miss training.
