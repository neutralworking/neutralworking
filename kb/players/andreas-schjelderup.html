Born in Bodø, north of the Arctic circle, Schjelderup attracted enormous interest from scouts very early on. They came to Norway to watch his talents, and Schjelderup visited Ajax and PSV Eindhoven academies four times each, as well as having trials at Liverpool, Tottenham, Juventus and Bayern Munich. Eventually, though, he chose to join Nordsjælland, the Danish club famous for nurturing youngsters – and the move has already paid off, the prodigy promoted to the first-team squad at the beginning of 2021. He immediately became a key performer thanks to his imagination, vision, audacity, dribbling skills and versatility – Schjelderup can be used as a central midfielder, left winger, second striker and even at centre-forward. He scored his first goal in the Superliga in March, added a brace a week later, and had three goals to his name in the new season by the end of September. He is already a star by Danish standards and – having recently signed a contract extension until 2024 – looks certain to be sold for a Nordsjælland record fee when he eventually goes. Michael Yokhin

Andreas Schjelderup: Player Research Brief

Recent Form

Andreas Schjelderup has demonstrated strong offensive contributions for Benfica during the 2025-2026 season. Across 23 appearances in the Liga Portugal and UEFA Champions League, he has registered 5 goals and 4 assists, accumulating 9 goal contributions in total. His performance includes an average FotMob rating of 7.26 in Liga Portugal, where he scores highly in rating, assists, and goals compared to other left wingers. Notably, he scored his first Benfica goal on October 27, 2024, in a 5-0 victory against Rio Ave in the Primeira Liga.

Schjelderup has also made an impact in cup competitions, scoring in the Taça da Liga final on January 11, 2025, as Benfica defeated Sporting, and in the Taça de Portugal three days later against Farense. Furthermore, he scored the only goal in a 1-0 win over Bayern Munich on June 24, 2025, securing Benfica's top spot in Group C of the 2025 FIFA Club World Cup. His recent match ratings in Liga Portugal include an 8.3 in a 2-0 win against Nacional and a 7.8 in a 1-1 draw against Casa Pia AC in April 2026.

Tactical Profile

Schjelderup is a highly skillful and technical player, primarily operating as a left winger, though he is versatile enough to be deployed as an attacking midfielder, striker, or second striker. His playing style involves hugging the touchline to receive possession before cutting inside and driving into more central areas, which allows him space and time to influence the game. He possesses quick feet and excellent agility in one-on-one situations, often using dribbling to beat defenders.

A key aspect of his game is his ability to draw defenders, thereby creating openings for teammates. He tends to make lateral attacking runs rather than runs in behind the defense. Without the ball, Schjelderup is an active player with a high work rate, consistently seeking to provide attacking options and dropping between lines as an advanced playmaker when marked on the wing. His strengths include holding onto the ball, passing, through balls, key passes, and finishing, while areas for development include aerial duels and defensive contribution.

Career Highlights

Schjelderup began his youth career at Bodø/Glimt before moving to Danish club Nordsjælland in July 2020. He made his senior debut for Nordsjælland in February 2021 at 16 years old and became the club's youngest-ever goalscorer in the Superliga. In January 2023, Benfica signed him on a five-year contract for a reported fee of around €14 million.

After a challenging initial period at Benfica, which included a loan return to Nordsjælland for the 2023-2024 season, Schjelderup had a breakthrough in the 2024-2025 season. He made his senior international debut for Norway on June 5, 2024, as a substitute for Erling Haaland. With Benfica, he has won the Liga Portugal, the Super Cup, and the League Cup.

Condition / Medical History

Andreas Schjelderup has a relatively clean medical history. Public records indicate a muscle injury in 2024, which kept him out from August 23 to September 14. Prior to that, he experienced an illness in October 2021.

Personality

Andreas Schjelderup is noted for his vocal presence on the pitch, frequently demanding the ball and combining this with effective scanning to prepare for his next action upon reception.
