As of April 18, 2026, he has recorded 6 goals and 15 assists in Serie A, achieving a historic milestone by becoming the first defender since the 2004/05 season to reach 21 goal involvements in a single league campaign. His performance includes 11 assists in 22 Serie A appearances as of February 9, 2026, positioning him as the league's leading assist provider at that time.

Tactical Profile

Federico Dimarco operates primarily as a left wing-back, a role in which he leverages his left-footed ability, pace, and tireless running. He is recognized for his powerful and accurate shots from outside the penalty area, as well as his keen eye for goal and ability to interpret the game. Dimarco is also a significant threat from set pieces, delivering precise crosses and dangerous free-kicks that consistently create scoring opportunities.

His tactical intelligence allows him to thrive within Inter's 3-5-2 system under coach Cristian Chivu, where he is fundamental to the team's high-energy pressing and aggressive counter-pressing. Dimarco's positioning and movement are crucial for Inter's build-up play, enabling the team to progress the ball effectively down the left flank through both short passing combinations and long balls. He is adept at creating midfield overloads and sustaining pressure in advanced areas, often overlapping or making attacking runs to generate chances. While his defensive ability has been noted as a past area for improvement, he has shown significant development in defensive discipline, including better tracking and positioning, making him a more balanced two-way player.

Career Highlights

Dimarco is a product of Inter Milan's youth academy, making his senior debut for the club in 2014 at the age of 17. He has been instrumental in Inter Milan's recent successes, winning the Serie A title in 2023–24, two Coppa Italia titles (2021–22, 2022–23), and three Supercoppa Italiana titles (2021, 2022, 2023). He also played a key role in Inter reaching the UEFA Champions League final in both the 2022–23 and 2024–25 seasons.

Individually, Dimarco was named in the UEFA Champions League Team of the Season for 2022–23 and the Serie A Team of the Season for 2023–24 and 2024–25. His spectacular goal from 56.08 meters against Frosinone in November 2023 earned him the Serie A Goal of the Month award and a nomination for the FIFA Puskás Award 2024. He was also recognized as the Serie A Player of the Month in January 2026. On the international stage, Dimarco received his first call-up to the Italian senior squad in 2021, debuted in June 2022, and scored his first international goal in September 2022, which was Italy's 1500th goal. In the 2024-25 Nations League, he won the Goal of the Round award for a stunning equalizer against France. He extended his contract with Inter Milan until 2027, solidifying his commitment to his boyhood club.
