## Snapshot

A right-back of considerable experience who remains a reliable, if unspectacular, operator in the Bundesliga's upper reaches. Lainer's value lies in his positional discipline and low-error defending; he reads the game cleanly and rarely gets caught in catastrophic positional lapses. His weakness is precisely what defines his ceiling: he lacks the pace to recover against genuinely rapid wingers and offers almost nothing in transition or progressive passing—he's a shuttle player, not a creator. At 33, he's a functional, durable option for a club expecting to compete domestically but not threatening Europe, a defender who will never cost you a match through recklessness but equally won't unlock anything on the ball.
