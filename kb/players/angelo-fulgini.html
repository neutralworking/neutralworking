Snapshot

A dogged, positionally intelligent midfielder who thrives in a defined role at Ligue 1 level—the sort of player who'd disappear in a game and still rack up 47 tackles and 6 interceptions because he knows where danger lives before it announces itself. Fulgini's best work happens in transition; he'll break lines with a simple pass or a progressive carry, and his reading of space is genuinely astute for a player who'll never wow you with technical range. The genuine limitation is creative ceiling—he's not the guy threading a through-ball from deep or unlocking a congested defense with invention—and his passing distribution, while competent, lacks the tempo-setting quality you'd want from a midfielder anchoring a European competition side. At 29, operating in mid-table Ligue 1, he's a reliable cog, but he's also probably peaked as a functional rather than impactful operator.

Angelo Fulgini, a 29-year-old central midfielder, is currently on loan at Al-Taawoun in the Saudi Pro League from his parent club Lens. Born in Ivory Coast, Fulgini represents New Caledonia internationally, having switched allegiance from France in December 2025. He is right-footed and stands at 1.83m.

Recent Form

Angelo Fulgini is currently performing for Al-Taawoun in the 2025/2026 Saudi Pro League season, where he has registered 3 goals and 8 assists in 24 appearances, accumulating 1,913 minutes of play. His performances have earned him an average FotMob rating of 7.3 in the league. He has also received two yellow cards during this period.

In March 2026, Fulgini made his international debut for New Caledonia, participating in a FIFA World Cup inter-confederation play-off game against Jamaica. His recent club matches for Al-Taawoun show consistent involvement, including a 2-2 draw against Al Hilal in April 2026 and a 3-2 victory over Al Fateh FC in March 2026.

Tactical Profile

Fulgini operates primarily as a central or attacking midfielder, embodying the "Playmaker" role with a strong emphasis on creativity and distribution. His tactical strengths include playmaking, high pressing, and an ability to deliver key passes. He is also proficient at taking set-pieces, demonstrating good technique with dead balls.

His playing style is characterized by a willingness to attempt risky, incisive passes that can unlock defenses, often leading to goal-scoring opportunities for his teammates. Fulgini possesses a good eye for through-balls and a broad passing range, occasionally utilizing his weaker left foot effectively. While his ball retention rating might be lower due to his adventurous passing, this reflects his commitment to creating dangerous situations. He is also noted for getting fouled often and being a counter-attack threat.

Career Highlights

Angelo Fulgini began his professional career at Valenciennes, breaking into the first team in 2015. He later moved to Angers in 2017, where he spent several successful seasons before joining Mainz 05 in July 2022. His return to French football came in February 2023, when he signed with Lens on an initial loan with an obligation to make the move permanent.

During his time at Lens, Fulgini was a key player, contributing to the club's European endeavors. He scored his first goal for Lens in the Coupe de France shortly after joining in February 2023, followed by his first Ligue 1 goal later that month. A notable achievement was scoring from a direct free kick on his Champions League debut in September 2023. In August 2025, he was loaned to Al-Taawoun in Saudi Arabia. On the international stage, Fulgini, who previously played for France youth teams, officially switched his allegiance to New Caledonia in December 2025 and earned his first senior cap in March 2026.

Personality

Angelo Fulgini has publicly expressed his feelings regarding his departure from Lens, indicating a sense of frustration and a perception of being pushed out by the club's management. In an interview from November 2025, he spoke of opaque communication and decisions made "from above," beyond the coaches, leading to a reduced playing time and his eventual loan move. He felt a "lack of respect" given his dedication and effort to the club.

Despite the challenging circumstances of his exit, Fulgini maintains positive relationships with his former Lens teammates, continuing to follow their progress and interact with them on social media. His decision to represent New Caledonia internationally is a source of pride, particularly for his family, and he views it as an "amazing" opportunity to contribute his experience to the national team.

Condition / Medical History

Angelo Fulgini has experienced several injuries throughout his career. More recently, he sustained a tendon injury in January 2025 and an unspecified injury in late October to early November 2024. Prior to these, his medical history includes a thigh injury in April-May 2021, a muscle injury in February 2021, and two separate ankle injuries in late 2020 and early 2020. He also dealt with groin injuries in 2019 and 2018, a hip injury in 2019, another muscle injury in 2018, and a knee injury in early 2018.
