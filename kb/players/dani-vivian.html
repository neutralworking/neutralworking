Dani Vivian: Player Research Brief

Recent Form

Dani Vivian has demonstrated strong and consistent performance in recent seasons for Athletic Bilbao. In the 2023/2024 season, he made 40 appearances, starting 37 of those matches, which contributed to Athletic Club winning the Copa del Rey and finishing fifth in La Liga. His impressive displays during this period earned him his debut for the Spanish national team in March 2024 and inclusion in the preliminary squad for the European Championships.

In the ongoing 2025/2026 La Liga season, Vivian has featured in 26 matches, scoring one goal. He notably found the back of the net in a 2-1 victory against Betis on March 23, 2026. His defensive metrics have shown continuous improvement, with his defensive actions and ground duel win rate reaching the 70th percentile in the 2023/2024 season. He consistently ranks in the top half of La Liga center-backs for defensive duels and possession-adjusted interceptions per 90 minutes.

Tactical Profile

Vivian operates as a central defender, known for his robust and concentrated approach to the game. His playing style includes a propensity to dribble and execute long balls, indicating an ability to contribute to build-up play. Athletic Club's chief, Mikel González, has lauded Vivian for his continuous evolution, winning mentality, concentration, speed, and forward-defending capabilities, all of which align well with the club's demanding tactical model.

While he has shown adaptability in his progressive passing and ball-carrying, with a significant jump in these metrics during the 2022/2023 season, tactical adjustments by Athletic Club in 2023/2024 saw these numbers return to a more median range. Standing at 1.84 meters (6 ft 0 in), Vivian possesses notable strengths in ball interception and overall physical strength. However, his aerial abilities have been identified as an area without significant improvement, with his aerial duel win rate consistently remaining below the 3.5th percentile.

Career Highlights

Dani Vivian's career has been marked by significant milestones, including signing a long-term contract with Athletic Club until June 2032. A crowning achievement came in the 2023/2024 season when he was part of the Athletic Club squad that won the Copa del Rey. His strong club form led to his senior international debut for Spain against Colombia in March 2024, and he was subsequently included in Spain's squad for the UEFA European Championship, which Spain won.

Earlier in his career, Vivian made his Athletic Bilbao debut on August 23, 2019. He gained valuable experience and leadership during a successful loan spell at CD Mirandés in the 2020/2021 season, where he served as captain. He scored his first La Liga goal for Athletic Bilbao on August 16, 2021, and reached his 50th La Liga appearance for the club on October 1, 2023. In recognition of his outstanding performances, Vivian was also named in the La Liga Team of the Season on June 14, 2023.

Condition / Medical History

Dani Vivian has experienced several minor injuries throughout his career, though he has generally maintained a good level of fitness in recent years. His injury history includes multiple thigh injuries, with reported periods of absence in January-February 2026 and March-April 2025.

Other recorded issues include an unspecified injury in April-May 2024, a contusion in February 2024, and previous ankle and muscle injuries in 2022 and 2021, respectively. Despite these minor setbacks, Vivian has been described as "fairly healthy the last few years," consistently contributing to his team's defensive solidity.
