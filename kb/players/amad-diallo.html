Amad Diallo: Player Research Brief

Recent Form

Amad Diallo has been an increasingly important figure in Manchester United's attack during the 2025-2026 season, contributing both goals and assists, alongside diligent defensive work. In the Premier League this season, he has made 27 appearances, scoring 2 goals and providing 2 assists. His performances under manager Ruben Amorim have seen him operate effectively within a 3-4-3/3-4-2-1 system, often as a right-sided wingback, where he balances his attacking flair with significant defensive responsibilities. His pressing numbers are noted as being among the best in the squad, highlighting his work rate and tactical awareness.

Beyond his current season statistics, Diallo has delivered several crucial moments. In the 2024-2025 season, he scored a winner against Manchester City and also registered a hat-trick against Southampton. Notably, he made an immediate impact on Ruben Amorim's debut by providing an assist. His breakthrough moments include scoring his first Premier League goal on May 15, 2024, against Newcastle, and netting the dramatic 121st-minute winner against Liverpool in the 2023-24 FA Cup quarter-finals.

Tactical Profile

Amad Diallo is characterized as a dynamic and intelligent left-footed winger who excels in one-on-one situations. His dribbling technique involves small, rapid touches, enabling quick changes of direction and effective exploitation of defensive gaps. He demonstrates exceptional close ball control, allowing him to navigate tight spaces and bypass defenders with ease. A key aspect of his attacking play involves diagonal runs from wide positions, where he frequently cuts inside to either shoot or deliver incisive passes, cutbacks, or low crosses into the box.

Tactically, Diallo's versatility allows him to be deployed as a right-winger, an attacking midfielder (right 8), or even a right-sided wingback. He possesses a strong understanding of space and angles, consistently scanning the pitch for optimal positioning. Beyond his offensive contributions, Diallo has developed a notable pressing intensity and ability to win duels, influencing games on both sides of the ball. His football IQ, spatial awareness, and off-the-ball movement are considered exceptional, allowing him to link up effectively with teammates and operate in congested areas.

Career Highlights

Amad Diallo has accumulated several significant achievements throughout his burgeoning career. He was a part of the Manchester United squad that won the FA Cup in 2024. Prior to this, he also secured a Scottish Cup winner's medal during his loan spell with Rangers in 2022.

During his time at Sunderland in the 2022-23 Championship season, Diallo played a starring role, scoring 14 goals and providing 3 assists, which earned him the EFL Young Player of the Month award. His youth career at Atalanta was also highly successful, where he won the Campionato Primavera 1 twice (2018-19, 2019-20) and the Italian Supercoppa Primavera. He made his senior debut for Atalanta in 2019, scoring on his first appearance. On the international stage, Diallo scored his first goal for Côte d’Ivoire in June 2021 and participated in the 2020 Summer Olympics with the U23 team. He also scored three goals in the 2025 Africa Cup of Nations.

Personality

Amad Diallo's character is described as one of grit, adaptability, and undeniable talent. He is seen as a "fighter" and "warrior" on the pitch, possessing a strong work ethic. Public reports suggest he has a positive attitude when in possession and demonstrates both desire and application in his play. His actions off the pitch also reflect a respectful demeanor, as evidenced by his gesture of shaking the hands of Pep Guardiola and every Manchester City player after scoring a crucial winner against them. Personality assessments classify him as an ISFJ and Enneagram Type 1w9, indicating traits such as valuing stability, generosity, a strong sense of obligation, and a desire to make a positive impact.

Condition / Medical History

Amad Diallo has experienced several periods of absence due to injuries throughout his career. In the 2025 calendar year, he sustained an ankle injury that kept him out from mid-February to late April. In late 2024, he dealt with a knock injury in November. A knee injury also sidelined him in May 2024. A more significant knee injury kept him out for an extended period from August to December 2023. Other past injuries include a muscle injury in March 2023 and a thigh injury from August to October 2021.
