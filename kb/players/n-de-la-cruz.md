De la Cruz is a gritty tuttocampista whose guile, duels, and long-range accuracy give him genuine character and scoring threat from midfield—his composure under pressure marks him as press-resistant despite moderate technical foundations. His pass accuracy weakness and undisciplined take-ons reveal a midfielder who is sometimes creative and sometimes reckless; he can shift play creatively in one moment and squander possession carelessly the next, suggesting inconsistency that requires structured team cover. Suits high-octane South American environments where individual quality and aggression trump positional rigour; his limited tactical scope and inconsistent output make him medium-risk depth rather than a foundational midfielder for European possession systems.
