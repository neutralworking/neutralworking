## Snapshot

A ball-progressor with genuine intent in the half-spaces, Dárdai has the technical security and positional intelligence to function as a legitimate 8 in a mid-table Bundesliga side. The issue is his processing speed under pressure—he'll find the right pass eventually, but against genuinely aggressive pressing he can look ponderous, which is a real problem in transition. He's the kind of player who looks better than he is when his team controls possession, and considerably worse when they don't.
