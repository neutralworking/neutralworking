Joe Worrall is a 29-year-old English centre-back who currently plays for Burnley. Known for his leadership and defensive capabilities, he has a right-footed preference and stands at 190 cm.

Recent Form

Joe Worrall's playing time for Burnley during the 2025/2026 Premier League season has been limited, featuring in 11 matches, with six of those appearances coming from the bench, accumulating 497 minutes on the pitch. He has yet to register a goal or an assist in the current Premier League campaign. Across all competitions this season, he has played 13 matches for 679 minutes, contributing to two clean sheets while 15 goals were conceded during his time on the field.

His recent match involvement shows a pattern of being an unused substitute or playing for short durations, such as 16 minutes against AFC Bournemouth and 57 minutes against Everton in March 2026. In the previous 2024/2025 season, Worrall joined Burnley in the summer and made 13 appearances across all competitions, despite sustaining a broken foot early in his tenure with the club. Burnley achieved promotion to the Premier League in the 2024/2025 season.

Tactical Profile

Joe Worrall operates primarily as a centre-back, a position he has consistently held throughout his career. Standing at 190 cm and preferring his right foot, he was once described by former manager Philippe Montanier as "strong and tall, but very clever too, tactically". His playing style is characterized by frequently clearing the ball out of defense and a tendency not to dive into tackles, indicating a measured and positional approach to defending.

Worrall is also noted for his ability to take set-pieces. His historical role as a captain for Nottingham Forest underscores his leadership qualities, which are valuable in organizing a defensive line. These attributes align with his internal archetype of 'Safety' and role as a 'Sweeper', emphasizing his capacity to protect the backline and initiate defensive clearances.

Career Highlights

Joe Worrall began his professional career with Nottingham Forest, signing in October 2011 and making his senior debut during a loan spell at Dagenham & Redbridge in January 2016. He gained further experience with a season-long loan at Scottish Premiership club Rangers in 2018-19. Upon his return to Nottingham Forest, Worrall became a pivotal figure, notably captaining the team to promotion to the Premier League in the 2021-22 season through the play-offs. His performances that season earned him a spot in the EFL Championship Team of the Season.

He was subsequently named Forest's captain for their 2022-23 Premier League campaign. In February 2024, Worrall moved on loan to Turkish side Beşiktaş, where he secured a Turkish Cup winner's medal in the 2023/2024 season. He then joined Burnley permanently in the summer of 2024 on a four-year deal. On the international stage, Worrall has represented England at U20 and U21 levels, and was part of the England U18 squad that won the Tournoi Maurice Revello (Toulon Tournament) in 2017.

Personality

Joe Worrall is widely regarded as a leader, a trait evidenced by his captaincy at Nottingham Forest. Interviews reveal him to be a mature and grounded individual with strong family values. He is known for his passionate approach to the game, often described as someone who "wears his heart on his sleeve".

Worrall has expressed a clear ambition to perform at the highest level and contribute significantly to Burnley's success, particularly in their aim to remain in the Premier League. He has also spoken openly about the profound support he received from his family and club following the tragic death of his uncle in August 2023, highlighting his resilience and the importance of his support network.

Condition / Medical History

Joe Worrall has experienced several periods of absence due to injury throughout his career. After joining Burnley in the summer of 2024, he suffered a broken foot early in the 2024/2025 season, which sidelined him for approximately 8 to 10 weeks. More recently, in early 2026, he sustained a muscle injury that caused him to miss matches in January and March.

His injury history also includes a calf injury and a broken rib in 2021, as well as knee injuries and a virus in 2018. He was also out with an unspecified injury from late December 2025 to late January 2026.
