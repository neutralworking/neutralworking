Benjamin Šeško is a 22-year-old Slovenian international centre-forward who currently plays for Manchester United. Standing at 195 cm and preferring his right foot, he is internally archetyped as a Marksman and plays the role of a Prima Punta.

Recent Form

Benjamin Šeško experienced a challenging start to the 2025-26 season at Manchester United, scoring only twice in 16 appearances under former manager Ruben Amorim. However, a significant upturn in his performance coincided with the managerial change in January 2026, which saw Michael Carrick take over. Following this transition, Šeško embarked on a strong goalscoring run, netting six goals throughout January and February, including crucial stoppage-time goals against Fulham and West Ham. His impressive contributions earned him the Manchester United Player of the Month award for February.

As of April 18, 2026, Šeško has accumulated 10 goals and 1 assist in 29 appearances for Manchester United across all competitions during the 2025/2026 season. He has registered 9 goals in the Premier League, maintaining a notable rate of 0.57 goals per 90 minutes. His efficiency in front of goal is highlighted by his ranking as the third-best finisher in the Premier League, despite making only 13 starts for Manchester United. His form has been particularly potent recently, with four goals in his last five matches and seven goals in his last eight as of early March 2026, positioning him to be United's top scorer despite limited minutes.

Tactical Profile

Šeško is a tall, athletic, right-footed centre-forward renowned for his powerful finishing, explosive pace, and aerial dominance. He possesses a powerful strike, capable of generating significant force even with minimal backlift, and generally prefers to finish with power. His physical attributes are complemented by surprising agility for his size, allowing him to comfortably take on opponents and link up with teammates, particularly when dropping between the lines after a deceptive movement.

In terms of his offensive play, Šeško has developed strong penalty-area instincts, demonstrating improved anticipation of goalscoring opportunities and the ability to adjust to imperfect deliveries. He exhibits excellent in-the-box instincts, effectively anticipating the ball, positioning himself well, and creating separation from defenders to get his shots away. While he tends to stay central in the final third, he is also adept at drifting wide into channels to exploit open spaces during counterattacks. His rapid speed, combined with good mobility and dribbling for a player of his height, makes him a dynamic threat in attacking transitions. Furthermore, he is strong on both feet for finishing and possesses a good shooting technique, often displaying patience when waiting for volleys.

Beyond his goalscoring prowess, Šeško is an active participant in his team's defensive efforts. He commits to immediate counter-pressing and is aggressive in close spaces, particularly after losing possession. He actively works to support midfield teammates by pressing back towards his own goal and can strategically force opponents into traps by curving his approach to cut passing options. Under Michael Carrick, he has been utilized as a pure 'number 9,' emphasizing his ability to play with his back to goal, link play effectively, and nod the ball onto others. His physical presence and quick acceleration make him a significant threat during counterattacks and dominant in aerial duels, averaging 2.75 aerial wins per 90 minutes.

Career Highlights

Benjamin Šeško began his professional journey by joining Red Bull Salzburg at the age of 16 in 2019. He spent two seasons on loan at FC Liefering, Salzburg's reserve team, where he was prolific, scoring 22 goals in 44 appearances in the Austrian second tier, including 21 goals in 29 games during the 2020-21 season. He made his first-team debut for Red Bull Salzburg in January 2021 and went on to win three Austrian Bundesliga titles and one Austrian Cup with the club.

In July 2023, Šeško moved to German club RB Leipzig, where he scored 27 goals in 64 appearances. His career continued its upward trajectory with a significant transfer to Premier League club Manchester United on August 9, 2025, signing a five-year contract until 2030. This move marked him as the first Slovenian player to represent Manchester United, with a reported transfer fee of an initial €76.5 million plus €8.5 million in performance-related add-ons. He made his debut for the club on August 17, 2025, and scored his first goal on September 27, 2025, against Brentford.

On the international stage, Šeško has been a key player for Slovenia. He was the top scorer for his nation in the UEFA Euro 2024 qualifiers, netting five goals in nine appearances, which helped Slovenia qualify for its first European Championship since 2000. He participated in all four of Slovenia's matches at Euro 2024, where the team reached the knockout stages for the first time. He also achieved a significant milestone by scoring his first international hat-trick on September 9, 2024, in a 3-0 UEFA Nations League B victory over Kazakhstan. Šeško holds the distinction of being Slovenia's youngest ever debutant for the senior national team (18 years and 1 day old) and youngest ever goalscorer (18 years, 4 months, and 8 days). His individual achievements were recognized when he was named the 2022 Slovenian Footballer of the Year.

Personality

Benjamin Šeško is characterized by a strong mindset focused on ambition, discipline, and mental strength. He openly discusses his philosophy that true greatness stems from a refusal to be satisfied, even after achieving success. He emphasizes the importance of maintaining hunger after scoring, always striving for more goals, and has learned from earlier career experiences where he would relax after finding the net. His approach to performance is driven by an internal demand to constantly improve; for example, if he scores three goals, he immediately aims for four.

Šeško views pressure as a privilege that motivates him to work harder. Away from the pitch, he practices meditation and enjoys playing basketball, activities he uses to maintain focus and clear his mind. His footballing idol is Zlatan Ibrahimović, admired for his charisma, personality, and leadership, while he also looks to Cristiano Ronaldo for inspiration regarding work ethic and professionalism. His fluency in Slovenian, German, and English further highlights his adaptability. Furthermore, he is dedicated to his physical development, taking gym sessions seriously to enhance his upper body and core strength, which contributes to his explosiveness, vertical leap, and speed.

Condition / Medical History

While initial concerns were high, early indications suggested the injury might not be as severe as first feared. He was subsequently ruled out for approximately a month due to this knee issue. In March 2026, he left national team duty due to another injury, though he was reportedly close to returning to action in April 2026 after a month on the sidelines with a knee problem. His medical history also includes an illness in May 2024 and a foot injury in August 2023.
