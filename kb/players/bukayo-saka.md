Bukayo Saka, the 24-year-old England international, continues to be a pivotal figure for Arsenal, operating effectively as a wide forward. His consistent performances and development have solidified his status as a key player for both club and country.

## Recent Form
In the 2025/2026 season, Bukayo Saka has maintained a strong attacking output for Arsenal. Across all competitions, he has featured in 42 games, scoring 9 goals and providing 5 assists. Specifically in the Premier League, he has recorded 6 goals and 3 assists in 27 or 28 appearances. His average playing time per game stands at 68 minutes. As of February 2026, Saka had made nearly 300 appearances for Arsenal, netting his 78th goal for the club, with 58 of those coming in the Premier League, placing him among Arsenal's top 10 all-time Premier League goalscorers. He has accumulated an impressive 147 goal involvements (goals plus assists) since his senior debut.

## Tactical Profile
Saka, a left-footed player primarily deployed on the right wing, excels as a wide attacking midfielder within a 4-2-3-1 formation. His tactical intelligence allows him to drift infield, occupying spaces between the lines, which is crucial for his role as a wide playmaker. This movement not only creates numerical superiority in central areas but also draws defenders, opening up space for overlapping runs from full-backs and facilitating interplay with central creative players like Martin Odegaard.

His technical attributes include brilliant close control and dribbling, often utilizing stepovers to bypass defenders and create attacking opportunities. Saka's ability to vary his play, either driving infield for shots or through balls, or beating his man on the outside for crosses, makes him highly unpredictable. Furthermore, his experience playing at left-back early in his career has instilled a comprehensive understanding of defensive duties and tactical discipline, distinguishing his play from more traditional wingers by blending defensive awareness with potent attacking instincts.

## Career Highlights
Bukayo Saka has accumulated significant accolades throughout his career. With Arsenal, he has won the FA Cup in the 2019/2020 season and two FA Community Shields in 2020/2021 and 2023/2024. His individual contributions have been recognized with him being named Arsenal's Player of the Season for both the 2020–21 and 2021–22 campaigns. He also received the prestigious PFA Young Player of the Year award for the 2022–23 season.

On the international stage, Saka has been a key player for England, participating in UEFA Euro 2020 and UEFA Euro 2024, where England finished as runners-up in both tournaments, and the 2022 FIFA World Cup. He was named England Men's Player of the Year for consecutive seasons, 2021–22 and 2022–23. Notably, he scored twice against Iran and once against Senegal during the 2022 World Cup. Saka also holds an Arsenal club record for 83 consecutive Premier League appearances, set in August 2023.

## Personality
Bukayo Saka is widely recognized for his humble and down-to-earth personality, a trait frequently praised by teammates and coaches. His first name, "Bukayo," originating from the Yoruba language, means "adds to happiness," which is said to reflect his jovial nature within the dressing room. Saka credits his discipline, humility, and professionalism to the traditional Nigerian values instilled by his parents. He has openly expressed his admiration for Cristiano Ronaldo's mentality and work ethic, drawing inspiration from the Portuguese star's commitment to continuous improvement.

## Condition / Medical History

His longest period out was due to a torn hamstring in December 2024, which sidelined him for over 100 days and caused him to miss 19 matches until April 2024.
