Facundo Medina is a 26-year-old Argentine central defender, currently playing for Olympique de Marseille on loan from Lens, with a mandatory purchase option set to be activated at the end of the 2025-2026 season. Standing at 184 cm, Medina is left-footed and is internally archetyped as a "Fortress" with a "Sweeper" role, reflecting his defensive solidity and ability to play out from the back.

Recent Form

Facundo Medina's 2025/2026 season with Olympique de Marseille has seen him feature in 13 Ligue 1 matches, where he has scored 1 goal and provided 1 assist across 1007 minutes of play. His goal involvement stands at 0.18 per 90 minutes. He has also made 4 appearances in the UEFA Champions League this season.

Medina experienced a significant ankle injury from late September 2025 until early January 2026, which sidelined him for two months. Upon his return, he was expected to immediately re-enter the starting lineup for Marseille. However, he is currently suspended for the match on April 18, 2026, due to an accumulation of yellow cards.

Tactical Profile

Medina is recognized as a modern "ball-playing center back" with strong technical abilities, comfortable carrying the ball and possessing an impressive passing range. He is particularly adept at long passes, a skill that allows him to switch play effectively from the left side of the defense. His role often involves defending on the front foot, actively seeking to regain possession quickly.

While primarily a center-back, Medina's versatility allows him to operate as a left-back who can transition into a left center-back in formations like a 3-2-2-3. His aggressive defensive style is a notable trait, though it sometimes leads to a high number of yellow cards. Areas identified for improvement include his weaker right foot and aerial duels, given his height of 184 cm. He also exhibits vocal leadership on the pitch.

Career Highlights

Medina's professional career saw him move from Talleres to Lens in July 2020 for a fee of €3.5 million. He played a crucial role in Lens's rise, contributing significantly to their second-place finish in Ligue 1 during the 2022-2023 season, which secured them a spot in the Champions League. His first goal for Lens was a memorable overhead kick in September 2020.

Internationally, Medina has represented Argentina at various youth levels, winning the 2019 Pan American Games with the U22 squad and the 2020 CONMEBOL Pre-Olympic Tournament with the U23s. He earned his first senior cap for the Argentina national team in October 2020 during a 2022 FIFA World Cup qualifying match and has since accumulated 7 caps. His recent move to Olympique de Marseille in July 2025, initially on loan with a mandatory purchase clause, was valued between €20 million and €22 million.

Personality

Facundo Medina is characterized by his passionate and aggressive approach to the game. He is known for his vocal leadership, which contributes to his presence on the field. His commitment and determination to win the ball back for his team are frequently highlighted.

Condition / Medical History

Medina has faced several injury setbacks throughout his career. Most recently, he sustained an ankle injury that kept him out of action from late September 2025 until early January 2026. Prior to this, he also had an ankle injury in August 2025. Other recorded injuries include a knee injury in March 2023 and December 2020, a hamstring injury in April 2022, and a period of illness in April 2021. His aggressive playing style has also led to a tendency to accumulate yellow cards, resulting in suspensions.
