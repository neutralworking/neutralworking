Recent Form

Julian Alvarez has demonstrated strong performance during the 2025-2026 season for Atlético Madrid, featuring in 46 games across all competitions, where he has scored 18 goals and provided 9 assists. In La Liga specifically, he has registered 8 goals and 4 assists over 29 appearances. His impact extends to the UEFA Champions League, where he has contributed 8 goals and 4 assists in 11 matches.

Alvarez's recent contributions include a notable free-kick goal against Barcelona in the Champions League and a decisive last-minute winner against Real Oviedo in La Liga. He also played a pivotal role in a Champions League fixture, being directly involved in three goals by assisting one and scoring two, including a "walk-in" goal and a finish from a fast break.

Last season (2024-2025), Alvarez was recognized as Atlético Madrid's Player of the Season, concluding the campaign with an impressive 29 goals in 57 appearances. His consistent goal-scoring and playmaking abilities highlight his importance to the team's attacking efforts.

Tactical Profile

Julian Alvarez is a highly versatile forward capable of operating in various attacking roles, including as a centre-forward, a right-winger in a three-man attack, or as part of a two-striker system. He thrives when partnered with another striker, demonstrating an ability to complement different player profiles. Alvarez is adept at playing as a number 9 or a second striker, often dropping between the lines to facilitate play and create opportunities for teammates.

His tactical intelligence is evident in his exceptional off-the-ball movement, allowing him to effectively exploit space behind defensive lines. Alvarez is clinical within the penalty area, utilizing his body to gain an advantage and timing his runs precisely. He consistently seeks out space to offer vertical passing options and makes sound decisions in the final third, contributing both goals and assists.

Known as "La Araña" ("The Spider") for his pressing, dynamic scoring, and link-up play, Alvarez possesses solid ball-striking mechanics with both feet, enabling a variety of finishes. While confident in shooting from range, he is particularly effective when active at the top of the box, combining with teammates before attacking the goal. His pressing ability includes a good understanding of setting traps and arching runs to direct opposition play.

Career Highlights

Julian Alvarez has accumulated an impressive array of titles and individual accolades throughout his career. With Manchester City, he was a key part of the team that achieved a treble in his debut campaign, winning the Premier League, FA Cup, and UEFA Champions League in 2022-2023. He further added the UEFA Super Cup and FIFA Club World Cup to his Manchester City honors.

Prior to his move to Europe, Alvarez enjoyed significant success with River Plate, securing the Copa Argentina, the 2018 Copa Libertadores, and the Argentine Primera División in 2021, where he finished as the league's top scorer. His outstanding performances in Argentina earned him the prestigious South American Footballer of the Year award in 2021.

On the international stage, Alvarez has been a vital component of the Argentina national team, winning the FIFA World Cup in 2022 and the Copa América in both 2021 and 2024. During the 2022 World Cup, he notably scored two goals in the semi-final against Croatia, becoming the youngest player since Pelé in 1958 to achieve this feat. He also contributed to Argentina's CONMEBOL–UEFA Cup of Champions victory in 2022 and the CONMEBOL Pre-Olympic Tournament win in 2020.

Personality

Julian Alvarez is consistently described as a quiet, responsible, and focused individual. His former coach noted his inherent talent and dedication from a young age, while Lionel Messi has referred to him as a "lovely guy". Sergio Aguero also highlighted Alvarez's "polished character," emphasizing his calm demeanor and eagerness to learn.

Alvarez's background instilled a strong work ethic and a driven mindset, which are evident in his professional approach. On the field, he displays confidence, aggression, and a distinct attitude, consistently fighting for victory and demonstrating a strong will to score and be decisive.

Condition / Medical History

Julian Alvarez has experienced a few minor setbacks in his medical history, most notably a knee injury in September 2025. This injury led to his substitution at halftime during a La Liga match against Villarreal and subsequently caused him to miss a UEFA Champions League fixture against Liverpool.

In November 2025, he also dealt with a brief illness. While he has had some minor injuries in previous years (2019, 2020) and a period of "lacking match fitness" in early 2023, there is no public reporting of recurring or long-term significant medical issues.
