Julian Ryerson, the 28-year-old Norwegian full-back for Borussia Dortmund, has demonstrated consistent performance and versatility throughout his career. Standing at 183 cm and preferring his right foot, Ryerson is a key component for both his club and national team.

Recent Form

Julian Ryerson has been in strong form during the 2025/2026 Bundesliga season, registering 12 assists in 1,830 minutes across 26 appearances, earning an average FotMob rating of 7.26. He is noted for his high number of assists and overall rating compared to other right wing-backs in the league. In a standout performance on February 13, 2026, Ryerson provided four assists, all from crosses, in a 4-0 victory over Mainz, making him only the fourth player in Bundesliga history and the second for Dortmund to achieve this feat. Across all competitions this season, he has accumulated 18 assists. His recent club form includes a 2-0 win against VfB Stuttgart on April 4, 2026, where he earned a 7.3 FotMob rating, and a 2-0 home win against Augsburg on March 14, 2026, contributing an assist and an 8.3 rating. Borussia Dortmund has also seen a strong run in the Bundesliga with Ryerson, remaining undefeated in their last 19 matches and winning their last seven home games.

Tactical Profile

Ryerson is a tactically flexible player, capable of operating as a full-back or wing-back, and has also been deployed as a wide center-back or even in midfield under coach Niko Kovač's 3-4-2-1 system at Borussia Dortmund. His versatility extends to playing effectively on both the left and right flanks. Dortmund's sporting director, Sebastian Kehl, has described Ryerson as an "intelligent and aggressive defender". His playing style is characterized by strong crossing ability, effective key passes, and proficiency in set-pieces, passing, dribbling, and concentration. Ryerson is known for his astute one-on-one defending and positional awareness. While he excels in attacking phases by delivering dangerous crosses and stretching play, he also diligently tracks back defensively. However, his aerial duels and discipline are noted areas for improvement, as he tends to commit fouls often.

Career Highlights

Julian Ryerson began his senior career with Viking in 2015 after joining their youth setup in 2013. He then moved to Union Berlin in July 2018, where he scored his first Bundesliga goal in October 2021 and his first European goal in the Conference League in November 2021. A significant achievement with Union Berlin was their qualification for the UEFA Europa League in May 2022. In January 2023, Ryerson transferred to Borussia Dortmund. He made his UEFA Champions League debut in February 2023 and was part of the squad that reached the Champions League final in the 2023/2024 season, finishing as a runner-up. A notable moment in his Dortmund career includes scoring in a 2-0 away victory against Bayern Munich in March 2024, marking Dortmund's first win in "Der Klassiker" since 2019 and their first at the Allianz Arena in a decade. On the international stage, Ryerson earned his first senior cap for Norway in 2020 and has since accumulated 41 caps with one goal as of March 31, 2026. He is expected to represent Norway at the 2026 FIFA World Cup, following their qualification.

Personality

Julian Ryerson is perceived as a highly competitive individual with a strong desire to win, a trait highlighted by Dortmund's sporting director Sebastian Kehl. Fans often refer to him as a "warrior" who plays with significant "passion and energy". Ryerson himself has expressed feeling "at home" at Borussia Dortmund and cherishes every match day, especially when playing at home.

Condition / Medical History

Ryerson has experienced several injuries and illnesses in recent seasons. In 2025, he dealt with a calf injury in August and flu in February. The previous year, 2024, saw him sidelined with illness in December and October, a groin injury in October, and physical discomfort in March. A more significant knee injury kept him out from December 2023 to February 2024. In August 2023, he sustained an ankle injury, and in February 2023, he had an eye injury. Notably, in March 2025, he was taken to the hospital after a Champions League match against Barcelona, reportedly fainting in the tunnel.
