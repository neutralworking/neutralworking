Recent Form

Joakim Mæhle's 2025/2026 Bundesliga season with Wolfsburg has been significantly impacted by injury, limiting him to 11 appearances where he has scored one goal and provided one assist. He has played 774 minutes in the current Bundesliga campaign, maintaining an average FotMob rating of 6.76. His season was interrupted by a serious shoulder injury sustained in late October 2025, which sidelined him until March 2026.

Prior to this injury-affected season, Mæhle had a more active 2024/2025 Bundesliga campaign, featuring in 28 matches for Wolfsburg. During that season, he contributed three goals and five assists, earning a higher average rating of 6.9.

Tactical Profile

Operating primarily as a wing-back, Mæhle is known for his dynamic and attacking play style, often pushing forward to contribute to the offensive phases. He is adept at getting involved in build-up play and driving to the byline from his position on the flank. His versatility allows him to play effectively on both sides of the defensive line, offering tactical flexibility.

Despite his offensive inclinations, Mæhle also contributes defensively, as evidenced by his 2025/2026 season statistics which include a 54.2% tackle success rate, 61.5% aerial duels won, 2.69 clearances per game, and 1.5 interceptions per game. His profile suggests he thrives in systems that allow him a more advanced role, such as a three-at-the-back formation.

Career Highlights

Mæhle began his senior career with AaB before moving to KRC Genk in 2017. With Genk, he secured the Belgian championship in the 2018/2019 season and participated in the UEFA Champions League. In January 2021, he transferred to Italian club Atalanta for a fee of €10 million, where he made 80 appearances and scored 4 goals across all competitions before joining VfL Wolfsburg in August 2023 for approximately €12 million.

On the international stage, Mæhle debuted for the Denmark national team in September 2020. He was a key player in Denmark's run to the semi-finals of UEFA Euro 2020, scoring crucial goals against Russia and Wales. He also scored the decisive goal that qualified Denmark for the 2022 FIFA World Cup and was included in their squad for both the 2022 World Cup and UEFA Euro 2024.

Personality

Joakim Mæhle is recognized for his strong energy and unwavering will, qualities that endeared him to the Danish public during the national team's successful Euro 2020 campaign. He became a symbol of the team's resurgence, celebrated for his relentless effort and determination.

Condition / Medical History

Mæhle has experienced a notable history of injuries throughout his career. Most recently, he sustained a serious shoulder injury in training on October 29, 2025, which kept him out of action until March 2026. In 2024, he also dealt with a hip injury in November, an ankle injury from August to October, and another shoulder injury between May and June. His medical history also includes previous shoulder, calf, thigh, and foot injuries.
