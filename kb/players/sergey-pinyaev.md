ClubKrylya Sovetov Samara

PositionWinger

Born2 November 2004







********

Pinyaev had been considered a future star of Russian football even before he had a trial at Manchester United in 2019. There, he impressed club officials in games for their under-19 side (while still 14) but because of UK rules he could not sign for them before his 18th birthday. The feedback he was given in Manchester made the headlines in Russia: “Pinyaev is the brightest player who has come to the club for a trial over the past 15 years.” He is a rather small (167cm), mobile, dynamic, creative player who can play anywhere in attack, be it as winger, No 10, false nine or a second striker. He has a good eye for goal and a proper killer instinct. He scored his first professional goal on his debut at 15, becoming the youngest goalscorer in history of the FNL (Russia’s second tier) and a year later became the youngest player to register an assist in the Russian Premier League at the age of 16. Pinyaev has featured in seven of eight Krylya Sovetov’s matches in Russia’s top division at the time of writing and kept his status of one of the brightest young players in the country. On 22 September he made his first start and immediately scored twice against Znamya in the cup. **[Artur Petrosyan](https://twitter.com/arturpetrosyan)**  
[Watch Sergey Pinyaev in action](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=r7Ze1-L6_XY)  
  
**September 2022 update** Keeps the status of being the brightest of Russian talents. He registered two goals and two assists coming off the bench last season and started the first game of the new season but was unlucky to be diagnosed with a collapsed lung. After being out for nearly two months he scored on his first start on 17 September.  
  
**October 2023 update** At 18, he maintains the status of one of Russia’s brightest talents. Made his debut for the national team in friendlies and moved to Lokomotiv Moscow amid talks of an inevitable departure for western Europe.
