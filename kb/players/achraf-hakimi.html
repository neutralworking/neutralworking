Achraf Hakimi, the 27-year-old Moroccan wing-back for Paris Saint-Germain, continues to be a dynamic force on the right flank, showcasing his blend of attacking prowess and defensive contributions. Known for his right-footed play and standing at 181 cm, Hakimi operates as a Conductor archetype with a Wing-back role, consistently influencing play from deep positions.

Recent Form

Achraf Hakimi has maintained a strong performance level in the 2025/2026 season with Paris Saint-Germain, contributing to the team's success in various competitions. In the current Ligue 1 season, he has registered 2 goals and 1 assist across 15 matches, playing 1,179 minutes with an average FotMob rating of 7.22. His recent match performances in April 2026 include a 7.7 FotMob rating in a 2-0 away win against Liverpool and another 7.3 rating in a 2-0 home win against the same opponent in the Champions League. He also achieved a high rating of 8.5 in a 2-1 international friendly win for Morocco against Paraguay in March 2026, where he provided two assists.

His impactful contributions were evident in PSG's recent Champions League-winning campaign, where he was involved in 27 goals across all competitions, highlighting his evolution into a decisive attacking player. Hakimi's consistent displays have seen him play a key role in maintaining balance between defense and attack for PSG, underscoring his importance to the squad.

Tactical Profile

Hakimi is recognized as one of the world's premier right-backs, possessing the versatility to also operate as a left-back or in more advanced midfield roles. His game is characterized by a high technical level, effective passing, and an astute understanding of when to make overlapping runs. Physically, he boasts exceptional acceleration and pace, which allows him to frequently overcome opponents in attacking situations.

Under manager Luis Enrique at Paris Saint-Germain, Hakimi's tactical role has undergone a significant transformation. He is now frequently deployed as an inverted full-back, moving into midfield during possession, which has redefined how he is perceived in modern football. This tactical shift, which sees him as a central component in both offensive and defensive phases, has allowed him to showcase his strengths more consistently and contribute to goal involvements, making him an indispensable part of PSG's strategy. While his defensive positioning and one-on-one defending were once considered weaknesses, he has shown considerable improvement in anticipation over the years.

Career Highlights

Achraf Hakimi's career is marked by numerous significant achievements at both club and international levels. He was part of the Real Madrid squad that won the UEFA Champions League in 2017–18, the UEFA Super Cup in 2017, and the FIFA Club World Cup in 2017. During his loan spell at Borussia Dortmund, he won the DFL-Supercup in 2019 and was named in the Bundesliga Team of the Year for 2019–20.

His move to Inter Milan saw him play a crucial role in their Serie A title victory in the 2020–21 season. Since joining Paris Saint-Germain, Hakimi has continued to accumulate silverware, including multiple Ligue 1 titles (2021–22, 2022–23, 2023–24, 2024–25), French Cups (2023–24, 2024–25), and French Super Cups (2022–23, 2023–24, 2024–25). Individually, he has been recognized as the CAF Youth Player of the Year twice (2018, 2019) and has been included in the IFFHS Men's World Team in 2021 and 2022. For his national team, Morocco, he made his World Cup debut in 2018 and scored a crucial goal against Belgium in the 2022 FIFA World Cup, contributing to Morocco's historic run.

Condition / Medical History

Achraf Hakimi sustained a syndesmosis tear with mild deltoid ligament damage in his left ankle in May 2025, an injury that did not require surgery but had an estimated recovery time of eight weeks. This injury, which occurred during a match against Bayern Munich following a tackle from Luis Díaz, ruled him out of the Africa Cup of Nations scheduled for December 2025.

His injury history also includes several muscle and thigh injuries in recent years, such as a muscle injury from March 2023 and a thigh injury from February 2023. Earlier in his career, he experienced a foot injury in 2019. While some speculate that the ankle injury could impact his primary strength of pace, he has generally shown resilience in returning from past setbacks.
