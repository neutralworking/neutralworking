Snapshot

As a central defender, Sanchís stood out for his defensive composure, placement and agility. He set a new standard within his club due to both his sporting and human skills.10(https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manolo_Sanch%C3%ADs#cite_note-RM-10) An aggressive yet fair player,11(https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manolo_Sanch%C3%ADs#cite_note-11) he was also noted for his tactical intelligence and positional sense, and usually played as a sweeper.

A part of the famous La Quinta del Buitre which stemmed from the Real Madrid youth academy, he was the only of its five members to spend his entire career with the club. His father Manuel Sanchís also played for Real Madrid, and both were Spanish internationals.2(https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manolo_Sanch%C3%ADs#cite_note-2)3(https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manolo_Sanch%C3%ADs#cite_note-3)4(https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manolo_Sanch%C3%ADs#cite_note-Fans-4)

Sanchís appeared in more than 700 competitive matches for his only club, and represented the national team in one World Cup and one European Championship.
