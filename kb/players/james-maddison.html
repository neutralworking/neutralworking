James Maddison is a 29-year-old English attacking midfielder who currently plays for Tottenham Hotspur, known for his role as a playmaker. He is right-footed and a key creative force for his club and country.

Recent Form

James Maddison's 2025-2026 season has been significantly impacted by a ruptured Anterior Cruciate Ligament (ACL) in his right knee, sustained during a pre-season friendly against Newcastle United. This injury, described as "bad" and a "brutal moment" by Tottenham's head coach Thomas Frank, is expected to keep him sidelined for most of the current season. Positive steps in his ACL recovery were reported in January 2026, and as of April 2026, he has rejoined training, with coach Roberto De Zerbi expressing optimism about his influence.

Prior to this major setback, Maddison had a productive 2024-2025 season. He scored his first Premier League goal of that season on September 29 against Brentford and netted two goals in a 4-0 away victory over Manchester City on his birthday. He also registered his first Europa League goal for Tottenham in January 2025 against Hoffenheim, where he also provided an assist. Despite suffering a calf strain in January 2025, which kept him out for three weeks, he returned to score the winning goal against Manchester United in February 2025. His 2024-25 campaign concluded prematurely in May 2025 due to a knee injury, after he had contributed an assist and a penalty win in the Europa League quarter-finals and scored in the semi-final first leg.

Tactical Profile

As a central midfielder and designated playmaker, James Maddison excels in an attacking midfield role, often operating as a number 10. His tactical intelligence allows him to drift into central positions even when deployed wider, effectively exploiting spaces between defensive lines. He possesses excellent vision and a wide passing range, enabling him to link the midfield with the final attacking third and create goal-scoring opportunities.

Maddison is also recognized for his accurate shooting from outside the penalty area, adding another dimension to his offensive threat. His on-field leadership and strong mentality were acknowledged when he was named vice-captain for Tottenham in August 2023. Coach Roberto De Zerbi has praised Maddison as a "top player on the pitch with the ball" and highlighted his significant "personality, mentality on the pitch".

Career Highlights

James Maddison began his professional career at Coventry City before moving to Norwich City in 2016. His impressive performances for Norwich in the 2017-18 season earned him a spot in both the Championship PFA Team of the Year and the EFL Team of the Season. In 2018, he transferred to Leicester City, where he achieved significant success, winning the FA Cup and the FA Community Shield in 2021. During the 2021-22 season, he was named Leicester City's Player of the Season, contributing 18 goals and 12 assists across all competitions.

Maddison joined Tottenham Hotspur in 2023 and quickly made an impact, earning the Premier League's Player of the Month award for August 2023 after recording one goal and two assists in his initial three league appearances. On the international stage, he made his senior debut for England in 2019 and was included in the squad for the 2022 FIFA World Cup. He has accumulated 7 senior caps for England. In the 2024-2025 season, he was part of the Tottenham squad that won the Europa League, although he missed the final due to injury.

Personality

James Maddison is recognized for his strong personality and leadership qualities both on and off the pitch. Tottenham's head coach, Roberto De Zerbi, has described him as a "real leader who talk inside the dressing room" and praised his "personality, mentality on the pitch". These attributes were further underscored when he was appointed vice-captain for Tottenham Hotspur in August 2023, demonstrating his influence within the squad.

Condition / Medical History

James Maddison is currently in rehabilitation for a ruptured Anterior Cruciate Ligament (ACL) in his right knee, an injury sustained during the 2025-26 pre-season. He underwent surgery in August 2025 and is making positive progress, having returned to training as of April 2026, with hopes of a return before the end of the current season.

Maddison has a documented history of injuries throughout his career, with knee problems being a recurring issue. Other notable absences include an ankle injury in the 2023-24 season, which sidelined him for 74 days, and a calf strain in January 2025. He also sustained a knee injury in May 2025, which prematurely ended his 2024-25 season.
