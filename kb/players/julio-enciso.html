Tactical Profile

Julio Enciso operates primarily as an attacking midfielder or left winger, demonstrating versatility that also allows him to play as a central forward or false nine. He is right-footed and is characterized by his agility, keen sense of goal, precise mid-range shooting, and adept dribbling skills. Enciso has expressed a willingness to adapt to various positions based on his coach's requirements.

His playing style is marked by verticality, mobility, and significant pace, coupled with strong ball dominance. Enciso is effective in both confined and open spaces, showcasing high-quality maneuvering and ball retention, alongside the pace to bypass defenders. He is a crucial asset in transitions and counterattacks, with intelligent off-the-ball movement, electrifying pace, and a tendency to make incisive diagonal runs behind defensive lines. Within the penalty area, his explosiveness and low center of gravity enable him to quickly evade markers and find optimal goal-scoring opportunities. Enciso also possesses excellent technical skills, creativity, vision, and playmaking ability when operating in free spaces between defensive lines. He is known for his precise passing, both short and long-range, and his ability to execute "nutmeg" passes. Enciso is also a high-volume attacking player, as evidenced by his 4.24 shots per 90 minutes in 2023. Defensively, he is a committed and industrious attacker, contributing significantly to pressing efforts and tracking back.

Career Highlights

Julio Enciso made his professional debut at the age of 15 for Club Libertad on March 17, 2019, becoming the youngest player to do so for the club. He transferred to Brighton & Hove Albion on June 17, 2022, for a fee of £9.5 million. His Premier League debut followed on October 29, 2022. Enciso scored his first goal for Brighton on April 4, 2023, against Bournemouth, and notably netted a 25-yard goal against Manchester City on May 24, 2023, which was recognized as both the BBC Goal of the Season and the Premier League Goal of the Season for the 2022-23 campaign.

In January 2025, he joined Ipswich Town on loan for the remainder of the 2024-25 season, where he made 13 appearances and scored two goals. Enciso then moved to Strasbourg on a permanent deal on September 1, 2025, for a reported €9.5 million, with Brighton retaining a sell-on clause. On the international stage, he has represented Paraguay's senior national team since June 14, 2021, accumulating 31 appearances and scoring four goals as of March 31, 2026. In July 2024, Enciso was recognized by the Centre International d'Etude du Sport (CIES) as the second-best U-21 player globally for creativity and effectiveness in finishing plays.

Condition / Medical History

Julio Enciso's career has been impacted by several injury concerns, particularly involving his knee. In August 2023, he sustained a left knee meniscus tear that required surgery and sidelined him for approximately six months. Following his return from this significant injury, he has experienced a series of recurring minor issues.

His injury history includes multiple knee injuries, a meniscus injury, muscle injuries, and knocks reported in 2023, 2024, and 2025. Concerns regarding his recurring knee issues were highlighted in August 2025 when a proposed transfer to the BlueCo group (which owns Chelsea and Strasbourg) reportedly collapsed after he failed a medical examination due to these ongoing problems and having undergone three operations on the same knee.
