Raw scouting narrative

class: defender

France are well stacked in central defence and Dayot Upamecano is among their top options thanks to his strength, speed and anticipation.

The centre back is always on the lookout for the right pass forward to get Bayern Munich moving, having impressed under Julian Nagelsmann at former club RB Leipzig.

Dayot Upamecano is a 27-year-old French central defender for Bayern Munich, known for his "Conductor" archetype and "Stopper" role, playing predominantly with his right foot. Standing at 186 cm, he has established himself as a key figure for both club and country.

Recent Form

Dayot Upamecano has maintained a strong presence in Bayern Munich's defense during the 2025/2026 season, featuring in 22 Bundesliga matches, 9 Champions League games, and appearances in the DFB Pokal and Supercup. He has contributed with one goal and one assist in the Bundesliga, and one assist in the Champions League. His passing accuracy remains a significant asset, completing approximately 92.46% of his passes in the Bundesliga, with 81.73 passes per 90 minutes, placing him in the top percentile among Bundesliga players.

Defensively, Upamecano has recorded 13 clean sheets and conceded an average of 0.97 goals per 90 minutes in the Bundesliga this season. He has received 4 yellow cards in 22 Bundesliga matches and one red card across all competitions. Notably, he excels in defensive duels, boasting an 87.8% win rate in the Bundesliga, which is among the highest for centre-backs in the top leagues. His build-up effectiveness rating also places him highly among Bundesliga players, highlighting his role in initiating attacks from the back.

Over the past year and a half, Upamecano has been lauded for his consistent, high-level performances, with Bayern officials recognizing his growth into a leading figure within the squad. His ability to combine physical prowess with technical skill allows him to dictate play from deep positions while maintaining defensive solidity.

Tactical Profile

Upamecano's tactical profile aligns with his "Conductor" and "Stopper" roles, characterized by an aggressive and proactive defensive style coupled with strong ball-playing capabilities. He frequently steps out of the defensive line to pressure opponents and win the ball higher up the pitch, a trait that benefits high-pressing teams. His physical attributes, including remarkable pace and awareness, enable him to recover quickly even when his aggressive positioning occasionally leaves him exposed.

As a "Conductor," Upamecano demonstrates excellent vision and accuracy in his passing, capable of executing long passes to initiate attacks and comfortable carrying the ball forward. He is an incisive passer who can pick out teammates with either foot, making him a primary ball progressor for Bayern Munich. His high pass completion rate and ability to make progressive passes underscore his importance in Bayern's build-up play.

In his "Stopper" role, Upamecano excels in off-the-ball situations, utilizing his athleticism and in-game awareness to protect space and track opponents within the penalty area. He is strong in aerial duels, tackles, and interceptions, contributing significantly to his team's defensive stability. While his aggressive style can sometimes lead to fouls or being caught out of position, his recovery speed and overall defensive acumen often mitigate these risks.

Career Highlights

Dayot Upamecano has accumulated a significant number of accolades throughout his career. With Bayern Munich, he has secured three consecutive Bundesliga titles from 2021 to 2024, and a fourth in the 2024/2025 season. He has also won three German Super Cups (2021-22, 2022-23, 2025-26) with the club. Prior to his move to Bayern, he won two Austrian Bundesliga titles and two Austrian Cups with Red Bull Salzburg.

On the international stage, Upamecano has been a vital part of the French national team. He was a winner of the UEFA Nations League in 2021 and played a key role in France's run to the FIFA World Cup final in 2022, where they finished as runners-up. His youth career also saw him achieve success, including winning the European Under-17 Championship in 2015 with France U17. He made his senior international debut for France on September 5, 2020. In February 2026, Upamecano extended his contract with Bayern Munich until June 2030, solidifying his long-term commitment to the club.

Personality

Dayot Upamecano presents a contrasting personality on and off the pitch. While he describes himself as generally calm and reserved in his personal life, he transforms into a "lion" when he steps onto the field, emphasizing his need to be vocal and encouraging to teammates as a defender. This on-field assertiveness has seen him mature into a leadership figure within the Bayern squad.

His mentality is deeply rooted in a desire to win and a strong work ethic. Upamecano has stated his commitment to giving his all for his teammates in every training session, aiming for clean sheets in every game, and aspiring to win as many titles as possible with Bayern. He also attributes his mental strength to his childhood experiences playing street football, where he learned to endure pain and develop a competitive drive. Upamecano maintains a private personal life, preferring not to share details on social media, though he has mentioned having a son who motivates him. He also views current Bayern manager Vincent Kompany as an important example and leader, praising Kompany's intense approach and guidance on positioning.

Condition / Medical History

Dayot Upamecano has experienced a notable history of injuries throughout his career, including various muscle strains, hip, knee, and thigh injuries, as well as periods of overload and illness. As of early 2026, his injury history records a total of 22 injuries, resulting in 434 days missed from play.

Recent injury archives indicate a muscle injury in January 2026 and an illness in the same month. In 2025, he dealt with overload, a knee injury (March to June), and further muscle injuries. While he was reported as fit in early 2026, his medical history suggests a pattern of recurring, though often short-term, absences due to various physical ailments.
