## Snapshot

A centre-back built for the lower tiers of English football—physical, direct, and unfussy about possession—Boyle brings the kind of no-nonsense defending that works when you're dealing with target-man strikers and direct play. His reading of the game is pedestrian; he relies on positioning and body shape rather than anticipation, which means he's easily exposed against technical forwards working in the half-spaces or making intelligent off-the-ball runs. At 30, he's exactly what he's always been: a reliable, unglamorous professional who will keep it simple and rarely embarrass himself, but he has no business anywhere near a top-six club.
