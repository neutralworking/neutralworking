## Snapshot

A pressing machine in the No. 8 role with genuine technical security on the ball, de Wit orchestrates Utrecht's work rate through the middle with the kind of tireless two-box running that keeps Eredivisie sides ticking. His range of passing is legitimate—he'll find a full-back in the half-space or switch play without drama—but he's not a creative catalyst; he's the glue, the organizer, the player who wins the ball back and immediately recycles it. The limitation is clear: he lacks the progressive carrying or incisive through-ball invention that elevates him from 'very good Eredivisie midfielder' to 'interesting European prospect,' and at 28, he's unlikely to acquire it.
