## Snapshot

Iheanacho is a capable, technically sound striker who can link play with clever movement in the half-space and finish with his left foot when angles favour him—he's a proper footballer, not just a poacher. His weakness is decisiveness in the final third; he takes an extra touch, he dribbles when he should pass, and he's occasionally indecisive when the chance arrives, which costs him goals at the highest level. He's a decent signing for Celtic's depth, a capable striker for a Scottish club battling in Europe, but not a seventeen-goal-a-season man.
