## Tactical Profile

Igor Thiago operates as a versatile center-forward, blending traditional number nine attributes with modern attacking movements. He excels in the central penalty area, where he positions himself to attack crosses, capitalize on loose balls, and finish cutbacks. Beyond the box, he drifts into half-spaces to create one-on-one situations and draw defenders out of position, showcasing a modern forward's understanding of space.

Thiago is also adept at linking play, dropping deep to receive the ball under pressure, turning, and combining with midfielders to initiate attacks. His physicality is a significant asset; he uses his strength to outmuscle defenders, shield the ball, and is a primary target for goal kicks, set pieces, and long balls, dominating aerial duels. While technically sound with good control and dribbling ability, his progressive carrying of the ball is less pronounced, which can be a limitation against deeply entrenched defenses. He demonstrates clinical, instinctive finishing, scoring with both feet and his head, and consistently hunts high-xG opportunities.

## Career Highlights

Igor Thiago began his professional career at Cruzeiro, making his debut at 18 in January 2020 and scoring 10 goals in 64 appearances over three seasons. He then moved to Bulgarian champions Ludogorets Razgrad in March 2022, where he quickly earned promotion from the reserve team and contributed to winning back-to-back Bulgarian First League titles (2021–22, 2022–23), the 2023 Bulgarian Cup, and the 2022 Bulgarian Supercup, scoring 21 goals in 55 games.

In June 2023, Thiago signed with Belgian Pro League club Club Brugge for a record €7.9 million fee for a Bulgarian club. He made an immediate impact, scoring in his first two league games and finishing the season with 29 goals and six assists as Club Brugge won the Jupiler Pro League and reached the Europa Conference League semi-final. Brentford secured his services on a five-year deal in February 2024 for a reported club-record fee, with the transfer becoming effective on July 1, 2024. On March 16, 2026, Thiago earned his first call-up to the Brazil national team and subsequently scored his first international goal.
