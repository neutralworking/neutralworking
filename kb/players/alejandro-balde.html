Alejandro Balde is a 22-year-old left-footed defender for Barcelona and the Spanish national team, known for his dynamic play as a wide defender (WD) with an "Invertido" role, aligning with a "Conductor" archetype.

Tactical Profile

Balde is recognized as a dynamic and promising left-back, characterized by a playing style that integrates explosive speed, technical skill, and tactical intelligence. His agility and pace enable him to make quick, overlapping runs, effectively adding width and depth to Barcelona's attacking movements. This speed is equally crucial in his defensive duties, allowing him to recover quickly and contribute significantly to the team's counter-pressing tactics.

Defensively, Balde adopts a proactive approach, actively closing down opponents and applying pressure to force errors and regain possession. He exhibits strong game-reading abilities, anticipating passes and making timely interceptions. Beyond his defensive contributions, he is noted for delivering accurate crosses and maintaining a high success rate of 85% on forward passes, showcasing his ability to advance the ball into dangerous areas. While his dribbling, passing, and key passes are considered strengths, his tackling is noted as a weakness, with a tendency to avoid diving into challenges. His attacking prowess and pace have also led to tactical experiments, including being tested as a left winger in training.

Career Highlights

Alejandro Balde joined Barcelona's prestigious La Masia youth academy at the age of eight in 2011, progressing through their ranks. He made his first-team debut for Barcelona on September 14, 2021, in a UEFA Champions League match against Bayern Munich. His role in the team became more prominent during the 2022-2023 season under coach Xavi, where he secured the left-back position.

A significant milestone in his club career was scoring his first goal for Barcelona on May 14, 2023, in a 4-2 victory over Espanyol, a match that clinched the league title for his team. He further solidified his commitment to the club by signing a new contract until 2028, featuring a substantial €1 billion buyout clause. Balde also scored his first El Clásico goal in the 2025 Supercopa de España final, contributing to a 5-2 win. By December 2024, he had already reached 100 official appearances for Barcelona's first team. On the international stage, Balde received a surprise call-up to the Spain national team for the 2022 FIFA World Cup as an injury replacement, making his debut on November 23, 2022, and featuring in four matches during the tournament. His trophy cabinet includes two La Liga titles (2022-23, 2024-25), one Copa del Rey (2024-25), and three Supercopa de España titles (2022-23, 2024-25, 2025-26) with Barcelona, alongside a UEFA Nations League A title (2022-23) with Spain.
