Brennan Johnson, a 24-year-old Welsh international, currently plays as a winger or forward for Premier League club Crystal Palace. Originally from Nottingham, Johnson is known for his pace, intelligent movement, and clinical finishing, operating effectively as a Raumdeuter and Inverted Winger. He joined Crystal Palace in 2026, following a successful tenure at Tottenham Hotspur, where he notably won the UEFA Europa League in 2025.

Recent Form

Brennan Johnson's recent form reflects his transition to Crystal Palace in 2026, following a productive 2024/25 season with Tottenham Hotspur. During his final season at Spurs, he was a key attacking player, finishing as the club's top scorer with 18 goals across all competitions by May 2025. He also contributed significantly with 10 assists, which was the joint-most for Tottenham that season. A notable highlight was scoring the decisive goal in the 2025 UEFA Europa League final against Manchester United, securing a major trophy for Spurs.

Since his move to Crystal Palace for the 2025/26 season, Johnson has made 28 appearances, including 14 as a substitute, recording 2 goals and 1 assist. His contributions have been made within a new tactical setup under Oliver Glasner, where he is expected to thrive in a system that favors explosive transition attacks. Despite limited minutes at times, his ability to impact games in the final third remains a key asset.

Tactical Profile

Johnson operates with a smart understanding of tactics, particularly excelling in off-the-ball movement and finding spaces to create chances. He is often described as a "wide shadow striker" or "poacher" rather than a conventional winger, possessing an innate ability to be in the right place at the right time to finish moves. His strength lies in providing a finishing touch in the final third with minimal touches, often arriving with momentum to strike the ball.

While his dribbling skills and speed allow him to beat defenders, he is not typically a player who consistently takes on and beats his man in isolated wide situations. Instead, he focuses on intelligent runs, often bursting from beyond the far post to meet low crosses, a trademark goal type praised by his former manager Ange Postecoglou. Johnson's clinical finishing and composure in front of goal are significant strengths, making him effective in crucial moments. His tactical intelligence and positional awareness make him hard for defenders to handle, as he consistently makes smart runs and finds open areas.

Career Highlights

Johnson's career has seen significant progression from his youth days to winning major European silverware. He began his professional journey at Nottingham Forest, where he played a crucial role in their promotion to the Premier League in 2022. During a loan spell at Lincoln City in 2020-21, he scored his first career hat-trick in April 2021.

His move to Tottenham Hotspur in 2023 for £47.5 million marked a new chapter, culminating in a UEFA Europa League triumph in 2025, where he scored the winning goal in the final against Manchester United. On the international stage, Johnson debuted for Wales in 2020, scored his first goal in 2022, and represented his country at the 2022 FIFA World Cup in Qatar.

Personality

Brennan Johnson is characterized by his mental toughness and resilience. He demonstrated this by overcoming online abuse in September 2024, leading him to temporarily close his Instagram account, yet he responded by scoring a crucial goal shortly thereafter. Described as an "affable" individual with a "buoyant grin," he is seen as determined to improve and possesses a well-grounded demeanor. His ability to block out external pressures and focus on his performance has been highlighted as a key aspect of his character, especially given the expectations stemming from his father's footballing legacy.

Condition / Medical History

Johnson has experienced several injury setbacks throughout his career, though he has generally returned to play without prolonged absences. In 2023, he sustained a muscle injury in March, a groin injury later that month, and a hamstring injury from late September to late October. A head injury in December 2023 required 16 stitches but was not a concussion.

In 2024, he dealt with a knock injury in April and another in December. The 2025 season saw him sidelined with a calf injury in January, returning in mid-February, and a thigh injury in January-February. Most recently, in 2026, he received a direct red card, resulting in a missed match round.
