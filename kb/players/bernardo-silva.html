Bernardo Silva is a highly accomplished Portuguese footballer, currently concluding his tenure as a false winger for Manchester City. At 31 years old, the left-footed player stands at 173 cm. He is set to depart Manchester City at the end of the 2025-2026 season upon the expiration of his contract, bringing an end to a nine-year stay with the club.

Recent Form

In the 2025-2026 season, Bernardo Silva has maintained a significant presence for Manchester City across all competitions. He has featured in 43 games, contributing 3 goals and 5 assists, while also receiving 14 yellow cards and 1 red card. In the Premier League specifically, he has recorded 2 goals and 4 assists over 2,335 minutes, earning an average FotMob rating of 7.14. He has appeared in all Premier League matches for City this season, playing 2,256 minutes.

Silva's contributions extend to European competitions, where he has played 8 matches in the 2025/2026 UEFA Champions League, scoring 1 goal and maintaining a passing accuracy of 92.38% over 552 minutes. His consistent involvement and high work rate are notable, with reports indicating he covers significant distances per game, often around 13-14 kilometers. He recently captained Manchester City to victory in the 2026 Carabao Cup.

Tactical Profile

Bernardo Silva is renowned for his exceptional versatility, capable of operating effectively as a central midfielder, attacking midfielder, or on either wing. His playing style is characterized by outstanding ball control, dribbling skills, and a high footballing IQ, allowing him to navigate tight spaces and dictate play. He possesses excellent passing range and vision, which he utilizes to create scoring opportunities for teammates.

Under Pep Guardiola, Silva has evolved, adapting to various tactical demands. While he initially showcased significant attacking prowess, his role has become more structured, often seeing him deployed deeper alongside Rodri, where he emphasizes intensity and tactical discipline. His relentless work rate, both in and out of possession, is a cornerstone of his game, enabling him to press effectively and regain the ball. This adaptability and willingness to prioritize collective function have made him a crucial component of Manchester City's success.

Career Highlights

Bernardo Silva's career is marked by significant club and international achievements. Before joining Manchester City in 2017, he won the Ligue 1 title with AS Monaco in the 2016-2017 season. His time at Manchester City has been exceptionally successful, accumulating 19 major trophies, including six Premier League titles, one UEFA Champions League, two FA Cups, and five Carabao Cups. He was also integral to City's domestic treble in 2018-2019 and the Champions League treble in 2022-2023.

Individually, Silva was named Manchester City's Player of the Year in 2018-2019 and was included in the PFA Team of the Year in the same season. He has made over 450 appearances for Manchester City, contributing 76 goals and 77 assists across all competitions, and has recently entered the top 10 of the club's all-time appearance makers. On the international stage, he has won the UEFA Nations League twice with Portugal, in 2019 and 2025, being named Player of the Tournament in 2019. He also played a pivotal role in Portugal's UEFA Euro 2024 qualification and scored his first major tournament goal at Euro 2024. He has been recognized globally, featuring in the top 10 of the Ballon d'Or classification multiple times, including ninth place in both 2019 and for the 2022/23 season.
