Snapshot

A centre-forward with technical intelligence and the ability to drop into midfield to facilitate play, which was his value at AC Milan before the move. De Ketelaere is excellent at finding pockets and rotating play, and he's a composed finisher in the box. However, his physicality doesn't match elite number nines — he can be bullied off the ball, and his lack of explosive power means he struggles as a pure target man in aerial situations.

Recent Form

Charles De Ketelaere's 2025/2026 season with Atalanta was interrupted by a knee injury in February 2026. Prior to undergoing arthroscopic surgery on his right knee on February 12, 2026, he had contributed five goals and five assists across 31 appearances in all competitions for Atalanta. He sustained this injury during the warm-up before a Serie A match against Cremonese on February 9, 2026.

De Ketelaere was expected to return to competitive action by the end of March 2026, following a successful rehabilitation program. His last recorded match before the injury was against Juventus, where he earned a Sofascore rating of 8.4. Earlier in the season, he scored a winner for Atalanta in December 2025 and also registered a brace in a Serie A fixture.

Tactical Profile

Charles De Ketelaere operates effectively as an offensive player, demonstrating a preference for initiating and concluding attacking sequences. He often drops deep to receive the ball, aiming to deliver the final pass or score himself, while also contributing defensively when his team is out of possession. His internal role as a Falso Nove aligns with his playing style, which involves dropping from a central attacking position into midfield to disrupt opposing defenses.

This "False Nine" role leverages his positional fluidity, allowing him to transition between striker, attacking midfielder, and playmaker. By moving across different zones, he creates space and numerical advantages in midfield, forcing defenders to make difficult decisions about whether to follow him or hold their position. His versatility also sees him deployed in various attacking midfield roles, and he has experience as a center forward, wingback, and even left back, though the trequartista position is often considered his most suitable role.

Career Highlights

De Ketelaere joined Club Brugge at the age of seven and made his senior debut in 2019. During his time there, he won the Belgian Jupiler Pro League three consecutive seasons and secured two Belgian Supercups. His individual accolades include being named Belgian Promising Talent of the Year in 2020 and Belgium's Best Young Player of the Year in 2021. Notably, he scored a 93rd-minute game-winning goal against Zenit St. Petersburg in the 2020-21 UEFA Champions League group stage.

In 2022, he transferred to AC Milan in a move that represented one of the club's top transfer fees. Following a loan spell, Atalanta made his move permanent on June 15, 2024. His first full season with Atalanta (2023-2024) was highly productive, as he recorded 14 goals and 11 assists across all competitions. On the international stage, De Ketelaere debuted for the Belgian senior squad in November 2020 and scored his first international goal against Italy in October 2021.

Personality

Charles De Ketelaere is generally described as a shy and quiet individual, whose considerable talent is often understated by his "quiet elegance". He is known for being humble and hardworking, qualities that aligned with the player profile sought by AC Milan's management during his transfer. He reportedly chose to focus on football over tennis, a sport where he found himself struggling with perceived unfair calls.

During a challenging period at AC Milan, former coach Fabio Capello suggested that De Ketelaere's struggles were primarily mental, indicating he appeared "disoriented" and played with a "handbrake pulled" due to fear. Despite this, his underlying motivation has been described as a desire to be part of a winning team rather than seeking personal accolades.

Condition / Medical History

Charles De Ketelaere underwent arthroscopic surgery on his right knee on February 12, 2026, after sustaining an injury during warm-up on February 9, 2026. He was expected to return to play by the end of March 2026.

His medical history indicates several previous injuries, including thigh injuries in October 2025 and April 2025, a muscle injury in November 2024, and a hamstring injury also in November 2024. Earlier records show an abdominal strain and a knee injury in late 2023, as well as muscle injuries in 2022 and 2023. He also had a period of illness due to Coronavirus in early 2021.
