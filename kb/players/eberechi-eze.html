Eberechi Eze is a highly creative attacking midfielder for Arsenal and the England national team, known for his exceptional dribbling and playmaking abilities. Standing at 178 cm, the 27-year-old right-footed player joined Arsenal in August 2025 and has quickly established himself as a key figure in the squad.

Recent Form

Eberechi Eze joined Arsenal in August 2025 for an initial fee of £60 million, potentially rising to £67.5 million with add-ons, and was assigned the number 10 shirt. He made his debut on August 31, 2025, and scored his first goal for the club in an EFL Cup match on September 24, 2025. His first Premier League goal for Arsenal came on October 26, 2025, against his former club, Crystal Palace. A significant highlight of his early Arsenal career was his first career hat-trick on November 23, 2025, in a North London derby against Tottenham Hotspur, making him the first player in the Premier League era to achieve this feat in the fixture. As of early April 2026, Eze has registered 9 goals and 5 assists in 43 appearances across all competitions during the 2025/2026 season. His strong performances led to him being named Arsenal Player of the Month for March.

Despite a recent calf/shin/heel injury that ruled him out in late March 2026 and caused him to miss international fixtures, Eze returned to full training by April 9, 2026, and was declared fit to play against Bournemouth, indicating a swift recovery.

Tactical Profile

Eberechi Eze operates primarily as an attacking midfielder or left winger, bringing a blend of technical prowess and creative vision to Arsenal's attack. His skillful dribbling allows him to navigate tight defensive spaces with ease, and his acute sense of positioning and spatial awareness makes him an outstanding playmaker. Eze is capable of executing precise passes and scoring from long distances, showcasing his versatility and offensive threat. His playing style is characterized by flair and agility, captivating audiences with his movements.

Mikel Arteta frequently deploys Eze off the left wing, a position where he has excelled for both Crystal Palace and England. He thrives in the left half-space, where his ability to slow down the game, lure defenders, execute feints, and then accelerate allows him to unlock stubborn defenses and create "magic moments". Eze's effectiveness in beating players in central areas often attracts multiple defenders, thereby creating space for his teammates. Furthermore, his presence has notably impacted Arsenal's set-piece routines, particularly with short corners, where his positioning and ability to draw pressure help to reshuffle the opposition's box and open up new attacking lanes.

Career Highlights

Eze began his senior career with Queens Park Rangers in 2016, where he had a breakthrough season in 2019-2020, scoring 14 goals and earning the club's Player of the Year award. Following this success, he was signed by Crystal Palace for £17 million. His international career for England saw him score his first senior goal on March 24, 2025, in a 3-0 FIFA World Cup qualifying victory against Latvia. He was also included in England's 26-man squad for UEFA Euro 2024, making his tournament debut in June 2024. Since joining Arsenal, a significant highlight has been his hat-trick against Tottenham Hotspur on November 23, 2025, marking a historic achievement in the North London derby.

Personality

Eberechi Eze is widely regarded for his positive personality both on and off the pitch. He is known for bringing joy and entertainment to football, aspiring to be remembered as a player who always played with a smile. Beyond his athletic endeavors, Eze is actively involved in charitable initiatives, particularly those aimed at providing opportunities and support for young people, demonstrating his commitment to his community. He is described as grounded, level-headed, and one of the most well-liked players in the game, with his humility evident in his controlled and composed playing style. Eze's character is further highlighted by his kindness, thoughtful interviews, ability to uplift teammates, and pride in his Nigerian heritage and London identity. He is seen as a role model, admired by players and praised by coaches for his unique approach to the game.

Condition / Medical History

Eberechi Eze has experienced several injury setbacks throughout his career. In 2021, while at Crystal Palace, he suffered a serious Achilles tendon injury, which he described as the "biggest test" of his career, though he made a remarkable return to full training within four and a half months. During his final two seasons at Crystal Palace, he also contended with three separate hamstring issues.

More recently, in the 2025/2026 season with Arsenal, Eze has faced a series of minor injuries. His absence history includes ankle/foot injuries in January and February 2025, a leg injury in December 2024, a thigh injury in October 2024, and a knee injury in April 2024. In March 2026, he sustained a calf/shin/heel injury that initially ruled him out with a potential return date around April 25, 2026, and caused him to miss international fixtures. However, he returned to full training by April 9, 2026, and was deemed fit for selection, indicating a quicker recovery than anticipated.
