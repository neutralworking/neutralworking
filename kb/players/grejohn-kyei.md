## Snapshot

A physical, direct centre-forward who operates as a functional outlet in transition rather than a refined finisher. Kyei has the athleticism to separate defenders on the shoulder and will compete aerially, but his touch is heavy and his positioning sense—particularly in the box—lacks the anticipation you'd want from a 30-year-old with serious aspirations. He works hard and offers that necessary brute-force element to Standard's attack, yet he'll never be a reliable goalscorer at a higher level because he doesn't read space with intelligence; he relies on chaos and service. Fine for a mid-table Belgian side grinding out results, but the margin for error in his game is already evident.
