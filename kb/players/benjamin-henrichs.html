Benjamin Henrichs, the 29-year-old German international for RB Leipzig, is a versatile right-footed player primarily operating as an inverted wide player.

Recent Form

Benjamin Henrichs' recent form has been significantly impacted by a severe Achilles tendon rupture sustained in December 2024 during a match against Bayern Munich. This injury led to an initial surgery, followed by a setback that necessitated a second surgery, delaying his return to play until early 2026. Consequently, his appearances in the 2025/2026 Bundesliga season have been limited, with him recording one goal and no assists across 217 minutes in 12 matches, primarily as short substitute appearances.

As of April 2026, Henrichs is in the final stages of his rehabilitation, having recently resumed training with the ball. His recent match participations, including brief stints of 4 to 16 minutes in April and March 2026, indicate a cautious reintegration into competitive play as he works towards regaining full match fitness. His average FotMob rating for the current season stands at 6.6, reflecting his limited on-field time.

Tactical Profile

Henrichs is renowned for his exceptional versatility, capable of excelling across multiple defensive and midfield roles. While his internal role is "Invertido," he is adept at playing both as a right-back and left-back, as well as in defensive midfield. This positional flexibility allows him to adapt to various tactical setups, providing options for his team.

His playing style draws comparisons to Joshua Kimmich, characterized by his quickness, aggressive tackling, and ability to deliver precise diagonal passes and crosses. These attributes enable him to contribute significantly to both defensive solidity and offensive transitions, making him a valuable asset in RB Leipzig's system.

Career Highlights

Benjamin Henrichs made his Bundesliga debut for Bayer Leverkusen in August 2015 at the age of 18, marking the beginning of a promising career. His international career commenced with his Germany National Team debut in November 2016, followed by winning the FIFA Confederations Cup with Germany in 2017. He also secured the UEFA European Under-21 Championship with Germany U21 in the same year.

After a spell with AS Monaco, which he joined in summer 2018, Henrichs returned to the Bundesliga, signing permanently with RB Leipzig in April 2021 after an initial loan spell from July 2020. With RB Leipzig, he has achieved significant domestic success, winning the DFB Pokal twice (2021/2022, 2022/2023) and the German Super Cup in 2023/2024. He also featured in RB Leipzig's UEFA Champions League quarter-final campaign in March 2023 and extended his contract with the club until 2028.

Personality

Benjamin Henrichs has demonstrated remarkable determination and resilience, particularly during his recovery from the Achilles tendon rupture. He has openly documented his rehabilitation journey through a YouTube series titled "Faith over Fear," showcasing his commitment to returning to full fitness and setting clear, achievable milestones for his recovery. This initiative not only provides insight into his personal challenges but also serves as a source of motivation.

Beyond his on-field persona, Henrichs has taken a firm stance against online abuse, filing charges against individuals who directed racist or excessively offensive messages towards him. He acknowledges that some insults are part of the game but draws a clear line at racism and "below the belt" attacks, highlighting his integrity and willingness to confront such issues. His proactive approach in contacting other players who have overcome similar Achilles injuries, such as Martin Terrier, Axel Witsel, and Presnel Kimpembe, further illustrates his proactive and supportive nature.

Condition / Medical History

Benjamin Henrichs' medical history is currently dominated by an Achilles tendon rupture suffered in December 2024 during a match against Bayern Munich. This significant injury required initial surgery, and a subsequent setback necessitated a second surgical procedure, extending his recovery timeline. He was initially projected to be sidelined for nine to twelve months.

Prior to this major injury, Henrichs had experienced a range of other issues throughout his career, including calf, back, knee, muscle, thigh, and groin injuries, as well as periods of illness. As of March 2025, the Achilles injury alone had caused him to miss 23 games, adding to the 45 games he had missed due to previous injuries over his career. He is presently undergoing intensive rehabilitation and has recently returned to training with the ball, indicating progress in his recovery.
