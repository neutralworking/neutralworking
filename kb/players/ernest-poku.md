ClubAZ Alkmaar

PositionStriker

Born28 January 2004

It is hard to believe that AZ picked up Poku from the amateur fourth division only two years ago because at the moment he is knocking loudly on the first-team door. Head of coaching at AZ, Paul Brandenburg, told Voetbal International how Poku, who can play as a central striker as well as on the right wing, ended up in Alkmaar. “We received a tip and went to watch a training session at FC Amsterdam. After a few minutes we knew enough: we must have him!” Poku is lightning fast but also has a great touch. Brandenburg says: “He is very special. His first youth tournament with us was unforgettable. At the Marveld Tournament, with many European top clubs, Ernest was top scorer and named best player. From that moment on, of course, other clubs also became interested. But Ernest and the people around him won’t be fooled. They believe in our way of educating.” **[Bart Vlietstra](https://www.theguardian.com/football/ng-interactive/2021/oct/07/”https://twitter.com/BVlietstra?lang=en")**  
[Watch Ernest Poku in action](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c09YVMY4iJ0#t=41s)  
  
**September 2022 update** Made the step up to AZ’s first team and even got some minutes in the Conference League. Signed a new contract until 2026 with the club having great belief in his potential.  
  
**October 2023 update** There were no Eredivisie appearances in 2022-23 but he played a huge part in AZ winning the Uefa Youth League, including two goals in the final against Hajduk Split. "I don’t know if many people expected this [at the beginning of the tournament]," he told ghanasoccernet.com. "They called us underdogs. But we'll fight to the bitter end for each other, and this is the result. This evening, it felt like we had the wind at our backs. Everything worked out."
