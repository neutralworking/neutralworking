Galeno: Player Research Brief

Galeno, the 28-year-old Brazilian winger, currently plies his trade for Al-Ahli SFC in the Saudi Pro League, having joined the club in January 2025 for a reported €50 million transfer fee. He is right-footed and stands at 179 cm.

Recent Form

In the 2025/2026 season with Al-Ahli SFC, Galeno has made 30 appearances, playing 2192 minutes across all competitions as of April 4, 2026. He has contributed to 10 goals, scoring 2 goals and providing 8 assists. Specifically in the Saudi Pro League, he has recorded 1 goal and 7 assists in 22 matches, accumulating 1758 minutes on the pitch with an average FotMob rating of 7.34. His recent matches in April 2026 include an assist in a 3-0 home win against Damac FC and another assist in a 2-3 loss away at Al Qadsiah. He also played 120 minutes in a 1-0 win against Al-Duhail SC and a 1-1 draw against Al Hilal in cup competitions.

Tactical Profile

Galeno operates primarily as a left winger, though he can also play as a right winger or even a left-back. His playing style is characterized by pace, dribbling ability, and directness, making him a dynamic threat. He is known for his ability to execute in 1v1 situations and stretch opposition defensive lines by providing width. While initially perceived as a raw talent with a "verticality bias" in his dribbling, his time at Braga and subsequent return to Porto saw him develop into a more refined and dynamic player, capable of being a significant box threat both in creating chances and scoring. His strengths include blocking the ball, strong finishing, long shots, holding onto the ball, passing, and key passes. He also exhibits high attacking and defensive work rates.

Career Highlights

Galeno has accumulated a significant number of titles throughout his career. With FC Porto, he won the Primeira Liga twice (2017–18, 2021–22), the Taça de Portugal four times (2021–22, 2022–23, 2023–24), the Taça da Liga (2022–23), and the Supertaça Cândido de Oliveira twice (2022, 2024). During his spell with Braga, he secured the Taça de Portugal (2020–21) and the League Cup (2019–20). Most recently, with Al-Ahli SFC, he won the AFC Champions League Elite in his debut season (2024–25), scoring in the final, and the Saudi Super Cup (2025–26). He also received his first call-up to the Brazil national team in March 2024, making his debut against Spain.

Condition / Medical History
