## Career Highlights

Julio Enciso made his professional debut at the age of 15 for Club Libertad on March 17, 2019, becoming the youngest player to do so for the club. He transferred to Brighton & Hove Albion on June 17, 2022, for a fee of £9.5 million. His Premier League debut followed on October 29, 2022. Enciso scored his first goal for Brighton on April 4, 2023, against Bournemouth, and notably netted a 25-yard goal against Manchester City on May 24, 2023, which was recognized as both the BBC Goal of the Season and the Premier League Goal of the Season for the 2022-23 campaign.

In January 2025, he joined Ipswich Town on loan for the remainder of the 2024-25 season, where he made 13 appearances and scored two goals. Enciso then moved to Strasbourg on a permanent deal on September 1, 2025, for a reported €9.5 million, with Brighton retaining a sell-on clause. On the international stage, he has represented Paraguay's senior national team since June 14, 2021, accumulating 31 appearances and scoring four goals as of March 31, 2026. In July 2024, Enciso was recognized by the Centre International d'Etude du Sport (CIES) as the second-best U-21 player globally for creativity and effectiveness in finishing plays.

## Condition / Medical History

Julio Enciso's career has been impacted by several injury concerns, particularly involving his knee. In August 2023, he sustained a left knee meniscus tear that required surgery and sidelined him for approximately six months. Following his return from this significant injury, he has experienced a series of recurring minor issues.

His injury history includes multiple knee injuries, a meniscus injury, muscle injuries, and knocks reported in 2023, 2024, and 2025.
