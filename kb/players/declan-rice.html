Recent Form

Declan Rice has maintained a strong performance level during the 2025/2026 season, contributing significantly to Arsenal's campaign. In the Premier League, he has recorded 4 goals and 5 assists across 31 appearances, earning an average FotMob rating of 7.62. His statistics for rating, starts, and minutes played are notably high among defensive midfielders in the league.

Rice has been a pivotal figure in Arsenal's recent Champions League run, helping the team advance to the semi-finals in April 2026. Despite battling illness that left him "shattered" and bedridden just before a crucial quarter-final match against Sporting CP, he played the full 90 minutes, even captaining the side. He later confirmed he was "fully fit" despite the illness, demonstrating his commitment and resilience. His current form highlights his role as a "complete modern midfielder" who excels in winning duels, driving play through midfield, and posing a goal threat from distance and late runs, effectively turning defensive actions into offensive momentum.

Tactical Profile

Declan Rice operates primarily as a central midfielder (CM), showcasing a versatile and comprehensive playing style. He is renowned for his exceptional athleticism and tackling ability, excelling in defensive duties through astute positional awareness and calculated decision-making. Rather than impulsively committing, he patiently patrols the midfield, timing his challenges effectively and boasting a high tackle win rate. His ability to intercept the ball and read the game is also a key strength.

Beyond his defensive prowess, Rice has evolved into a more complete midfielder, demonstrating strong ball-carrying skills and the capacity to score from long range, occasionally being deployed in a more advanced number eight role. He is highly press-resistant, comfortable receiving the ball under pressure, and adept at distributing it quickly to bypass opposition presses. His tactical intelligence allows him to effectively dictate Arsenal's central pressure, enabling other midfielders to push higher when he advances. Rice's versatility is further evidenced by his ability to perform as a 'Deep-Lying-Playmaker' for possession-oriented teams like England and a 'Midfield Destroyer' in counter-attacking setups, as seen during his time at West Ham.

Career Highlights

Declan Rice has accumulated a number of significant achievements throughout his career for both club and country. With West Ham United, he captained the team to a UEFA Europa Conference League title in 2023, an achievement for which he was also named the competition's Player of the Season. He was recognized multiple times as West Ham United's Player of the Year (2019–20, 2021–22, 2022–23) and was included in the UEFA Europa League Team of the Season (2021–22) and UEFA Europa Conference League Team of the Season (2022–23).

Since his record-breaking £100 million transfer to Arsenal in July 2023, Rice has continued to add to his accolades, winning the English FA Community Shield in the 2023-2024 season. On the international stage, he has been a key player for England, reaching the UEFA European Championship final twice, in 2020 and 2024, finishing as a runner-up on both occasions. He also contributed to England's third-place finish in the UEFA Nations League in 2019. As of April 2026, Rice has made 104 appearances for Arsenal, scoring 15 goals, following 245 appearances and 15 goals for West Ham.

Personality

Declan Rice is widely regarded as a natural leader, both on and off the pitch. He has demonstrated composure and guidance under pressure, notably encouraging his teammates to regroup after a mistake during a North London Derby. His leadership qualities are highly valued within Arsenal, with teammates voting for him to captain the side in the absence of Martin Ødegaard, a decision supported by manager Mikel Arteta, who describes Rice as a "great leader" who takes ownership in challenging situations.

Rice describes himself as more relaxed and confident with age, valuing balance in his life and pursuing creative interests such as photography and fashion outside of football. He exhibits strong emotional intelligence, maintaining calmness under stress and engaging in reasoned discussions with officials rather than aggressive confrontations. While some, like Roy Keane, have suggested he might be "too nice" to hold teammates accountable in an old-school manner, his dedication, ability to withstand physical and mental pressure, and vocal presence on the field are undeniable aspects of his leadership.

Condition / Medical History

Declan Rice is currently fit with no active injuries as of April 2026. Despite this, he recently played through a period of illness in April 2026, even captaining Arsenal in a Champions League quarter-final, underscoring his resilience and commitment to playing.

His career injury record is generally considered minimal, with a low percentage of missed matches. Past minor issues have included knock injuries in 2026 and 2024, physical discomfort in 2026, 2025, and 2024, and various short-term ailments such as knee, muscle, leg, back, hip, and calf injuries between 2023 and 2025, along with occasional illness. His most significant recorded absence was in April 2021 due to a lateral knee ligament injury sustained on international duty, which kept him out for four weeks and six league games, though he returned before the end of that season.
