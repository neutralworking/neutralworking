Brajan Gruda: Player Research Brief

Recent Form

Brajan Gruda joined RB Leipzig on a six-month loan from Brighton & Hove Albion on February 2, 2026, for the remainder of the 2025/2026 season. Since his return to the Bundesliga, Gruda has shown strong offensive output, recording 3 goals and 2 assists in 515 minutes across 8 Bundesliga appearances for RB Leipzig as of early April 2026, earning an average FotMob rating of 7.25. A notable performance includes a 5-0 victory against Hoffenheim on March 20, 2026, where he played 90 minutes, scored two goals, and provided one assist, achieving a 9.5 FotMob rating. He also contributed a goal and an assist in a 2-2 draw against Wolfsburg on February 15, 2026, matching his previous Premier League goal contribution total in just his second Bundesliga match back.

Prior to his loan move, Gruda made 18 appearances for Brighton & Hove Albion in the Premier League during the 2025/2026 season, contributing 1 goal and 1 assist. Across all competitions for Brighton, he featured 45 times, scoring four goals and providing eight assists. Overall for the 2025/2026 season, including his time at both clubs, Gruda has accumulated 6 goals and 5 assists in 28 matches, playing 1412 minutes.

Tactical Profile

Brajan Gruda, a left-footed attacking midfielder and winger, is recognized for his technical prowess, creativity, and ability to influence games. Standing at 178 cm, he is adept at playing centrally as an attacking midfielder (number eight or ten) or wide on either wing. His playing style is characterized by strong dribbling, a clean first touch, and the ability to find gaps and anticipate play, often described as having "laser vision" and thinking quickly on the field. He is considered a counter-attack threat and is noted for his key passes and willingness to take risks.

While highly skilled with the ball, Gruda also demonstrates defensive commitment, tracking back diligently and winning possession. However, some analyses suggest his passing, finishing, and tackling can be areas for improvement, and he may occasionally make naive decisions. The intensity and physicality of duels have also been highlighted as a potential challenge for him. RB Leipzig's managing director for sport, Marcel Schäfer, praised Gruda's pace, technical ability, creativity, and excellent close control, noting his suitability for the club's playing style both with and without the ball.

Career Highlights

Brajan Gruda's career began in youth football with FV Speyer and Karlsruher SC before he joined Mainz 05 at the age of 14. He signed his first professional contract with Mainz in November 2022 and made his Bundesliga debut for the club in January 2023 at just 18 years old. A significant early achievement was captaining Mainz's U19 team to the U19 Bundesliga title in the 2022/2023 season. In October 2023, he scored his first senior goal for Mainz in a 2-2 draw against Borussia Mönchengladbach.

The 2023/2024 season was a breakthrough for Gruda, as he played a crucial role in Mainz 05 avoiding relegation, contributing four goals and three assists in 28 Bundesliga appearances. His performances led to a move to Premier League club Brighton & Hove Albion on August 14, 2024, for a reported fee of €30-31.5 million, making him Mainz's most expensive departure. He made his Premier League debut in October 2024. Internationally, Gruda has represented Germany at various youth levels, including U15, U16, U18, U19, and U21, making his U21 debut in September 2023 with three assists. He was part of the Germany U21 squad that finished as runner-up in the UEFA European Under-21 Championship in 2025. In May 2024, he trained with the senior German national team ahead of Euro 2024, but a calf injury prevented his further involvement.

Condition / Medical History

Brajan Gruda experienced a calf injury in June 2024, which led to his withdrawal from the German senior national team's training camp prior to the UEFA Euro 2024 tournament. More recently, before his loan move to RB Leipzig in February 2026, he had been dealing with adductor issues while at Brighton. However, he returned to team training and was deemed fully fit upon joining RB Leipzig, with reports indicating he would not require significant time to settle in.

Personality

Gruda is described as a player with a strong attitude and commitment, who is determined to succeed. His playing style, often likened to that of a "boy from the streets," highlights his creative and free-spirited approach to football, enjoying dribbling and taking on opponents. He expressed a strong desire to join RB Leipzig when the opportunity arose, indicating his ambition for regular playing time and a belief that the club's style suits him. Gruda has publicly stated his biggest dream is to represent the German national team at the 2026 World Cup, underscoring his dedication to improving his performances.
