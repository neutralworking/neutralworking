The playmaker who has represented France at various youth levels became the first player of Lyon’s 2004 generation to sign as a professional in the summer. After the excitement surrounding Rayan Cherki, El Arouch is tipped to follow him into the first team after appearances in pre-season for Petr Bosz’s side having impressed with the club’s development sides. Born in Orange in the Vaucluse department, El Arouch grew up as a Lyon supporter and turned down interest from Paris Saint-Germain and Manchester City during his youth career. He is also a free-kick specialist and has revealed he used to have a poster of Juninho Pernambucano, the former Brazil midfielder who is now Lyon’s sporting director, on his wall. “It’s not for nothing that No 8 is his favourite number,” said one of El Arouch’s relatives. **[Ed Aarons](https://twitter.com/ed_aarons)**  
  
**September 2022 update**  
  
**September 2022 update** Followed up his goal in the prestigious Gambardella Cup final in May with a panenka penalty in the shootout to help Lyon win the trophy. Signed a contract extension until 2025 and has been training with the first team this season.  
  
**October 2023 update** Made his professional debut for Lyon as a substitute against Angers in February and has been tipped as one of Ligue 1’s young players to watch this season.
