Player Research Brief: Alisson Becker

Recent Form

Alisson Becker has maintained his standing as one of the Premier League's top-performing goalkeepers during the 2025-2026 season, demonstrating his quality when available. However, his recent form has been interrupted by injuries. He sustained a muscular injury before April 3, 2026, which has sidelined him, with a projected return towards the end of the current season. This latest setback followed closely after he had just recovered from a hamstring injury.

Throughout late 2024 and 2025, Alisson experienced several periods of absence due to various issues, including illness, a hip injury, concussion, and other muscle-related problems. Despite these interruptions, when fit, he has continued to be a commanding presence for Liverpool, with reports from December 2025 highlighting him as an "undisputed number one goalkeeper" and a "game-changing figure". In December 2024, he expressed the team's determination to return to winning ways, indicating his active involvement and leadership during that period.

Tactical Profile

Alisson Becker's tactical profile is defined by his exceptional abilities as a sweeper-keeper, crucial to Liverpool's high-pressing system. He consistently operates outside his penalty area, effectively defending the large spaces behind his high defensive line by reading the game and intercepting danger before it escalates. This proactive approach allows Liverpool's defenders to maintain a higher line, confident in Alisson's ability to sweep up through balls and long passes.

Beyond his defensive actions, Alisson is integral to Liverpool's build-up play, showcasing remarkable calmness and comfort with the ball at his feet. His distribution is a key asset, enabling the team to play out from the back under pressure and bypass opposition presses to initiate attacks. He possesses an excellent range of passing, capable of delivering accurate long balls to quickly transition play and exploit opponents, while also maintaining possession with short passes to his center-backs. Furthermore, he excels in one-on-one situations, demonstrating quick reactions, confident decision-making, and the ability to make himself appear large to narrow the angle for attackers.

Career Highlights

Alisson Becker has amassed an impressive collection of team and individual honors throughout his career. With Liverpool, he has been a pivotal figure in winning the Premier League, FA Cup, EFL Cup, UEFA Champions League, and the FIFA Club World Cup. He also played a crucial role in Brazil's Copa América victory in 2019.

Individually, Alisson has received significant recognition, including being named The Best FIFA Goalkeeper and the recipient of the inaugural Yashin Trophy, both in 2019. He has also been selected for the FIFA FIFPRO Men's World 11 twice and was named Serie A Goalkeeper of the Year during his time at Roma. Notably, his transfer to Liverpool in 2018 set a record as the most expensive goalkeeper transfer at the time. His offensive contributions are also remarkable, holding the Premier League record for most goal contributions by a goalkeeper with one goal and three assists, including a memorable header against West Bromwich.

Personality

Alisson Becker is known for his calm demeanor and strong character, qualities frequently highlighted by his managers and teammates. He is described as a leader who instills confidence in his team. His interviews often reveal a determined and focused individual, emphasizing the importance of hard work, teamwork, and a desire to "fight for something big".

He approaches challenges with enthusiasm and self-reflection, consistently striving to be the best version of himself and learn from mistakes. Alisson has also shown an interest in understanding different cultures and religions, engaging in discussions with teammates like Mohamed Salah to gain new perspectives.

Condition / Medical History

Alisson Becker has faced several injury concerns in recent seasons. He is currently sidelined with a muscular injury sustained before April 3, 2026, and is not expected to return until the end of the 2025-2026 season. This injury occurred shortly after he had recovered from a hamstring issue.

His medical history for 2025 includes periods out due to illness, a hip injury, a concussion, and a head injury. In 2024, he also experienced hamstring and muscle injuries. These recurring issues have led to intermittent absences from the Liverpool squad, impacting his availability throughout recent campaigns.
