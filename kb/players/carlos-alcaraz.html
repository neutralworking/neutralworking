Snapshot

Argentine playmaker who operates in the half-spaces with genuine technical intelligence and an eye for the killer pass; he's progressive in possession and can thread through-balls that split lines. The issue is decisiveness under pressure—he'll occasionally overthink rather than commit, and defensively he's a passenger who'll cost you in the middle third. Still, on his day he's a genuine creator for a mid-table side.

Carlos Alcaraz is a 23-year-old Argentine attacking midfielder who currently plays for Everton. Standing at 183 cm tall and preferring his right foot, Alcaraz is known for his dynamic play and ability to impact the game from various midfield positions.

Recent Form

Carlos Alcaraz has had a season impacted by injuries during the 2025-2026 campaign. For Everton in the Premier League, he has registered one assist across 16 appearances, six of which were starts, playing 673 minutes in total. He has also received three yellow cards this season. His last recorded match was on January 31, 2026, where he played 12 minutes against Brighton.

Alcaraz has been recovering from a knock injury that kept him sidelined from late February until mid-April 2026. As of April 17, 2026, he has returned to training and is available for selection, though he is not expected to start due to his recent lack of competitive minutes. Prior to this, he also experienced a knock from late December 2025 to late January 2026.

Tactical Profile

Alcaraz primarily operates as an attacking midfielder but is versatile enough to play as a central midfielder, box-to-box midfielder, or even as a false nine. His playing style is characterized by a high work rate, strong ball-carrying ability, and intelligent off-the-ball movement, enabling him to drift into dangerous spaces between defensive lines. He possesses good physicality, allowing him to drive past defenders and crash the box effectively.

He is noted for having a "killer through ball" and generally good decision-making in attacking phases, often looking for free spaces in the middle and final third to orchestrate attacks. Alcaraz has a strong ball-striking ability and is considered a goalscoring midfielder, having previously taken free-kicks and penalties for former clubs. Defensively, he is a willing "chaser" who tracks back and intelligently commits to winning loose balls, contributing to his team's defensive efforts. However, areas for development include his short passing and comfort in deeper midfield roles, and he can sometimes be prone to rash tackles, leading to a higher foul rate.

Career Highlights

Carlos Alcaraz began his youth career at Deportes Infantiles de Villa Elisa before joining Racing Club's academy in 2017. He made his professional debut for Racing Club on January 26, 2020. A significant early career highlight was scoring the winning goal in extra-time against Boca Juniors in the 2022 Trofeo de Campeones Final, a match that famously ended with numerous red cards.
