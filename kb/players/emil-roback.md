## Snapshot

ClubMilan

PositionForward

Born3 May 2003

Swedish legend Zlatan Ibrahimovic plays for Milan and co-owns Hammarby, so it was no surprise when Milan managed to sign Hammarby’s great attacking talent, Emil Roback, this summer, despite competition from Arsenal and Bayern Munich. The Italian club paid €1.5m for the 17-year-old, who is part of a talented generation with the Gothenburg defender Jesper Tolinsson and Imam Jagne, who moved from Hacken to Everton in September. Roback made his debut for Hammarby this spring in a cup quarter-final and made an immediate impact at Milanello, training with the senior squad and setting up a goal in his first friendly. Unfortunately he hurt his arm in his first game for Milan’s primavera team, but it seems to be just a small bump in a career path that points in one direction: upwards. **[Olof Lundh](https://twitter.com/oloflundh?lang=en)  
  
**October 2021 update** Has been given added responsibility in the primavera side but has also been on the bench for the first team a few times. “It feels as if the club believes in me, and I believe in them,” he says. “It is just a joy to be around Zlatan. I have ever seen anyone train so hard in my whole life.”  
  
**September 2022 update** After playing for Milan’s primavera side, Roback has this season joined Nordsjælland on loan for the 2022-23 campaign. The Danish club’s sporting director, Jan Laursen, said: “Emil is a really exciting player who we have followed for many years – from Hammarby and on in Milan – so several years of work are the basis for the fact that we have now succeeded in getting him to FC Nordsjælland.”  
  
**October 2023 update** It did not work out for Roback in Denmark and he returned to Milan before joining Swedish side IFK Norrköping on loan for the 2023 season. But has played just a few Allsvenskan minutes at the time of writing and looks likely to be heading back to Italy to consider his options once again.**
