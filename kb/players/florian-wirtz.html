Florian Wirtz, the 22-year-old German attacking midfielder, joined Liverpool in June 2025 for a club-record fee, establishing himself as a key figure in Arne Slot's squad. Standing at 176 cm and preferring his right foot, Wirtz is recognized for his elite creativity and tactical intelligence on the field.

Recent Form

Florian Wirtz has had a productive debut season with Liverpool in 2025-2026, accumulating 6 goals and 8 assists across 44 appearances in all competitions, including the Premier League, Champions League, FA Cup, and Community Shield. In the Premier League specifically, he recorded 4 goals and 3 assists in 29 appearances. His Champions League contributions include 1 goal and 3 assists in 11 matches.

Wirtz has also maintained strong form on the international stage for Germany. In March 2026, he delivered a standout performance, scoring two goals and providing two assists in Germany's 4-3 victory over Switzerland. As of April 2026, he has amassed 10 goals and 11 assists in 39 appearances for the national team. While his transition to the Premier League involved a "learning curve," he has consistently impressed in his debut campaign, particularly after a tactical adjustment saw him operate more effectively in a hybrid left-wing role.

Tactical Profile

Wirtz operates as a "central magician" for Liverpool, excelling in pockets behind midfield lines to orchestrate play and generate scoring opportunities through quick combinations and incisive passes. His playing style is characterized by sublime dribbling skills, an exceptional football IQ, and a high work rate, allowing him to cover significant ground during matches. He demonstrates remarkable spatial intelligence, receiving the ball in tight spaces and making unexpected passes that dismantle defenses.

While primarily an attacking midfielder, Wirtz has shown versatility, often featuring as a left-sided attacking midfielder or in a central role. He naturally drifts towards the left flank to cut inside with his preferred right foot, though he is not a traditional winger. His ability to manipulate defenses by drawing players out of position creates crucial space for teammates, making him a difficult player to contain. This blend of technical precision, tactical awareness, and tenacious pressing makes him an ideal fit for Arne Slot's fluid and high-energy system at Liverpool.

Career Highlights

Florian Wirtz made a high-profile move to Liverpool on June 20, 2025, in a transfer valued at approximately £100 million (€117.5 million) or £107 million (€125 million), with potential add-ons, setting a new club record for Liverpool and a Bundesliga record for an outgoing player. He signed a long-term contract with the club, extending until June 2030.

Prior to his move to Anfield, Wirtz was instrumental in Bayer Leverkusen's historic 2023-24 season, where they achieved an unbeaten domestic double, winning both the Bundesliga title and the DFB-Pokal. His exceptional performances earned him the Bundesliga Player of the Season award for 2023-24. He also contributed to Leverkusen's DFL-Supercup victory in the 2024-25 season. Individually, Wirtz has been recognized with multiple accolades, including Germany's Footballer of the Year in 2025 and the UEFA European Under-21 Championship in 2021 with Germany.

Condition / Medical History

Florian Wirtz has experienced a few injury concerns during his career, including a significant anterior cruciate ligament (ACL) tear in March 2022, which sidelined him for ten months. More recently, during his debut season at Liverpool, he sustained a back injury in late February and early March 2026, causing him to miss several matches. He also dealt with a muscle injury in November 2025 and an ankle injury in March-April 2025.

Despite these setbacks, Wirtz demonstrated impressive fitness levels for much of his first season in the Premier League, which his manager Arne Slot highlighted as a significant accomplishment given the league's physical demands. While he initially experienced cramp as an adjustment to the pace of English football, his overall durability has been noted.
