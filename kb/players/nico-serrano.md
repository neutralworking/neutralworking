A left-footed attacker who can play on either wing or behind the striker, offering goals and combination play, Nico Serrano was at Osasuna before joining Villarreal at 12 and Athletic at 15, for €300,000. Under contract until 2024, his buyout clause is €30m. A Spain U18 international, this summer he was promoted directly from Athletic’s Juvenil A (U19) – for whom he scored 14 goals in 19 games before lockdown – to the second team, Bilbao Athletic, in Spain’s third tier. He trained with the first team in pre-season and his prospects of promotion are reasonable at a club that relies heavily on youth development. “Despite his young age, he’s a player who understands the game well, where to position himself and how to move to create danger,” says the Bilbao Athletic coach, Joseba Etxeberria. “He strikes the ball well with his left foot, especially with the top of the foot, and has a goalscorer’s mentality. In Segunda B this year he will have adapt to a faster game and assisting defensively. He has a lot of talent and expectations are high.” **[Sid Lowe](https://twitter.com/sidlowe)**
