Abdukodir Khusanov is a 22-year-old Uzbek center-back who joined Manchester City in January 2025, becoming the first player from Uzbekistan to sign for a Premier League club. Standing at 186 cm and preferring his right foot, Khusanov operates with an internal archetype of "Conductor" and a role of "Sweeper".

Recent Form

Abdukodir Khusanov has rapidly established himself as a key player for Manchester City since his arrival in January 2025. Despite a challenging debut against Chelsea, where he made an early mistake, he quickly recovered and accelerated his development under Pep Guardiola. Guardiola expressed satisfaction with Khusanov's progress in January 2026, calling him a "top signing". By April 2026, he had accumulated nearly 900 Premier League minutes, contributing 21 tackles and 21 interceptions, demonstrating his effectiveness in high-pressure situations.

Khusanov's recent performances include a strong showing in Manchester City's 3-0 victory over Chelsea on April 12, 2026, where he played a steady and reliable match. He also impressed in the FA Cup quarter-final win against Liverpool, showcasing his growing ability as a ball-playing defender. His consistent displays have led to him being considered one of Guardiola's most trusted options, particularly during periods of defensive injury crises.

Tactical Profile

Khusanov is recognized as a physically imposing defender, possessing a large wingspan, strength, speed, and aggression, making him effective in both aerial and ground duels. His pace is a significant asset, allowing him to recover quickly and mitigate defensive errors, with a clocked speed of 36.3 km/h in the 2024-25 Premier League season, one of the fastest for a central defender. Pep Guardiola has even compared his physicality to that of Kyle Walker in his prime, highlighting his ability to cover space effectively in City's high defensive line.

Technically, Khusanov exhibits strong ball control and sharp passing, crucial for initiating attacks from the back. He is adept at both short passes to link defense with midfield and long-range deliveries to launch counter-attacks, a skill honed under Guardiola's ball-possession philosophy. His tactical intelligence is evident in his positional play, anticipation, and decision-making, allowing him to read the game and eliminate danger proactively. While primarily a center-back, he has also been deployed at right-back, demonstrating versatility, though Guardiola views him more as a valuable rotational option in that position rather than a full-time solution.

Career Highlights

Abdukodir Khusanov began his youth career at Bunyodkor in Uzbekistan before moving to Belarusian club Energetik-BGU in 2022, where he quickly became a key player and was included in the league's symbolic team. In 2023, he transferred to French Ligue 1 side Lens, becoming the first Uzbek to play in the league and later the youngest Uzbek to play in the UEFA Champions League at 19 years and 9 months old. His impressive performances at Lens, including being named in the Ligue 1 Team of the Season for the first half of 2024/2025, attracted the attention of top European clubs.

In January 2025, Khusanov made a historic €40 million move to Manchester City, signing a four-and-a-half-year deal and becoming the first Uzbek player in the English Premier League. He scored his first goal for City in an FA Cup match against Leyton Orient in February 2025. On the international stage, Khusanov has represented Uzbekistan from the U17 level upwards, winning the AFC U-20 Asian Cup in 2023 and a silver medal at the AFC U-23 Asian Cup in 2024. He has earned 25 senior international caps and played a pivotal role in Uzbekistan's qualification for the 2026 FIFA World Cup. In January 2026, he was named Asia's Best Young Footballer of 2025.

Personality

Abdukodir Khusanov is described as a quiet, humble, and disciplined individual, both on and off the pitch. He is known for his strong work ethic and determination, which helped him overcome a challenging debut at Manchester City. His father, a former footballer, has been a significant influence, providing guidance and motivation. Khusanov's team-oriented mindset was evident when Uzbekistan qualified for the World Cup, as he attributed the success to making "38 million people happy" rather than focusing on personal achievement. Pep Guardiola has noted Khusanov's relaxed nature and singular focus on football, even traveling to away matches with minimal belongings, which has made him a beloved figure within the Manchester City dressing room.

Condition / Medical History

Khusanov has experienced a few minor injury setbacks during his time at Manchester City. In September 2025, he sustained a minor calf muscle injury during a Premier League match against Brighton, which sidelined him for approximately two weeks and caused him to miss the CAFA Nations Cup. Later in 2025, he also dealt with an ankle injury. In February 2026, he took a knock to the head during a match against Liverpool, but Guardiola confirmed he was fine and able to play shortly after. Despite these minor issues, he has generally recovered well and quickly returned to action, demonstrating resilience.
