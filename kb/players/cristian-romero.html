Tottenham fans quickly fell in love with aggressive right-sided Argentina international Cristian Romero.

A fiery tackler who links up well in possession, Romero won Serie A's defender of the year award for his displays with Atalanta before joining Spurs.

Now a World Cup winner, Romero is key for Tottenham.

Recent Form

Cristian Romero has been a prominent figure for Tottenham Hotspur during the 2025/2026 Premier League season, having been named club captain prior to its commencement and extending his contract until 2029. In the current Premier League campaign, he has registered 4 goals and 1 assist across 23 appearances, accumulating 1,872 minutes on the pitch. His performance metrics place him highly among Premier League center-backs for both goals and overall rating.

Despite his contributions, Romero's recent form has also seen disciplinary issues. He received two red cards during the 2025/2026 season, one in a 2-1 loss to Liverpool on December 20, 2025, and another on February 7, 2026, against Manchester United. He has also accumulated 11 yellow cards in the Premier League this season. As of April 2026, Romero is currently sidelined with a knee injury, with concerns that it could be season-ending.

Tactical Profile

Cristian Romero operates as a "proactive" defender, frequently stepping out of the defensive line to make crucial interceptions in midfield, embodying a "front foot" approach. He is recognized for his aggressive and physical style of play, often engaging in tackles and utilizing his strength to dominate opponents and win aerial duels. His exceptional anticipation and ability to read the game allow him to effectively disrupt opposition attacks early.

Romero demonstrates comfort and proficiency in one-on-one situations, adeptly controlling both the opposing striker and the space behind him. His positioning is consistently accurate, and he can adapt by shifting deeper to defend wide areas of the box. Beyond his defensive prowess, Romero is composed on the ball, capable of dribbling through pressure and executing passes even when under duress, contributing to build-up play from the back. His strong confidence and leadership are also key attributes, enabling him to take calculated risks and motivate teammates.

Career Highlights

Cristian Romero has amassed significant honors for both club and country. With Argentina, he was a key member of the squads that won the Copa América in both 2021 and 2024, the FIFA World Cup in 2022, and the CONMEBOL-UEFA Cup of Champions (Finalissima) in 2022.

At the club level, Romero achieved a notable success with Tottenham Hotspur, winning the UEFA Europa League in the 2024-25 season. He was awarded the Player of the Match in the final, marking Tottenham's first major trophy in 17 years and their first European title in 41 years. Individually, he was named Serie A's Best Defender for the 2020–21 season and was included in the Copa América Team of the Tournament in 2021 and 2024, as well as the UEFA Europa League Player of the Season and Team of the Season for 2024–25.

Personality

Cristian Romero is widely regarded as a fierce competitor and a loyal teammate on the field. Off the pitch, he presents as a dedicated family man, with his primary ambition being to support and make his family proud. His leadership qualities are evident, as he is considered a leader for both his club and national team.

Romero's playing style is often described as intuitive and emotional, mirroring an artistic approach to the game. He possesses strong confidence and mental fortitude, asserting that he has "always had spirit, desire and aggression" and would consider it a problem if he were to lose those traits. Notably, he has also been known for making outspoken comments in the media, particularly regarding the club's leadership and their transfer strategy amidst injury concerns.

Condition / Medical History

Cristian Romero is currently dealing with a knee injury, sustained around April 13, 2026, which has raised concerns about a potentially season-ending absence. This recent setback follows a series of injuries throughout his career.

In the 2025-26 season alone, he has experienced a head injury in March 2026 and an illness in February 2026. The previous year, 2025, saw him contend with a groin injury in October and a toe injury in May. His medical history also includes a thigh injury from December 2024 to March 2025, and an ankle injury in November 2024. Earlier seasons documented various muscle-related issues, including hamstring and thigh injuries in 2023, 2022, and 2021, as well as concussions and general knock injuries.
