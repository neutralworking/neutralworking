## Career Highlights
Justin Kluivert emerged from the renowned Ajax youth academy, making his senior debut for the club in 2017. A significant early career moment came when he scored his first Eredivisie goal exactly ten years and one day after his father, Patrick Kluivert, scored his last. In 2018, he moved to Italian club Roma for €18.75 million, where he became the youngest player to score for the club in the UEFA Champions League.

Kluivert's career has seen him experience various top European leagues through a series of loan spells, including RB Leipzig (Bundesliga, 2020-2021), Nice (Ligue 1, 2021-2022), and Valencia (La Liga, 2022-2023). This diverse experience culminated in a unique achievement: he became the first player to score in all of Europe's "Big Five" leagues (Premier League, La Liga, Serie A, Bundesliga, Ligue 1) as well as the Eredivisie. He joined Bournemouth permanently in 2023, scoring his first goal for the club in November 2023 and his first Premier League goal later that month.
