Recent Form

Joaquín Panichelli has been in exceptional form for Strasbourg during the 2025/2026 season, establishing himself as the team's leading scorer. Prior to a recent injury, he had netted 16 goals and provided 1 assist in 27 Ligue 1 appearances, contributing to 19% of his team's goals. Across all competitions, he recorded 20 goals and 4 assists in 37 matches, playing 2932 minutes. His performances earned him an average FotMob rating of 7.25.

Panichelli's impact was particularly notable early in the season, where he scored 8 goals in his first 9 Ligue 1 matches by October 2025, briefly topping the scoring charts. He also delivered a standout two-goal performance in a 3-3 draw against Paris Saint-Germain. Although Strasbourg experienced a difficult period in December 2025, which saw a dip in Panichelli's shot volume, his overall season-long indicators remained strong. His consistency and discipline have been valuable assets to Strasbourg's young squad.

Tactical Profile

Panichelli operates primarily as a classic number nine, but his game incorporates modern elements, making him a versatile forward. Standing at 190 cm, he is strong, technically gifted, and composed in front of goal. His off-ball movement is a significant asset, as he constantly seeks to create separation from defenders, making intelligent runs into space, particularly behind high defensive lines. He is adept at timing his runs, often starting in offside positions before stepping back onside to receive the final pass, which generates high-value scoring opportunities.

Beyond goal-scoring, Panichelli contributes significantly to Strasbourg's build-up and defensive efforts. He possesses a clean first touch, a powerful right-footed strike, and strong aerial finishing ability, with 20% of his goals coming from headers. While his hold-up play is still developing, he is physically capable of pinning centre-backs to create space for teammates. He also demonstrates strong defensive contributions, ranking highly among forwards for interceptions, blocks, tackles, and clearances, indicating a high pressing intensity. His tactical adaptability allows him to function effectively as a second striker, dropping deep to link play or drifting wide to manipulate defenders.

Career Highlights

Born in Córdoba, Argentina, Panichelli began his youth career at local clubs before joining River Plate in 2020, following an unsuccessful trial at Boca Juniors. His progression was temporarily halted in 2023 when he suffered a severe ACL injury after moving to Deportivo Alavés in Spain. However, he made a remarkable comeback during a loan spell at Mirandés in the 2024-25 season, where he scored 21 goals in 40 appearances, marking a significant breakout campaign.

This impressive performance led to a €16.5 million transfer to Strasbourg in July 2025, where he signed a contract until 2030. Panichelli quickly made an impact, scoring on his debut for Strasbourg on August 17, 2025. His exceptional form in Ligue 1 earned him a senior call-up to the Argentina national team in November 2025, where he made his debut against Angola, coming on as a substitute for Lionel Messi.

Personality

Joaquín Panichelli is characterized by his patience, resilience, and determination, qualities that have been evident throughout his career, particularly in overcoming significant injuries. He is described as possessing an "unbelievable mentality" and a "winning mentality," consistently maximizing his efforts in training. His calm presence on the field is noted as rare for a player of his age. Panichelli has openly expressed his ambition to play in the Premier League and the Champions League, as well as to secure a spot in the Argentina national team for the World Cup. Following his recent ACL injury, he shared a positive outlook, viewing it as a personal challenge to gain even more momentum.

Condition / Medical History

Joaquín Panichelli has a notable medical history, including two significant knee injuries. In 2023, while with Deportivo Alavés, he sustained a severe ACL injury that sidelined him for 185 days. More recently, in late March 2026, he suffered a second ACL injury, this time to his right knee, during an international training session with the Argentina national team. He underwent surgery for this injury in early April 2026 in Lyon. This latest injury has ruled him out for the remainder of the 2025-2026 season and has unfortunately ended his hopes of participating in the 2026 World Cup. Recovery from an ACL injury typically spans between nine and twelve months, suggesting he will likely miss the beginning of the 2026-2027 season. His injury history also includes minor head injuries in September 2025 and December 2024, and a calf injury in April 2024.
