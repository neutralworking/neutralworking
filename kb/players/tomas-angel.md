## Snapshot

ClubAtlético Nacional

PositionForward

Born20 February 2003

Ever since he burst on the scene at the esteemed Pony Fútbol junior tournament aged 12, there’s been bubbling interest in the budding career of Tomás Ángel. A reputation undoubtedly enhanced by the fact his dad is none other than the ex-Aston Villa striker Juan Pablo, Tomás has nonetheless generated significant intrigue on his own merit by regularly topping the goal-scoring charts for his U15s side, CD Estudiantil, and through the many viral videos that have swept Twitter in recent years. At the start of 2020 he followed in the footsteps of his father by joining the Under-17s at mighty Atlético Nacional, quickly earning an invite from first-team manager Juan Carlos Osorio to train with the seniors. Mature beyond his tender years and boasting exquisite technique and a sweet left foot, Tommy is more than a chip off the old block. “Tomás Ángel has generated logical expectations and not only because of his surname,” says Medellín-based journalist Santiago Aristizábal, who has followed his career for several years. “He has demonstrated his talent playing in official tournaments and this has confirmed he’s a footballer with a bright future.”**[Carl Worswick](https://twitter.com/cworswick)**  
[Watch Tomás Ángel in action](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_QH2qmjFwE4)  
  
**October 2021 update** Made his professional debut at the start of this year for Colombian giants Atlético Nacional and scored his first goal in his second start in March. Twenty seven-years earlier, his old man, Juan Pablo, had bagged his first professional goal playing for the same club. “This is a dream come true,” Angel Jr said. “I’m writing my own story, my own career, but hopefully I’ll be even better than him.” Received a call up to the Colombia under-20 squad in May.  
  
**September 2022 update** Struggled a bit at the U23s Toulon tournament this summer where Colombia fielded a young U19s side, but he is still expected to be the team’s No 9 for the South American U20s championship held on home soil at the start of next year. A regular member of the Atlético Nacional first-team squad.  
  
**October 2023 update** Controversially axed from the Colombia Under-20 squad for the South American qualifiers held on home soil at the start of the year, but then fired three goals after being brought back into the fold at the Argentina finals as Colombia’s No 9 in June. Still to find consistency for Nacional, but was the Medellín side’s hero in February’s epic 4-3 Super League win over Pereira, scoring twice and assisting an 87th-minute winner.
