Snapshot

Dani Olmo: Player Research Brief

Dani Olmo, a 27-year-old Spanish attacking midfielder for Barcelona, has established himself as a creative and tactically intelligent player with a notable career trajectory. Standing at 179 cm and preferring his right foot, Olmo embodies the "Artisan" archetype and "Enganche" role, characterized by his ability to orchestrate play and create opportunities from advanced positions.

Recent Form

In the current 2025-2026 season, Dani Olmo has made 25 appearances for Barcelona in La Liga, contributing 7 goals and 7 assists. Across all competitions for Barcelona in 2025-2026, he has played 42 matches, scoring 8 goals and providing 9 assists. His performance includes an assist in a 2-1 away win against Atletico Madrid on April 14, 2026.

During the 2024-2025 season, his first back at Barcelona, Olmo played an important role in the team's domestic treble success, scoring 12 goals in 39 appearances across all competitions. In La Liga specifically, he recorded 10 goals and 3 assists in 25 matches during the 2024-2025 campaign.

Tactical Profile

Olmo is known for his versatility, capable of playing as an attacking central midfielder, winger on either side, or even as a false nine. His playing style is marked by intelligent positioning, incisive inside play, and a keen understanding of his teammates' movements. He possesses excellent passing quality and quick decision-making, allowing him to create gaps and numerical superiority by drawing defenders out of position.

His dribbling ability is a key asset, with a high success rate in one-on-one situations, often shifting the ball to his preferred right foot before advancing. Olmo is also adept at combining with teammates and releasing them into space, demonstrating an improved decision-making process where he is more inclined to combine rather than over-dribble into crowded areas. Barcelona manager Hansi Flick has explored utilizing Olmo as a false nine, a role he has shown proficiency in, highlighting his quick thinking, creativity, and finishing ability.

Career Highlights

Dani Olmo's career has been marked by significant achievements at both club and international levels. After developing in Barcelona's La Masia academy, he moved to Dinamo Zagreb in 2014, where he made 124 appearances, scored 34 goals, and won five league titles and three Croatian Cups. He then transferred to RB Leipzig in 2020, winning two DFB-Pokals in 2022 and 2023, as well as the 2023 DFL-Supercup, where he notably scored a hat-trick against Bayern Munich.

Internationally, Olmo has been a key player for Spain. He won the UEFA European Under-21 Championship in 2019 and earned a silver medal at the 2020 Olympic tournament. He was part of the Spain squad that reached the semi-finals of UEFA Euro 2020 and won the 2023 UEFA Nations League. A significant highlight came in 2024 when he was a member of the Spain team that won Euro 2024, finishing as the tournament's joint-top scorer with three goals. He returned to his boyhood club Barcelona in 2024, signing a six-year contract.

Condition / Medical History

Dani Olmo has faced a challenging history with injuries throughout his career, often missing a significant percentage of matches. Between 2020 and 2025, he suffered 11 injuries, including muscle tears, calf problems, and a shoulder operation, resulting in over 370 days sidelined. His injury record includes missing 25 matches in the 2023-2024 season and 17 matches in the 2024-2025 season at Barcelona.

In the 2025-2026 season, he sustained a shoulder injury in December 2025, which kept him out for approximately one month. Other injuries in 2025 included a calf injury, overload, muscle injury, and foot injury. Barcelona has implemented a special physical plan, similar to one used for Pedri, to strengthen Olmo's body and help him remain injury-free. Despite the recurring issues, Olmo has stated in a February 2026 interview that he does not consider himself fragile and is working to overcome these setbacks, viewing them as part of the sport.
