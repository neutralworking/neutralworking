T. Devlin, a Marshal archetype and Holding Midfielder, thrives as a Tuttocampista, characterized by exceptional physical attributes including stamina, balance, pace, and aggression, making him highly effective in shielding the ball. His strong technical pillar allows for effective distribution, supporting a high-press system, yet his limited mental attributes are highlighted by a lack of guile and disciplinary issues, alongside weaknesses in defensive blocking. This player fits the mould of a classic ball-winning midfielder, a combative engine room presence who sets the tempo through relentless work rate and physical intimidation, reminiscent of the traditional enforcer. Devlin would be a valuable acquisition for clubs prioritizing a physically dominant, high-energy midfield presence capable of breaking up play and protecting the defensive line, but his significant weaknesses suggest he might struggle in systems requiring intricate build-up play or consistent tactical discipline.
