Scouting Notes

A proper number nine who leads the line with intelligence and positioning rather than explosive athleticism; he's tidy in the box, good at holding play up, and understands how to create space for runners arriving late. His issue is a lack of genuine pace and penetration—he's not winning races in behind, and against aggressive backlines he can become peripheral. Good player, good player, but lacks the x-factor that separates the very good from the elite.

Born28 January 2000

All-time top scorer in the European under-17 championship with 16, eight of them at the finals, Abel Ruiz was the captain of the Spain side who won the competition this summer – scoring against France, Italy (2) and Turkey. He had been in the team that finished runners-up the previous season, getting goals against Germany, Italy, Holland and Serbia. An out-and-out goalscorer, finishing from inside the area, he stands out for his movement, work and positioning. A fan of Zlatan Ibrahimovic, he was signed by Barcelona from Valencia at the age of 12 – much to the chagrin of the club from Mestalla. Scored on his infantil debut and continued to progress. Attracted interest from the Premier League but signed a three-year deal in 2016 and has played, and scored, for Barcelona B in Spain’s second division this season. Sid Lowe

October 2018 update In 2017 Ruiz top-scored for Spain in the Under-17 European Championship and Under-17 World Cup. Earlier this year, shortly after he made his first-team debut as a substitute in the Supercopa de Catalunya – scoring in the penalty shootout – Ruiz signed a new three-year contract, complete with €100m release clause. He is a regular in the Barcelona B team, relegated last season to Spain’s Segunda Division B.

October 2019 update Made his senior debut against Getafe in May, after the league title had been secured, coming on with 20 minutes remaining. Went on the US tour with the first team and won the Under-19 Euros with Spain. He wanted to go out on loan but Barcelona turned down offers, including a two-year stint at Sporting.
