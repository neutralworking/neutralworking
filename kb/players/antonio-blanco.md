Joined from Séneca, the Córdoba-based club that has a convenio with Real Madrid. A small but hardworking, technical central midfielder: he plays and makes others play. Blanco stands out above all for his elegance, intelligence and positioning, the simple continuity he gives to the game, starting moves from deep. Vital in the Spain squad that won the European under-17 championship in the summer, scoring a crucial goal against Croatia in the group phase, and was called up again this month for the World Cup. **[Sid Lowe](https://twitter.com/sidlowe)**
 
**October 2018 update** Blanco often found his path to Real Madrid’s under-19 team blocked last season by Martín Calderón, but since the latter’s promotion to Castilla Blanco has become their captain and midfield heartbeat. He was also a key member of Spain’s Under-17 side, beaten by England in the World Cup final in India last October. Scored a fantastic goal from inside his own half for the Under-19s this season.
 
**October 2019 update** Scored from the halfway line [in the youth clásico](https://www.realmadrid.com/en/fans/videos/2019/06/antonio-blancos-wonder-goal-from-the-halfway-line-in-the-youth-clasico) in the summer and is now formally part of the Real Madrid Castilla team.
 
**October 2020 update** AS reported in September that Zinedine Zidane had been “surprised by the progress” made by Blanco, who had been training with the first team during pre-season after an outstanding campaign under Raul at Real Madrid Castilla in 2019-20. This is likely to be a key season for the midfielder, who is seen as a potential long-term successor to Casemiro in Real’s midfield, given that his current contract expires in 2021.
