He is described as a "Poacher Supreme" who excels at capitalizing on rebounds, loose balls, and chaotic situations within the penalty area. While he is adept at holding the ball to facilitate team play, his weaknesses are noted in general ball retention and passing. His current success at Feyenoord is partly attributed to the team's vertical, high-pressing system, which aligns well with his instinctive play in transitional moments and broken plays.

## Career Highlights

Ueda's professional journey began after developing his skills at Hosei University, leading to his signing with Kashima Antlers in 2019. He quickly established himself as a prolific scorer in the J-League, consistently reaching double-digit goal tallies. Notably, in 2021, he was Kashima's top goalscorer and the fourth highest in the league with 14 goals in 29 appearances.

His move to European football came on July 1, 2022, when he joined Cercle Brugge on a four-season contract. He made a significant impact in Belgium, scoring 22 goals and providing 2 assists in his debut campaign in the Belgian Pro League. This impressive performance led to his transfer to Feyenoord in the summer of 2023 for approximately €9 million. With Feyenoord, Ueda has secured silverware, winning the KNVB Cup in 2024 and the Johan Cruyff Shield in the same year, where he notably scored in the penalty shootout. On the international stage, Ueda debuted for the Japan senior national team on June 17, 2019. He scored his first international goal on June 15, 2023, against El Salvador and achieved his first international hat-trick on November 16, 2023, during a 2026 FIFA World Cup qualification match against Myanmar. He has represented Japan in major tournaments including the 2022 FIFA World Cup, the 2023 AFC Asian Cup (where he scored three goals), and the Copa América, accumulating 16 goals in 38 caps.
