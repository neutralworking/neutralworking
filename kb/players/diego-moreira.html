Recent Form

Diego Moreira has demonstrated solid performance for Strasbourg during the 2025/2026 Ligue 1 season, recording 3 goals and 4 assists in 1,838 minutes across 23 appearances (21 starts and 2 as a substitute). His average FotMob rating for the season stands at 7.13, indicating a consistently strong contribution, particularly in terms of assists, minutes played, and overall rating compared to other left wing-backs in the league. He has also contributed to nearly three shot-creating actions per 90 minutes, highlighting his offensive impact.

Moreira has shown a knack for performing in high-stakes matches, with two of his three Ligue 1 goals for Strasbourg coming against prominent clubs like PSG and Marseille. Beyond league play, he has also made an impact in international youth competitions, scoring 1 goal and providing 2 assists in 2 matches during the UEFA U21 Championship Qualification 2027 season, with impressive per-90-minute statistics.

However, Moreira's recent form has been interrupted by injury. In March 2026, he sustained an adductor/groin injury, which led to him missing three matches for Strasbourg. As of early April 2026, he was still in the recovery phase, participating in running drills but not yet expected to return to full match fitness, making him doubtful for upcoming fixtures.

Tactical Profile

Diego Moreira operates primarily as a left-footed winger, but his tactical versatility allows him to be deployed effectively as a left wing-back or left midfielder, and he has even played as an attacking midfielder on the right. He is characterized as an "old school winger" who maintains width along the touchline, thereby creating space for his full-back to overlap or underlap. His ability to combine with fullbacks, delivering well-weighted passes down the line and drifting inside to create crossing or pull-back opportunities, is a notable aspect of his play.

Moreira's standout attribute is his dribbling, marked by explosive acceleration and a quick first step that enables him to beat defenders with a combination of skill and pace. He is dangerous in transition, capable of leading counterattacks over long distances. While his open-play crossing can sometimes lack consistency, his inswinging corners are delivered with pace and whip, posing a threat to opposition defenses. He also possesses a strong shot with his left foot.

Beyond his offensive contributions, Moreira demonstrates a disciplined and hard-working approach to defense, actively tracking back and covering his fullback, including clearing crosses from the back post. His strengths include long shots, key passes, dribbling, concentration, ball interception, and defensive contribution. Areas identified for improvement include holding onto the ball, finishing, and tackling, which are considered weaknesses in his game.

Career Highlights

Diego Moreira has accumulated several significant achievements early in his career. With Benfica U19, he was a key part of the teams that won the Júniores U19, UEFA Youth League, and FIFA U20 Intercontinental Cup, all in the 2021/2022 season. He also contributed to Benfica's Liga Portugal title win in the 2022/2023 season.

His career trajectory saw him join Chelsea in July 2023, where he won the Florida Cup and Premier League Summer Series in the same year. After a loan spell at Lyon in the 2023/2024 season, he moved to Strasbourg in the summer of 2024, where he has since played over 50 league games. On the international stage, after representing Portugal at various youth levels, including U16, U18, U19, U20, and U21, Moreira officially switched his international allegiance to Belgium in May 2025 and was subsequently called up to the senior Belgium squad for 2026 FIFA World Cup qualifying matches.

Personality

Diego Moreira is noted for his positive personality within the team environment. He is described as having a "great sense of humour" and has become a fan favorite at Strasbourg, with the club's social media often featuring clips of him playing pranks on teammates. His background, growing up in a football-oriented family, instilled in him discipline and professional responsibility from an early age, which has contributed to his mental resilience. His journey through various clubs and academies highlights his adaptability and competitive ambition.
