The son of a former ice hockey player and nephew of a former footballer, the winger made one of the most surprising transfers of the summer. The 17-year-old had played only three matches for SC Braga when Lazio agreed to pay a potential €17m for him (€7.5m for a two-year loan, €4.5m to buy him and up to €5m in bonuses) in a deal that also included the 18-year-old midfielder Bruno Jordão. Neto, who scored in his first match for Braga after nine minutes on the pitch, has similarities to Manchester City’s Bernardo Silva, one of his idols. Left-footed but most comfortable playing from the right to the middle, he loves to take on opponents and make use of his excellent technique. **[Nuno Travassos](https://twitter.com/nunotravassos?lang=en)**
 
**October 2018 update** Was included in a few match-day squads at Lazio - and featured on the bench - during the 2017-18 season and played in several pre-season games in the summer of 2018. Continuing to develop.
 
**October 2019 update** Played only five games for Lazio before signing for Wolves together with Bruno Jordão, the pair having joined the Italian side from Braga together in 2017.
 
**October 2020 update** Impressed as Wolves took the Premier League and Europa League by storm. Has already made 48 appearances for the club and Nuno Espírito Santo believes there is more to come from the 20-year-old. Neto told Sky Sports this summer: “One of the things that I have improved a lot here is how to use my body strength,” he says. “I have improved a lot tactically too. I think those two factors have been very important.”
 
**October 2021 update** Made his debut for the senior national team in November 2020, becoming the first player born in the 2000s to do so and was voted Wolves fans’ player of the season. However, he fractured his kneecap that ruled him out of Euro 2020.
