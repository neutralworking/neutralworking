David Brooks: Player Research Brief

David Brooks is a 28-year-old Welsh attacking midfielder currently playing for AFC Bournemouth. Known for his left-footed technical ability and versatility across attacking roles, Brooks has overcome significant challenges in his career to remain a key figure for both club and country.

Recent Form

In the 2025-2026 Premier League season, David Brooks has made 26 appearances for Bournemouth, contributing one goal and two assists over 1,070 minutes of play. He has also accumulated seven yellow cards during this period. His performance metrics for the current season show an average FotMob rating of 6.69. Brooks recently signed a new contract with Bournemouth in September 2025, extending his stay until 2029, a move he described as feeling "wanted" by the club. He has featured in recent matches, including a substitute appearance in a 2-1 victory away at Arsenal in April 2026, and played 74 minutes in a World Cup qualifier for Wales against Bosnia & Herzegovina in March 2026.

Tactical Profile

Brooks primarily operates as a right winger or attacking midfielder, often drifting centrally, which aligns with his internal archetype of a 'Drifter' and role as a 'Trequartista'. He is a left-footed technician recognized for his individual skills, quality passes, and effective kicks. His playing style involves cutting inside, crossing, and tackling, demonstrating a well-rounded contribution to both attack and defense. When positioned on the right, he tends to move into central areas, facilitating pass plays with teammates rather than relying solely on individual dribbling. This ability to exploit interior channels and create overloads was a notable aspect of his play in earlier seasons, allowing for dangerous passing options into the final third.

Career Highlights

Brooks joined Bournemouth in July 2018 for £11.5 million from Sheffield United, where he had previously helped them win League One in the 2016-2017 season. His debut Premier League season with Bournemouth in 2018-2019 was particularly impressive, earning him the club's Player of the Month awards in October and December, and a PFA Young Player of the Year nomination. He scored his first Premier League goal against Crystal Palace and finished that season with seven goals across 33 appearances. In January 2024, Brooks had a loan spell at Southampton, contributing to their promotion from the Championship. He has also represented Wales at senior international level since 2017, accumulating 43 caps and scoring 6 goals.

Personality

David Brooks is described as a reserved character, yet he possesses a strong passion for football and a desire to contribute meaningfully to his team. His perspective on life and the game has notably shifted following his battle with cancer, leading him to appreciate the simple things more. He has openly discussed his journey, emphasizing that his health and mentality became paramount, and he aims for his comeback story to offer hope to others facing similar challenges. Brooks expressed feeling valued by Bournemouth after signing his new contract, indicating a desire for stability and recognition within the club.

Condition / Medical History

David Brooks has a well-documented medical history, most significantly his diagnosis with Stage 2 Hodgkin lymphoma in October 2021. He underwent six months of chemotherapy and was given the all-clear in May 2022, making a return to competitive football in March 2023. His recovery journey involved regaining fitness after losing significant muscle mass and being deconditioned by treatment.

Since his return, Brooks has faced several injury setbacks. He suffered a hamstring problem in August 2022, and his pre-season in 2024 was impacted by a dislocated shoulder sustained during the 2023-24 Championship play-off final while on loan at Southampton. More recently, in the 2025-2026 season, he has experienced a minor hamstring injury in October 2025 while on international duty, and an ankle injury in January 2026, which kept him out for a period. Coach Andoni Iraola has noted that Brooks has been working to regain full fitness, acknowledging that he hasn't been "100 percent yet" but can still contribute from the bench.
