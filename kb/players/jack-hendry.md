## Career Highlights

Jack Hendry has achieved significant success throughout his career, securing eight titles across various clubs. With Club Brugge, he won the First Division A in the 2021/2022 season and the Super Cup in 2022/2023. His time at Celtic saw him lift multiple trophies, including two Scottish Premiership titles (2017/2018, 2018/2019), two Scottish Cups (2017/2018, 2018/2019), and two League Cups (2018/2019, 2019/2020). Notably, during a loan spell at KV Oostende in the 2020/2021 season, he was recognized as the highest-rated player in the Belgian league by Sport/Voetbalmagazine. He also earned a Player of the Match award in a UEFA Champions League draw against Paris Saint-Germain while playing for Club Brugge.

On the international stage, Hendry made his debut for Scotland in March 2018 and has since earned 37 caps, scoring three goals. His international goal tally includes his first against the Netherlands in June 2021 and a crucial equalizer against the Republic of Ireland in the UEFA Nations League in September 2022. He was part of Scotland's squads for both the delayed UEFA Euro 2020 finals and the UEFA Euro 2024 finals, starting in the opening match of the latter tournament.

## Condition / Medical History

Jack Hendry has experienced several injuries throughout his career. In 2017, he sustained a head injury, followed by an ankle injury in 2018. During a loan spell at Melbourne City in early 2020, he suffered a significant knee ligament injury that required surgery after only his second appearance for the club. He also dealt with hamstring injuries in 2019 and 2021, and a calf injury in 2022.
