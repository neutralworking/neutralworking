## Snapshot

The positional sense and reading of the game remain almost supernatural—Busquets sits in spaces opponents haven't discovered yet, and his short passing out of pressure is a genuine craft. But the legs are gone, and he can't cover ground laterally anymore, which in MLS's higher tempo becomes a structural problem rather than a minor flaw. He's excellent as a base layer if the system protects him, but he's no longer a player you build around.
