## Scouting Notes

A veteran shot-stopper at an age where most keepers are retired, Mannone remains a functional presence between the sticks — he reads the game with the composure you'd expect from someone who's survived in European football for two decades. His distribution is workmanlike rather than progressive, and at 38 his reflexes have predictably dulled; he's neither quick off his line nor commanding in the air against modern strikers. Fine as a cup keeper or insurance policy, but this is a man managing decline rather than contributing at Lille's level.
