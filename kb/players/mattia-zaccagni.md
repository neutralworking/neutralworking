## Scouting Notes

A left winger of genuine technical quality who operates with intelligence in the half-space, finding teammates through cutbacks or moving the ball laterally with precision. His pace is adequate rather than explosive, but his movement—especially his timing of inverted runs—is sharp and allows him to function as a creative outlet in possession. His main weakness is an occasional failure to track runners defensively; he's a good player but not an elite one.

The 2022/23 season was a really good one for Mattia Zaccagni. His Lazio form was one of the main reasons they managed to finish second in Serie A and make it into the Champions League, although he has failed to gain many admirers outside of Italy.  
  
Zaccagni got ten goals and seven assists in the Italian top flight last season and had Ciro Immobile been fit for more of the campaign, that assist figure likely would have got into double figures.
