## Snapshot

A left-back operating in the second tier who remains serviceable as a left-flank outlet, though his game has calcified somewhat with age. Lenz can still beat a man in transition and isn't afraid of the ball at his feet—he'll carry it forward to break pressure—but he's lost the explosive first step that once made him dangerous one-v-one, and his recovery pace is now a genuine vulnerability against quick, direct attackers. In a compact defensive structure he functions adequately, but he's neither the presence you want on the ball in buildup nor the kind of left-back who can suffocate a tricky winger over ninety minutes; he's a depth option masquerading as a starter, useful for Düsseldorf's level but exposed at higher intensity.
