While technically gifted, his defensive contributions are notably weak, evidenced by poor tackling and interceptions, coupled with discipline issues, indicating a player primarily focused on offensive flair. He represents the lineage of creative wide forwards who operate in the half-spaces, akin to an Inside Forward, aiming to destabilize defenses with individual brilliance rather than tracking back diligently, ideal for attacking systems that can absorb his defensive liabilities. Hadj-Moussa offers significant market appeal for clubs seeking a high-impact, direct attacker with strong one-on-one ability, fitting into a system that values individual brilliance in the final third. His moderate physical and mental pillars are acceptable, but his defensive frailties and discipline risks mean he is a specific tactical fit for clubs that can either manage his responsibilities or offer cover for his lack of defensive contribution.
