Nicknamed _Hugol_ and _Pentapichichi_, Sánchez was a prolific goalscorer, who usually played as a [centre-forward](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forward_(association_football)#Centre-forward); he is widely regarded as Mexico's greatest-ever footballer, and one of the greatest players of his generation.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hugo_S%C3%A1nchez#cite_note-RM-1)[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hugo_S%C3%A1nchez#cite_note-FIFA-4) A quick and mobile [striker](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forward_(association_football)#Striker "Forward (association football)"), with good skills and an eye for goal, he was known for his intelligence, positional sense, movement, and anticipation in the area, and was an accurate and efficient finisher, who was capable of scoring with few touches.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hugo_S%C3%A1nchez#cite_note-RM-1)[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hugo_S%C3%A1nchez#cite_note-FIFA-4)[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hugo_S%C3%A1nchez#cite_note-The_Hugo_S%C3%A1nchez_record_that'll_never_be_broken-17)[[28]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hugo_S%C3%A1nchez#cite_note-Liga_penalty_record-28)

Due to his athleticism, Sánchez was good in the air, despite his diminutive stature, and was also known for his ability to score acrobatic and flamboyant goals from spectacular strikes and volleys from any position on the pitch, both inside or outside the area; his mastery of the "Chilena", or "[Bicycle kick](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bicycle_kick "Bicycle kick")", was a result of his own early training in gymnastics, and his goals scored in this manner were later dubbed _Huguinas_.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hugo_S%C3%A1nchez#cite_note-RM-1)[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hugo_S%C3%A1nchez#cite_note-FIFA-4)[[29]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hugo_S%C3%A1nchez#cite_note-29) His trademark was to perform a celebratory somersault followed by a fist pump after each goal he scored, in honour of his sister, who was a gymnast and participated in the Montreal Olympics.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hugo_S%C3%A1nchez#cite_note-FIFA-4)[[30]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hugo_S%C3%A1nchez#cite_note-30) Sánchez is considered to be the first noted exponent of the [backflip](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Backflip "Backflip").[[31]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hugo_S%C3%A1nchez#cite_note-31)

According to his FIFA profile, Sánchez is credited as the creator of the scorpion kick, which was later popularised by Colombian goalkeeper [René Higuita](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Higuita "René Higuita"). Though he regularly practised the trick in training, the Mexican striker never scored a goal with it in an official match.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hugo_S%C3%A1nchez#cite_note-FIFA-4)

An accurate [penalty taker](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Penalty_kick_(association_football) "Penalty kick (association football)"), Sánchez held the record for most penalties scored in La Liga with 56, until [Cristiano Ronaldo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cristiano_Ronaldo "Cristiano Ronaldo") broke the record in 2017 after scoring his 57th penalty-kick.
