## Snapshot


ClubReal Madrid

PositionDefender

Born24 February 2004







********

The son of Tomeu Obrador, who played for just about every club on the island except Mallorca itself in the 1980s and 1990s, Rafael Obrador is a tall, athletic left-back who was given the chance to make his first division debut for the club aged just 16 against Osasuna in July 2020. Despite having renewed his contract that spring, Real Madrid paid to take him to the capital in October amid interest from Barcelona too. “He’s a full-back with the physical capacity to go up and down and repeat those movements consistently,” says Maheta Molango, the PFA chief who was CEO at Mallorca at the time. “He’s pretty offensive, a Marcos Alonso-type left back with who can go up and down, has good delivery of the ball and is reasonably solid defensively. He has an all-round profile, an interesting player, although when he first broke through with us it was maybe a little early still.” **[Sid Lowe](https://twitter.com/sidlowe)**  
  
**September 2022 update** A regular in the Spain Under-19 squad, he is making good progress ascending the Real Madrid youth ranks. A starter for Real Madrid in the Uefa Youth League and scorer of important goals and assists in the Copa del Rey Juvenil, he was key to Real Madrid lifting the domestic youth trophy last season. In the summer he joined Real Madrid Castilla, who are managed by club legend Raúl, and has already featured in three matches for the academy team.  
  
**October 2023 update** Continues to grow after establishing himself as a regular in the Real Madrid B team, Castilla, who play in the Spanish third tier. The left-back clocked up more than 2,000 minutes last term and is considered one of the academy names to keep an eye on for Carlo Ancelotti in the near future.
