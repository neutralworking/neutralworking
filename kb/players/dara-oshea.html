Recent Form

Dara O'Shea has been a consistent presence for Ipswich Town during the 2025/2026 Championship season, featuring in 41 matches and accumulating 3,510 minutes on the pitch. During this period, he has contributed one goal and one assist, maintaining an average FotMob rating of 7.17. His recent performances in March and April 2026 include strong displays against Leicester City (8.1 FotMob rating) and Hull City (7.9 FotMob rating), demonstrating his continued impact for the club.

In August 2025, O'Shea was appointed club captain for Ipswich Town ahead of the 2025-26 EFL Championship campaign, a role he embraced after signing a new five-year contract in June 2025. On the international stage, he remains a key defender for the Republic of Ireland, having started in six UEFA Nations League matches during the 2024-25 season, contributing to their promotion from League B. He also received call-ups for Ireland's 2026 FIFA World Cup qualifiers in 2025 and has amassed 58 caps for his country as of November 2025.

Tactical Profile

O'Shea primarily operates as a centre-back, but his tactical versatility allows him to play effectively across a back-three or even as a full-back. He is noted for his strength in aerial duels and his ability to block shots, often opting to play long balls out of defence and clear the ball when necessary. Despite his 189 cm height, he possesses quick feet and excellent judgment, capable of reaching top speeds of 36.70 km/h.

His playing style is characterized by strong positional awareness, enabling him to make effective tackles and interceptions. He is considered a capable one-on-one defender and is pivotal in build-up play, demonstrating an understanding of when to draw out an opponent's press and when to distribute the ball quickly. O'Shea's physicality and high duel win rate further underscore his combative approach. While strong in many defensive aspects, his tackling is identified as a relative weakness, and he tends to avoid diving into tackles.

Career Highlights

Dara O'Shea began his senior career at West Bromwich Albion, where he was instrumental in their 2019–20 EFL Championship promotion campaign, making 17 league appearances and scoring three goals as the team secured a spot in the Premier League. He later made 28 appearances in the Premier League and captained West Brom for the first time in August 2022, reaching his 100th club appearance in February 2023.

In June 2023, O'Shea transferred to Burnley for £7 million, signing a four-year contract, and played 37 games, scoring four goals during the 2023-24 season. He then joined Ipswich Town in August 2024 on a five-year deal, where he was appointed club captain in August 2025. Internationally, O'Shea made his senior Republic of Ireland debut in October 2020 and was recognized as the FAI Young Player of the Year for 2020. His early career also included successful loan spells at Hereford FC, where he won the Southern League Premier Division title, and Exeter City.

Personality

Dara O'Shea is described as a "very quiet guy" who prefers to let his actions on the pitch speak for themselves, known for being a "nice bloke" and "down to earth". His competitive spirit and "battle" mentality are attributed to his upbringing in Gaelic football, which he credits for shaping his tenacious defending.

As Ipswich Town's captain, O'Shea is seen as an approachable leader who sets high standards and actively encourages teammates to participate in community events, taking his responsibilities off the pitch seriously. He leads by example, embodying the principle that leadership is demonstrated through actions rather than constant vocalization.

Condition / Medical History

O'Shea has experienced a few notable injuries throughout his career. In September 2021, he suffered a fractured ankle and ligament damage while on international duty, which required surgery and sidelined him for four to six months. More recently, in late 2024, he revealed he had been playing through a fracture in his back, specifically in the transverse part. He managed this injury with the club's physios and was given clearance to continue playing, nearing full fitness by November 2024. His medical history also includes a knock injury in September 2023 and a red card suspension in April 2024.
