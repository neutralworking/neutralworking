PositionDefender

Born23 April 2004

An intelligent full-back with enough versatility to play anywhere across the backline and in defensive midfield, Rawlins is a graduate of Perth Glory’s academy and was the youngest player to start in Australia’s top flight last season aged 16 years and 272 days. The swift promotion – he started eight of his 12 games – under manager Richard Garcia was little surprise given he went to the 2019 Fifa Under-17 World Cup in Brazil while still a member of the under-15 squad and in the same year came off the bench in a friendly with Manchester United. Last year Rawlins became the youngest player overall in the rebooted Asian Champions League when he debuted against Shanghai Shenhua – the first of four group-stage appearances. **[Emma Kemp](https://twitter.com/emmavkemp)**  
  
**September 2022 update** Was a bright spark in a dark season for Perth Glory in 2021-22; starting 15 games and making seven substitute appearances while also representing Australia at the AFC Under-23 Asian Cup. Earned a move to FC Utrecht on a long-term deal and is now starting regularly in Domstedelingen’s youth setup.  
  
**October 2023 update** Playing for the under-23 Utrecht side and still yet to make his senior debut, but has impressed in his youth national team appearances. Captained the Young Socceroos to the quarter-finals at the Under-20 Asian Cup in March where they lost on penalties to Uzbekistan. Started in central defence for the Olyroos in June where he helped keep two clean sheets and impressed as one of the squad’s youngest players.
