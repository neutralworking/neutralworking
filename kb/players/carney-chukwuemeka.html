Carney Chukwuemeka is a 22-year-old attacking midfielder for Borussia Dortmund, known for his right-footed play and standing at 187 cm. While he previously represented England at youth levels, he switched his international allegiance to Austria in March 2026, making his debut for his birth country that same month.

Recent Form

Carney Chukwuemeka has contributed to Borussia Dortmund's campaign in the 2025-26 season, recording 3 goals and 2 assists across 33 total appearances in the Bundesliga, UEFA Champions League, and DFB-Pokal. In the Bundesliga specifically, he has made 22 appearances, scoring 2 goals. His recent performances include a goal on his debut for the Austrian national team in March 2026. Although he has seen varied minutes in recent club matches, he registered an assist in a 1-4 loss to Atalanta in February 2026 and scored in a 5-1 win against Ghana in March 2026.

Dortmund coach Niko Kovac has praised Chukwuemeka's positive impact, noting his ability to change games and his continuous improvement, particularly in the defensive aspects of his play. Chukwuemeka himself acknowledges his progress, stating he feels his body is fit and he is consistently demonstrating his capabilities, with a strong focus on incorporating defensive duties into his attacking game.

Tactical Profile

Chukwuemeka operates primarily as an attacking midfielder, though he can also be deployed as a left winger or central midfielder. He is recognized as an excellent ball carrier, adept at dribbling with speed and dynamism to advance play up the field. His playing style is characterized by a blend of technical skill, tactical intelligence, and bravery, allowing him to control the game's tempo and break down defenses with his vision and accurate passing. He frequently engages in short passes and dribbles, often drawing fouls from opponents.

Defensively, Chukwuemeka possesses strong positional awareness and can make crucial interceptions, utilizing his imposing physique to hold off attackers and win aerial duels when engaged. While he has been noted for occasional lapses in defensive exertion, his coach emphasizes his role as the "first defender" in the press, a facet of his game he is actively working to improve. His adaptability allows him to fit into various formations and seamlessly transition between attacking and defensive responsibilities. His dribbling ability and passing range have drawn comparisons to players like Jack Grealish, while his languid style and physique have been likened to Paul Pogba.

Career Highlights

Chukwuemeka has achieved significant success at youth international level, winning the UEFA European Under-19 Championship in 2022 with England U19 and being named in the Team of the Tournament. He played a pivotal role in the final, scoring England's second goal in their 3-1 victory over Israel. At club level, he was recognized as Aston Villa's Academy Player of the Season for the 2020–21 campaign and was part of the Aston Villa U18 squad that won the FA Youth Cup in the same season. He made his senior debut for Aston Villa in May 2021. After an initial loan spell, he completed a permanent transfer to Borussia Dortmund in August 2025, signing a long-term contract until 2030.

Personality

Carney Chukwuemeka is described as a calm, vocal, and at times, a leading presence on the pitch. Off the field, he is known for being bubbly, sociable, and enjoys making jokes. He exhibits a strong ambition and a relentless drive for self-improvement, consistently setting goals for himself and using them as motivation, even keeping them visible on his phone's lock screen. Chukwuemeka maintains that he is never satisfied with his current achievements and always strives for more, a mentality he attributes to his background and family. He has a positive and communicative relationship with his coach, Niko Kovac, particularly regarding his development in defensive duties.

Condition / Medical History

Carney Chukwuemeka has experienced a notable history of injuries throughout his career, which has impacted his availability. In the 2025 calendar year, he dealt with a hip injury in April, a lack of fitness and a knock injury in March, and two separate knee injuries in February. The 2024 calendar year saw him sidelined with illness in December, an unknown issue in April/May, a knock in March, and an ankle injury in February. More recently, he sustained a muscle injury in February/March 2026, which caused him to miss matches before returning to play a limited role off the bench. He has been working to regain full fitness following these setbacks and struggled with fitness upon his initial arrival at Borussia Dortmund. There were reports in August 2025 that he was dealing with knee problems and might require surgery during the winter break.
