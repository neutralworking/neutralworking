Snapshot

A willing, high-intensity wide forward who compensates for modest technical ceiling with excellent pressing triggers and aggressive positioning in the half-spaces. He's quick enough to threaten in transition and reasonably clinical in the box, but lacks the dribbling range or creative awareness to unlock a stubborn defence—he's a finisher first, a creator nowhere near last. At 27, playing in the Belgian top flight, he's a reliable source of goals and energy without being the sort of winger who fundamentally changes how an attack moves.

Recent Form

Anders Dreyer has enjoyed an exceptional period since his transfer to San Diego FC in January 2025, culminating in him being named the 2025 MLS Newcomer of the Year. During his debut MLS season, Dreyer tied the league record for the most goal contributions by a first-year player, tallying an impressive 19 goals and 19 assists for a total of 38 contributions. This output meant he was directly involved in 59.4% of San Diego FC's 64 goals, playing a pivotal role in the club securing the top seed in the Western Conference and qualifying for the 2026 Concacaf Champions Cup.

His outstanding performance in 2025 also earned him recognition as an MLS All-Star and saw him named MLS Player of the Month on two occasions, in June and August. In the ongoing 2026 MLS season, Dreyer continues to be a key offensive force, having recorded 3 goals and 3 assists in 7 appearances, accumulating 572 minutes of play, and maintaining an average FotMob rating of 7.8. He has demonstrated strong attacking intent, attempting 18 shots with 10 on target, creating 2 big chances, and contributing 12 key passes and 35 crosses.

Tactical Profile

Anders Dreyer operates primarily as a right winger, often functioning as an inverted winger due to his preferred left foot, allowing him to cut inside and threaten the goal directly. Standing at 174 cm, he is known for his strong finishing, ability to play incisive through balls, and creating key passes. His playing style is characterized by frequently playing the ball off the ground, posing a significant counter-attack threat, and a tendency to cut inside from the flank.

Dreyer's elite field vision is a notable asset, enabling him to identify and exploit spaces for dangerous opportunities. While his offensive contributions are highly valued, areas identified for potential improvement include offside awareness and defensive contribution. His former teammate highlighted his clinical left foot and preference for cutting inside to finish from both inside and outside the box.

Career Highlights

Dreyer's career is marked by several significant achievements across different leagues. He was the top goal scorer in the 2017-18 Danish 1st Division with 18 goals, which helped Esbjerg fB achieve promotion to the Superliga. After a move to Rubin Kazan, he made an immediate impact by scoring a hat-trick on his debut in September 2021, a first in the league's history.

He has secured two major titles with FC Midtjylland: the Danish Superliga in the 2019/2020 season and the DBU Pokalen (Danish Cup) in 2021/2022. His individual accolades include being named Danish Superliga Player of the Month in February 2020 and Anderlecht Player of the Season for 2023–24. On the international stage, Dreyer was part of Denmark's squad for UEFA Euro 2024 and made his senior national team debut in November 2021. His move to San Diego FC in 2025 saw him become the MLS Newcomer of the Year, an MLS All-Star, and part of the MLS Best XI, contributing to San Diego FC setting new MLS records for points and wins by an expansion team.

Personality

Anders Dreyer is characterized by his humility and team-first mentality, despite his individual successes. He has publicly stated that winning with the team is more important to him than personal awards, even if it means not scoring himself. San Diego FC's head coach, Mikey Varas, has praised Dreyer for embodying "talent, character, and purpose," highlighting his dedication to putting the team first and his strong desire to win.

Dreyer values a close-knit team environment, noting that the "family feeling" within the San Diego FC dressing room is exceptional and something he hasn't frequently encountered in his career. He approaches high-stakes games, such as playoffs, with a calm demeanor, indicating a readiness for significant moments. He has expressed enjoyment for playing in the MLS and thrives in San Diego FC's attacking style of play.

Condition / Medical History

Anders Dreyer has experienced a few periods of absence due to illness and knee injuries throughout his career. In 2025, he was sidelined by illness from November 18 to November 24. Prior to that, in 2024, he sustained a knee injury that kept him out from September 14 to September 28, and an unknown issue from April 24 to May 5.

Earlier in his career, Dreyer also dealt with an illness in August 2022 and a knee injury from late April to late June in 2019. He also missed rounds due to a direct red card in 2024, which are suspensions rather than medical issues.
