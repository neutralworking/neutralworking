José María Giménez: Player Research Brief

Recent Form

José María Giménez has featured in 27 matches across all competitions for Atlético Madrid and the Uruguay national team during the 2025-2026 season, accumulating 1755 minutes of play. In La Liga, he has made 15 appearances, providing one assist and receiving four yellow cards. His defensive contributions include 23 tackles (12 won), 16 interceptions, and 64 clearances, maintaining a 90% pass accuracy rate from 779 attempts. Giménez has also participated in six Champions League matches, scoring one goal and earning one yellow card.

Despite his contributions, the 2025-2026 season has seen recurring injury concerns. He suffered a hamstring injury from December 2025 to January 2026 and a thigh injury from June to October 2025. More recently, he was sidelined with a muscular injury sustained during the Club World Cup in late June, and his absence continued into April 2026, making him a doubt for upcoming matches. These injuries have impacted his consistent availability, although he has still managed to contribute defensively when fit.

Tactical Profile

José María Giménez operates as a central defender, known for his aggressive and committed style of play, coupled with speed over short distances. His physical prowess and strong aerial ability make him a formidable presence in defense and a threat during set pieces in attack. He exhibits excellent tactical reading, allowing him to position himself effectively and anticipate plays, often cutting off dangerous situations before they fully develop.

As a "Conductor" and "Sweeper," Giménez demonstrates strong leadership qualities, organizing the defensive line and instilling intensity in his teammates. He is adept at playing long balls, a strength noted in his passing game. His defensive approach is characterized by defending "to the limit," engaging strongly in duels, and utilizing his tactical awareness to excel within systems that prioritize defensive solidity, such as under Diego Simeone at Atlético Madrid.

Career Highlights

José María Giménez has enjoyed a decorated career primarily with Atlético Madrid, where he has spent over a decade and played more than 350 games. He has been instrumental in securing multiple domestic and international titles for the club, including two La Liga titles in the 2013–14 and 2020–21 seasons. His European successes include winning the UEFA Europa League in 2017–18 and the UEFA Super Cup in 2018. He also contributed to Atlético Madrid's Supercopa de España victory in 2014.

On the international stage, Giménez has been a key figure for the Uruguay national team since his debut in 2013, representing his country in three FIFA World Cups and five editions of the Copa América. He was part of the Uruguay U20 squad that finished as runner-up in the 2013 FIFA U-20 World Cup. His leadership qualities have also seen him captain the Uruguay national team.

Condition / Medical History

José María Giménez has a notable history of injuries throughout his career, which has often impacted his availability. In the 2024 calendar year alone, he experienced multiple muscle and thigh injuries, leading to periods of absence. Looking into the 2025-2026 season, he sustained a thigh injury from June to October 2025 and a hamstring injury in December 2025, which kept him out until January 2026.

Most recently, Giménez suffered a muscular injury during the Club World Cup in late June 2025 and was still in the rehabilitation process as of April 2026, training individually. This ongoing issue has caused him to miss recent matches and has raised doubts about his immediate return to full team training. His recurring injury concerns, particularly muscular problems, have been a consistent challenge throughout his career.

Personality

José María Giménez is often perceived as an ISFJ personality type, characterized by quiet dedication, empathy, and a role as a "protector" who works behind the scenes to maintain harmony and stability. This aligns with his on-field persona as a leader who orders the defensive line and transmits intensity to his teammates.

He possesses significant mental fortitude and a relentless work ethic, qualities that have been shaped by his long tenure under Diego Simeone at Atlético Madrid. Giménez has expressed a deep connection to Atlético Madrid, comparing its passionate atmosphere to that of the Uruguayan national team and South American football. He has also demonstrated a willingness to put the team's needs before his own, even considering leaving the club at times for personal reasons but ultimately remaining committed.
