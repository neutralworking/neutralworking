Giuliano Simeone is a 23-year-old Argentine center forward for Atlético Madrid, known for his dynamic and versatile attacking play. Standing at 173 cm and preferring his right foot, Simeone embodies the "Drifter" archetype, operating effectively as a "Seconda Punta" within his team's tactical setup.

Recent Form

Giuliano Simeone has demonstrated significant growth and impact in the current 2025/2026 La Liga season, recording 4 goals and 6 assists across 1,919 minutes of play, earning an average FotMob rating of 7.22. His performance places him highly among right midfielders in the league for assists, rating, and goals. This follows a productive 2024/2025 La Liga campaign where he contributed 2 goals and 6 assists in 33 matches, accumulating a total of 8 goal contributions.

His return to Atlético Madrid for the 2024-25 season saw him quickly integrate and make an impact, scoring his first goal for the club on November 3, 2024, against Las Palmas. He further marked his presence by scoring his first Champions League goal and providing an assist on January 29, 2025, in a 4-1 away victory over RB Salzburg. Simeone's recent form has been characterized by a "remarkable evolution in his game," showcasing maturity and character, and he has become an "absolute undisputed starter" for Atlético Madrid, providing "tremendous energy" to the team.

Tactical Profile

Simeone is recognized for his energetic and versatile playing style, capable of operating as a forward, winger, or attacking midfielder, offering significant tactical flexibility. His game is built on exceptional dribbling ability, close ball control, and remarkable pace and acceleration, which make him particularly dangerous on counter-attacks where he can exploit defensive vulnerabilities. He is physically strong and balanced, allowing him to retain possession under pressure and fend off defenders.

Beyond his offensive attributes, Simeone possesses an excellent football IQ, enabling him to read the game, anticipate opponents' actions, and make intelligent decisions under pressure. His movements are described as clever and unpredictable, as he expertly times his runs to exploit defensive gaps. He is also noted for his intense work ethic and significant defensive contributions, including pressing opponents to regain possession, which aligns perfectly with Atlético Madrid's tactical philosophy. Following a serious injury, he refined his pressing cadence, adopting shorter, sharper bursts that effectively funnel play, further enhancing his defensive utility.

Career Highlights

Giuliano Simeone began his football journey in Argentina with River Plate's youth academy before joining Atlético Madrid's youth program in September 2019. He made his senior debut for Atlético Madrid's B-team in January 2021, scoring in his first appearance. A standout season in 2021-22 saw him net 8 goals in 11 matches in the Tercera División RFEF, playing a crucial role in the team's promotion. His senior debut for Atlético Madrid came on April 20, 2022, in a La Liga match against Granada.

Simeone gained valuable experience through loan spells, first with Real Zaragoza in the Segunda División for the 2022-23 season, where he scored his first professional goal and contributed 9 goals in 36 appearances. He then moved to Deportivo Alavés in La Liga for the 2023-24 season, a move preceded by a contract renewal with Atlético Madrid until 2028. During his time at Alavés, he scored his first top-flight goal. Upon returning to his parent club, he extended his contract with Atlético Madrid until June 30, 2030, on January 15, 2026, solidifying his long-term future. On the international stage, Simeone represented Argentina at the 2024 Summer Olympics and made his senior national team debut on November 19, 2024, in a 2026 FIFA World Cup qualifier against Peru, later scoring his first international goal on March 25, 2025, against Brazil.

Personality

Giuliano Simeone is characterized by an intense work ethic and a highly dedicated approach to his career, prioritizing self-care and continuous improvement in all aspects of his game, including speed, strength, muscle mass, diet, and rest. He exhibits a strong character and maturity, particularly in how he has addressed public scrutiny regarding his surname. Simeone has openly spoken about critics who suggested he played only due to his father's influence, stating that he focused on his own development and that his performances speak for themselves.

He is described as self-critical, meticulously reviewing his matches to identify areas for improvement. Despite his father, Diego Simeone, being his coach, Giuliano acknowledges the high demands placed upon him, understanding that he receives no preferential treatment and must earn his place through effort. His family shares a deep passion for football, often discussing match actions in a group chat. A notable anecdote highlights his father's approach to fostering independence, telling Giuliano to "get out of my house and become a man" when he turned 18.

Condition / Medical History

Giuliano Simeone has faced significant medical challenges in his career, most notably a fractured left fibula and ankle injury sustained on August 6, 2023. This serious injury occurred during a pre-season friendly while on loan at Deportivo Alavés and sidelined him for several months, with his return to play occurring in January 2024. The injury was severe enough that it was described as potentially jeopardizing his career.

His recovery and subsequent return to form have been highlighted as a testament to his persistence and resilience. Beyond this major setback, Simeone's medical history includes other, less severe issues such as a hamstring injury in November 2025, an illness/virus in January-February 2026, and instances of overload in December 2022, physical discomfort in May 2023, and a back injury in January 2023.
