Recent Form

Diego López has been a consistent presence for Valencia during the 2025/2026 La Liga season, featuring in 27 matches and scoring 3 goals. He has accumulated 1,360 minutes of play and received 2 yellow cards in the current campaign. His shooting accuracy stands at 36.36%, with 8 shots on target from 22 attempts, averaging 1.45 shots per 90 minutes.

While his goal and assist numbers for the current season are modest, López demonstrated strong form in the 2024/2025 campaign, playing in all 38 official La Liga matches. During that season, he scored 8 goals and provided 5 assists, making him Valencia's second-highest scorer and leading in assists. His performances in 2024/2025 earned him a nomination for Best U-23 Player of the La Liga EA Sports season and he was recognized as the Best U-23 Player of the Month in March.

Career Highlights

Diego López joined Valencia CF on July 7, 2021, initially playing for the reserves before making his professional and La Liga debut with the first team on August 29, 2022, against Atlético Madrid. He scored his inaugural top-tier goal on May 21, 2023, netting the winning goal in a 1-0 home victory over Real Madrid. His commitment to the club was solidified with a contract renewal until June 2029.

López reached a significant milestone in October 2025, completing 100 official matches for Valencia CF. He is also recognized as the fourth-youngest Valencia player to achieve 15 goals in La Liga in the 21st century. On the international stage, López has represented Spain at the U21 level, making 20 appearances and scoring 2 goals between 2023 and 2025, and was part of the Spain U23 squad that won the Olympic gold medal at the Paris 2024 Games.

Condition / Medical History

Diego López has experienced a few notable injuries during his career. In February 2024, he sustained a fractured left zygomatic bone (cheekbone) due to facial trauma, but he returned to play in March 2024 and subsequently made 57 consecutive appearances in La Liga. More recently, he suffered a knee injury that kept him out from February 15 to March 1, 2026.

Prior to these, López also dealt with physical discomfort in August 2025 and a hamstring injury in May 2025. Earlier in his career, he had instances of muscle injuries, including torn muscle fibers and an adductor muscle injury.
