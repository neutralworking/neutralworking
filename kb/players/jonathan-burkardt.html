Jonathan Burkardt: Player Research Brief

Recent Form

Jonathan Burkardt joined Eintracht Frankfurt on July 4, 2025, signing a contract valid until 2030. He quickly integrated into the squad, making his Bundesliga debut for the club on August 23, 2025, in a 4-1 victory against Werder Bremen. His impact was immediate, as he scored a brace in his UEFA Champions League debut on September 18, 2025, contributing to a 5-1 win over Galatasaray.

Burkardt's strong performances continued, earning him the Bundesliga Player of the Month award for October 2025, following impressive braces against Freiburg and St. Pauli. In the 2025/2026 Bundesliga season, he has recorded 10 goals in 17 appearances, playing 1149 minutes, and maintaining a shooting accuracy of 48.57% with 17 shots on target out of 35. Across all competitions (Bundesliga, Champions League, and DFB-Pokal), he has tallied 12 goals and 1 assist in 25 appearances, accumulating 1522 minutes on the pitch. Before a calf injury in November 2025, he was in excellent scoring form, with 10 goals in 15 matches across the Bundesliga and UEFA Champions League, placing him second in the Bundesliga goal-scoring charts with 8 goals through 11 matchdays.

Tactical Profile

Jonathan Burkardt is primarily described as a poacher, with the majority of his goals originating from inside the penalty area, showcasing an exquisite finishing ability. While adept at finding goal-scoring opportunities, he is not limited to this role; he can also function as a playmaking forward, though his strong desire for goals makes him most comfortable as an out-and-out center-forward. His tactical versatility allows him to operate anywhere across the front three, whether as a lone striker, in a two-man pairing, or on either wing.

He is known for his high work rate off the ball, demonstrating significant pressing and defensive actions for a forward. As of October 2021, he averaged nearly 18 pressures and 1.5 tackles per 90 minutes. At Eintracht Frankfurt, under coach Dino Toppmöller, the team employs an aggressive attacking pressing style and a more dynamic, possession-based approach, which contrasts with the more direct play of his former club, Mainz. This system requires Burkardt to adapt to having less space and time on the ball. While his finishing is a clear strength, his aerial duels and overall defensive contribution are noted as areas for improvement based on recent seasons' data. He frequently plays the ball off the ground and is known for attempting long passes.

Career Highlights

Burkardt has achieved notable success at both club and international youth levels. He was a key member of the Germany U21 squad that won the 2021 UEFA European Under-21 Championship. His senior Bundesliga debut came on September 15, 2018, with Mainz 05. During his time at Mainz, he scored against perennial champions Bayern Munich in the 2020-21 season and became the club's top scorer in the 2021-22 season with 11 Bundesliga goals.

His performances earned him the "Rising Star of the Season" title in a Kicker poll during the first half of the 2021-22 season. He achieved his personal best goal tally of 18 in the 2024/25 season. In October 2024, he received his first call-up to the senior Germany national team for UEFA Nations League matches. Most recently, he was recognized as the Bundesliga Player of the Month for October 2025.

Personality

Jonathan Burkardt maintains a private personal life, notably avoiding social media entirely. He has described social media as a "make-believe world" and stated that he misses nothing by not engaging with it. Furthermore, he is known for not consuming alcohol, specifically never drinking beer. Markus Krösche, Eintracht Frankfurt's board member for sport, has characterized Burkardt as a dedicated "team player both on and off the pitch".

Condition / Medical History

Jonathan Burkardt has a considerable history of injuries throughout his career. A significant setback occurred between November 2022 and November 2023, when he was sidelined for a full year due to a lengthy knee injury that required surgery.

In the current 2025/2026 season, he has faced several physical challenges. He experienced a back injury in August 2025. More recently, he sustained a calf injury during a UEFA Champions League match against Atalanta BC on November 27, 2025, which led to concerns from Eintracht Frankfurt about its severity. This calf issue kept him out of action for an extended period, from November 2025 to February 2026, with his return further delayed by an illness in February 2026. As of April 18, 2026, he is still listed as "out with calf" for Eintracht Frankfurt's upcoming match against RB Leipzig, despite having been spotted in team training in the preceding weeks.
