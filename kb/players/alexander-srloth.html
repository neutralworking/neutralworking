Alexander Sørloth is a 30-year-old Norwegian international forward who currently plays for Atlético Madrid. Standing at 194 cm, he is known for his physical presence and goal-scoring ability, operating primarily as a center-forward or shadow striker. His preferred foot is left, despite some earlier reports indicating otherwise.

Recent Form

Alexander Sørloth has demonstrated strong attacking form during the 2025-2026 season for Atlético Madrid. In La Liga, he has appeared in 30 matches, scoring 10 goals in 1698 minutes played, averaging 0.53 goals per 90 minutes. His overall goal involvement (goals + assists) also stands at 0.53 per 90 minutes, with a non-penalty expected goals (npxG) output of 0.57 per 90 minutes, placing him in the top 98 percentile of La Liga players. He has maintained a shooting accuracy of 51.85%, with 28 shots on target from 54 attempts.

Beyond domestic league play, Sørloth has also been impactful in the UEFA Champions League, contributing 6 goals in 13 appearances (590 minutes). A notable performance includes a hat-trick scored on February 24, 2026, in a 4-1 victory over Club Brugge, which secured Atlético Madrid's qualification for the Round of 16. He further contributed a crucial goal in the Champions League quarter-final first leg against Barcelona on April 8, 2026, helping his team to a 2-0 away win. This follows a prolific 2024-2025 season where he finished as Atlético's top scorer in La Liga with 20 goals, including a record-breaking first-half quadruple against Real Sociedad on May 10, 2025.

Tactical Profile

Sørloth is characterized as a powerful striker who combines physical strength with tactical intelligence and a surprising touch for his size. His 194 cm frame allows him to effectively overpower defenders, particularly in aerial duels, where he ranks in the 99th percentile among La Liga strikers. He excels in a hold-up role, providing a focal point for direct attacks and counter-attacking opportunities, winning duels, and linking up with midfield runners.

While primarily a "Marksman" and "Shadow Striker," Sørloth's game involves more than just being a target man; he positions himself wisely and works diligently both with and without the ball. His strengths include aerial prowess, headed attempts, and clinical finishing. However, his defensive contribution is considered a weakness. Although his dribbling has improved, it is most effective in transitions rather than in congested central areas, and he tends to lay the ball off rather than consistently driving play forward.

Career Highlights

Alexander Sørloth's career has seen him play across several European leagues before his move to Atlético Madrid. He joined Atlético Madrid in August 2024 from Villarreal for a fee of €32 million, signing a four-year contract. He made an immediate impact, scoring on his debut against his former club Villarreal on August 19, 2024. Later that year, he netted a stoppage-time goal to secure Atlético's first away victory over Barcelona since 2006 on December 21, 2024.

A standout moment in his career came on May 10, 2025, when he scored a first-half quadruple against Real Sociedad, including a hat-trick within four minutes, setting a new La Liga record for the fastest hat-trick before the 12th minute. He concluded his debut season with Atlético Madrid as the club's top scorer in La Liga with 20 goals. Prior to joining Atlético, Sørloth won the Danish Superliga with Midtjylland in 2017-2018 and the Turkish Cup with Trabzonspor in 2019-2020, where he was also the league's top scorer. He was also recognized as La Liga Player of the Month during his time with Real Sociedad in the 2022-2023 season. As of March 31, 2026, he has earned 70 caps for the Norwegian national team, scoring 26 goals.

Personality

Alexander Sørloth has shown significant mental growth throughout his career, acknowledging that earlier in his career he was "too emotional" and "too angry" when not starting. He now takes pride in being an important substitute and maintaining a strong mental focus, especially when competing for trophies. He views playing for Atlético Madrid as a "dream come true" and has expressed a long-standing desire to play for the club, considering Madrid his favorite city in Spain.

Sørloth is a family man, with a daughter born in 2024, and he aims to surpass his father's footballing achievements, particularly by playing in a World Cup. He enjoys the physical aspects of the game, preferring to battle against larger defenders. He also values team cohesion, noting the positive atmosphere and lack of "idiots" within the Atlético Madrid squad. His ambition to play consistently is driven by a desire to be in peak condition for the 2026 World Cup
