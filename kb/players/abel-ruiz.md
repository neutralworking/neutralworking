## Scouting Notes

A proper number nine who leads the line with intelligence and positioning rather than explosive athleticism; he's tidy in the box, good at holding play up, and understands how to create space for runners arriving late. His issue is a lack of genuine pace and penetration—he's not winning races in behind, and against aggressive backlines he can become peripheral. Good player, good player, but lacks the x-factor that separates the very good from the elite.

All-time top scorer in the European under-17 championship with 16, eight of them at the finals, Abel Ruiz was the captain of the Spain side who won the competition this summer – scoring against France, Italy (2) and Turkey. He had been in the team that finished runners-up the previous season, getting goals against Germany, Italy, Holland and Serbia. An out-and-out goalscorer, finishing from inside the area, he stands out for his movement, work and positioning. A fan of Zlatan Ibrahimovic, he was signed by Barcelona from Valencia at the age of 12 – much to the chagrin of the club from Mestalla. Scored on his infantil debut and continued to progress.
