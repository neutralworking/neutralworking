Tactical Profile

F. Cristaldo, primarily an attacking midfielder, operates with a right-footed preference and is known for his creative capabilities on the field. He is adept at delivering incisive through balls and key passes, demonstrating an ability to provide deep passes that exploit defensive spaces. His playing style also incorporates agile dribbling in tight areas and effective ball retention.

Beyond his offensive contributions, Cristaldo is recognized for his proficiency in set-piece delivery and accurate crosses, often targeting the first post. He also contributes defensively, indicating a well-rounded profile for a wide playmaker.

Career Highlights

Franco Cristaldo began his professional journey as a youth graduate of Boca Juniors, making his first-team debut in 2014. During his time with Boca Juniors, he secured significant titles, including the Argentine Primera División in 2015 and the Copa Argentina in the 2014/2015 season. After several loan spells, he joined Huracán permanently before moving to Grêmio in December 2022 for approximately R$ 20 million.

At Grêmio, Cristaldo was a key player in 2023 and contributed to the club's success, winning the Campeonato Gaúcho in 2023 and 2024, as well as the Recopa Gaúcha in 2023 and 2025. Across his tenure with Grêmio, he accumulated 165 appearances, scoring 28 goals and providing 21 assists. In February 2026, Cristaldo transferred to Talleres, marking a new chapter in his career.

Condition / Medical History

F. Cristaldo has experienced several injuries throughout his career. Most recently, he sustained a muscle injury in July 2025, which kept him out of action for approximately nine days. Other notable past injuries include another muscle injury in July 2023 and a period of absence due to Coronavirus in late 2020.
