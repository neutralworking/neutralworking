Raw scouting narrative

class: attacker

Khvicha Kvaratskhelia has already garnered the 'Kvaradona' tag after joining Napoli last summer.

The Georgian superstar's direct running, instinctive nature and penchant to entertain have seen him immediately emerge as a fan favourite. He's an incredibly exciting winger to watch, with his unpredictability and variation in possession being distinct factors in his meteoric rise.

Kvaratskhelia led Serie A for assists in 2022/23 (10) and also netted 12 times during Napoli's title winning campaign.

Recent Form

Khvicha Kvaratskhelia joined Paris Saint-Germain in January 2025 from Napoli for a reported fee of €70-80 million, immediately becoming a pivotal player for the French club. In his debut season with PSG (2024-2025), he played a significant role in the team achieving a continental treble, securing the Champions League, Ligue 1, and Coupe de France titles, and notably scoring in the Champions League final. He also contributed to Napoli's Serie A title win in the first half of the same season before his transfer, making him a Serie A winner for the 2024-2025 campaign as well.

In the current 2025-2026 season, Kvaratskhelia has continued to be an important attacking force for PSG, contributing with goals and assists across various competitions. As of April 2026, he has scored 4 goals and provided 4 assists in Ligue 1, and 8 goals and 4 assists in the Champions League, demonstrating his consistent output. Despite his overall impact, there were reports indicating a slight dip in his Ligue 1 scoring form during the first half of the current campaign, with only one goal since December, though he remains a key component of PSG's attacking strategy.

Tactical Profile

Kvaratskhelia primarily operates as a left winger, utilizing his right foot to cut inside and pose a direct threat to the opposition goal. His playing style is characterized by exceptional dribbling, agility, and speed, allowing him to bypass defenders effectively. He is adept at creating scoring opportunities for himself and his teammates, delivering precise crosses and crucial assists.

His tactical versatility enables him to adapt his role, functioning effectively as an inverted winger or even a second striker, and he possesses a keen ability to identify and exploit open spaces during counter-attacks. Kvaratskhelia also exhibits considerable strength on the ball, using his upper-body to hold off opponents and maintain possession, even in tight spaces within the final third. While known for his attacking prowess, he also contributes defensively with intensity and aggression, actively engaging in counter-pressing, although his angling in defensive situations could sometimes be refined.

Career Highlights

Khvicha Kvaratskhelia's career saw a significant breakthrough during the 2022-2023 season with Napoli, where he played a starring role in their Serie A title victory, their first in 33 years. His outstanding performances earned him the Serie A Most Valuable Player award and the UEFA Champions League Young Player of the Season accolade for that period. He was also recognized multiple times as the Serie A Player of the Month.

Following his move to Paris Saint-Germain in January 2025, Kvaratskhelia quickly added numerous major honors to his collection. In the 2024-2025 season alone, he won the UEFA Champions League, Ligue 1, Coupe de France, UEFA Supercup, FIFA Intercontinental Cup, and the French Super Cup with PSG. Individually, he has been named Georgian Footballer of the Year on multiple occasions and received the Russian Premier League Young Player of the Season award twice earlier in his career. He also played a vital role in the Georgia national team's qualification for UEFA Euro 2024, where he scored his first goal in the tournament during a significant victory over Portugal. His distinctive dribbling and overall playing style have led to him being affectionately nicknamed "Kvaradona" and the "Georgian Messi".

Personality

Khvicha Kvaratskhelia has expressed immense happiness and pride in joining Paris Saint-Germain, describing the move as a dream come true. He is deeply motivated by PSG's ambitious project and the opportunity to achieve significant success alongside other top-tier players. His commitment extends beyond personal accolades, as he focuses on giving his utmost to help the team achieve its collective goals.

Becoming a father has introduced a new dimension to his motivation, instilling a greater sense of responsibility and driving him to work even harder. Kvaratskhelia values the support he receives from the fans, noting that their welcome helped him settle quickly and feel at home in Paris with his family. He also holds his coaches in high regard, particularly appreciating their character and the way they communicate and inspire players.

Condition / Medical History

Khvicha Kvaratskhelia has experienced several minor injuries throughout his career, with a few recorded instances during his time at PSG and Napoli. In January 2026, he sustained an ankle injury during a Champions League match against Newcastle, which was not serious and resulted in an expected absence of two to three weeks. Prior to this, in 2025, he dealt with a thigh injury, calf injury, and muscle injury in September and August, respectively, along with an illness in March and a knee injury in January and December. His medical history also includes a back injury in late 2022 and various other minor issues in previous years.
