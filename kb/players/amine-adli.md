Amine Adli is a 25-year-old Moroccan international winger who currently plays for Bournemouth. Standing at 174 cm, Adli is a left-footed player known for his role as an Inverted Winger.

## Recent Form
Amine Adli joined Bournemouth in August 2025 and has since made 27 appearances in the 2025/2026 Premier League season, contributing 3 goals and 1 assist in 1032 minutes of play. He scored his first goal for the club in November 2025 and notably netted a dramatic 95th-minute winner against Liverpool in January 2026, securing a 3-2 victory. His performances have been recognized, earning him a nomination for Bournemouth Player of the Month in February. Adli has also featured for the Moroccan national team, including a 23-minute appearance against Paraguay in March 2026.

## Tactical Profile
Operating primarily as an inverted winger, Amine Adli excels at cutting inside from the flank to influence play in central areas, creating midfield overloads and linking up with teammates. He is recognized for his exceptional ball-carrying ability, agility, and capacity to navigate tight spaces effectively. Adli's playing style is characterized by intelligent movement, pace, and a distinct preference for shooting with the inside of his left foot. While versatile across the front line, including as an attacking midfielder or forward, he has expressed a preference for the right wing to maximize his dribbling and shooting opportunities. Beyond his attacking prowess, Adli contributes defensively through effective pressing and counter-pressing.

## Career Highlights
Adli's professional journey began at Toulouse, where he signed his first contract in 2018 and made his debut in 2019. His breakthrough came in the 2020-21 Ligue 2 season, where his contribution of 7 goals and 8 assists earned him the Ligue 2 Player of the Year award and a spot in the Ligue 2 Team of the Year. In August 2021, he transferred to Bayer Leverkusen, where he was an integral part of the squad that achieved an unbeaten Bundesliga title in 2023-24, also winning the DFB-Pokal as the top goalscorer and the DFL-Supercup in 2024. Adli moved to Bournemouth in August 2025 for a reported £25.1 million, marking his entry into the Premier League. He also represents the Moroccan national team, making his debut in 2023 and being named in the squad for the 2023 Africa Cup of Nations.

## Personality
Amine Adli is known for his positive and energetic demeanor, often described as having a smile and bringing laughter to the changing room. He identifies as a dedicated "teammate player" who prioritizes hard work and running for his team, including fulfilling defensive responsibilities. His time under Xabi Alonso at Leverkusen contributed to his maturity, notably after a sending-off incident where he later demonstrated growth with a strong performance.

## Condition / Medical History
Adli has faced several significant injuries during his career. These include a broken collarbone in August 2022 and a hip injury in May 2023. A major setback occurred in October 2024 when he fractured his left fibula during a Champions League match, which kept him sidelined until January 2025. Upon joining Bournemouth, Adli acknowledged this "big injury" and expressed his desire for consistent playtime to fully regain his confidence.
