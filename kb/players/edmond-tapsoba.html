Recent Form

Edmond Tapsoba has been a crucial component of Bayer Leverkusen's highly successful 2023/2024 season, which saw the club win the Bundesliga title and the DFB Pokal, remaining unbeaten in the league. He also played a significant role in their UEFA Europa League campaign during this period. In the 2025/2026 Bundesliga season, Tapsoba has registered 24 appearances and scored 5 goals, demonstrating his continued impact on the team's performance.

His recent match ratings in the Bundesliga and Champions League for the 2025/2026 season indicate consistent performance. Tapsoba has faced some recent periods of absence, including a muscle injury after returning from the Africa Cup of Nations, though he was later cleared to train. He has also incurred suspensions due to yellow card accumulation, leading him to miss some matches.

Tactical Profile

Edmond Tapsoba is recognized as an elite ball-playing center-back, highly comfortable and composed in possession, with exceptional distribution skills. He is adept at controlling the tempo of the game from deep positions, averaging five passes into the final third per game, placing him in the top 9% across Europe's top five leagues. His passing accuracy is consistently high, maintaining an 88% completion rate last season and 93% in the 2023-24 Bundesliga season, and he is also proficient at carrying the ball forward.

Standing at 190 cm, Tapsoba is physically imposing and utilizes his long arms effectively in duels. While naturally right-footed, he is impressive with both feet and can operate effectively on either side of the central defense. Under Xabi Alonso, he has frequently featured as a right center-back, but has also been deployed as a left center-back in both three and four-man defensive setups. Defensively, he possesses good pace and acceleration, enabling him to keep up with quick attackers and recover swiftly when out of position. He excels in one-versus-one situations and demonstrates intelligent body positioning to neutralize threats. Although statistically his aerial duel success rate has been noted as mediocre in some analyses, he has shown improvement in his overall defensive engagement, having previously been described as occasionally rash. Tapsoba has cited John Stones, Per Mertesacker, and Virgil van Dijk as inspirations and has been compared to Jérôme Boateng for his speed, strength, passing ability, and composure. He has expressed a preference for playing in a back three, specifically on the left, believing it enhances the team's defensive security.

Career Highlights

Edmond Tapsoba's professional career has seen a rapid ascent through European football. After playing for various clubs in Burkina Faso, he moved to Portugal, making his Primeira Liga debut for Vitória SC in September 2018. His performances helped transform Vitória Guimarães into a top-six team before his move to Germany.

In January 2020, Tapsoba joined Bayer Leverkusen for a fee of €18 million, making his Bundesliga debut the following month. He scored his first Bundesliga goal for the club in February 2021. Tapsoba has been a consistent presence in European club competitions, playing a pivotal role in Leverkusen's UEFA Europa League group stage matches in 2020. On the international stage, he made his senior debut for Burkina Faso in June 2021 and was a key player in their run to the semi-finals of the 2021 Africa Cup of Nations, earning a spot in the tournament's best eleven. He continued to represent his nation at the 2023 and 2025 AFCON tournaments, scoring his first AFCON goal in 2025. Most notably, Tapsoba was an integral part of the Bayer Leverkusen squad that achieved a historic Bundesliga title and DFB Pokal victory in the 2023/2024 season. He also secured a DFL Super Cup win in 2024.

Personality

Edmond Tapsoba is generally described as a calm individual who maintains his composure in critical situations and prefers not to speak extensively. If annoyed, he tends to withdraw and prefers to discuss problems after the immediate event. He has taken on a mentoring role at Bayer Leverkusen, acting as an "older brother" to younger African players to help them adjust to life in Germany, drawing on his own experiences. During the COVID-19 pandemic, Tapsoba demonstrated his community spirit by donating masks, gloves, and hand sanitizer to a market in his hometown of Ouagadougou.

Condition / Medical History
