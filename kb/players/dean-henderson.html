Recent Form

Dean Henderson has established himself as Crystal Palace's undisputed first-choice goalkeeper and captain during the 2025-26 season, starting every Premier League match (27 appearances, 2,430 minutes played as of February 22, 2026). As of April 16, 2026, he has made 30 appearances in the Premier League, recording 88 saves and conceding 36 goals. He has also achieved 10 clean sheets in the Premier League this season. His performances have been consistently strong, including a penalty save in a recent victory against Wolverhampton Wanderers. Henderson's average FotMob rating for the 2025/2026 Premier League season is 7.15.

Beyond the league, Henderson played a pivotal role in Crystal Palace's FA Cup triumph in 2025, where he was named Player of the Match in the final against Manchester City for his heroic saves, including stopping a penalty. He also helped the club secure the Community Shield in 2025. Crystal Palace recently reached the semi-finals of the UEFA Conference League, with Henderson playing a key role in their aggregate victory over Fiorentina.

Tactical Profile

Dean Henderson is recognized for his strong shot-stopping abilities, quick reflexes, and commanding presence in goal. His background as an outfield player in his youth provides him with a distinct advantage in distribution, making him comfortable with the ball at his feet and capable of launching quick counter-attacks with precise throws and kicks. This skill set allows him to turn defensive situations into offensive opportunities for his team.

Henderson demonstrates good concentration on the ball, often seeing the play through to the end to make crucial saves, and is noted for his ability to respond effectively in crisis situations, such as dealing with second balls or being caught off balance. He also possesses strong leadership qualities, actively organizing and encouraging his defense through vocal communication. His penalty-saving ability is a notable strength, with a professional career save rate of approximately 57% on penalties.

Career Highlights

Henderson has accumulated several significant achievements throughout his career. He was a member of the England U-20 squad that won the FIFA U-20 World Cup in 2017. With Crystal Palace, he has won the FA Cup in the 2024-2025 season and the FA Community Shield in the 2025-2026 season. He was also part of the England senior team that finished as runners-up in UEFA Euro 2020 and UEFA Euro 2024.

Individually, Henderson has received recognition for his performances, including the PFA Team of the Year in League One (2017–18), the EFL Championship Golden Glove (2018–19), and the Sheffield United Young Player of the Year award twice (2018–19, 2019–20). He was also named Crystal Palace Player of the Month in September 2024. He has earned 4 senior caps for England.

Personality

Dean Henderson is known for his confidence and strong character, which have been evident throughout his career. He has been described as having an "immovable" confidence and a desire to be the best. His leadership qualities were formally recognized with his appointment as Crystal Palace club captain in 2026.

Henderson is also noted for his engaging personality and connection with fans. Following Crystal Palace's UEFA Conference League semi-final qualification in April 2026, he interrupted a post-match interview to lead supporters in a chant, showcasing his rapport with the fanbase. He has expressed a strong ambition to become England's number one goalkeeper, believing he has the form to achieve this goal.

Condition / Medical History

Dean Henderson has experienced several periods of absence due to injury and illness throughout his career. In the 2025-2026 season, he had an illness from March 14-30, 2026, and another illness from January 5-14, 2025. In the 2023-2024 season, he sustained a thigh injury that kept him sidelined for over two months, from September 27 to December 15, 2023, shortly after joining Crystal Palace.

Prior to this, he had a significant thigh injury during his loan spell at Nottingham Forest in the 2022-2023 season, which kept him out from January 17 to August 13, 2023. Other reported issues include illness in June 2021, and a hip injury that led to his withdrawal from the UEFA Euro 2020 squad. He also contracted COVID-19 in 2021, which developed into long COVID and affected his availability for Manchester United.
