Snapshot

A left-winger with genuinely dangerous acceleration and the ability to create space on the ball through explosive dribbling; he's direct, aggressive off the ball, and capable of beating a full-back in isolation. His decision-making in the final third can be rushed, and his crossing is inconsistent—he beats defenders with his feet but doesn't always convert the chance into a clear opportunity. A good Bundesliga starter with high upside, provided he sharpens his end product.

Tactical Profile

Leweling, primarily operating as a right winger, is noted for his versatility and ability to play with both feet. His playing style emphasizes short passes, shooting from distance, and delivering crosses. He also contributes defensively with high pressing.

As an inverted winger, Leweling exhibits strong dribbling capabilities and close control, allowing him to take on and beat defenders effectively. He is adept at carrying the ball into dangerous positions and creating scoring opportunities, drawing comparisons to Leroy Sané for his attacking versatility and knack for being in the right place at the right time in the box.

Career Highlights

Jamie Leweling began his professional career at Greuther Fürth, making his debut in the 2. Bundesliga in July 2019. He scored his first goal and assist for the club later that year and contributed to their promotion to the Bundesliga, scoring five goals in the 2021-2022 season. In 2022, he moved to Union Berlin, where he played a role in their historic fourth-place finish in the Bundesliga and qualification for the Champions League.

Leweling initially joined VfB Stuttgart on loan in July 2023, making the move permanent in July 2024 with a contract extended until June 2029. He scored his first goal for Stuttgart in January 2024 and has since become a key player. On the international stage, Leweling has represented Germany at various youth levels (U19, U20, U21) before making his senior national team debut in October 2024, scoring the winning goal against the Netherlands. He has since earned 4 caps and scored 1 goal for the senior German national team. Leweling also won the DFB Pokal with VfB Stuttgart in the 2024/2025 season.
