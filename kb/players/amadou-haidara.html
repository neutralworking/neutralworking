He has since begun to integrate into the Lens squad, making seven appearances in the 2025/2026 Ligue 1 season, contributing one goal. His goal came on March 8, 2026, in a 3-0 victory against Metz, where he scored shortly after being introduced at half-time.

Despite limited starting opportunities due to midfield competition, Haidara has shown efficiency and experience when coming off the bench, earning praise from Lens head coach Pierre Sage. His recent performances indicate a player steadily regaining rhythm. Tactical Profile

Amadou Haidara operates primarily as a central midfielder, embodying the "tuttocampista" role with his box-to-box energy and contributions in both defensive and attacking phases. He is a versatile player, capable of also performing as a defensive ball-winner, and has even been deployed in wing-back and full-back roles. His playing style is characterized by high energy, a strong work rate, and tenacity, enabling him to cover significant ground throughout a match.

Technically, Haidara possesses a good first touch, a reliable short passing range, and close control, allowing him to maintain possession effectively. He is known for his intelligence in finding space to receive progressive passes and for making runs from central midfield to wide areas, which helps create openings for his team. Defensively, he excels in aerial duels, blocking shots, tackling, and intercepting the ball. He thrives in pressing situations, particularly in the middle and final thirds, demonstrating an astute ability to anticipate when opponents are about to lose control of the ball. At Lens, his tactical flexibility has been utilized by dropping between central defenders during possession to facilitate higher positioning for full-backs, thereby maintaining defensive balance.

Career Highlights

Amadou Haidara began his European career with Red Bull Salzburg in July 2016, where he quickly made an impact. He won the UEFA Youth League with Salzburg U19 in the 2016/2017 season. During his time with the senior team, he secured three Austrian Bundesliga titles (2016/17, 2017/18, 2018/19) and two Austrian Cups (2016/17, 2018/19). He was also a key figure in Salzburg's impressive 2017-18 Europa League campaign, which saw them reach the semi-finals.

In January 2019, Haidara moved to RB Leipzig, where he spent seven successful years. He made 222 competitive appearances for the club, contributing 15 goals and 13 assists. With Leipzig, he won the DFB-Pokal twice (2021/2022, 2022/2023) and the DFL-Supercup in 2023/2024. He also played a significant role in Leipzig's five UEFA Champions League qualifications, including their run to the semi-finals in 2019/20. Internationally, Haidara has represented Mali at various youth levels and has earned 52 senior caps.

Personality

Amadou Haidara is recognized for his strong character and exemplary conduct, particularly noted during challenging periods in his career. Marcel Schäfer, RB Leipzig's Managing Director for Sport, highlighted his football intelligence and strength in tackles, as well as his respectful demeanor off the pitch, where he served as an important figure in the dressing room, especially for French-speaking players. Haidara is multilingual, fluent in German, French, and English.

His dedication to football is evident, as he describes it as his "whole life," appreciating environments like Leipzig that allow young players to focus and develop. Haidara also gives back to his home country through the Amadou Haidara Foundation, which supports charitable initiatives in Mali and invests in a youth academy to nurture local talent. He has openly expressed his admiration for Steven Gerrard, modeling his game after the former English midfielder.

Condition / Medical History

Amadou Haidara has experienced several injuries throughout his career, impacting his availability at various times. Most recently, he suffered a groin injury from March 19 to April 2, 2026. Prior to that, he was noted as lacking match fitness between January 15 and January 29, 2026.

In May 2025, while at RB Leipzig, he sustained a medial ligament injury in his left knee during training, which prematurely ended his 2024-25 season. However, he was expected to be available for the start of the 2025-26 pre-season. Other past injuries include muscle injuries, concussion, calf issues, hip problems, ankle injuries, and previous knee injuries. His initial season at RB Leipzig was also complicated by a prolonged injury.
