Snapshot

CD Sweeper Libero Dortmund GER B 83.6 Frank Beckenbauer, Dominant, Passing Range, giving in to his instincts as a former winger. He was often seen covering 30, 40 meters in a few seconds, topping it off by winning 1996 European Championship and the ‘Golden Ball’ as the best player in Europe 1996. He had to retire aged 30 in 1998 due to a long-going severe knee injury. Matthias Sammer is one of three defenders in history to win European footballer of the year. He early start as central midfielder. He was ranked in international-class 3.5 seasons in Bundesliga until the end of Germany in world cup 1994, moving down to play as attacking libero instead of Lothar Matthaeus, showing his fantastic vision when support attacking game. As libero, He reached world-class 2 seasons leading both Dortmund and Germany winning the biggest title of Europe. He was even compared as the same footage with Franz Beckenbauer. Unfortunately, Sammer was gotten a big injury leading to retirement eventually. All in all, His career as defensive was done only 3 years and this affect him could not be ranked among the best ever. But still, He was voted 4th FIFA world player of the year in 1996.
