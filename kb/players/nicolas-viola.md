## Snapshot

A deep-lying metronome in his mid-thirties who's earned his longevity through positional intelligence and an ability to recycle possession in tight spaces. Viola operates as a screen for Novara's back line with reliable short passing and decent press resistance, but his legs have gone enough that he's genuinely vulnerable in transition—you see him caught between lines, unable to cover the ground when turnovers happen in midfield. For a lower-table Serie A side, he's a serviceable steady-state operator, but he's not moving the needle in any direction anymore; this is squad depth football, the kind of player who keeps things ticking but won't create anything that wasn't already there.
