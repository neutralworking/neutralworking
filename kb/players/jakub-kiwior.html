Recent Form

Jakub Kiwior joined FC Porto on a season-long loan from Arsenal on September 1, 2025, with an obligation to buy. He quickly integrated into the squad, making his debut in a 1-0 home victory against C.D. Nacional in the Primeira Liga. As of April 2026, Kiwior has made 22 appearances for Porto during his loan spell.

His performances have been consistently strong, establishing him as a key figure in Porto's pursuit of the league title. In the 2025/2026 Liga Portugal season, he has accumulated 1,953 minutes of play, registered one assist, and maintained an average FotMob rating of 7.46, indicating a high level of performance for a centre-back in the league. His debut for Porto was particularly impressive, showcasing immediate quality and composure with a near-96% passing accuracy and a perfect duel record in that match.

Kiwior's significant impact has led to widespread expectation that FC Porto will activate the obligation to buy clause, making his transfer permanent by May 2026. He contributed to a crucial 3-1 victory over Estoril, further advancing Porto's title aspirations. While generally robust, he did experience a muscle injury from February 9 to February 27, 2026, which briefly sidelined him.

Tactical Profile

Kiwior operates primarily as a centre-back, but his versatility allows him to be deployed effectively as a left-back or even a defensive midfielder. Standing at 189 cm and naturally left-footed, he is known for his composed defensive play and ability to distribute the ball from the back. His tactical profile aligns with a "Conductor" archetype, demonstrating excellent ball control and passing range.

His playing style emphasizes short passes, contributing to build-up play with high accuracy, as evidenced by a near-96% passing completion rate in his Porto debut. He also possesses a "piercing" possessional presence, capable of executing effective long passes into channels. This ability to withstand pressure and confidently move the ball makes him an effective player in possession.

Defensively, Kiwior embodies a "Sweeper" role, characterized by strong defensive positioning, excellent tackling, and an intelligent reading of the game. He is comfortable managing the defensive line and has been praised for his ability to find solutions in challenging situations. His former manager at Spezia, Thiago Motta, frequently utilized him as a defensive midfielder due to his adeptness at possessing and controlling the ball.

Career Highlights

Jakub Kiwior's career has seen a steady progression through various European leagues. He made a significant move to Premier League club Arsenal in January 2023, in a deal reportedly worth £20 million, after making 39 appearances for Spezia. During his tenure at Arsenal, he accumulated 68 appearances and earned a Community Shield winners' medal in 2023.

Among his most notable performances for Arsenal were his "flawless" displays in both legs of the UEFA Champions League quarter-finals against Real Madrid in April 2025, where he started as a centre-back. He also scored his first goal for the club in a 5-0 victory over Wolverhampton Wanderers during the 2022-23 season and found the net in a 2-2 draw against Crystal Palace in April 2025.

On the international stage, Kiwior has been a consistent presence for Poland, representing his nation at all youth levels before making his senior debut on June 11, 2022. He was a key player for Poland in both the 2022 FIFA World Cup and Euro 2024, starting every match in both tournaments. He scored his inaugural international goal in a friendly match against Germany on June 16, 2023, and currently holds 43 caps and two goals for his country. His current loan move to FC Porto, with an obligation to buy for a fee that could reach €27 million, positions him as one of the most expensive central defenders in Portuguese football history.

Kiwior is married to Claudia Kowalczyk, a professional dancer, and they maintain a relatively private personal life.
