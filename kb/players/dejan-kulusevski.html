Dejan Kulusevski: Player Research Brief

Recent Form

Dejan Kulusevski has been sidelined for the majority of the 2025-2026 season due to a significant knee injury. He sustained a patella injury in May 2025 and underwent initial surgery, followed by a "small intervention" in March 2026 to clean up the joint after experiencing setbacks. As of April 2026, he has not played competitive football in the current season and is unlikely to return before its conclusion.

In his last full season, 2024-2025, Kulusevski was a key contributor for Tottenham Hotspur, recording 10 goals and 11 assists across all competitions, helping the club secure a historic Europa League trophy. In the Premier League during that season, he registered 7 goals and 4 assists in 32 appearances. He is currently focused on his rehabilitation with the aim of being fit to represent Sweden in the upcoming World Cup in summer 2026.

Tactical Profile

Kulusevski is a versatile and physically strong left-footed attacker, adept at playing various roles across midfield and attack, including as an attacking midfielder, box-to-box midfielder, or winger on either flank. His playing style emphasizes work rate, stamina, vision, and passing ability, allowing him to deliver assists and incisive through balls to teammates. He possesses good technique, dribbling skills, and striking ability, making him a dual threat for both scoring and creating chances.

His capacity to carry the ball forward at pace and drive his team up the pitch is a notable strength, often utilizing dummies and feints to bypass defenders. While primarily left-footed, he is comfortable using his weaker right foot, enabling him to cut inside for shots or crosses, or to take on players down the by-line. In the 2024-2025 season, he was increasingly deployed in more central attacking midfield roles (number 8 and number 10) by his manager, which reportedly enhanced his output and creativity from the middle of the park. Kulusevski also contributes defensively with a strong work ethic, actively engaging in pressing and winning back possession.

Career Highlights

Dejan Kulusevski has accumulated several significant individual and team accolades throughout his career. He was recognized as Serie A's Best Under-23 player in the 2019–2020 season. He has been named Swedish Midfielder of the Year in 2020, 2023, and 2024, and Swedish Forward of the Year in 2022. Kulusevski also won the prestigious Guldbollen (Swedish Player of the Year award) in both 2022 and 2023, becoming the youngest recipient since 1990 and only the second player to win it back-to-back after Zlatan Ibrahimovic and Victor Lindelof.

At club level, he won the Coppa Italia and Supercoppa Italiana with Juventus in the 2020–2021 season, notably scoring the opening goal in the Coppa Italia final. His breakthrough came during a loan spell at Parma in 2019-2020, where he registered 10 goals and 8 assists in Serie A, leading to his transfer to Juventus in January 2020. After joining Tottenham Hotspur on loan in January 2022, his move was made permanent in June 2023 for €30 million. With Tottenham, he was part of the squad that won the Europa League in the 2024-2025 season. He scored his first international goal for Sweden in November 2020.

He is known for his outgoing nature, ability to make quick decisions, and adaptability on the field. Interviews and interactions suggest he is practical and straightforward. Condition / Medical History

Dejan Kulusevski is currently recovering from a significant right patella (knee) injury sustained in May 2025, which has kept him out of competitive play for the entire 2025-2026 season. He underwent initial surgery for the injury in May 2025. In March 2026, he had a "small intervention" on his knee to address setbacks and clean the joint. While he is nearing a return to fitness, he is not expected to play before the end of the current campaign. Kulusevski has expressed optimism about his recovery and aims to be fit for the World Cup in summer 2026. His past medical history includes a foot injury (March-April 2025), muscle injuries (December 2022-January 2023, February 2020, January 2020), thigh injuries (September-November 2022, January-February 2020), and an illness (June 2021).
