Serviceable in a back-four that protects him with midfield cover, but exposed against fluid, possession-dominant opponents.

Alessio Romagnoli: Player Research Brief

Recent Form

Alessio Romagnoli has been a consistent presence in Lazio's defense during the 2025/2026 Serie A season, featuring in 27 matches and playing a total of 2250 minutes. He has started 25 of 31 possible matches for the club, underscoring his role as a regular starter. Across these appearances, the central defender has maintained a clean sheet ratio of 44%, contributing to 12 clean sheets.

While primarily a defender, Romagnoli has not registered any goals or assists in the current Serie A campaign. He has taken 7 shots, with 3 on target, resulting in a shooting accuracy of 42.86%. His disciplinary record shows 6 yellow cards and no red cards in Serie A, committing an average of 0.64 fouls per 90 minutes. He loses possession at a rate of 10.96% per game.

Tactical Profile

Romagnoli operates as a left-footed central defender, known for his strong passing, aerial duels, and concentration. He prefers to play short passes and is described as a ball-playing center-back who excels in a covering role. His tactical intelligence allows him to make timely interventions and anticipate opponent movements, often neutralizing dangerous attacking plays.

He is not characterized by physical dominance in duels and can face challenges against physically stronger or highly mobile opponents, particularly when the play moves behind him. Romagnoli's distribution is a notable strength, aligning with his "Conductor" archetype. While effective in reading the game when play is in front of him, his lack of exceptional pace means he is less suited to an aggressive, high-pressing defensive line.

Career Highlights

Alessio Romagnoli's career includes significant achievements at both club and international levels. With AC Milan, he secured a Serie A title in the 2021-22 season and won the Supercoppa Italiana in 2016-17. Earlier in his career, he also won the Coppa Italia Primavera with AS Roma U19 in 2011-12.

He has been a participant in both the UEFA Champions League and UEFA Europa League with AC Milan and Lazio. Romagnoli also earned 13 caps for the Italian national team between January 2016 and October 2023, scoring 2 goals, including his first senior international goal in October 2019. He was named captain of AC Milan at the start of the 2018-19 Serie A season.

Condition / Medical History

Romagnoli has experienced several injuries throughout his career. In the current 2025/2026 season, he has dealt with a thigh injury in November 2025 and a calf injury in March 2026. His most recent reported injury was a calf issue from March 12, 2026, to March 20, 2026.
