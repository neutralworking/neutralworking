Dávid Hancko is a commanding Slovak center-back who joined Atlético Madrid in July 2025, bringing leadership and defensive stability to the La Liga side. At 28 years old, he is known for his left-footed play and stands at 188 cm tall. Hancko's internal archetype is a Conductor, and his internal role is a Distributor, reflecting his ability to play out from the back and influence the game from defense.

Recent Form

In the 2025-26 season with Atlético Madrid, Dávid Hancko has made 26 appearances across all competitions, including La Liga and the Champions League. While he has yet to score a goal, he has contributed two assists, demonstrating his involvement in offensive plays. His statistical performance includes 19 shots with 5 on target, and he has received two yellow cards this season. Hancko's passing accuracy in the UEFA Champions League for 2025/26 stands at an impressive 89.55%, further supporting his role as a distributor from the back. He has maintained an average FotMob rating of 6.96 in La Liga, scoring highly in assists, minutes played, and starts compared to other center-backs in the league.

Tactical Profile

Hancko is a versatile defender capable of playing both as a center-back and a left-back, a quality that has been particularly utilized in the Slovakian national team where he often operates as a left-back. His playing style is characterized by strength in duels, intelligent positioning, and a notable ability to distribute the ball from defense. This capacity to play out from the back aligns with his "Distributor" role, allowing him to initiate attacks and maintain possession. His proactive nature and consistent quality are evident regardless of his specific defensive assignment.

Career Highlights

Hancko's transfer to Atlético Madrid in July 2025 marked a significant step in his career, following a successful tenure with Feyenoord. He scored his first goal for Atlético Madrid on December 9, 2025, in a Champions League match against PSV Eindhoven. Throughout his career, Hancko has accumulated five titles, including the Super Cup (2024/2025), Eredivisie (2022/2023), and KNVB Cup (2023/2024) with Feyenoord, the Czech Cup (2019/2020) with Sparta Prague, and the 1. liga (2016/2017) with Zilina. He played a crucial role in Feyenoord's domestic success, notably winning the 2022-23 Eredivisie title. Recognized early in his career, UEFA included him on its list of most promising talents in 2018.

Personality

Dávid Hancko is noted for his resilience and consistency, qualities that have defined his career trajectory. Off the pitch, he leads a grounded and family-oriented life, being married to Czech tennis player Kristýna Plíšková, with whom he has a son. Despite his achievements, Hancko has openly expressed humility, even admitting to experiencing "imposter syndrome" before significant matches, such as facing Real Madrid. He has also publicly stated his desire to remain with Atlético Madrid, expressing his wish to renew his contract and stay with the club long-term.

Condition / Medical History

Hancko has demonstrated significant resilience throughout his career, having overcome two broken legs as a teenager and managing knee problems early on. His recent medical history includes a thigh injury in June 2024 and a knee injury in mid-2020. More recently, he was sidelined due to personal reasons in October 2025 and missed a league round due to disciplinary points. As of April 2026, he has been listed with an ankle injury and missed recent matches against Barcelona and Sevilla.
