Kai Havertz, the 26-year-old German international, currently operates as a center-forward for Arsenal. Standing at 193 cm and preferring his left foot, Havertz is known for his role as a Falso Nove, a position he has embraced and excelled in.

Recent Form

Kai Havertz has experienced a challenging but impactful 2025-2026 season, marked by a return from significant injuries. After tearing his hamstring in February 2025, which sidelined him for the remainder of the 2024-25 season, he then sustained a knee injury shortly after the opening match of the 2025-26 season, keeping him out for several months. He made his first start in nearly a year on January 29, 2026, contributing a goal and an assist in a Champions League victory against Kairat Almaty.

Since his return, Havertz has shown flashes of his quality, including a crucial 97th-minute winner against his former club Chelsea on February 3, 2026, securing Arsenal's place in the Carabao Cup final. He also scored an 89th-minute penalty to equalize in a 1-1 UEFA Champions League draw against Bayer Leverkusen on March 11, 2026. While his Premier League statistics for the 2025-26 season currently stand at 0 goals and 1 assist in 318 minutes, he has recorded 3 goals and 1 assist in 5 UEFA Champions League appearances (136 minutes). His recent performances in March and April 2026 include a goal against Sporting CP in a 1-0 Champions League win and another goal in a 2-1 international friendly victory against Ghana.

Tactical Profile

Havertz, primarily deployed as a center-forward or attacking midfielder, has found a defined role as a striker and focal point in Arsenal's attack under Mikel Arteta, a transition from his initial deployment as a left-sided central midfielder. He thrives in a false nine role, a position he also favored at Chelsea, utilizing his intelligence to drop deep and create space for teammates, while also making intelligent attacking runs into congested areas. His physical attributes, including his height and aerial presence, have made him increasingly effective as a traditional striker, particularly in winning aerial duels and acting as a target man.

His playing style is characterized by a blend of technical skill and tactical awareness. Havertz possesses excellent dribbling ability, close control, and an outstanding first touch, allowing him to navigate tight spaces and manipulate the ball effectively. His vision and accurate passing contribute to creating scoring opportunities for himself and others. Furthermore, Havertz is a significant contributor defensively, demonstrating intense pressing, winning duels, and effectively regaining possession high up the pitch, aligning with Arteta's philosophy of defending from the front. He has expressed that he sees himself as a number 9 for both club and country, emphasizing his enjoyment of making deep runs rather than being a static presence in the box.

Career Highlights

Kai Havertz has accumulated several significant achievements throughout his career. He holds the distinction of being the youngest player to reach both 50 and 100 league appearances in the German top flight, and was also Bayer Leverkusen's youngest-ever debutant and goalscorer in the Bundesliga.

His time at Chelsea saw him secure major European and global titles, including scoring the winning goal in the 2021 UEFA Champions League final against Manchester City, a moment he described as surreal. He also netted the winning goals in the 2021 UEFA Super Cup and the 2021 FIFA Club World Cup. After joining Arsenal in June 2023 for £65 million, Havertz continued to make an impact, becoming the first German player to score in four consecutive Premier League games in March 2024. He concluded his first season at Arsenal (2023-24) with a personal best of 20 goal contributions in the Premier League, comprising 13 goals and 7 assists. He has also represented Germany in multiple major tournaments, including UEFA Euro 2020, the 2022 FIFA World Cup, and UEFA Euro 2024, scoring two goals in each.

Condition / Medical History

Havertz has faced notable injury setbacks in recent seasons. In February 2025, he sustained a hamstring tear during training, which necessitated surgery and kept him out for the remainder of the 2024-25 season. Following his return, he suffered a knee injury shortly after the opening match of the 2025-26 season in August 2025, leading to several months on the sidelines until December 2025. He also experienced a muscle injury in February 2026. These injuries meant he missed the majority of the 2025-26 campaign, and upon his return in January, he has been in the process of regaining full match fitness.
