Life keeps getting better for the teenager who was born in Fulda in central Germany to a German mother and Nigerian father. Having left Chelsea’s academy last summer, Musiala progressed swiftly through Bayern’s age-group sides and has been part of Hansi Flick’s first-team squad since the restart, becoming the youngest player to make a Bundesliga appearance for the club against Freiburg in June. The elegant attacking midfielder – who moved to England at the age of seven and attended Whitgift School in Croydon – surpassed Roque Santa Cruz as Bayern’s youngest-ever goalscorer in the 8-0 demolition of Schalke in September and has been earmarked by his manager as a potential star of the future for club and country. Whether that will be England or Germany is still yet to be resolved, however, with Musiala having represented both at youth level and struck up a close friendship with Borussia Dortmund’s Jude Bellingham on international duty. “I would certainly try to get him,” said Flick. “But it is his decision. Tactical Profile

Jamal Musiala operates primarily as an attacking midfielder but is recognized for his exceptional versatility, capable of playing as a central midfielder, attacking winger, or even a false nine. His playing style is characterized by outstanding technical skills, dribbling ability, and vision. He excels at navigating tight spaces, beating defenders in one-on-one situations with quick acceleration, close ball control, and agility. Musiala's composure, intelligent movement to find space, and precise passing in congested areas are key attributes that allow him to dictate tempo and create scoring opportunities.

Beyond his offensive prowess, Musiala possesses a strong defensive work rate, balancing his attacking creativity with effective pressing and ball recovery. He is adept at applying pressure on opponents, forcing errors, and contributing to turnovers in dangerous zones. His tactical intelligence enables him to read the game, make swift decisions, and adapt to various tactical systems, making him a valuable asset in both transitional play and breaking down compact defenses.

Career Highlights

Jamal Musiala has amassed an impressive collection of team and individual honors early in his career. With Bayern Munich, he has secured multiple Bundesliga titles (2019–20, 2020–21, 2021–22, 2022–23, 2024–25), DFL-Supercups (2020, 2021, 2022), the UEFA Super Cup (2020), FIFA Club World Cup (2020), and the UEFA Champions League (2019–20).

Individually, Musiala has been recognized as the German national Player of the Year twice (2022, 2024) and has been named in the Bundesliga Team of the Season multiple times (2022–23, 2023–24, 2024–25). He holds the distinction of being Bayern's youngest goalscorer in the UEFA Champions League (February 2021) and the youngest player to reach 100 Bundesliga appearances for the club (aged 20 in the 2023/24 season). Notably, he scored crucial title-clinching goals for Bayern in both the 2021/22 and 2022/23 Bundesliga campaigns, including an 89th-minute winner on the final day of the 2022/23 season. For the German national team, Musiala has earned 40 caps, scoring 8 goals, and was the joint top scorer and named in the Team of the Tournament at UEFA Euro 2024.
