Recent Form

Daniel Ballard has been a pivotal figure in Sunderland's recent success, playing a crucial role in their promotion to the Premier League at the end of the 2024-25 season. He notably scored the winning goal in extra time during the EFL Championship play-off semi-finals against Coventry City, a moment considered one of the most iconic at the Stadium of Light, and earned two man-of-the-match awards across both legs before Sunderland secured promotion by defeating Sheffield United in the final.

In the current 2025-26 Premier League season, Ballard has continued to feature prominently, making 26 appearances and scoring 2 goals, accumulating 2,007 minutes on the pitch. His performances have been recognized, including an average FotMob rating of 7.03, and he was named in the Premier League Team of the Week in August 2025. His consistent contributions were further acknowledged with a new contract extension until the summer of 2029, with an option for an additional year.

Tactical Profile

As a right-footed center-back, Daniel Ballard is recognized for his robust defensive capabilities and leadership qualities. He is described as a "tough-tackling defender" who also possesses the "passing range of a midfielder," indicating a well-rounded skill set for his position. His aerial prowess is a significant asset, evident in his goal-scoring contributions from set-pieces.

Ballard has established himself as one of the most dominant defenders in the Premier League, contributing to Sunderland earning eight clean sheets in 27 league games during the current campaign, alongside registering four top-flight goal contributions. His ability to influence games from the back, coupled with his physical attributes and technical skills, aligns with the internal archetype of a 'Titan' and the role of a 'Stopper'.

Career Highlights

Ballard's career has seen significant milestones, including his instrumental role in Sunderland's promotion to the Premier League in the 2024-25 season. He scored on his Premier League debut for Sunderland in a 3-0 victory over West Ham United on the opening matchday of the 2025-26 season. Prior to his permanent move to Sunderland in 2022, he gained valuable experience through loan spells, notably winning a League One Play-Off winners' medal with Blackpool and also playing for Millwall.

Internationally, Ballard represents Northern Ireland, having made his senior debut in September 2020 in a Nations League game against Romania. He has accumulated 33 caps for his country, scoring 5 goals. Earlier in his career, he was part of the Arsenal youth system, winning the FA Cup in 2019-20 (though without making an appearance) and the Premier League 2 with Arsenal U23 in 2017-18.

Condition / Medical History

Daniel Ballard has experienced a notable history of injuries throughout his career. In the current 2025-26 season, he has faced several setbacks, including muscle injuries in March 2026 and December 2024, and an ankle injury in December 2025. He also sustained a knee injury in June 2024, which sidelined him for 47 days, and a hamstring injury in February 2025 that led to an absence of 68 days.

Earlier in his career, a severe knee injury cut short his loan spell at Swindon Town in 2019, requiring five months of recovery. Other past injuries include a broken foot in the 2022-23 season, which kept him out for 132 days, and knee surgery in the 2021-22 season, resulting in an 89-day absence. He has also missed international fixtures for Northern Ireland due to injury in September 2023, October 2024, and August 2025.

Personality

Daniel Ballard is regarded as a professional, consistent, and spirited individual, traits that have contributed to him becoming a leader within the Sunderland dressing room. He has expressed a strong sense of pride in representing his country, Northern Ireland, and has cited players like Jonny Evans and Gareth McAuley as figures he looks up to. His commitment to the club was evident in his decision to extend his contract, stating his delight and desire to continue improving and helping Sunderland progress.
