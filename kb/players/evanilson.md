Evanilson operates primarily as a center-forward, serving as a direct replacement for Dominic Solanke at Bournemouth. His playing style is characterized by strong goalscoring instincts within the box, combined with an athletic and hard-working approach that includes aggressive pressing from the front. He is well-suited to manager Andoni Iraola's tactical system, providing a crucial focal point in possession and a consistent goal threat. Described as a "Raumdeuter" and "Shadow Striker," Evanilson excels in off-the-ball movement, frequently drifting into channels and exploiting pockets of space between defenders or behind the last man.

His exceptional movement is a key asset, as he remains alert during build-up play, takes clever positions to evade markers, and makes penetrative, vertical runs, often darting in behind opposition central defenders. Although a low-touch striker, averaging around 24.66 touches per 90 minutes, his industriousness is evident in his frequent sprints and ability to regain possession in the final third. Evanilson is naturally right-footed but is also highly accomplished with his left foot, allowing him versatility in his finishing and play. He predominantly takes shots from inside the penalty area, contributing to a high expected goals (xG) rate.

## Career Highlights

A product of Fluminense's youth academy, Evanilson made his senior debut in 2018 and notably scored a brace in his first start for the club in 2019. In September 2020, he moved to Porto for approximately £7.5 million, where he quickly made an impact by scoring the winning goal on his debut. During his four years at Porto, Evanilson achieved significant success, winning five major trophies, including the Primeira Liga title in the 2021-22 season as part of a domestic double. His individual performances were recognized in January 2022 when he scored a hat-trick and was named both the Primeira Liga Goal of the Month and Forward of the Month.

Evanilson joined Bournemouth in August 2024 for a club-record fee of £40.2 million. He scored his first goal for the club on September 30, 2024, and notably contributed to a historic 2-1 victory over Manchester City on November 2, 2024, scoring Bournemouth's second goal and ending City's 32-game unbeaten league run. In 2024, he earned his first call-up to the Brazil senior national team and was included in the squad for the Copa América.
