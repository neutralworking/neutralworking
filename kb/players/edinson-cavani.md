## Snapshot

A late-career resurrection that's genuinely moving to watch—the man still understands positioning and movement in tight spaces with the clarity of someone who's terrorized defences for two decades. His pressing remains intelligent, he still finds pockets in the box, and his heading is a genuine threat from set pieces. But the acceleration is gone, the explosive first step that made him lethal on the turn has withered, and he cannot drive a match when it demands mobility or sustained intensity; at 39, he's a finisher, not a creator, and teams need to service him constantly or he becomes a passenger.
