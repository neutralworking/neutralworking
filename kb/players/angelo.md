## Snapshot

ClubSantos

PositionForward

Born21 December 2004

The best way to explain how good Ângelo is probably through the stats and records. He is the youngest goalscorer in the history of Copa Libertadores, at the age of 16 years, four months and 16 days, breaking a record which had been held by Juan Carlos Cardena since 1962. At the age of 15 years, 10 months and four days, he also became the second youngest player to make his Santos debut, beating even the king Pelé. Santos have a history of producing stars as the careers of Neymar, Robinho and Rodrygo have proved. Born in Brasília, Ângelo was picked up by the club when he was nine. His style is similar to Rodrygo, mixing technique and a great power when dribbling. “He is a guy that breathes football and I think we have an atmosphere that will help this development,” says the Santos under-17 assistant coach, Bruno Silva. “It’s easy to find legends of our club like Lima and Clodoaldo visiting Vila Belmiro. The Neymar effect is still present here.” **[Thiago Rabelo](https://twitter.com/thiagorabelo1?lang=en)**  
  
**September 2022 update** Progress has stalled somewhat, with Santos' six managers in two years making it impossible to have a settled team. After a hugely positive 2021 Ângelo has been struggling to get into the starting XI recently.  
  
**October 2023 update** Struggled for goals with Santos – scoring only two in the first half of the season – but did enough to catch the attention of Chelsea, who signed him for €15m in August. Will spend the 2023-24 season on loan at Strasbourg, the French club Chelsea bought a majority stake in in June who are managed by Patrick Vieira.
