Raw scouting narrative

Lewis Hall

Chelsea lost both games - but Hall attracted praise for his composure, willingness to contribute at both ends, and quality with the ball. 

Though his first-team experience has mostly come at LB, Hall predominantly played as a midfielder during his academy days, with experience further forward too.

As you’d expect from a Cobham graduate, he’s technically brilliant. He’s slick in tight spaces, and able to receive under pressure. 

He scans a lot, and uses his body well to protect the ball, and then uses his close control to shift it quickly. His passing range is equally as impressive, with a good level of general consistency with his weight of pass, but also with the ability to play more ambitious passes too. His big switches are strong, and his pass selection in the final third is very good. 

Hall’s acceleration - both with and without the ball - is such a vital part of his game. 

In possession, he uses that acceleration to often drive away from danger, and really thrives at driving with the ball inside into central spaces.

This ball-carrying is part of what has allowed Hall to seamlessly play in a wider role, as a fullback, wingback or winger. His 1v1 dribbling is good on both feet, and it’s rare to see a natural CM excel at that at such a young age.

Perhaps the biggest point of uniqueness for Hall in comparison to other MFs though, is his crossing. 

Throughout youth and senior football, the consistency, quality and pace of his deliveries has been superb, both in open play and from set-pieces.
