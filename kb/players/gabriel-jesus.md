## Tactical Profile

Gabriel Jesus is a highly versatile forward, capable of operating across the front line as a center forward, out-and-out striker, false nine, inside forward, or winger. He is recognized for his technical proficiency, dribbling ability, creativity, movement, and strong work ethic. His tactical contributions extend beyond goal-scoring, as he is known for his energetic pressing and defensive contributions, ranking among the top center-forwards in Europe for tackles in the final third.

Jesus excels in one-on-one situations, utilizing his pace and power to beat defenders, and demonstrates an excellent perception of space to find advantageous positions. While he can play as a traditional number nine, his effectiveness is often highlighted when he operates as a false nine, dropping deep to link play and create space for wide attackers. He prefers playing off the flank, where he can target the space between defenders and deliver low, driven crosses. However, his decision-making and end product can be less effective if he takes more than three touches, suggesting he performs best when acting on instinct.

## Career Highlights

Gabriel Jesus has amassed a significant collection of trophies throughout his career. With Manchester City, he secured four Premier League titles, four EFL Cups, and one FA Cup. He also contributed to Manchester City's Community Shield victories in 2018 and 2019.

On the international stage, Jesus won the Copa América with Brazil in 2019 and earned an Olympic gold medal at the 2016 Summer Olympics with the Brazil U23 team. He was Brazil's highest scorer in the CONMEBOL World Cup qualifiers, netting 7 goals in 10 matches. Before his move to England, he won the Campeonato Brasileiro Série A and the Copa do Brasil with Palmeiras. Jesus also holds the distinction of being the first Arsenal player since Marouane Chamakh in 2011 to score in his first three consecutive UEFA Champions League matches.
