Snapshot

Joshua Kimmich 

Joshua Kimmich tried to take the lead but often wanted too much. He was the central build-up player in Bayern's game, as central defense lacked strong players. The 28-year-old took responsibility as a penalty taker and was involved in important goals. In terms of football, he is indisputably one of the best sixes in Germany. In the national team, however, he did not live up to the increasing responsibility and is one of the faces of the DFB crisis.

Joshua Kimmich: Midfield Maestro with Unyielding Drive

Joshua Kimmich, the 31-year-old German international and Bayern Munich's defensive midfielder, continues to be a pivotal figure for both club and country. Known for his "Heartbeat" archetype and "Regista" role, Kimmich consistently demonstrates his influence through exceptional passing, tactical intelligence, and formidable leadership. Standing at 177 cm and preferring his right foot, he remains a cornerstone in midfield.

Recent Form

Joshua Kimmich has maintained a high level of performance for Bayern Munich during the 2025-2026 season, featuring in every Bundesliga and Champions League match prior to an international break in November 2025. Under coach Vincent Kompany, he has thrived in a deeper midfield role, significantly enhancing Bayern's offensive play. His contributions in the 2024/25 season included 71 key passes, making him joint-highest in the league, alongside five assists.

Kimmich's passing accuracy remains a hallmark of his game, with a 93% completion rate and an impressive 91% success rate for passes made under pressure, underscoring his press resistance. He also marked his 100th appearance for the German national team in June 2025, contributing an assist. However, his season was interrupted by an ankle injury sustained in November 2025 during national team training, which sidelined him for several matches in late 2025 and early 2026, including Bayern's first competitive fixture of the new year. By January 2026, he was progressing well in rehabilitation, having resumed sprinting.

Tactical Profile

Joshua Kimmich primarily operates as a defensive midfielder, a position he favors and where he can best leverage his strengths as a deep-lying playmaker. His versatility is a key attribute, having also competently filled roles as a right-back and occasionally as a center-back, showcasing his tactical intelligence and ability to adapt to various team requirements. This adaptability allows him to understand and execute the responsibilities of different roles within the overall tactical framework.

As a Regista, Kimmich orchestrates play from the midfield with exceptional vision and a broad passing range, capable of delivering precise whipped crosses and incisive long balls that exploit defensive gaps. His elite decision-making in possession and off-the-ball movement are crucial for initiating attacks and creating scoring opportunities. Defensively, he is characterized by an aggressive approach to winning the ball and excellent positional awareness, contributing significantly to defensive stability and covering substantial ground during matches. He often drops deep to facilitate build-up play, linking the defensive line with the midfield.

Career Highlights

Joshua Kimmich has amassed an impressive collection of honors throughout his career with Bayern Munich, including nine Bundesliga titles, the UEFA Champions League in the 2019–2020 season, and three DFB-Pokal trophies. He was an integral part of the historic sextuple-winning Bayern team in 2020, which also secured the UEFA Super Cup and FIFA Club World Cup. Additionally, he has lifted seven DFL-Supercups with the club.

On the international stage, Kimmich has been a consistent performer for Germany, winning the FIFA Confederations Cup in 2017 and the UEFA European Under-19 Championship in 2014. He made his senior national team debut in 2016 and was subsequently named in the UEFA European Championship Team of the Tournament that same year. His individual accolades include being selected for the UEFA Team of the Year in 2019–2020 and multiple inclusions in the UEFA Champions League Squad of the Season. He was also recognized as Germany's national team Player of the Year in 2017.

Personality

Joshua Kimmich is widely regarded as a "mentality monster," known for his "unstoppable mentality" and unwavering drive on the pitch. He is a vocal leader who constantly directs his teammates and embodies an unyielding desire to win, setting a high standard through his relentless effort and composure in high-pressure situations. This leadership style inspires those around him and fosters a culture of accountability.

Beyond his intense on-field presence, Kimmich demonstrates a strong sense of social responsibility. In March 2020, he co-founded the "WeKickCorona" initiative with teammate Leon Goretzka, making a significant personal donation to aid in the fight against the coronavirus pandemic and encouraging widespread contributions. This commitment highlights his role model status and leadership qualities extending beyond football.

Condition / Medical History

Joshua Kimmich sustained an ankle injury in November 2025 during training with the German national team, which was diagnosed as a capsule and ligament issue in his right ankle. This injury led to his absence from a 2026 World Cup qualifier and several Bayern Munich matches in late 2025 and early 2026. By January 2026, Kimmich was reportedly making good progress in his rehabilitation, having started running exercises and sprints, though his full return to team training and match play was still pending. He also experienced another ankle injury in December 2025-January 2026 and a thigh injury in February-March 2025.
