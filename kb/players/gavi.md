“It’s ahead of time? Maybe, yeah, I won’t deny that,” Luis Enrique said, but the Spain manager insisted he was still convinced when he called up Gavi to the senior squad to face Italy in October – even though he had only just turned 17 and played just 289 minutes in the Barcelona first team. “When you see him play it surprises you,” Luis Enrique said, “and in the three or four times he has played, he has left me with no doubt at all that he can be a very important player for the future not just for Barcelona but also the national team.” Luis Enrique was right of course and Gavi became the youngest ever Spaniard to represent La Roja at 17 years and 62 days as he not only started but impressed in the 2-1 Nations League win. Luis Enrique had watched Gavi in the Barcelona youth system, which he joined from Betis aged 11 having led them to a 5-0 win over Barça, that according to the national team manager he is the “standard bearer” at La Masia, an inside midfielder, and that one member of the technical staff at the Spanish federation says he is the star of this generation – “very much a Barcelona profile, but with the physicality of the modern game too, intense and with a tremendous character”.
