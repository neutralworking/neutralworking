Alexis Mac Allister, a 27-year-old right-footed central midfielder from Argentina, currently plays for Liverpool. Standing at 176 cm, he is recognized internally as a "Connector" with a "Mezzala" role.

Recent Form

Alexis Mac Allister's debut season at Liverpool in 2023/24 was highly successful, featuring in 46 games across all competitions, where he contributed 7 goals and 7 assists over 3,500 minutes. Despite often playing out of his preferred position, he was considered Liverpool's second-best player and earned the club's Goal of the Season award for a stunning 30-yard strike against Fulham. His strong performances also saw him named Liverpool's Player of the Month for March.

In the 2024/25 season, Mac Allister continued to be a consistent starter under new manager Arne Slot. He made his UEFA Champions League debut in September, scoring his first goal in the competition in October against Bologna, and was named Man of the Match after scoring against Real Madrid in November. By December, he had also provided two assists against Leicester City. As of early 2026, he had scored three goals from midfield, including a brace against Qarabag.

Tactical Profile

Mac Allister is a highly versatile midfielder, capable of operating as a deep-lying playmaker, an advanced creator, a box-to-box midfielder, or an attacking midfielder (No. 10). His playing style is characterized by a strong understanding of space, intelligent off-the-ball movement, and the ability to control the pace of the game with accurate passes. He possesses excellent technical precision, allowing for seamless ball distribution even under pressure, and a remarkable first touch.

Beyond his offensive contributions, Mac Allister is also effective defensively, demonstrating a strong work ethic, tracking back, making key interceptions, and winning back possession. He is known for his counter-pressing with controlled aggression and his ability to carry the ball effectively into the final third. While his finishing and aerial duels are noted as areas for improvement, his strengths include long-range shooting and concentration. At Liverpool, he has adapted to various roles, including a "lone five" (holding midfielder) under Jürgen Klopp, and is now asked to maintain position more under Arne Slot, though the core defensive principles remain consistent.

Career Highlights

Alexis Mac Allister has achieved significant success at both club and international levels. With Argentinos Juniors, he won the 2016–17 Argentine Second Division. During his time at Brighton & Hove Albion, he became a key player, was the club's top scorer with 10 goals in the 2022-23 season, and helped them qualify for the UEFA Europa League, earning him the Brighton Player of the Season award. A notable moment was his 99th-minute penalty winner against Manchester United.

His move to Liverpool saw him continue his trophy-winning ways, securing the 2023–24 EFL Cup and the 2024–25 Premier League title. On the international stage, Mac Allister has been a vital part of the Argentina national team's recent successes. He won the 2020 CONMEBOL Pre-Olympic Tournament with the U23 squad. With the senior team, he was instrumental in winning the 2022 Finalissima, the 2022 FIFA World Cup, and the 2024 Copa América. During the 2022 World Cup, he started six games, including the final, scored his first international goal against Poland, and assisted Ángel Di María's goal in the final.

Personality

Alexis Mac Allister is identified as an INFJ personality type and an Enneagram Type 1w9, suggesting a thoughtful and principled individual. He is described as a quick thinker with strong intuition and empathy, particularly effective in high-pressure situations. Mac Allister is seen as a natural leader, possessing self-assurance, a personable demeanor, and a strong sense of justice. He is generally introverted and reflective, carefully considering his words, and values both independence and group cohesion. His personal philosophy is encapsulated by his statement, "I play like I'm out on the street, fighting for the ball as if it were my last chance". He is also noted for his ability to calm and relax his teammates, and for his honest and collected approach when discussing challenging team performances.

Condition / Medical History

Alexis Mac Allister has experienced some fitness challenges, particularly in the lead-up to the 2025-26 season. He missed the final two games of the 2024-25 campaign and early pre-season friendlies due to an injury described as a combination of "two or three different things" that had troubled him for months. By July 2025, he declared himself "feeling good" and focused on regaining sharpness for the upcoming season. His pre-season in 2025 was disrupted, leading to careful management of his workload by Liverpool's coaching staff. By December 2025, he had completed 90 minutes in three consecutive matches for the first time since April. In September 2025, he played a full 90 minutes for Argentina despite prior fitness concerns from his national team manager. His injury history also includes a knee injury in December 2023, various groin issues (July 2025, August 2024, July 2022), an ankle injury (September 2025, 2025), and previous back, muscle, and illness-related absences.
