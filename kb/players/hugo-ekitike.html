Hugo Ekitike: Player Research Brief

Recent Form

Hugo Ekitike has made a significant impact since joining Liverpool in July 2025 for a reported €95 million (£82.4 million) from Eintracht Frankfurt. In the current 2025/2026 Premier League season, he has registered 11 goals and 4 assists across 28 appearances, accumulating 1,810 minutes on the pitch, and holds an average FotMob rating of 7.08. His total goal contributions for Liverpool in the 2025/2026 season stand at 15 (11 goals and 4 assists), placing him as the squad's leading contributor ahead of established players like Mohamed Salah and Luis Díaz. Across all competitions this season, Ekitike has amassed 17 goals and 6 assists in 44 appearances.

Prior to his move to Liverpool, Ekitike enjoyed a highly successful 2024-2025 season at Eintracht Frankfurt. During this period, he scored 15 goals in 33 league games, contributing to Frankfurt's 3rd-place finish in the Bundesliga and earning a spot in the 2024–25 Bundesliga Team of the Season.

Tactical Profile

Operating as a center-forward, Hugo Ekitike embodies a "Virtuoso" archetype with a "Falso Nove" role, demonstrating a blend of traditional striker attributes and playmaking capabilities. Despite his 190 cm height, which might suggest a target man, his playing style emphasizes intelligence and technical skill over raw pace to disrupt defensive structures. Under Arne Slot's tactics at Liverpool, Ekitike functions as a "Gravity Striker," strategically positioning himself to occupy opposition center-backs, thereby creating crucial space for inverted wingers and overlapping full-backs.

His tactical versatility is evident in his heat map, which shows a notable presence in Zone 14 and the left half-space, indicating his tendency to drop deeper to overload midfield areas. Ekitike is integral to Liverpool's "up, back, and through" attacking sequences, adept at receiving the ball under pressure, shielding it effectively, and executing precise one-touch lay-offs to advancing midfielders. His impressive 82% pass accuracy in the final third highlights his playmaking ability, making him a crucial "connector" in Liverpool's possession-oriented system.

Career Highlights

Ekitike began his professional career at Reims at the age of 18. He gained early experience through loan spells at Vejle Boldklub and Paris Saint-Germain (PSG) between 2021 and 2022. During his time at PSG, he secured a Ligue 1 title in the 2022-23 season, contributing three goals and four assists in 25 appearances. His transfer to PSG was made permanent in 2023 for €28.5 million.

In February 2024, Ekitike moved to Eintracht Frankfurt on loan, with the club activating a buy option in April 2024 for €16.5 million. His full season at Frankfurt in 2024-25 was particularly successful, where he scored 15 goals in 33 league games, helped the team achieve a 3rd-place Bundesliga finish, and was included in the Bundesliga Team of the Season. He subsequently joined Liverpool in July 2025 for a significant transfer fee. On the international stage, Ekitike has represented France at U20 and U21 levels, notably scoring five goals in five appearances for the U21 squad, and has made 8 appearances for the senior France national team, scoring 2 goals.

Personality

Hugo Ekitike is described as a "quiet, chilled character" off the pitch, who values his home life and family. He is known to be a fan of NFL, basketball, and French rap music. Ekitike maintains two separate Instagram accounts: one dedicated solely to his football career and another for personal expression, a deliberate choice to manage his public image and protect his private life from the intense scrutiny that comes with being a high-profile footballer. He possesses a calm and level demeanor, often playing without appearing to feel excessive pressure, and approaches life with a focus on enjoyment and achieving his goals. During his time at Vejle, he was remembered for his infectious smile and his efforts to encourage teammates to engage in social activities.

Condition / Medical History

Hugo Ekitike recently sustained a severe injury, rupturing his Achilles tendon on April 14, 2026, during a Champions League quarter-final match against Paris Saint-Germain. This injury is expected to sideline him for the remainder of the current season and potentially until 2027, ruling him out of the upcoming World Cup. The typical recovery timeline for an Achilles rupture is approximately nine months.

Throughout his career, Ekitike has experienced several other injuries, including hamstring issues in 2022, 2023, 2025, and 2026, knee injuries in 2024 and 2025, a back injury in 2025, hip, leg, and groin injuries in 2024, and thigh problems in 2025 and 2026.
