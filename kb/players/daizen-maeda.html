Daizen Maeda is a 28-year-old right-footed winger for Celtic and the Japan national team, known for his "Raumdeuter" archetype and "Wide Playmaker" internal role. Standing at 173 cm, Maeda's career has been marked by relentless energy and significant goal contributions.

Recent Form

Daizen Maeda experienced a highly prolific 2024-25 season, scoring over 30 goals, with his performance significantly improving from January onwards. During this period, he netted four goals in the UEFA Champions League against prominent clubs like Slovan Bratislava, Borussia Dortmund, Club Brugge, and Bayern Munich, matching the record for most Champions League goals by a Japanese player. His exceptional form led to his nomination for the PFA Scotland Players' Player of the Year and inclusion in the PFA Scotland Team of the Year for 2024-25. He also achieved a notable feat in April 2025, becoming the first Celtic player since Henrik Larsson in 2004 to score in eight consecutive starts at Parkhead.

However, the 2025-26 season has seen a slight dip in his overall performance, with reports indicating his form has "fallen off". Despite this, he continues to deliver in crucial matches, particularly in Glasgow derbies. As of March 5, 2026, Maeda has played 46 matches in the current season, contributing 8 goals and 8 assists across all competitions. A period of poor form in September and October 2025, where he scored only two goals, was reportedly linked to his desire to leave Celtic, which did not materialize.

Tactical Profile

Maeda is characterized by his lightning-quick pace, relentless pressing, and exceptional work rate, making him a constant nuisance for opposing defenses. His primary strength lies in his speed, which he effectively utilizes to attack opponents and create scoring opportunities. While he is listed as a wide forward, his tactical deployment has seen him operate effectively from the left wing, exploiting spaces between fullbacks and center-backs, and making late runs into the box to finish crosses.

Under Brendan Rodgers, Maeda has also been deployed as a striker, playing through the middle, a role in which he has thrived. Tactical analyses suggest he is most effective as a striker, especially when paired with a more physically imposing partner who can hold up the ball, allowing Maeda to capitalize on his finishing ability. His commitment and determination are unquestioned, and he has shown improvements in his left-foot delivery, adding another dimension to his attacking play.

Career Highlights

Maeda's career saw him emerge as the J.League Top Scorer and a member of the J.League Best XI in 2021, having scored 23 goals for Yokohama F. Marinos. He joined Celtic on loan in January 2022, making an immediate impact by scoring on his debut, with the move becoming permanent later that year. He played a crucial role in Celtic securing the Scottish Premiership title in the 2021-22 season.

His contributions continued into the 2022-23 season, where he was instrumental in Celtic's historic eighth treble, winning the SPFL, League Cup, and Scottish Cup. On the international stage, Maeda scored the opening goal for Japan in their 2022 World Cup Round of 16 match against Croatia. In July 2023, he committed his future to Celtic by signing a new four-year contract until 2027. The 2023-24 season saw him contribute to another league and Scottish Cup double, notably scoring after just 30 seconds in the April 2024 Glasgow Derby. By March 2026, Maeda had reached 200 appearances for Celtic, accumulating 70 goals and 36 assists during his tenure.

Personality

Daizen Maeda is regarded as a model professional who understands the importance of recovery and downtime from his demanding playing style. He finds relaxation in unconventional ways, such as chasing his children at soft play areas. His family plays a significant role in his life, with his daughter even influencing his decision to change his hairstyle from a shaved head. Maeda consistently emphasizes team success over individual accolades, stating his focus is always on contributing to the team's victory through goals or assists. He maintains a calm yet focused mindset and believes that Celtic, as a major club, demands strong determination from its players.

Condition / Medical History

Maeda has experienced several injuries throughout his career. In October 2025, he sustained a hamstring injury that ruled him out of a match against Dundee, having also suffered a similar hamstring issue in April 2024. Other reported injuries include a foot injury in October 2025, a strain injury in January 2025, and a bruised knee in July 2024. He also dealt with a muscle injury in April 2024 and a knee injury in November 2023. Earlier in his career, he had issues with lumbago in 2019 and leg and ankle injuries in 2018. A recent report from April 2026 suggests a hamstring injury might rule him out for the remainder of the current season.
