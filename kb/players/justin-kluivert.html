Justin Kluivert is a 26-year-old Dutch attacking midfielder for Bournemouth, known for his artisan archetype and trequartista role. He is right-footed and stands at 171 cm.

Recent Form

Justin Kluivert's 2025-2026 Premier League season has seen him make 18 appearances, scoring 2 goals with no assists, accumulating 920 minutes on the pitch before his recent injury. He has also received four yellow cards during this period. His shooting accuracy for the current season stands at 25%.

In the preceding 2024-2025 season, Kluivert was a standout performer for Bournemouth, finishing as the club's top goalscorer with 12 goals and providing six assists. He also took on penalty duties, successfully converting all six attempts. A notable highlight from that season was becoming the first player in Premier League history to score a hat-trick of penalties in a single match, achieved on November 30, 2024, against Wolverhampton Wanderers. He followed this with an open-play hat-trick on January 18, 2025, against Newcastle United, which earned him the EA SPORTS Player of the Month award.

Tactical Profile

Kluivert operates primarily as an attacking midfielder or winger, demonstrating versatility across both flanks and occasionally in a central attacking role or as a second striker. His playing style is characterized by explosive pace, directness, and strong dribbling skills, making him a threat in one-on-one situations. He possesses good technique and a keen eye for goal, often drifting inside onto his preferred right foot to link with teammates and take quick shots.

While his attacking prowess is evident, Kluivert has areas for development. His aerial duels, passing, and defensive contributions are considered weaknesses. He can sometimes be overly focused on dribbling past defenders when a pass might be a more effective option, and could improve on using his physical strength more effectively against opponents. However, his work ethic, including tracking back and pressing, highlights a team-first mentality.

Career Highlights

Justin Kluivert emerged from the renowned Ajax youth academy, making his senior debut for the club in 2017. A significant early career moment came when he scored his first Eredivisie goal exactly ten years and one day after his father, Patrick Kluivert, scored his last. In 2018, he moved to Italian club Roma for €18.75 million, where he became the youngest player to score for the club in the UEFA Champions League.

Kluivert's career has seen him experience various top European leagues through a series of loan spells, including RB Leipzig (Bundesliga, 2020-2021), Nice (Ligue 1, 2021-2022), and Valencia (La Liga, 2022-2023). This diverse experience culminated in a unique achievement: he became the first player to score in all of Europe's "Big Five" leagues (Premier League, La Liga, Serie A, Bundesliga, Ligue 1) as well as the Eredivisie. He joined Bournemouth permanently in 2023, scoring his first goal for the club in November 2023 and his first Premier League goal later that month.

Personality

Despite the inherent pressure of being the son of a legendary footballer, Justin Kluivert has consistently demonstrated a strong character and handles comparisons with his father with grace. He is noted for his team-first mentality, showing a willingness to adapt to different roles as required by his coaches. His dedication extends to his work ethic, as he actively contributes defensively by tracking back and pressing opponents. Kluivert also notably dedicated his first goal for Roma to his former Ajax teammate Abdelhak Nouri, who suffered a career-ending cardiac event.

Condition / Medical History

Justin Kluivert is currently sidelined due to a knee injury sustained in January 2026, which required surgery. The recovery period is expected to be several months, with a possibility that he could miss the remainder of the current season. Throughout his career, Kluivert has experienced a variety of injuries, including muscle, calf, thigh, ankle, and groin issues, as well as several knock injuries in previous seasons.
