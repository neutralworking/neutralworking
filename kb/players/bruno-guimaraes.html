Snapshot

A centre midfielder of genuine excellence who combines technical sophistication—his progressive carries and range of passing are legitimately world-class—with a surprisingly robust ball-winning presence. He glides through space with Brazillian elegance and rarely loses the ball in dangerous areas. The limitation is that he's not the most positionally disciplined defender, and against the very best pressing teams he can occasionally be hurried into mistakes.

Tactical Profile

Bruno Guimarães operates as a right-footed central midfielder, capable of playing in a deep-lying role or further forward within a midfield two or three. His exceptional passing range, particularly his forward passing, is a defining characteristic, enabling him to break defensive lines and advance play effectively. He excels in central areas by varying his passes based on opponent and teammate movements, possessing the ability to unlock defenses with precise through balls.

Beyond his offensive contributions, Guimarães is a robust and competitive presence without the ball, demonstrating aggression and effectiveness in regaining possession. He is versatile in his defensive duties, often covering for teammates and actively winning the ball early. Described as a "quintessential defensive midfielder," he combines the attributes of a number 6 and an 8, showcasing strengths in tackling, intelligent passing, maintaining possession with elegance, creating chances, and exhibiting excellent spatial awareness. He is considered the "midfield heartbeat" for Newcastle, orchestrating the team's rhythm through his passing, ball-carrying, and consistent involvement, while also contributing to key attacking moments.

Career Highlights

Bruno Guimarães has accumulated several significant achievements throughout his career. With Newcastle United, he was part of the team that won the English League Cup in 2025, a historic victory as he captained the side to their first domestic trophy in 70 years.

Internationally, he earned an Olympic gold medal with Brazil in 2021. During his time with Athletico Paranaense, Guimarães secured multiple titles, including the Copa do Brasil in 2019, the Copa Sudamericana in 2018, the J.League Cup / Copa Sudamericana Championship in 2018-2019, and the Campeonato Paranaense in 2018. He also represented Brazil at the 2022 FIFA World Cup and has accumulated 41 international caps with 2 goals for his national team.
