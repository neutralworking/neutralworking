Snapshot

A right winger with genuine attacking intent and the ability to find space in the half-space through intelligent off-ball movement. His crossing is serviceable and his weak foot shots represent a real threat from distance. However, his defensive workload exposes a lack of physicality, and he can be isolated on the flank if Lazio's build-up doesn't function smoothly—a player better suited to counter-attacking shapes where he's not required to press high.

Recent Form

Gustav Isaksen has been a consistent presence for Lazio in the 2025/2026 Serie A season, featuring in 25 matches and contributing 4 goals and 1 assist across 1408 minutes of play, earning an average FotMob rating of 6.72. His recent performances in March 2026 included a brace in Denmark's 4-0 victory over North Macedonia, where he achieved a 9.1 FotMob rating, and a goal for Lazio in a 1-0 win against AC Milan, receiving an 8.0 rating.

In the 2024/2025 season, Isaksen played 49 games for Lazio, scoring 6 goals, and was recognized as the club's Player of the Month for both March and April 2025. He earned his first senior international cap for Denmark on March 27, 2024, during a 2-0 win against the Faroe Islands, and scored his inaugural international goal on October 15, 2024, in a UEFA Nations League draw against Switzerland. As of March 31, 2026, he has accumulated 15 caps and 6 goals for the Danish national team.

Tactical Profile

Isaksen primarily operates as a right winger, a position he has occupied for approximately 80% of his career. While his preferred foot is right, some reports indicate he is left-footed but adept with his right, or even both-footed, and frequently cuts inside from the right flank, aligning with an inverted winger role. His playing style is characterized by his speed, strong dribbling ability, and effectiveness in counter-attacking situations, often driving forward with the ball. He excels in offensive aspects such as accelerations, touches in the box, and winning offensive duels, with comparisons drawn to Arjen Robben due to his roaming capabilities and pace.

Despite his attacking prowess, Isaksen's defensive contributions are noted as a weaker aspect of his game. He has room for improvement in decision-making, link-up play, and quick short-passing. Additionally, while he can deliver crosses effectively with his left foot, his shooting technique and rhythm sometimes require refinement, and he occasionally struggles to set himself before taking a shot.

Career Highlights

Gustav Isaksen joined Italian Serie A club Lazio on August 8, 2023, following a successful period with FC Midtjylland. His transfer to Lazio was reportedly for around €12 million plus bonuses. He marked his debut season in Italy by scoring his first goal for Lazio on December 30, 2023, against Frosinone, also providing an assist in the 3-1 victory.

Before his move to Italy, Isaksen made his professional debut for Midtjylland in 2019 at the age of 18. During his time with the Danish club, he played a pivotal role in their success, winning the Danish Superliga title in the 2019–20 season and the Danish Cup in 2021–22. His most prolific season came in 2022–23, where he was the top scorer in the Danish Superliga with 18 goals and recorded 22 goals and 9 assists across all competitions. Notably, he scored a hat-trick against Viborg in February 2023 and netted two goals against his future club, Lazio, during their Europa League encounters in the same season.

Personality

Gustav Isaksen is known for his strong work ethic and serious approach to training, maintaining positive relationships with teammates and exhibiting a lack of ego. He possesses a "winner's mentality" that has been evident throughout his career.

His transition to Italian football presented initial challenges, as he found it difficult to adapt to the mentality and coaching styles of Maurizio Sarri and Igor Tudor. Isaksen publicly stated that he and Tudor "didn't get each other 100%" and that the coach preferred different player types. However, he has since flourished under Marco Baroni, praising the coach for his clear tactical ideas and supportive treatment, which has allowed Isaksen to thrive.

Condition / Medical History

Gustav Isaksen has experienced a few notable medical issues during his career. In December 2025, he sustained a muscle injury, specifically a low-grade lesion affecting his left adductor, which kept him sidelined for approximately three weeks. Earlier in 2025, he was diagnosed with glandular fever (also known as kissing disease), an illness that caused him to miss three weeks of pre-season training and required an individualized rehabilitation program due to its impact on his physique.

Prior to his time at Lazio, Isaksen also dealt with a thigh injury in December 2023 and a more significant knee injury that kept him out of action from March to August 2020.
