Recent Form

Antonio Nusa has demonstrated a growing influence at RB Leipzig during the 2025/2026 season, contributing significantly to the team's attack. Across all competitions, he has registered 6 goals and 6 assists in 37 appearances, playing a total of 2529 minutes. His contributions include 3 goals and 3 assists in 26 Bundesliga matches, where he has accumulated 1,774 minutes and maintained an average FotMob rating of 7.22.

Beyond the Bundesliga, Nusa has also found success in cup competitions and international fixtures. He scored 1 goal and provided 1 assist in 4 DFB Pokal appearances, and added 1 goal and 2 assists in 4 World Cup Qualifiers for Norway. As of early April 2026, he had accumulated eight goals across all competitions, with his direct running and energy adding a new dimension to Leipzig's offensive play.

Tactical Profile

Antonio Nusa operates primarily as a winger, often deployed on the left flank, but possesses the versatility to play across the entire attacking front three and even in more central roles as a second striker or playmaker. His playing style is characterized by exceptional skill, pace, and a keen understanding of space, allowing him to operate freely and effectively occupy various areas of the pitch. RB Leipzig's managing director for sport, Marcel Schäfer, highlighted Nusa's ability to handle one-on-one situations at a high level, coupled with his speed and hunger to score.

Nusa is a technically gifted player who excels in one-on-one duels, utilizing a repertoire of tricks, body feints, and stepovers to beat defenders with flair, acceleration, and quick changes of pace. He demonstrates close ball control and fast footwork, enabling him to navigate tight spaces and make quick decisions under pressure. While not primarily an associative player, he is adept at delivering incisive, space-opening passes and is considered a "two-way dribbler" who can cut inside to shoot or cross, or drive to the byline for cutbacks. His defensive work rate is also notable, particularly in high pressing situations, where his pace aids in counter-pressing efforts.

Career Highlights

Antonio Nusa joined RB Leipzig from Club Brugge in the summer of 2024 for an initial fee of €21 million, signing a five-year deal until 2029. His transfer to Leipzig followed a period of significant interest from several top European clubs, including a collapsed deal with Brentford due to medical concerns. Nusa made an immediate impact at his new club, scoring in his DFB Pokal debut and the opening Bundesliga match, and providing an assist in the second league game against Bayer Leverkusen.

Prior to his move to Germany, Nusa established himself as a promising talent at Club Brugge, whom he joined from Stabæk in 2021. During his time in Belgium, he became the youngest player to score on his Champions League debut, finding the net against Porto on September 13. He also contributed to Club Brugge winning three titles: the First Division A in 2021/2022 and 2023/2024, and the Super Cup in 2022/2023. On the international stage, Nusa has made a significant impact for the Norwegian national team, playing a key role alongside Erling Haaland in their push towards the World Cup.

Personality

Antonio Nusa is described as a calm and measured individual who expresses contentment with his current situation at RB Leipzig. He views his progress in football with humility, stating he doesn't consider himself a "wonderkid" but rather someone who enjoys playing and is dedicated to advancing his career. Nusa values flexibility in his daily routine and finds joy in celebrating victories with his teammates. He takes immense pride in representing Norway internationally and harbors ambitions of winning trophies with RB Leipzig. His on-field demeanor also reflects a resilient personality, as he is noted for his willingness to continue attempting dribbles even after initial failures.

Condition / Medical History

Antonio Nusa has experienced several injuries throughout his career, with a notable period of concern in early 2024 when a transfer to Brentford fell through due to issues identified during his medical, specifically related to his back and knees. However, Nusa later confirmed that these concerns were resolved after further investigation, stating that "nothing was really a problem".

More recently, in 2025, Nusa faced a knee injury that sidelined him for 70 days and 11 matches, lasting until April 21, 2025. He also sustained an ankle injury from December 6, 2025, to January 10, 2026, and a forearm injury from September 13 to September 24, 2025. Other recorded injuries include a thigh injury in early 2024, a back injury in late 2023, and a muscle injury in mid-2022. Despite these setbacks, he was reported as fit after recovering from his knee injury as of April 2025.
