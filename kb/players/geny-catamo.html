Recent Form

Geny Catamo has demonstrated strong performance for Sporting CP in the 2025/2026 season, recording 6 goals and 2 assists across 23 Liga Portugal matches, playing 1,575 minutes with an average FotMob rating of 7.42. He has also contributed significantly in European competition, with 1 goal and 2 assists in 11 UEFA Champions League appearances. In the Taça de Portugal, he has scored 1 goal in 4 appearances this season.

His recent contributions include a 7.4 FotMob rating in a 1-0 Liga Portugal win against Estrela da Amadora on April 11, 2026, and an 8.2 rating in a 4-2 Liga Portugal victory over Santa Clara on April 3, 2026. Catamo was notably active in Sporting's Champions League quarter-final against Arsenal in April 2026, where his pace on the wing caused defensive issues for the opposition, and he came close to scoring, hitting the post in one instance.

The 2024/2025 season was particularly successful for Catamo, as he made 41 appearances and scored 6 goals across all competitions. He was instrumental in Sporting CP securing a domestic double, scoring both goals in a 2-1 Lisbon derby win against Benfica on April 6, 2024, and contributing to the Primeira Liga title with a 2-0 victory over Vitória Guimarães on May 17, 2025. He also played a key role in the Taça de Portugal triumph, scoring the opening goal in the semi-final first leg against Rio Ave and featuring in the final win against Benfica on May 25, 2025. On the international stage, Catamo scored a penalty and provided an assist in Mozambique's historic 3-2 victory over Gabon in the 2025 Africa Cup of Nations, marking the nation's first-ever win in the competition.

Tactical Profile

Geny Catamo operates primarily as a winger or wing-back for both Sporting CP and the Mozambique national team, often deployed on the right side where he works effectively with supporting full-backs. His attacking prowess is highlighted by his success in duels, effective dribbling, and strong finishing ability. He is known for adding speed to his team's play down the right flank.

His technical attributes include very strong through balls, strong long shots, and strong passing, complementing his preference for dribbling. Under the guidance of Rúben Amorim at Sporting CP, Catamo has evolved into a more refined professional, capable of fulfilling hybrid roles, with his defensive acumen developing alongside his attacking instincts. This versatility allows him to take risks in attack while also contributing defensively.

Career Highlights

Catamo began his football journey in Mozambique, training at CD Maxaquene and the Black Bulls youth academy before moving to Portugal in 2018 to play for Amora FC. His performances there attracted the attention of Sporting CP, which he joined in 2019, signing his first professional contract in September 2020 after a period in the club's youth academy. He made his senior team debut for Sporting CP on December 29, 2021.

Following loan spells at Vitória Guimarães in 2022 and Marítimo from 2022 to 2023, Catamo returned to Sporting CP and signed a new five-year contract on December 22, 2023, which includes a €60 million release clause. He has been a part of several title-winning campaigns with Sporting CP, including two Primeira Liga titles (2023/2024, 2024/2025), one Taça de Portugal (2024/2025), and the League Cup (2021/2022). Internationally, Catamo debuted for Mozambique on September 10, 2019, scoring in his first senior appearance, and has since accumulated 43 caps with 14 goals as of January 5, 2026.

Personality

Despite a reported shyness in interviews, Geny Catamo's demeanor transforms on the pitch, where he exhibits a confident and assertive playing style. He is recognized for his acceleration, strength, speed of execution, and decision-making, which collectively mark him as a "very special player".

Condition / Medical History

Geny Catamo has experienced several muscle-related injuries throughout his career. In 2025, he sustained a muscle injury in February-March and an overload in September. Previous muscle injuries were recorded in May 2024, and multiple instances in March-April, November, and December of 2023. In 2022, he had a muscle injury from October to December, and also dealt with an illness (Coronavirus) in January of that year. During a Champions League match against Arsenal on April 15, 2026, he required medical attention for an injury, leading to a delay in play before he was substituted.
