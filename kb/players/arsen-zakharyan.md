## Scouting Notes


ClubDynamo Moscow

PositionMidfielder

Born26 May 2003

Coming from the city of Samara, 1,000km south-east of Moscow, Zakharyan caught the attention of Dynamo Moscow scouts and moved to the Lev Yashin Academy in Russia’s capital at the age of 14. Two years later he received his first call-up to the Russia’s under-17s, and scored on his debut in a European qualifier. Zakharyan is an elegant, two-footed, versatile central midfielder who intercepts, wins second balls, moves with intelligence, creates and scores, and his ability to read the game has helped him stand out from a young age. After scoring two goals in three games for Dynamo’s youth team at the age of 16 he was promoted to their reserve side before the start of this season. In his first seven starts Zaharyen scored five goals and made two assists. **[Artur Petrosyan](https://twitter.com/arturpetrosyan)**  
  
**October 2021 update** Has had a fantastic rise over the past year – from playing in Russia’s third tier (when he appeared in this list) to becoming a regular in the Russia national side. Sandro Schwarz put trust in the youngster when he took charge of Dynamo last October and was rewarded with Zakharyan’s dramatic progress. He became a star of the senior team, went to the Under-21 European Championship (when still 17), where he became the youngest ever goalscorer, only missed out on Euro 2020 because of tonsillitis and now is a part of the new Russian national team under Valery Karpin.  
  
**September 2022 update** Had another impressive season with Dynamo Moscow, leading the club to the third place in the Premier League with seven goals, six assists and commanding displays during the whole campaign. Had everything ready for his desired move to Chelsea this summer only for administrative issues standing in the way of the transfer. Slowed down after the end of the transfer window with no goals or assists in the following four games.  
  
**October 2023 update** Secured a well-deserved move to Real Sociedad this August but got Covid soon after his arrival, which slowed down his adaptation period with Imanol Barrenetxea’s exciting team.
