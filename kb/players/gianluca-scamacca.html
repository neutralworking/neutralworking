Gianluca Scamacca - Player Research Brief

Recent Form

Gianluca Scamacca has registered 12 goals and 1 assist across all competitions for Atalanta during the 2025/2026 season, playing in 36 matches for a total of 1744 minutes. In Serie A, he has contributed 8 goals and 1 assist in 14 starts and 7 substitute appearances, earning an average FotMob rating of 6.89. His Champions League campaign this season has seen him score 3 goals in 10 matches, playing 504 minutes.

However, Scamacca's recent form has been impacted by a series of injuries. In March 2026, he sustained a muscle injury, and earlier in February 2026, he also dealt with an unspecified injury. His recent match performances in March 2026 include a 5.6 FotMob rating in a 1-4 loss to Bayern München and a 6.4 rating in a 1-1 draw against Inter.

Tactical Profile

Scamacca is a tall, right-footed center-forward known for his explosive physical strength and powerful shot, capable of scoring from both inside and outside the penalty area, including acrobatic efforts. Despite his size, he is a technical player with a good first touch and often drops deep to link up with teammates, functioning almost as a second striker. This versatility allows him to operate effectively as a lone striker in formations like 4-2-3-1 or 4-3-3, or as part of a strike partnership.

His playing style emphasizes holding up the ball and making clever movements in the final third, bending runs across the defense to create space. While initially noted for needing improvement in his aerial game, he has developed this aspect during his time at Atalanta. Scamacca also exhibits a strong defensive work rate, actively pressing defenders and covering passing lanes. He has been compared to Zlatan Ibrahimović due to his physique and playing style, and lists Ibrahimović and Gabriel Batistuta as inspirations.

Career Highlights

Gianluca Scamacca has achieved significant success in recent seasons, including winning the Europa League with Atalanta in the 2023/2024 season. In that final, he provided an assist for Ademola Lookman's third goal in a 3-0 victory over Bayer Leverkusen. Prior to his move to Atalanta, he also secured the Conference League title with West Ham United in 2022/2023.

During the 2021/2022 Serie A season with Sassuolo, Scamacca was a prolific scorer, netting 16 league goals in 36 appearances, making him the second-highest scoring Italian in the league that year. On the international stage, he became the first Italian to score a brace against Liverpool at Anfield during a Europa League quarter-final in April 2024. He scored his first goal for the Italian national team in April 2022 against North Macedonia and has represented Italy extensively at youth levels, accumulating 85 caps and 36 goals from U15 upwards. He was also a leading scorer in the Coppa Italia on two occasions, in 2018/19 for Ascoli and 2020/21 for Genoa.

Condition / Medical History

Gianluca Scamacca has experienced a challenging period with injuries over the past two years. In August 2024, he suffered a ruptured ACL during a pre-season fixture, which sidelined him for several months before his return in January 2025. This was followed by a torn hamstring in February 2025, necessitating surgery.

His injury woes continued into the 2025/2026 season, with a knee injury in September 2025 leading to his withdrawal from the Italy camp due to discomfort in the previously injured knee. More recently, he sustained a thigh injury in January 2026, an unspecified injury in February 2026, and a muscle injury in March 2026. These recurring issues have significantly impacted his availability and consistency on the field.

Personality

Gianluca Scamacca exhibits a strong ambition and a determined mindset, aspiring to emulate the imposing and confident aura of players like Eric Cantona. He has stated his belief in reaching his full potential within a short timeframe, indicating a high level of self-assurance. His work ethic is evident in his personal mantra to "train while others sleep, study while others have fun," reflecting his dedication to continuous improvement. He is described as mentally mature, capable of confronting experienced defenders without losing his drive for goals.
