Recent Form

Gregor Kobel has been a consistent presence for Borussia Dortmund during the 2025-2026 season, making 29 appearances across all competitions. In the Bundesliga, he has played 2,610 minutes, maintaining an average FotMob rating of 7.14. Across these matches, Kobel has conceded 29 goals while making 75 saves, contributing to 13 clean sheets. His save percentage for the season stands at 72.1%, and he is credited with preventing 3.11 goals.

In recent matches leading up to April 2026, Kobel's performances have varied, including a 7.9 FotMob rating in a 2-0 win against VfB Stuttgart and an 8.0 rating in a 2-0 victory over Augsburg. He also featured in a 0-1 loss to Bayer Leverkusen and a 3-4 loss to Germany, receiving ratings of 6.0 and 6.2 respectively. Throughout the season, he has received two yellow cards.

Tactical Profile

Gregor Kobel operates as an "advancing goalkeeper," frequently playing off his line to intercept 50-50 balls and neutralize potential scoring threats. Standing at 194 cm, he is recognized for his exceptional reflexes, positioning, and shot-stopping ability, often proving to be a "clutch" player for his team. His quickness on his feet is a notable attribute.

Beyond his shot-stopping, Kobel is proficient in distribution, demonstrating high accuracy in both short and long passes in previous seasons. He is known for radiating calmness and actively encouraging his defenders. While highly effective in many aspects, an area identified for improvement is his command in aerial situations, where his numbers for punches and aerial duels are considered relatively low for a goalkeeper of his stature. He has been compared to fellow Swiss international Yann Sommer for his agility, quick feet, ability to get down rapidly, and strength in one-on-one situations.

Career Highlights

Kobel began his professional career with TSG Hoffenheim, making his debut in the DFB-Pokal in 2018. He gained significant experience through loan spells at FC Augsburg in 2019 and VfB Stuttgart from 2019 to 2020, before signing permanently with Stuttgart. In 2021, he transferred to Borussia Dortmund for 15 million euros, solidifying his position as a key player for the club.

A significant highlight of his career includes reaching the UEFA Champions League final with Borussia Dortmund in the 2023-24 season, a first for a Swiss goalkeeper. His performances have earned him multiple individual accolades, including being named in the UEFA Champions League Team of the Season for 2023-24, and the Bundesliga Team of the Season for both 2022–23 and 2023–24. Internationally, Kobel has represented Switzerland at various youth levels and made his senior national team debut in 2021, participating in UEFA Euro 2020 and the 2022 FIFA World Cup. He extended his commitment to Borussia Dortmund by signing a new long-term contract until June 2028.

Personality

Gregor Kobel's upbringing saw him diverge from his father's professional ice hockey career, as he was determined to pursue football and become a goalkeeper from a young age. He places significant emphasis on mental strength and the importance of disconnecting from football during breaks, viewing it as crucial for overall well-being. Kobel believes that recovery is as vital as training, highlighting the need for a holistic approach to his physical and mental condition. He expresses deep respect for other players, particularly goalkeepers like Manuel Neuer, admiring their composure and reliability on the field.

Condition / Medical History

Gregor Kobel has experienced a notable history of injuries throughout his career, encompassing various issues such as thigh, back, muscle, hip, ankle, and knee problems, alongside instances of illness. In the 2024-2025 period, he dealt with a thigh injury in October 2024, a back injury in August 2024, and muscle injuries in March and February 2024, in addition to periods of illness in March and January 2024. He notably missed three games in late 2024 due to a hip injury but subsequently returned to action. Kobel has publicly acknowledged the increasing demands and workload in modern football, underscoring the critical need for effective recovery strategies to manage the physical strain.
