He has maintained a high average FotMob rating of 7.76, indicating consistent high-level performances. Notably, García excels in clean sheets and overall rating compared to other goalkeepers in La Liga. His recent match performances include a 7.8 FotMob rating in a 2-1 away win against Atletico Madrid on April 14, 2026, and an 8.8 rating in a 1-0 home win against Rayo Vallecano on March 22, 2026. He has been praised for providing a sense of calmness and confidence to the team and its supporters.

Tactical Profile

Joan García is recognized as a modern goalkeeper, combining exceptional shot-stopping abilities with strong distribution skills, making him a suitable fit for Barcelona's playing style. His strengths include long passing, shot-stopping (reflexes), and saving close-range shots. He is noted for his calm demeanor and ability to make crucial saves in high-pressure situations, often preventing what appear to be certain goals. García's reflexes are described as "lightning quick," allowing him to excel in one-on-one scenarios, which is particularly vital given Barcelona's high defensive line.

Beyond his shot-stopping, García is comfortable with the ball at his feet, acting as an integral part of Barcelona's build-up play. He demonstrates good decision-making, knowing when to execute short or medium passes and when to launch longer balls to initiate attacks. Furthermore, he operates effectively as a sweeper-keeper, reading the game well and proactively coming off his line to intercept through balls and clear danger behind the defensive line. This comprehensive skill set has made him a transformational signing for Barcelona, bringing stability and an additional dimension to their play.

Career Highlights

Joan García joined Barcelona in June 2025 after the club activated his release clause, signing a six-year contract until 2031. Prior to his move, he had a significant impact at Espanyol, where he graduated from their youth academy and established himself as a first-choice goalkeeper in 2024. In the 2024/2025 La Liga season, he was the goalkeeper with the most saves in the league (146) and prevented the most goals, playing a key role in Espanyol's survival.

His time at Barcelona began with an unofficial debut in a friendly win against Vissel Kobe on July 27, 2025, followed by his UEFA Champions League debut on September 18, 2025, in a 2-1 victory over Newcastle United. Internationally, García has represented Spain at various youth levels (U17, U18, U19, U21) and earned an Olympic gold medal at the 2024 Paris Games. He made his senior debut for the Spain national team on March 31, 2026, as a substitute in a goalless draw against Egypt.

Personality

Joan García is characterized by his calm and composed demeanor, particularly under pressure. This trait was notably evident during a challenging match against his former club Espanyol in January 2026, where he remained unperturbed by a hostile crowd and delivered a match-winning performance for Barcelona. He has expressed a strong desire to succeed at Barcelona, viewing the move as a necessary step for his career and family, despite understanding the disappointment it caused among Espanyol fans.

García maintains a focused and dedicated approach to his profession, constantly seeking ways to improve. He is described as a player with "great character," exuding confidence and security in his abilities. He also adheres to certain routines and superstitions, believing they help him perform at his best. Upon joining Barcelona, he quickly integrated into the squad, noting the young, enthusiastic, and welcoming locker room environment.

Condition / Medical History

Joan García has experienced a few notable injuries during his career. In the 2025/2026 season, he sustained a calf injury from March 18 to March 19, 2026. Earlier in his Barcelona tenure, he underwent surgery for a torn medial meniscus in his left knee on September 27, 2025, with an estimated recovery time of 4 to 6 weeks. This injury occurred after featuring in a 3-1 win over Real Oviedo. He returned to action on November 22, 2025. Previously, in 2021, he was affected by Coronavirus from December 29 to January 7.
