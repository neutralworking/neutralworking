Tactical Profile

Bruno Fernandes operates primarily as an attacking midfielder, renowned for his creative output, vision, and goal-scoring ability. He possesses an exceptional passing range, capable of delivering accurate short and long passes, including incisive through balls that create one-on-one chances for teammates. His strong right foot allows him to score from various positions, including powerful mid-range shots, and he is an accurate free-kick and penalty taker. Fernandes' movement off the ball is also a key attribute, as he frequently roams attacking areas or drops deep to initiate play.

Under manager Ruben Amorim, Fernandes' role has evolved, seeing him operate in a deeper, more multifaceted position, often as a deep-lying playmaker. This adaptation allows him to orchestrate build-up play and dictate the game's tempo from a more withdrawn area, leveraging his vision across the pitch. While his instincts are attacking, his technical quality and tactical intelligence enable him to adapt to these deeper responsibilities, contributing to midfield control and positional discipline. He has also demonstrated versatility, occasionally playing on the right wing due to his crossing ability or even in a deeper central midfield role.

Despite his offensive prowess, Fernandes also contributes significantly defensively, known for his high work rate and pressing immediately after losing possession. He exhibits strong defensive contributions, including tackles, interceptions, and recoveries, which are considered elite-level for an attacking midfielder. However, identified weaknesses include a relative lack of pace and discomfort with dribbling, which can make him less effective when played out wide or when forced to hold the ball under pressure. Career Highlights

Bruno Fernandes has amassed a significant number of individual and team accolades throughout his career. During his time at Sporting CP, he was a prolific goalscorer, netting 33 goals in all competitions during the 2018–19 season, making him the highest-scoring Portuguese midfielder and the highest-scoring midfielder in Europe in a single season. He also won back-to-back Taças da Liga in 2018 and 2019, and the Taça de Portugal in 2019, earning him the Primeira Liga Player of the Year award in both 2018 and 2019.

Since joining Manchester United in January 2020, Fernandes has become a pivotal figure, making over 300 appearances for the club. He has won the FA Cup in 2024 and the EFL Cup in 2023. Individually, he has been awarded the Sir Matt Busby Player of the Year award a record-equalling four times (2020, 2021, 2024, 2025) and the FSA Men's Player of the Year Award in 2020. He was also named the Europa League top scorer in both the 2019–20 and 2024–25 seasons and has received multiple Premier League Player of the Month awards.

On the international stage, Fernandes has represented Portugal at various youth levels and the senior team. He was part of the squad that won the UEFA Nations League in 2019 and again in 2025, being named in the "Team of the Tournament" for his performances in the 2019 edition. Condition / Medical History

This remarkable availability highlights his physical resilience and dedication to the game.
