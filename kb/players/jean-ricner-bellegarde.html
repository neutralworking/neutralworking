Jean-Ricner Bellegarde is a 27-year-old French-born footballer who currently plays for Wolverhampton Wanderers. While born in France and having represented French youth national teams, he now plays internationally for Haiti. Primarily right-footed, Bellegarde stands at 172 cm. Although capable of playing out wide, he is often recognized for his abilities as a central midfielder.

Recent Form

In the 2025/2026 Premier League season, Jean-Ricner Bellegarde has registered 1 goal and 1 assist across 1,150 minutes of play, earning an average FotMob rating of 6.41 and accumulating 5 yellow cards. His recent club appearances include a 0-4 loss to West Ham United on April 10, 2026, and a 2-2 draw against Brentford on March 16, 2026, where he achieved a FotMob rating of 7.8.

Internationally, Bellegarde has made 8 appearances for Haiti since switching his allegiance in 2025. His season has been impacted by injuries, including a hamstring injury in December 2025 that sidelined him for several weeks, and an illness in October 2025.

Tactical Profile

Bellegarde is noted for his abundance of pace, energy, and positive play on the ball. He is a dynamic player who excels at progressing the ball forward and helping his team advance up the pitch, often through dribbling into dangerous areas. His ability to carry the ball and his impressive assist numbers have been highlighted, with his actions rated among the top central midfielders in Europe's major leagues for value.

While capable of playing wide in attacking positions, his natural talent and strengths are often seen when operating in central midfield. He thrives when driving forward from deeper positions, utilizing his vision and movement to be a dangerous player in transition. However, tactical setups at Wolves have sometimes seen him deployed in wider roles, which some analyses suggest may diminish his ball progression abilities compared to a central role. His strengths include key passes and defensive contribution, though weaknesses in concentration, finishing, aerial duels, and passing have been noted.

Career Highlights

Jean-Ricner Bellegarde began his senior career with Lens, making his professional debut in 2016 and playing over 50 times in the French second tier before moving to Strasbourg in 2019. During his four years at Strasbourg, he made nearly 130 appearances in Ligue 1 and featured in the 2019/20 Europa League qualifiers. His 2022/23 campaign at Strasbourg was considered his strongest yet, where he posted impressive numbers for progressive passes, carries, and key passes.

He joined Wolverhampton Wanderers on September 1, 2023, for £12.8 million on a five-year deal. He scored his debut goal for Wolves on November 4, 2023, against Sheffield United. Bellegarde represented France at U19, U20, and U21 levels. In 2025, he switched his international allegiance to Haiti, making his debut on September 5, 2025, and becoming the first Haitian-represented player to feature in the Premier League.
