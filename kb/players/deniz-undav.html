Snapshot

A poacher's number nine with predatory positioning and movement in and around the box; he thrives on scraps and half-chances through intelligent checking and off-ball awareness. His hold-up play is limited, and he rarely creates for others, so he demands service—set him loose in a chaotic box and he'll profit, but he's not a focal point for build-up play. A very good finisher at a Bundesliga side, but not a complete forward.

Deniz Undav is a 29-year-old German center-forward for VfB Stuttgart, known for his right-footed play and role as a seconda punta. Standing at 178 cm, he has established himself as a prolific marksman.

Recent Form

Deniz Undav has demonstrated exceptional form since joining VfB Stuttgart, particularly in the 2023-2024 season where he scored 18 goals and provided 9 assists in the Bundesliga, contributing significantly to Stuttgart's second-place finish and Champions League qualification. During this period, he earned the Bundesliga Player of the Month award in November 2023 and January 2024, and was recognized as the VDV Bundesliga Newcomer of the Season for 2023-24.

Following his permanent transfer to Stuttgart in August 2024, Undav continued his strong performances, scoring on his Champions League debut against Real Madrid on September 17, 2024. As of February 14, 2026, he marked his 100th competitive appearance for the club by scoring his 48th goal in a 3-1 victory over 1. FC Köln. In the ongoing 2025-2026 Bundesliga season, Undav has already tallied 18 goals and 5 assists in 1,909 minutes played, maintaining an average FotMob rating of 7.48. His recent scoring streak includes goals in each of his last six Bundesliga matches, and he has not experienced a loss in the league during this period. He also scored the winning goal for Germany in a friendly against Ghana in March 2026.

Tactical Profile

Undav is recognized as a versatile and complete forward with a multifaceted skill set. He excels as a clinical finisher, often described as a poacher who consistently finds himself in advantageous positions to capitalize on scoring opportunities, demonstrating a variety of finishing techniques including lobs and precise shots into corners. Beyond his goal-scoring prowess, Undav actively contributes to build-up play by frequently dropping into midfield to link play, showcasing intelligence and effective hold-up play.

His tactical awareness extends to creating space for teammates and delivering key passes and through balls. When deployed alongside another striker like Serhou Guirassy, Undav adapts his movement, dropping between the lines to connect play before making runs in behind, often operating from a right-of-center position. While primarily an offensive threat, he also contributes defensively by recovering the ball and winning possession in the final third, though defensive contribution is also noted as an area for development.

Career Highlights

Deniz Undav's career has been marked by significant achievements across different leagues. During his time with Union Saint-Gilloise from 2020 to 2022, he was pivotal in their resurgence, helping the club secure promotion to the Belgian First Division A in 2020-21, scoring 17 goals in 26 games. In the 2021-22 season, he was the Belgian Pro League's top goalscorer with 26 goals and 12 assists, earning him the title of Belgian Professional Footballer of the Year.

His move to VfB Stuttgart saw him become a Bundesliga runner-up in the 2023-24 season, playing a key role in the club's Champions League qualification. Individually, he was named the VDV Bundesliga Newcomer of the Season for 2023–24 and received the Bundesliga Player of the Month award three times (November 2023, January 2024, March 2026). On the international stage, Undav received his first call-up to the German national team in March 2024, made his debut against France, and was included in Germany's UEFA Euro 2024 squad, scoring his first international goal in September 2024.

Personality

Deniz Undav is known for his distinctive personality, often referred to as German football's "clown prince" due to his sense of humor. He demonstrates a strong commitment to team spirit, notably prioritizing collective success over individual accolades, such as opting to pass to a teammate rather than attempting a hat-trick. His career trajectory, which involved being released by Werder Bremen's youth academy due to concerns about his height and subsequently working his way up from lower divisions, including a period working in a factory, highlights his determination and underdog mentality. Undav has also publicly identified as Kurdish, a stance that has unfortunately led to him facing racist abuse and being targeted with epithets like "traitor" and "terrorist" by some Turkish nationalist groups.

Condition / Medical History

Deniz Undav has experienced several injuries and illnesses in recent seasons. In the 2025-2026 season, he sustained a medial collateral ligament tear in his left knee on August 31, 2025, which sidelined him for approximately four to six weeks until October 8, 2025. He also dealt with illnesses in April 2025 and February 2025.

In the 2024-2025 season, Undav faced a thigh injury from November 7, 2024, to January 11, 2025, and a muscle injury in October 2024. During the 2023-2024 season, he had a thigh injury in February-March 2024 and a muscle injury in February 2024. Earlier in the 2023-2024 season, he missed time due to a torn lateral knee ligament from August 9, 2023, to September 15, 2023.
