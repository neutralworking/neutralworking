Snapshot

A left-winger with genuine elasticity in his movements and an underrated ability to progress the ball into the final third through intelligent half-space positioning. His finishing is clinical when afforded space, and he offers Marseille directness they sometimes lack, but he can be desperately predictable when crowded—too reliant on his left foot, too often checking back rather than committing defenders with explosive acceleration.

Born16 February 2000

The list of French strikers developed at the Lyon academy is long and illustrious. It includes Frédéric Kanouté, Ludovic Giuly, Sidney Govou, Karim Benzema, Loïc Rémy, Anthony Martial and Alexandre Lacazette. The latest to emerge is Gouiri, whose exploits at the 2017 European under-17 championship impressed scouts at Europe’s top clubs. Gouiri scored a tournament record eight goals in four games – a few months after scoring all five goals for France in a 5-1 win over Belgium. “Amine has a special profile,” said Armand Garrido, who has coached Lyon’s under-17 side since 1992. “He has a little bit of everything.” Gouiri’s target this season is to get some playing time in the Lyon first-team – he did not look out of place while playing pre-season matches – but there will be no shortage of suitors should he want to look elsewhere. Gouiri has shrugged off the inevitable comparisons to Benzema and said: “I go my own way, free from pressure.” Ben Lyttleton

October 2018 update A brilliant year has seen the striker blossom, having made seven Ligue 1 appearances since his debut for Lyon in November 2017. He is yet to find the net but made up for that by scoring five goals at the Under-17 World Cup and another four at the European Under-19 Championship.

October 2019 update A difficult season that saw him fail to make a league appearance culminated in France being eliminated at the last-16 stage of the Under-20 World Cup in Poland despite Gouiri’s three goals.

October 2020 update Joined Nice in July for €7m and scored twice in his first competitive match for the club in the win over Lens.

October 2021 updateA blistering start to the season that yielded five goals in his first eight Ligue 1 appearances has seen Gouiri linked with a moves to Manchester City, Liverpool and Real Madrid among others in recent weeks. A name to look out for.
