Anthony Gordon is a 25-year-old English winger currently playing for Newcastle United, known for his dynamic attacking play and significant contributions to both club and country. Standing at 183 cm and primarily right-footed, he operates effectively as an inverted winger, but has also demonstrated versatility across the front line.

Recent Form

Anthony Gordon has displayed strong form during the 2025-2026 season, contributing significantly to Newcastle United's attacking output. In the Premier League, he has recorded 6 goals and 2 assists across 26 appearances, playing 1814 minutes. His overall performance in all competitions is even more impressive, with 17 goals and 5 assists in 46 appearances. Notably, Gordon scored four goals in a UEFA Champions League playoff match against Qarabağ in February 2026, marking his first senior hat-trick and becoming the first Newcastle player to score four goals in a single European match. He also became the first Newcastle player to score in three consecutive Champions League matches. His consistent goal-scoring and chance creation have been vital, positioning him as a key player for Newcastle, especially in a season where the club is striving for European qualification.

Tactical Profile

Gordon's tactical profile is characterized by his high energy, blistering pace, and directness, making him a formidable opponent for full-backs. As an inverted winger, he frequently attacks defenders with quick feet and constant movement, cutting inside or driving to the byline to create opportunities. He is also a crucial component of Newcastle's pressing game under Eddie Howe, contributing heavily to tracking back and forcing defensive errors, embodying the team's emphasis on intensity and vertical progression. His ability to manipulate defenders with subtle movements, both staying wide and drifting centrally, adds unpredictability to his play. While primarily a winger, Gordon's versatility allows him to adapt to roles such as an attacking midfielder or even a central striker, where his pressing and off-the-ball movement are particularly effective.

Career Highlights

Anthony Gordon has accumulated several significant achievements throughout his career. A standout moment came in 2023 when he was a key member of the England U21 squad that won the UEFA U21 European Championship, where he was also named Player of the Tournament and included in the Team of the Tournament. He made his senior England debut in March 2024 and scored his first international goal in November 2024 during a UEFA Nations League victory against the Republic of Ireland. Gordon was also part of the England squad that reached the final of UEFA Euro 2024. At club level, he was named Newcastle United's Player of the Season for the 2023-24 campaign. He played a crucial role in Newcastle's EFL Cup triumph in 2025, scoring in both legs of the semi-final against Arsenal. Gordon began his professional journey at Everton, making his senior debut in 2017, before joining Newcastle United in January 2023 for a reported fee of £40-45 million.

Personality

Anthony Gordon is described as an individual with an "addictive personality" and an introverted nature off the pitch, which he believes fuels his strong work ethic and desire to constantly challenge himself. He is known for his honesty in interviews, preferring to share his genuine opinions rather than providing "PR answers," aiming to offer insight into his true self. Gordon acknowledges a public perception of him as a "Scouse scally" but hopes that meeting him changes people's initial impressions. He possesses a "blind confidence" in his abilities that has been present since a young age. His goal celebration, an "A-sign," is dedicated to his child, not himself. Growing up, he idolized Steven Gerrard and considers Mohamed Salah the best player he has faced.

Condition / Medical History

Anthony Gordon has experienced a range of injuries throughout his career, including issues with his hamstring, hip, ankle, thigh, groin, and knee. In the 2025-2026 season, he sustained a thigh injury in February 2026, a hip injury in November 2025, an ankle injury in August 2025, and a groin injury in April 2025. His playing style, characterized by explosive bursts of speed, contributes to him being considered a "hamstring risk," though his injury history encompasses various leg-related problems. While he has missed games due to these issues, they have generally not resulted in prolonged absences.
