Silvère Ganvoula is a physical presence up front, using his impressive jumping ability and pace to bully defenders in the air and on the ground, but his lack of tactical awareness often leaves him isolated and unable to link play effectively. As a target man, he excels at holding up the ball and bringing others into play, but his poor marking and mental attributes make him a liability in games that require a high press or intense defensive work. Despite his ability to finish chances in the box, his long-range shooting can be a double-edged sword, often leading to wasted opportunities and a lack of progressive carries from deeper positions.
