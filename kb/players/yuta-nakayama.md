## Snapshot

A left-sided centre-back with genuine ball-carrying ability and a willingness to step into midfield, Nakayama uses his range of passing and composed temperament to play a progressive role from deep—he's not just clearing lines but constructing from the back. His weakness, though, is a curious one: he can be passive in the duel, preferring to read the play rather than impose himself, which becomes a serious liability against physical strikers or in chaotic penalty-box situations where you need someone to just win the ball. For a mid-table side or a Championship club, he's valuable; for a top-six team where centre-backs must be complete defenders, he's a squad rotation option with a ceiling.
