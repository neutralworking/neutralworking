Keinan Davis, the 28-year-old English center forward for Udinese, is currently enjoying a productive season in Serie A, despite a recent injury setback. Standing at 189 cm and preferring his left foot, Davis embodies the "Hunter" archetype and operates effectively as a "Seconda Punta" within his team's tactical setup.

Recent Form

Keinan Davis is experiencing his most successful professional season to date, having recorded 10 goals and 3 assists in 26 Serie A appearances for Udinese in the 2025/2026 season, playing 1,903 minutes with 25 starts. This marks his first time achieving a double-digit goal tally in his career, positioning him as the third-highest scorer in Serie A as of late March 2026. His strong performances have earned him an average FotMob rating of 7.02, indicating he scores highly on goals, starts, and overall rating compared to other strikers in the league.

His recent contributions include 5 goals and 1 assist in his last 10 Serie A matches. Notable goal-scoring performances in early 2026 include strikes against Genoa, Atalanta, Fiorentina, Hellas Verona, and Pisa. Davis has largely maintained fitness throughout this campaign, a significant improvement from his first two seasons in Italy which were hampered by injuries. However, he was recently diagnosed with a right biceps femoris strain on April 14, 2026, which is expected to sideline him for at least 10 days, causing him to miss Udinese's match against Parma and likely the subsequent game against Lazio. He also served a suspension on April 6, 2026.

Tactical Profile

Davis is characterized as a creative deep-lying forward who excels at contributing goals while also creating opportunities for teammates. He deviates from a traditional target man role, preferring to drop deeper to influence play and utilize his impressive dribbling ability and success in offensive duels. His playing style is noted for its physical strength and composure under pressure, allowing him to shrug off defenders and carve out space effectively.

As a "Seconda Punta," Davis is adept at taking control of the game both physically and technically, whether operating from deeper areas or within the penalty box. He is known for his layoffs, dribbling prowess, and posing a significant threat on counter-attacks, often preferring to play short passes. His ability to drift into wide areas, drawing defenders and creating numerical advantages, was evident in a recent match against AC Milan, where he helped expose their defensive structure. Davis's effectiveness is further enhanced by his partnership with players like Nicolò Zaniolo, who attracts defensive attention, thereby creating space for Davis to exploit. He has been described as a "poacher" and can also function as a "pressing forward" or "mobile striker," with an aggressive, "front-footed" approach to pressing opponents.

Career Highlights

Keinan Davis joined the Aston Villa academy at the age of 17 in December 2015, making his first-team debut just over a year later in January 2017. He scored his first professional goal for the club in September 2017 and played a role in Aston Villa's promotion to the Premier League in 2019. His first Premier League goal came in April 2021, an injury-time equalizer.

Davis gained further experience through loan spells at Nottingham Forest in 2022 and Watford from 2022 to 2023. During his time at Nottingham Forest, he contributed to their success, helping the club win the 2022 Championship play-offs. In September 2023, Davis made a permanent move to Udinese for €2.3 million. A significant highlight in his Udinese career was scoring a crucial goal on the final day of a previous season that secured the club's Serie A survival, a moment he regards as his most cherished career memory due to his injury struggles that year. Throughout his career, he has won two titles: the Queensland Champions Cup in 2022 with Aston Villa and the Premier League Cup in the 2017/2018 season with Aston Villa U23. He has also represented England at the U20 level.

Personality

Davis describes himself as a humble individual, a trait he attributes to his journey from lower league football to Serie A, which has taught him not to take anything for granted. He considers himself a "very simple guy," preferring to spend his time at home with his daughter and girlfriend, maintaining a football-focused lifestyle where his free time is dedicated to recovery. He acknowledges that moving away from England and taking on new responsibilities in Italy has significantly strengthened him mentally. Davis has adapted well to life in Udine, appreciating the kindness of the local people and finding the Italian culture interesting and insightful.

Condition / Medical History

Keinan Davis has an extensive medical history marked by numerous injuries throughout his career. Most recently, on April 14, 2026, he was diagnosed with a right biceps femoris strain, which is expected to keep him out of action for at least 10 days. This follows a pattern of various injuries, including a previous injury in February 2026, a thigh injury in October-November 2025, and another injury in April 2025. In late 2024 and early 2025, he suffered a calf injury, and an earlier injury occurred in April 2024.

His initial period at Udinese was also affected by a calf injury sustained shortly after his transfer in September 2023, which sidelined him for two months. Prior to joining Udinese, he experienced an illness in August-September 2022, a knee injury from July to October 2021, and a foot injury in November-December 2020. Davis has also had multiple thigh injuries, notably in February 2020, October 2019 to January 2020, and February-March 2019, as well as a groin injury from July to December 2018. Davis has openly stated that his first two seasons in Italy were heavily impacted by these injuries, but he has managed to stay largely fit for the majority of the 2025/2026 campaign, emphasizing that remaining injury-free is crucial for his career aspirations, including a potential call-up to the England squad.
