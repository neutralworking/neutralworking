Raw scouting narrative

class: goalkeeper

There are times watching five-time Zamora Trophy winner Jan Oblak when you might genuinely believe he has the power to attract the ball towards his hands. It's like his gloves have their own gravitational pull.

However, he hasn't looked his formidable self over the last 12 months while his competitors have thrived, so his ranking has dropped as a result.

Jan Oblak, the 33-year-old Slovenian goalkeeper for Atlético Madrid, continues to be a pivotal figure for his club, demonstrating consistent elite performance and leadership. Standing at 188 cm, Oblak operates as a "Wall" with a "Comandante" role within his team's tactical setup.

Recent Form

Jan Oblak concluded the 2024/25 season in strong form, making 36 appearances and securing 15 clean sheets while conceding less than one goal per game, which led to him winning the Zamora Trophy for a record sixth time. This achievement cemented his place in LaLiga history, surpassing previous record holders. For the current 2025/26 season, Oblak has featured in 37 games across all competitions, recording 10 clean sheets and conceding 47 goals. In La Liga specifically, he has maintained a save percentage of 69.88% over 26 matches, conceding an average of 0.96 goals per game.

He recently returned to availability for Atlético Madrid's Champions League return leg against Barcelona on April 13, 2026, after missing six matches due to an undisclosed injury. This muscle injury was recorded from March 13, 2026, to April 12, 2026. Despite recent injury concerns, Oblak's consistent performance suggests he remains a top-tier goalkeeper.

Tactical Profile

Oblak is renowned for his technical prowess and confidence in goal, characterized by exceptional shot-stopping abilities and a high save percentage. His 188 cm height allows him to command his penalty area, while his agility and speed are crucial for denying close-range shots. A key aspect of his game is his excellent reading of play, enabling him to anticipate opponent movements and position himself optimally to make saves. He maintains composure in high-pressure situations, particularly in one-on-one encounters, often forcing attackers into errors.

Beyond his individual saving capabilities, Oblak acts as a leader within Atlético Madrid's defense, actively communicating and organizing the backline. While historically noted for less developed foot skills compared to some elite peers, which sometimes led to long passes under pressure, Atlético's evolving tactical approach has seen Oblak's involvement in ball-playing increase. He focuses on simple, effective one-touch passes to avoid pressure and is willing to venture out of his box to intercept through balls and relieve defensive pressure.

Career Highlights

Jan Oblak joined Atlético Madrid in 2014 and has since amassed an impressive list of accolades. He has won the prestigious Zamora Trophy a record six times (2015–16, 2016–17, 2017–18, 2018–19, 2020–21, and 2024–25), establishing himself as the goalkeeper with the most wins in La Liga history. His contributions were instrumental in Atlético Madrid's La Liga title win in the 2020-21 season, where he recorded 18 clean sheets in 38 matches and an 80% save rate, also earning him the La Liga Player of the Season award.

His trophy cabinet with Atlético Madrid also includes the 2018 UEFA Super Cup and the 2017/18 Europa League. Prior to his move to Spain, he won the Portuguese league with Benfica in the 2013/14 season. Oblak's consistent world-class performances have also led to Ballon d'Or nominations in 2017 and 2018. He became Atlético Madrid's most-capped foreign player on May 9, 2023, with 390 appearances, and made his 100th Champions League appearance on January 21, 2026. Furthermore, he became the first goalkeeper in Champions League history to win three penalty shootouts with one club after saving two penalties in a March 2024 Round of 16 match. He has also been recognized as the Slovenian Footballer of the Year on nine occasions.

Personality

Jan Oblak is widely described as a loyal, responsible, and hard-working individual. His personality is often characterized by calm pragmatism, logical thinking, and a hands-on approach to problem-solving, aligning with an ISTP personality type. Within the Enneagram system, he is recognized as a 6w7, known as the "Loyalist" or "Skeptic," indicating a drive for reassurance and stability.

He views himself as a leader within Atlético Madrid and expresses happiness when on the field. Oblak is deeply committed to winning more trophies with the club, consistently stating his dedication to giving his best. A former Slovenia manager noted his "very relaxed, serious and mature" demeanor from a young age, highlighting his coolness under pressure and intense focus on becoming a great goalkeeper.

Condition / Medical History

Jan Oblak recently recovered from a muscle injury that sidelined him from March 13 to April 12, 2026, causing him to miss six matches before returning to availability for a Champions League fixture. In the preceding months, he also experienced an unspecified injury in November 2025 and periods of physical discomfort in April and January 2025. In May 2025, reports indicated he was considering surgery for a persistent cervical contraction issue that had caused him pain throughout the season, though he ultimately opted for conservative treatment.

In September 2025, Oblak requested a six-month break from the Slovenian national team to address longstanding physical issues, including a dislocated shoulder from two seasons prior and a glute injury from the previous season. Other notable past injuries include a neck injury from April to July 2023 and a concussion with direct trauma to his right shoulder in October 2022, though neurological tests showed no concerning symptoms. A significant shoulder injury in December 2016 required surgery, keeping him out for three to four months.
