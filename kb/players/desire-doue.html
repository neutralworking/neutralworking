Recent Form

Désiré Doué has demonstrated strong performances during the 2025-2026 season, notably contributing to Paris Saint-Germain's campaign in both domestic and European competitions. In Ligue 1, he has recorded 5 goals and 2 assists across 1005 minutes played, maintaining an average FotMob rating of 7.31. His impact extends to the UEFA Champions League, where he has scored 5 goals and provided 2 assists in 10 matches, accumulating 608 minutes on the pitch. Doué also registered a goal in the FIFA Club World Cup and another in the Coupe de France this season.

His impressive form follows a highly successful 2024-2025 season, where he was named the Champions League Young Player of the Season and played a pivotal role in Paris Saint-Germain winning the UEFA Champions League. In the 2025 Champions League final, he notably scored two goals and delivered an assist, earning him the player of the match award. Doué's exceptional talent was further recognized with the prestigious Golden Boy award in 2025.

Tactical Profile

Désiré Doué is recognized for his dynamic and versatile attacking play, often operating as an attacking midfielder or winger. His playing style is characterized by exceptional ball control, smooth dribbling, and accurate passing, allowing him to accelerate quickly and navigate tight spaces effectively. Doué's ability to shoot well with both feet makes him highly unpredictable to defenders.

Beyond his offensive prowess, Doué possesses a robust and determined approach to the game, contributing significantly to defensive efforts through his tackling and work rate off the ball. While he exhibits a high degree of technical skill and decision-making in accelerating offensive moves, some analyses suggest continued development in his overall tactical positioning and game reading. His capacity to adapt to various roles within the attacking third underscores his value as a "Virtuoso" and "Wide Playmaker" within his team's structure.

Career Highlights

Désiré Doué's career has been marked by rapid ascent and significant achievements at both club and international levels. He joined Paris Saint-Germain on August 17, 2024, in a high-profile transfer worth €50 million from Rennes, signing a contract until June 2029. With PSG, he quickly contributed to major successes, including winning the Ligue 1 title and the UEFA Champions League in the 2024-2025 season. His performance in the Champions League final, where he scored twice and assisted once, earned him the Player of the Match title and the UEFA Champions League Young Player of the Season award for 2024-2025.

On the international stage, Doué earned his first call-up to the France national team in 2025, making his debut in the UEFA Nations League where France secured a third-place finish. Prior to this, he was a key member of the France Olympic team that won a silver medal at the 2024 Olympic Games. His youth career also saw him clinch the UEFA European Under-17 Championship in 2022. Individually, Doué was honored with the prestigious Golden Boy award in 2025 and was named PSG Player of the Month in March 2024.

Condition / Medical History

Désiré Doué has experienced several periods of absence due to injuries throughout his career. In the current 2025-2026 season, he sustained a thigh injury that sidelined him for 41 days, causing him to miss 7 matches between October and December 2025. Earlier in the same season, a calf injury led to a 40-day absence, resulting in 6 missed games in September and October 2025. Most recently, he incurred a leg injury in April 2026.

His injury history also includes an ankle injury in the 2024-2025 season, which kept him out for 10 days, and a back injury in the 2022-2023 season, leading to a 31-day recovery period and 8 missed matches. Doué has also dealt with multiple ankle issues and illnesses across previous seasons.
