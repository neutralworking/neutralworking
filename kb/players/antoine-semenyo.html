Antoine Semenyo: Player Research Brief

Recent Form

Antoine Semenyo has demonstrated exceptional form in the 2025/2026 season, marked by a significant transfer to Manchester City in January 2026 for a reported fee of £64 million or €74 million. Before his move, he scored 8 goals for Bournemouth in the first half of the season. Since joining Manchester City, Semenyo has continued his impressive goal-scoring, netting 15 goals and providing 4 assists in 30 Premier League appearances for the season. Across all competitions in 2025/2026, he has accumulated 18 goals and 6 assists in 40 games.

His impact at Manchester City was immediate, scoring a goal and assisting another on his debut in the FA Cup against Exeter City, which ended in a 10-1 victory. He also scored in an EFL Cup semi-final against Newcastle and registered his first Premier League goal for City in a 2-0 win over Wolverhampton Wanderers. Semenyo was a standout performer in Manchester City's Carabao Cup final victory over Arsenal in March 2026, where he notably challenged Arsenal's Piero Hincapie. His strong performances earned him the Premier League Player of the Month award in February 2026 and the PFA Premier League Fans' Player of the Month in September 2025.

Tactical Profile

Antoine Semenyo is a dynamic and versatile winger, known for his pace, power, and tactical adaptability, capable of playing across various attacking roles including as a lone striker or second striker. His movement is key to his effectiveness, creating space for teammates and disrupting defensive lines through well-timed and directed bursts into the box and behind the defense. Semenyo possesses excellent ball control, a sharp first touch, and the ability to manipulate the ball skillfully under pressure, making him difficult to dispossess.

He is comfortable and effective with both feet for dribbling and shooting, which adds an element of unpredictability to his play, allowing him to outmaneuver defenders both inside and outside. Semenyo exhibits strong spatial awareness and decision-making in the final third, often keeping his head up while dribbling to make intelligent choices, including delivering incisive through balls and low crosses or cutbacks. While he is a "vertical player" who intensely attacks depth, there are instances where he might stay too wide or make non-optimal decisions in transition, not always exploiting available space behind defenses.

Career Highlights

Antoine Semenyo's career has seen a steady ascent, culminating in a high-profile transfer to Manchester City in January 2026. This move, valued at £64 million or €74 million, established him as the most expensive Ghanaian player in history. Prior to this, he had signed a contract extension with Bournemouth until 2030 on July 1, 2025, underscoring his value to the club.

He joined Bournemouth midway through the 2022/2023 season, having developed his skills at Bristol City, where he began his professional career. During his time at Bristol City, he had loan spells at Bath City, Newport County, and Sunderland. Semenyo was recognized for his talent early on, winning the EFL Championship Player of the Month award in January 2022 and being named Bristol City's Young Player of the Year for the 2021-22 season. On the international stage, he made his debut for the Ghana national team in June 2022 and was part of their squad for the 2022 World Cup in Qatar, making two substitute appearances. He secured his first major trophy with Manchester City, winning the English League Cup in 2026.

Personality

Semenyo describes himself as a "powerful, quick, strong, clinical player" who is continuously striving for improvement. His journey in football has been characterized by persistence and determination, having overcome early rejections as a young player. He attributes his strength and resilience to the unwavering support of his family, friends, and fiancée, who have consistently uplifted and pushed him through challenging periods. Semenyo projects an image of being "strong, fearless, and ready to attack the world," and expresses that he plays his best football with a smile on his face. He is deeply dedicated to his craft, focused on development, and actively engages in prayer and self-reflection to identify areas for growth. His move to Manchester City was met with excitement and emotion, viewing the opportunity to play in the Champions League as a "dream come true". Despite joining a highly successful team, he remains confident and unintimidated, believing in the collective experience of the squad to maintain composure.

Condition / Medical History

Antoine Semenyo has experienced several injuries throughout his career. In November 2025, he dealt with an ankle injury and an illness. Prior to that, in November 2024, he sustained a foot injury. The year 2024 also saw him sidelined with both a knee injury in April/May and a calf injury in April. In 2023, he suffered a leg injury that required surgery in May, keeping him out of action until the following season. Earlier in his career, he had a muscle injury in March 2022 and a knee injury from October to November 2021. While some absences were brief, lasting less than two weeks, others, like his leg injury in 2023, resulted in longer recovery periods.
