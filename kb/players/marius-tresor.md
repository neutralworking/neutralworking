## Snapshot

CD		Defender			FRA	B	0.0	Marius Tresor was a stopper and sweeper extraordinaire for France and his club team. He had excellent physical options, marking ability and overall technique, and was also a threat on goal. Tresor had a brilliant goal in the 1982 France versus West Germany World Cup semi-final, Spectacularly volleying a pass in overtime but France lost in penalty takeout and finishes fourth place in that tournament.		Originally from Guadeloupe in the West Indies, he was regarded as the first-rate defender in history of French football. He was named by Pele as one of the top 125 greatest living footballers.
