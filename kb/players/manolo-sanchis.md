## Snapshot

As a [central defender](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Centre-back "Defender (association football)"), Sanchís stood out for his defensive composure, placement and agility. He set a new standard within his club due to both his sporting and human skills.[10](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manolo_Sanch%C3%ADs#cite_note-RM-10) An aggressive yet fair player,[11](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manolo_Sanch%C3%ADs#cite_note-11) he was also noted for his tactical intelligence and positional sense, and usually played as a sweeper.

A part of the famous _[La Quinta del Buitre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/La_Quinta_del_Buitre "La Quinta del Buitre")_ which stemmed from the [Real Madrid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Real_Madrid_CF "Real Madrid CF") youth academy, he was the only of its five members to spend his [entire career](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_one-club_men_in_association_football "List of one-club men in association football") with the club. His father [Manuel Sanchís](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manuel_Sanch%C3%ADs "Manuel Sanchís") also played for Real Madrid, and both were [Spanish internationals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spain_national_football_team "Spain national football team").[2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manolo_Sanch%C3%ADs#cite_note-2)[3](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manolo_Sanch%C3%ADs#cite_note-3)[4](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manolo_Sanch%C3%ADs#cite_note-Fans-4)

Sanchís appeared in more than 700 competitive matches for his only club, and represented the national team in one [World Cup](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup "FIFA World Cup") and one [European Championship](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UEFA_European_Championship "UEFA European Championship").
