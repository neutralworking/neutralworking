## Snapshot

Defensive midfielder with exceptional positioning intelligence and a natural reading of the game that transcends his age; he breaks play effectively and his distribution is clean and progressive. He can occasionally be bypassed by opponents who isolate him physically, and his limited forward-driving ability means creative responsibility falls entirely elsewhere. At 25, he's an excellent functional midfielder but not a game-changer.

Skipp played 18 games for Tottenham’s under-18s last season and he even got on as a substitute for the under-23s in February – all this while he was still a schoolboy. This season he has stepped again up to become a regular at under-23 level and the overall impression is of a player who is mature beyond his years. A firm favourite of the club’s head of player development, John McDermott, Skipp is a tenacious defensive midfielder with composure, an eye for a pass and an excellent attitude. **[David Hytner](https://twitter.com/DaveHytner)**  
  
**October 2018 update** A regular for the under-23s last season, despite being a first-year scholar, he impressed on tour with the first-team over the summer. Christian Eriksen described him as an “all-round midfielder”, adding: “What I like about him is his courage.”  
  
**October 2019 update** Has established himself as a fixture in Mauricio Pochettino’s first-team squad, playing 12 times last season – a remarkable achievement that felt as though it drifted under the radar somewhat.  
  
**October 2020 update** Played only 73 minutes of Premier League football - including being substituted at half-time in his only start against Burnley - last season so it was no surprise that he opted to join Norwich on loan for this campaign.  
  
**October 2021 update** Helped Norwich win promotion to the Premier League and was included in the 2021 PFA team of the year for the Championship. Back at Spurs, he has regularly been trusted with a starting place in midfield by Nuno Esperíto Santo so far this season.  
  
**September 2022 update** Nuno Esperíto Santo counted on him in the early weeks of last season and so did Antonio Conte when he took over.
