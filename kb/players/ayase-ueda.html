Snapshot

Ueda operates as a Complete Forward, perfectly embodying the Marksman archetype with strong physical attributes for jumping and acceleration, combined with astute movement and composure in close-range finishing. While technically sound, his limited mental pillar manifests in poor tackling, lack of awareness, and minimal creativity, positioning him as a pure goal-scorer rather than a playmaking striker. As a rising talent at Feyenoord, his market valuation is increasing for clubs in search of a specialized finisher for systems that create numerous opportunities, accepting his minimal defensive or creative input.

Recent Form

Ayase Ueda is currently enjoying a prolific season as Feyenoord's leading striker, demonstrating exceptional goal-scoring ability in the 2025/2026 campaign. He has netted 23 goals in 28 Eredivisie matches, playing a total of 2269 minutes, which translates to an impressive average of 0.91 goals per 90 minutes. This places him first among Feyenoord players and sixth in the entire Eredivisie for players with at least three appearances. His strong form is further highlighted by a four-goal performance in a 6-1 victory over PEC Zwolle on December 6, 2025, contributing to his best-ever scoring run in the Eredivisie with 18 goals in 15 matches at that point.

Beyond domestic league play, Ueda has also made an impact in European competitions during the 2025/2026 season, scoring one goal in the Europa League and another in the Champions League. His Champions League scoring debut came on October 23, 2024, when he scored the opening goal in a 3-1 win against Benfica. In the preceding 2024/2025 season, he registered 14 goals across all competitions, including a hat-trick in the Asian Cup, showcasing his consistent offensive threat.

Tactical Profile

Ayase Ueda operates as a central forward, embodying the "Marksman" archetype with a "Shadow Striker" role, characterized by his intelligent movement and clinical finishing. He is described as a "Poacher Supreme" who excels at capitalizing on rebounds, loose balls, and chaotic situations within the penalty area. His ability to time runs perfectly, earning him the moniker "Off-the-Ball Ninja," allows him to find prime scoring positions.

Ueda's playing style is further defined by his clinical precision in front of goal and his effectiveness as a "pressing machine". He possesses strong attributes in finishing, long shots, and headed attempts, with his heading ability being a notable strength due to his jumping power and technique. While he is adept at holding the ball to facilitate team play, his weaknesses are noted in general ball retention and passing. His current success at Feyenoord is partly attributed to the team's vertical, high-pressing system, which aligns well with his instinctive play in transitional moments and broken plays.

Career Highlights

Ueda's professional journey began after developing his skills at Hosei University, leading to his signing with Kashima Antlers in 2019. He quickly established himself as a prolific scorer in the J-League, consistently reaching double-digit goal tallies. Notably, in 2021, he was Kashima's top goalscorer and the fourth highest in the league with 14 goals in 29 appearances.

His move to European football came on July 1, 2022, when he joined Cercle Brugge on a four-season contract. He made a significant impact in Belgium, scoring 22 goals and providing 2 assists in his debut campaign in the Belgian Pro League. This impressive performance led to his transfer to Feyenoord in the summer of 2023 for approximately €9 million. With Feyenoord, Ueda has secured silverware, winning the KNVB Cup in 2024 and the Johan Cruyff Shield in the same year, where he notably scored in the penalty shootout. On the international stage, Ueda debuted for the Japan senior national team on June 17, 2019. He scored his first international goal on June 15, 2023, against El Salvador and achieved his first international hat-trick on November 16, 2023, during a 2026 FIFA World Cup qualification match against Myanmar. He has represented Japan in major tournaments including the 2022 FIFA World Cup, the 2023 AFC Asian Cup (where he scored three goals), and the Copa América, accumulating 16 goals in 38 caps.

Personality

Ayase Ueda is characterized by his polite and modest demeanor, often displaying an understated way of celebrating goals, during which he reflects on his family in Japan and his wife. Despite initial challenges and a period of being a reserve striker at Feyenoord, he maintained an imperturbable and diligent approach, focusing on self-improvement rather than insecurity.

Ueda is known to prefer his own company, though he values the social aspect of his teammates, particularly benefiting from the arrival of his compatriot Tsuyoshi Watanabe, which has positively impacted his confidence and performance. He holds a "rising trend" mindset, believing that goals will naturally follow when he feels confident and in good form. Off the pitch, he is reportedly known as a "huge glutton," typically consuming around three servings per meal.

Condition / Medical History

Ayase Ueda has experienced several periods of absence due to injuries throughout his career. In the 2025/2026 season, he sustained a knock from February 2 to February 14, 2026, and had a brief "rest" period from September 23 to September 25, 2025. The 2024/2025 season saw him out with an unspecified injury from April 13 to April 24, 2025, and a muscle injury from February 13 to March 4, 2025.

A more significant setback occurred from October 31, 2024, to January 17, 2025, due to a hamstring injury, which sidelined him for the remainder of 2024. Other past injuries include an unspecified injury in December 2023, a muscle injury in September 2023, a groin strain in June 2021 that caused him to miss nearly two months, and an ankle injury in July-August 2020.
