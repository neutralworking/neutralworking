## Scouting Notes

A locksmith in the middle of the pitch who reads the game with the stillness of a man who's seen everything twice. Westwood sits, he screens, he recycles possession with metronomic accuracy—the kind of midfielder who makes defenders' lives bearable by never asking them to cover for his mistakes. What he won't do is drive through the lines or carry the ball into the half-space; he's a passer, not a progressor, which means Charlotte's build-up play will only move as fast as his next outlet. At 35, he's exactly what a mid-table MLS side needs: reliable, intelligent, utterly without ego, but also comprehensively limited in what he can impose on a game.
