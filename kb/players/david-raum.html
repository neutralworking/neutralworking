David Raum, the 27-year-old German left-back for RB Leipzig, has established himself as a dynamic and influential figure for both club and country. Known for his attacking prowess and leadership qualities, Raum continues to be a key player in the Bundesliga.

Recent Form

David Raum has demonstrated strong form during the 2025/2026 Bundesliga season, featuring in 28 matches where he has scored 3 goals and provided 6 assists. His overall goal involvement stands at 9 (goals + assists), averaging 0.33 per 90 minutes. As of early 2025, he was creating 2.3 chances per game, ranking him among the top 7 in the Bundesliga for this metric. Recent performances in March and April 2026 highlight his consistent impact, including a high FotMob rating of 8.9 in a 1-0 win against Borussia Mönchengladbach and an assist in a 5-0 victory over Hoffenheim. On the international stage, Raum scored his first senior goal for Germany on October 10, 2025, converting a free-kick in a 4-0 FIFA World Cup qualification win against Luxembourg. His growing influence at RB Leipzig was further recognized on August 14, 2025, when he was named the club's new captain.

Tactical Profile

Operating primarily as a left-back or left wing-back, David Raum is renowned for his exceptional attacking contributions, characterized by his speed and stamina. He possesses a top speed of 35.25 km/h, making him one of the fastest players in the Bundesliga, which he leverages for long, attacking runs. Raum's crossing ability is a significant asset, delivering precise and powerful crosses from both open play and set-piece situations, often after driving down the flank. His technical skills, including a strong first touch and linear dribbling, enable him to create numerous dangerous opportunities.

Defensively, Raum is an aggressive and robust player, winning an average of 6.7 tackles per game as of early 2025. He is active in high-press scenarios, often winning possession in the attacking third, and boasts a defensive stability rating of 85.6, along with winning 63.25% of his ground duels. His tactical versatility, stemming from his youth career as a forward, allows him to be comfortable on the ball and adapt to various formations, such as a lateral player in a 3-5-2 system, drawing comparisons to David Alaba for his all-around game.

Career Highlights

David Raum has accumulated several significant honors throughout his career. With RB Leipzig, he won the DFB-Pokal in the 2022–23 season and the DFL-Supercup in 2023. Internationally, he was a key member of the Germany U21 squad that triumphed in the UEFA European Under-21 Championship in 2021. He made his senior debut for the German national team on September 5, 2021, and was included in the squad for the 2022 FIFA World Cup, starting all three group stage matches. Raum also participated in UEFA Euro 2024, notably assisting Niclas Füllkrug's equalizing goal against Switzerland. His club career also includes achieving promotion to the 1st Bundesliga with SpVgg Greuther Fürth in the 2020/21 season.

Personality

David Raum is characterized by a self-confident and passionate demeanor, actively motivating his teammates both on and off the pitch. He is recognized as an emotional leader who brings energy and positivity to his team. His appointment as RB Leipzig captain in August 2025 was met with immense pride, which he viewed as a significant honor and a validation of his consistent performance. Raum is known for being a vocal presence on the field, though he strives for clear communication with referees despite his emotional intensity. Outside of football, he enjoys playing golf and has a keen interest in Mixed Martial Arts. He values his close relationships, particularly with his wife, who has been a supportive figure throughout his professional journey. Raum approaches public criticism with humor, likening it to having a bad workday publicized, and has learned to manage it effectively.

Condition / Medical History

David Raum sustained an ankle ligament injury in his right ankle on October 6, 2024, during a match against Heidenheim, which necessitated surgery and sidelined him for an extended period, making him unlikely to play again in 2024. This injury also led to his early withdrawal from the German national team camp in October 2024. His rehabilitation involved an initial phase in Leipzig, followed by two weeks at the Red Bull Athlete Performance Center near Salzburg, before returning to team training. Other recorded injuries in his career include muscle injuries in April 2025 and May 2024, another ankle injury from December 2022 to January 2023, a hip injury in April 2025, and minor issues such as a knock in September 2023, a leg injury in November 2021, and rib problems in late 2022.
