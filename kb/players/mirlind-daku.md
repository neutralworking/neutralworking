## Scouting Notes

A stocky, aggressive centre-forward who thrives in direct, physical football — exactly the profile Rubin Kazan's system demands. Daku is a genuine handful in the box: strong hold-up play, decent aerial presence, and the kind of workrate that endears him to Russian football's no-nonsense culture. The problem is his technical ceiling. He's not a false nine or a number nine who can drop into build-up; he's a battering ram, and once defences sit deep and compact, his influence narrows considerably. Limited with his back to goal beyond the immediate chest-down-and-spin routine. Fine for the Russian Premier League, where he'll score against mid-table sides, but he'd be genuinely exposed in a top-five league where defenders are sharper and don't give him the space to lumber into.
