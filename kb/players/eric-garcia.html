Recent Form

Eric Garcia has demonstrated strong and consistent form for Barcelona during the 2025/2026 season, featuring in 29 La Liga matches and playing a total of 2343 minutes. He has contributed offensively with 1 goal and 2 assists in the league, while maintaining a solid defensive record of 0.77 goals conceded per 90 minutes and a 38% clean sheet ratio. His passing accuracy is particularly notable, ranking in the 98th percentile with 75.60 passes per 90 minutes.

Under Hansi Flick's management, Garcia has become a consistent starter, with his defensive and offensive output significantly improving. His tackles per 90 minutes have increased by 50%, and interceptions by 8%, indicating a strong adaptation to the tactical demands of the current system. Garcia's versatility was also evident in the 2024/2025 season, where he played important roles across center-back, midfield, and right-back positions, scoring a crucial goal in a 4-3 victory against Real Madrid and reaching 100 appearances for Barcelona in March 2025.

Tactical Profile

Eric Garcia is primarily a center-back but is also adept at playing as a full-back or defensive midfielder, showcasing significant tactical flexibility. He is characterized as a "classic Barça defender" who excels in playing the ball out from the back, demonstrating exceptional intelligence in reading the game and anticipating opponent movements. His confidence on the ball and technical giftedness are central to his style.

In possession, Garcia shines in build-up phases, perfectly suiting Barcelona's possession-based play. He is skilled at filtering passes into midfield, identifying gaps in the opposition's press to effectively progress play. His passing range covers short, medium, and long distances, executed with high accuracy (89-93% completion rate) and effective use of both feet. Defensively, he prioritizes tactical intelligence, commanding and organizing the backline, and preferring to intercept rather than engage in physical duels he is unlikely to win, thereby minimizing risky actions.

While not known for his raw physicality or pace, Garcia has shown improvement in aerial duels, winning 1.81 per 90 minutes, a high rate for his position. His success under Hansi Flick's high-line, press-oriented system highlights his tactical IQ and adaptability, which are valued over pure physical attributes in this setup. This makes him a crucial component in a system that demands composure and technical skill from its defenders.

Career Highlights

Eric Garcia is a product of Barcelona's renowned La Masia academy, where he developed from 2008 to 2017 before joining Manchester City. During his time at Manchester City, he made his Premier League debut in 2019 and contributed to the team winning the Premier League in the 2020-21 season, along with three EFL Cups and the Community Shield in 2019.

He rejoined Barcelona in June 2021 on a free transfer. In the 2023-2024 season, Garcia had a successful loan spell at Girona, where his contributions helped the club secure a third-place finish in La Liga and qualify for the Champions League. Upon his return to Barcelona in 2024-2025, he played a significant role under Hansi Flick.

Garcia has accumulated numerous titles throughout his career, including two La Liga titles (2022-23, 2024-25), one Copa del Rey (2024-25), and three Supercopa de España titles (2022-23, 2024-25, 2025-26) with Barcelona. On the international stage, he has been a part of successful Spain youth teams, winning the UEFA European Under-17 Championship in 2017 and the UEFA European Under-19 Championship in 2019. He also earned an Olympic Gold Medal with Spain U23 at the 2024 Paris Olympics and a Silver Medal at the 2020 Tokyo Olympics, in addition to winning the UEFA Nations League with the senior national team in 2023.

Personality

Eric Garcia is described as an "incredibly clear guy" and a natural leader, praised by former coach Pep Guardiola for his intelligence. His determination as a defender and his composure when playing the ball out from the back are highly valued qualities. He demonstrates strong leadership on the field, actively commanding and organizing his teammates to maintain defensive shape. Garcia is also noted for his high commitment to the club.

Condition / Medical History

Eric Garcia's medical history includes a few past injuries, such as head injuries in June and September 2020, an overload in December 2020, and more recently, a knee injury and a back injury in January 2024, followed by a muscle injury in October 2024. In January 2026, he experienced a minor fitness niggle during a Champions League match against Copenhagen, leading to a precautionary substitution. However, this was not serious, and he was expected to be fit for the subsequent match.
