Born26 March 2004

Adelaide manager Carl Veart has described him as a “player who can go all the way to the top”, which is why the Guinea-born Australia striker is staying put for now. As other young local talents with little match experience rush abroad at the first chance, Toure is taking the time to develop and condition his body to utilise his speed – he can run up to 36km/h but has only two senior starts. In February 2020 he became the A-League’s youngest-ever goal scorer at 15 years and 326 days, having been drafted into the senior squad as injury cover. Since then the younger brother of fellow Reds forward Al Hassan, and older sibling of youth player Musa, has scored a further three goals over 15 appearances, mainly off the bench. **[Emma Kemp](https://twitter.com/emmavkemp)**  
[Watch Mohamed Toure in action](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8pMyzehGbkM)  
  
**September 2022 update** Battled injury during Adelaide United’s 2021-22 A-League Men season but still earned a move, alongside teammate Yaya Dukuly, to Ligue 1 side Stade de Reims. Has already begun scoring for the club’s youth team.  
  
**October 2023 update** After transferring to Reims, Touré impressed for the reserve team, scoring eight goals in 14 appearances, before making his Ligue 1 debut as a substitute in May. But in search of more senior game time the forward made the move to Ligue 2 club Paris FC on loan. Touré got his first national team call-up for the AFC Under-23 Asian Cup qualifiers but had to withdraw because of injury.
