Snapshot

The Irish goalkeeper has genuine shot-stopping ability and distributes the ball with composure for a keeper of his ilk. He reads the game well, comes off his line confidently, and has the physical tools. The issue is that he's a steady, dependable operator rather than an elite one — his reflexes aren't consistently sharp, he can be vulnerable to cutbacks from wide positions, and his kicking range, while fine, doesn't unlock play the way a truly top-level keeper's would. He's a quality Championship or mid-table Premier League option.

Caoimhin Kelleher is a 27-year-old Irish goalkeeper currently playing for Brentford. Standing at 188 cm and preferring his right foot, Kelleher is known for his "Wall" archetype and "Comandante" role, indicating a strong presence and shot-stopping ability.

Recent Form

During the 2025-2026 season with Brentford, Caoimhin Kelleher has been a regular starter, making 30 to 31 appearances in the Premier League. He has recorded 82 to 84 saves and conceded 39 to 41 goals, maintaining 8 clean sheets across these matches. His save percentage for the season stands at approximately 67-67.7%.

Kelleher demonstrated strong performances in October 2025, notably making a crucial penalty save against Manchester United and delivering an accomplished all-round display in a match against Manchester City. His long-range distribution has also been highlighted as a strength, leading all Premier League goalkeepers in completion rate for passes over 40 yards at that time. Recent match results for Brentford in March and April 2026 have included several draws and losses, reflecting a mixed period for the team.

Tactical Profile

Kelleher is recognized for his excellent technical skills, combining agility and calmness with effective distribution, which allows him to not only stop shots but also initiate quick attacks. His strengths include exceptional shot-stopping abilities, particularly his reflexes and proficiency in saving close-range efforts. He is also noted as a penalty specialist, capable of remaining calm under pressure and reading the body language of penalty takers.

His tactical intelligence is evident in his understanding of game flow, enabling him to position himself effectively to block shots and make crucial saves. Kelleher exhibits strong command of his penalty area, confidently handling crosses and communicating clearly to organize his defense. Furthermore, he is adept with his feet and confident in ball distribution, a vital attribute for modern goalkeepers.

Career Highlights

Caoimhin Kelleher joined Brentford in June 2025 from Liverpool, signing a five-year contract in a transfer reportedly worth £12.5 million. During his decade at Liverpool, he was part of squads that achieved significant success, including winning the Premier League twice (2019-2020, 2024-2025), the UEFA Champions League (2018-2019), the FA Cup (2021-2022), and the UEFA Super Cup (2019). He also secured the EFL Cup twice (2021-2022, 2023-2024).

A memorable moment in his career came in the 2022 Carabao Cup final, where he scored the winning penalty in a shootout against Chelsea. Kelleher is also recognized for his penalty-saving prowess, notably denying Kylian Mbappé from the spot in a Champions League match in November 2024. He made his senior international debut for the Republic of Ireland in June 2021 and has since accumulated 31 caps as of March 2026.

Personality

Caoimhin Kelleher is frequently described as laidback, chilled, calm, and composed, a trait he believes helps instill confidence in his teammates. He maintains a level head regardless of performance, not letting successes or mistakes overly affect him. While he prefers to avoid the spotlight, he understands it is a part of achieving success in football.
