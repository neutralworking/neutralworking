Keane Lewis-Potter is a 25-year-old English wide player for Brentford, known for his right-footed play and role as a wide playmaker. Standing at 170 cm, he has demonstrated versatility and a strong work ethic throughout his career.

Recent Form

In the 2025-2026 Premier League season, Keane Lewis-Potter has made 29 to 30 appearances, contributing 3 goals and 2 assists for Brentford. His performance statistics indicate an average of 0.19 goals per 90 minutes and a total of 5 goal contributions (goals + assists) for the season. He has registered 22 shots, with 7 on target, resulting in a shooting accuracy of 31.82%. Lewis-Potter maintains a passing accuracy of 71.74% and has been noted for his crossing ability, particularly when deployed as a left-back, where he has completed three or more accurate crosses in recent matches.

Tactical Profile

Lewis-Potter primarily operates as a winger, left-back, or left wing-back, showcasing significant positional versatility. His playing style is characterized by a preference for dribbling, shooting from distance, and playing long balls. Strengths in his game include crossing, finishing, and direct free-kicks, while areas for development include holding onto the ball, aerial duels, and general passing and tackling. He possesses offensive intelligence, pace, and an eye for goal, making him well-suited for creating chances as an inside forward. Notably, he has adapted to playing as a full-back in a four-man defense, learning defensive responsibilities and utilizing his winger's insight to anticipate opponents.

Career Highlights

Keane Lewis-Potter joined Brentford in July 2022 from Hull City in a club-record deal, initially valued at £16 million and potentially rising to £20 million with add-ons. He signed a six-year contract with the club, including an option for an additional 12 months. Prior to his move to the Premier League, Lewis-Potter was a product of Hull City's youth academy. During his time at Hull, he was instrumental in their League One title win in the 2020-2021 season. In the 2021-2022 season, he scored 13 goals and provided 4 assists, earning him three significant accolades at Hull City: Player of the Year, Players' Player of the Year, and Supporters' Player of the Year. He made his senior debut for Hull City in January 2019 and scored his first professional goal in November 2019.

Condition / Medical History

Keane Lewis-Potter has experienced several injuries throughout his career. In 2024, he sustained a muscle injury in August, while 2023 saw him sidelined with a calf injury from October to November and a knee injury from March to June. He also dealt with a knock injury in late 2022 and a hamstring injury in early 2020. A hip problem during Brentford's pre-season in 2024 threatened his campaign, but he demonstrated resilience to overcome it. His first season at Brentford saw limited Premier League minutes, largely due to a knee injury in March 2023.
