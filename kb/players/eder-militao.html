Recent Form

Éder Militão has experienced a season marked by significant injury setbacks, impacting his consistent involvement with Real Madrid. In the 2025/2026 La Liga season, he has made 15 appearances, contributing 2 goals and 1 assist, maintaining an average FotMob rating of 7.41. He has also featured in 5 Champions League matches during this period.

A serious hamstring injury sustained in December 2025 against Celta Vigo sidelined him for approximately four months, with an anticipated return in April 2026. Prior to this, he had been considered one of Real Madrid's most reliable defenders, even earning La Liga's September Goal of the Month award. Real Madrid has been carefully managing his rehabilitation to prevent further relapses, and he has recently resumed training with the ball.

Tactical Profile

Éder Militão is a versatile defender, primarily operating as a center-back but also capable of playing at right-back. He is characterized by his speed, aggression, and athleticism, which provide him with a significant advantage in defensive duels and make him formidable in aerial contests due to his impressive jumping ability. His agility and maneuverability are notable for a player of his stature, enabling quick turns in one-on-one situations.

As a modern defender, Militão's pace allows him to cover substantial ground, effectively regaining possession during counter-attacks and defending on the front foot against fast attackers. He demonstrates strong tactical awareness, anticipating plays and adapting his positioning based on opponents' preferred foot. Furthermore, he is proficient in playing diagonal passes from the back, a skill that disrupts opposition formations and initiates offensive movements. While occasionally impulsive in his tackling and passing choices, he has shown continuous improvement in these areas with experience.

Career Highlights

Éder Militão has amassed an impressive collection of titles since joining Real Madrid in 2019 for a reported €50 million after a single season with Porto. With Real Madrid, he has secured multiple La Liga titles (2019–20, 2021–22, 2023–24), UEFA Champions League trophies (2021–22, 2023–24), UEFA Super Cups (2022, 2024), the Copa del Rey (2022–23), FIFA Club World Cups (2022, 2024), and Supercopa de España titles (2019–20, 2021–22, 2023–24).

On the international stage, Militão was part of the Brazil squad that won the Copa América in 2019. He made his senior international debut for Brazil in 2018 and has since established himself as a key figure in the national team's defense. His individual accolades include being named in the Primeira Liga Team of the Year for the 2018–19 season and the La Liga Team of the Season for both 2021–22 and 2022–23.

Personality

Éder Militão is widely regarded as a "real winner" who embodies a "South-American mentality," consistently dedicating his full effort to achieve victory. Off the pitch, he is described as introverted, yet he undergoes a transformation on the field, becoming a vocal and assertive presence who does not hesitate to motivate his teammates. He is generally calm, though there is an acknowledgment that he could improve in managing his emotions during matches and avoiding confrontations with opponents or referees. His personal values strongly emphasize time spent with family and friends. He is also characterized by a strong drive for success, adaptability, and an awareness of his public image, traits often associated with "The Achiever" personality type.

Condition / Medical History

Éder Militão has faced a challenging period with recurring injuries over the past few seasons. Most recently, he suffered a hamstring injury in December 2025, which was expected to keep him out of action until April 2026. This followed a groin injury in November 2025 and an ankle injury in September 2025.

A more significant setback occurred in August 2023 when he tore the Anterior Cruciate Ligament (ACL) in his left knee, leading to a nine-month recovery period that lasted until March 2024. He also experienced another ACL tear in his right knee with meniscus damage, which sidelined him for eight months and limited his appearances, including a brief return during the Club World Cup semifinal in July 2025. These consecutive injuries have considerably impacted his availability for Real Madrid over the last three seasons, prompting the club to adopt a cautious approach to his rehabilitation to prevent further relapses.
