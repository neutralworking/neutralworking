# Box Entry

> Not every attack creates a shot, but consistent access into the box is one of the clearest signs that an attack is working.

## Definition

A box entry is a controlled action that brings the ball into the penalty area, whether by pass, carry, or reception. It is a useful way of describing attacking access before the final action.

## What It Is For

- measure meaningful attacking penetration
- understand how teams access dangerous zones
- evaluate whether circulation is producing threat
- connect progression patterns to shot creation

## Common Types

### Pass Into the Box

The ball is delivered into the area for a receiver.

### Carry Into the Box

A player drives the ball into the area directly.

### Cut-Back Access

The attack reaches the byline or half-space and enters the box through a pull-back.

## Why It Matters

Teams can accumulate territory, possession, and field tilt without truly threatening. Box entries are one of the cleaner indicators that the attack is actually entering dangerous ground.

## Role Implications

This matters for:

- wingers who can beat the first defender
- interiors who slip passes through traffic
- strikers who receive on the move
- full-backs who create cut-back structures

## Scouting Relevance

Useful questions:

- does the player create box access or just circulate around it?
- are their entries controlled and repeatable?
- do they enter from good angles or low-value ones?

Box-entry value also depends on the route. Not every touch in the area is equally dangerous.

## Common Failure Modes

- counting hopeful balls as quality box entries
- confusing mere presence in the box with controlled access
- ignoring the route and body shape of the receiver
